<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Hurricane Patricia</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Hurricane Patricia</b> was the second-most intense <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> on record worldwide, behind <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Tip" title="Typhoon Tip">Typhoon Tip</a> in 1979, with a minimum <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">atmospheric pressure</a> of 872 mbar (hPa; 25.75 inHg).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Originating from a sprawling disturbance near the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Tehuantepec" title="Gulf of Tehuantepec">Gulf of Tehuantepec</a>, south of Mexico, in mid-October 2015, Patricia was first classified a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" title="Tropical depression">tropical depression</a> on October 20. Initial development was slow, with only modest strengthening within the first day of its classification. The system later became a tropical storm and was named Patricia, the twenty-fourth named storm of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="2015 Pacific hurricane season">annual hurricane season</a>. Exceptionally favorable environmental conditions fueled <a href="/wiki/Rapid_intensification" title="Rapid intensification">explosive intensification</a> on October 22. A well-defined <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> developed within an intense <a href="/wiki/Central_dense_overcast" title="Central dense overcast">central dense overcast</a> and Patricia grew from a tropical storm to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_5" title="SSHS">Category 5 hurricane</a> in just 24 hours—a near-record pace. On October 23, the hurricane achieved its record peak intensity with <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> of 215 mph (345 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[nb 1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[nb 2]</a></sup> This made it the most intense tropical cyclone on record in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>, and the strongest globally in terms of 1-minute maximum sustained winds.</p>
<p>Late on October 23, dramatic weakening ensued and Patricia made <a href="/wiki/Landfall" title="Landfall">landfall</a> near <a href="/wiki/Cuixmala" title="Cuixmala">Cuixmala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jalisco" title="Jalisco">Jalisco</a>, with winds of 150 mph (240 km/h). This made it the strongest landfalling hurricane on record along the Pacific coast of Mexico. Patricia continued to weaken extremely quickly, faster than it had intensified, as it interacted with the mountainous terrain of Mexico. Within 24 hours of moving ashore, Patricia degraded into a tropical depression and dissipated soon thereafter late on October 24.</p>
<p>The precursor to Patricia produced widespread flooding rains in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. Hundreds of thousands of people were directly affected by the storm, mostly in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>. At least six fatalities were attributed to the event: four in <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>, one in Guatemala, and one in <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>. Torrential rains extended into southeastern Mexico, with areas of <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">Veracruz</a> reporting accumulations in excess of 19.7 in (500 mm). Damage in <a href="/wiki/Chetumal" title="Chetumal">Chetumal</a> reached 1.4 billion <a href="/wiki/Mexican_peso" title="Mexican peso">pesos</a> (US$84.1 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[nb 3]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a tropical cyclone, Patricia's effects in Mexico were tremendous; however, the affected areas were predominantly rural, mitigating a potential large-scale disaster. Violent winds tore roofs from structures and stripped coastal areas of their vegetation. Preliminary assessments indicated hundreds of homes to be destroyed; seven fatalities were linked to the hurricane directly or indirectly, including one during evacuations. Total damage was estimated to be in excess of 5.4 billion pesos (US$323.3 million), with agriculture and infrastructure comprising the majority of losses. Flooding partially associated with remnant moisture from Patricia inflicted US$52.5 million in damage across <a href="/wiki/South_Texas" title="South Texas">Southern Texas</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_history_of_Hurricane_Patricia" title="Meteorological history of Hurricane Patricia">Meteorological history of Hurricane Patricia</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Patricia_2015_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1854" data-file-width="3000" height="155" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Patricia_2015_track.png/250px-Patricia_2015_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Patricia_2015_track.png/375px-Patricia_2015_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Patricia_2015_track.png/500px-Patricia_2015_track.png 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Patricia_2015_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On October 11, 2015, an area of disturbed weather traversed <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> and emerged over the eastern Pacific Ocean. The disturbance moved slowly over the next few days, later merging with a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> on October 15. The merger of these systems and the effects of a concurrent <a href="/wiki/Tehuantepecer" title="Tehuantepecer">Tehuantepec gap wind event</a> spurred the formation of a broad <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">area of low pressure</a>. This feature gradually consolidated and became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" title="Tropical depression">tropical depression</a> shortly after 00:00 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on October 20; at this time the depression was situated roughly 205 mi (335 km) south-southeast of <a href="/wiki/Salina_Cruz" title="Salina Cruz">Salina Cruz</a>, Mexico. A <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">mid-level ridge</a> to the north steered the depression generally west and later pulled it north along an arcing path.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Development was initially slowed by locally cooler <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperatures</a> and dry air, and the depression became Tropical Storm Patricia later that day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Once clear of the unfavorable region, Patricia traversed anomalously warm waters within an environment exceptionally conducive to <a href="/wiki/Rapid_intensification" title="Rapid intensification">rapid intensification</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[2]</a></sup> Dramatic strengthening began late on October 21 and continued through October 23. Patricia reached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_1" title="SSHS">hurricane strength</a> shortly after 00:00 UTC on October 22,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> featuring prominent <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a>, well-defined banding features,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D8_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-D8-6">[3]</a></sup> and a developing <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D7_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-D7-7">[4]</a></sup> Data from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a> investigating the cyclone indicated Patricia to have reached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_4" title="SSHS">Category 4 status</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> by 18:00 UTC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D10_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-D10-8">[5]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">

<div class="thumbcaption">

Satellite animation of Patricia making landfall on October 23</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>By the early hours of October 23, a clear northward turn took place followed by acceleration northeast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D11_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-D11-9">[6]</a></sup> A solid ring of −130 °F (−90 °C) cloud tops surrounded the hurricane's 12 mi (19 km) wide eye and signaled its intensification into a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_5" title="SSHS">Category 5 hurricane</a>. In a 24-hour time span, Patricia's <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> increased by 120 mph (195 km/h), the fastest such intensification in any hurricane observed by the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC). The hurricane achieved its peak around 12:00 UTC on October 23, with estimated winds around 215 mph (345 km/h) and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of 872 mbar (hPa; 25.75 inHg); these values are based upon continued intensification after a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunter" title="Hurricane Hunter">Hurricane Hunter</a> mission into the storm six hours prior. In the NHC's report on Patricia, it is noted that the hurricane may have surpassed <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Tip" title="Typhoon Tip">Typhoon Tip</a> as the strongest tropical cyclone ever observed, but lack of direct observations at the time of its peak prevent analysis of such.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later on October 23, rapid weakening ensued as an <a href="/wiki/Eyewall_replacement_cycle" title="Eyewall replacement cycle">eyewall replacement cycle</a> took shape and wind shear increased. In the five hours up until <a href="/wiki/Landfall" title="Landfall">landfall</a> in Mexico, Patricia weakened at an unprecedented rate while still over water. However, it remained a violent hurricane and moved ashore around 23:00 UTC near <a href="/wiki/Cuixmala" title="Cuixmala">Cuixmala</a>, Jalisco, with winds of 150 mph (240 km/h) and an analyzed pressure of 932 mbar (hPa; 27.49 inHg). This made it the most intense Pacific hurricane on record to strike Mexico. Once onshore, the high terrain of the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Occidental" title="Sierra Madre Occidental">Sierra Madre mountains</a> accelerated Patricia's weakening. The low- and mid-level circulations of the tropical cyclone decoupled, with the latter accelerating northeast, and Patricia dissipated on October 24 over central Mexico, less than 18 hours after moving ashore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Records">Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_history_of_Hurricane_Patricia#Records" title="Meteorological history of Hurricane Patricia">Meteorological history of Hurricane Patricia § Records</a></div>
<p>Hurricane Patricia set multiple records for maximum strength, rate of intensification, and rate of weakening throughout its relatively short existence. With maximum sustained winds of 215 mph (345 km/h) and a minimum pressure of 872 mbar (hPa; 25.75 inHg), Patricia is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_tropical_cyclone_records" title="List of tropical cyclone records">most intense tropical cyclone</a> ever observed in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>. In terms of central pressure, it is also the second-most intense tropical cyclone ever recorded worldwide, just shy of <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Tip" title="Typhoon Tip">Typhoon Tip</a> in 1979 which had a minimum pressure of 870 mbar (hPa; 25.69 inHg).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Patricia's one-minute maximum sustained winds ranked as the highest ever reliably observed or estimated in a tropical cyclone, surpassing <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Haiyan" title="Typhoon Haiyan">Typhoon Haiyan</a> of 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StrongestWHem_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-StrongestWHem-10">[7]</a></sup> The magnitude of Patricia's rapid intensification is among the fastest ever observed. In a 24-hour period, 06:00–06:00 UTC October 22–23, its maximum sustained winds increased from 85 mph (140 km/h) to 205 mph (335 km/h), a record increase of 120 mph (195 km/h). During the same period, Patricia's central pressure fell by 95 mbar (hPa; 2.81 inHg). Despite record over-water weakening prior to striking Mexico, Patricia became the most intense Pacific hurricane to make landfall, with a pressure of 932 mbar (hPa; 27.52 inHg).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Before Hurricane Patricia arrived, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_Mexico" title="Government of Mexico">Government of Mexico</a> issued multiple <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">watches and warnings</a> for coastal communities. A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_watch" title="Hurricane watch">hurricane watch</a> was first raised at 09:00 UTC on October 21, encompassing areas of <a href="/wiki/Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Michoacán">Michoacán</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colima" title="Colima">Colima</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jalisco" title="Jalisco">Jalisco</a>. A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm_watch" title="Tropical storm watch">tropical storm watch</a> also covered portions of <a href="/wiki/Guerrero" title="Guerrero">Guerrero</a>. As Patricia intensified, the government issued a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_warning" title="Hurricane warning">hurricane warning</a> for areas between <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Corrientes,_Jalisco" title="Cabo Corrientes, Jalisco">Cabo Corrientes</a>, Jalisco, and Punta San Telmo, Michoacán; a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm_warning" title="Tropical storm warning">tropical storm warning</a> supplemented this for areas farther north and east. The hurricane warning was extended northward to include areas south of <a href="/wiki/San_Blas,_Nayarit" title="San Blas, Nayarit">San Blas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nayarit" title="Nayarit">Nayarit</a>, on October 23. Once the storm moved inland and the threat of damaging winds diminished, these warnings were gradually discontinued on October 24.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

Evacuation from hotel Riu Vallarta on morning before Hurricane Patricia made landfall</div>

<p>Following Patricia's upgrade to Category 5 status, the National Hurricane Center called the storm "potentially catastrophic",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[8]</a></sup> a source of criticism and praise from various media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes1_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes1-12">[9]</a></sup> Citing the relatively limited damage and loss of life, some outlets, including the <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[10]</a></sup> claimed the agency was exaggerating the danger posed by the storm. However, most outlets praised them for effectively communicating the dire threat and potentially saving lives from an exceptionally powerful hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes1_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes1-12">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Across Michoacán, Colima, Jalisco, and Nayarit, 1,782 shelters were opened on October 22 with a collective capacity of 258,000 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[12]</a></sup> Officials in <a href="/wiki/Manzanillo,_Colima" title="Manzanillo, Colima">Manzanillo</a> began distributing sandbags during the afternoon of October 22.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[13]</a></sup> Schools across <a href="/wiki/Guerrero" title="Guerrero">Guerrero</a> and Jalisco suspended activities for October 22 and 23, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[15]</a></sup> Civil protection officials planned to evacuate roughly 50,000 people from Colima, Jalisco, and Nayarit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[16]</a></sup> To expedite the process, 2 aircraft and 600 buses were used to shuttle evacuees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cabo_Corrientes_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cabo_Corrientes-20">[17]</a></sup> Ultimately, 8,500 people evacuated prior to the storm's arrival, including 2,600 in Cabo Corrientes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cabo_Corrientes_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cabo_Corrientes-20">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElUniversalDamage_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-ElUniversalDamage-21">[18]</a></sup> One person died during an evacuation in Jalisco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[19]</a></sup> Roughly 25,000 personnel from the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Army" title="Mexican Army">Mexican Army</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Navy" title="Mexican Navy">Navy</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_Federal_Police" title="Mexican Federal Police">Federal Police</a> were deployed preemptively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UnivisionOct26_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-UnivisionOct26-23">[20]</a></sup> More than 500 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> volunteers were on standby.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RW2_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-RW2-24">[21]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Red_Cross" title="Mexican Red Cross">Mexican Red Cross</a> prepositioned food for 3,500 families.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RW1_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-RW1-25">[22]</a></sup> Approximately 30 kg (66 lb) of aid was prepositioned in Colima for distribution after the hurricane's passage. The Electric Federal Commission dispatched 2,500 crewmen, 152 cranes, 15 all-terrain vehicles, 4 helicopters, and 84 generators to cope with potential power outages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RW2_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-RW2-24">[21]</a></sup> Mexican authorities received praise for effectively carrying out emergency preparations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTOct24_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOct24-26">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_from_precursor_disturbance">Impact from precursor disturbance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Impact from precursor disturbance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pre-Patricia_2015-10-17_1930Z.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="8593" data-file-width="6897" height="274" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Pre-Patricia_2015-10-17_1930Z.jpg/220px-Pre-Patricia_2015-10-17_1930Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Pre-Patricia_2015-10-17_1930Z.jpg/330px-Pre-Patricia_2015-10-17_1930Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Pre-Patricia_2015-10-17_1930Z.jpg/440px-Pre-Patricia_2015-10-17_1930Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pre-Patricia_2015-10-17_1930Z.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The sprawling precursor to Hurricane Patricia over Central America on October 17</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The precursor of Patricia was a large and sprawling system that affected much of <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> with heavy rain for several days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guat1_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guat1-27">[24]</a></sup> Rainfall was enhanced by the presence of multiple systems, including a tropical wave and a second disturbance over the Caribbean Sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> One person was killed in the <a href="/wiki/Alta_Verapaz_Department" title="Alta Verapaz Department">Alta Verapaz Department</a> and approximately 2,100 people required evacuation across Guatemala.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guat1_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guat1-27">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guat2_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guat2-28">[25]</a></sup> A total of 442 homes and 28,200 ha (70,000 acres) of crops were damaged while roughly 223,000 people were affected by flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guat3_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guat3-29">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[27]</a></sup> Government officials deployed emergency teams and 40 million <a href="/wiki/Guatemalan_quetzal" title="Guatemalan quetzal">quetzal</a> (US$5.4 million) was available for relief operations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guat3_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guat3-29">[26]</a></sup> In nearby <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a> between 6.3 and 7.3 in (160 and 185 mm) of rain fell, causing similar floods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElSalv1_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-ElSalv1-31">[28]</a></sup> Dozens of homes were affected and four people were killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElSalv1_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-ElSalv1-31">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElSalv2_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-ElSalv2-32">[29]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Goascor%C3%A1n_River" title="Goascorán River">Goascorán River</a> overflowed its banks twice in two days, inundating surrounding communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElSalv1_31-2"><a href="#cite_note-ElSalv1-31">[28]</a></sup> Owing to widespread flooding, authorities suspended school activities across the country on October 19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElSalv2_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-ElSalv2-32">[29]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>, a landslide buried four miners in <a href="/wiki/Bonanza,_North_Caribbean_Coast_Autonomous_Region" title="Bonanza, North Caribbean Coast Autonomous Region">Bonanza</a>; one died while the others were rescued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[30]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Ul%C3%BAa_River" title="Ulúa River">Ulúa River</a> in <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> over-topped its banks for the first time in 17 years on October 18, prompting the evacuation of more than 200 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[32]</a></sup> Furthermore, flooding damaged 10 homes in <a href="/wiki/Jac%C3%B3,_Costa_Rica" title="Jacó, Costa Rica">Jacó, Costa Rica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>The aforementioned rains extended into Southwestern and Northeastern Mexico, falling from October 18–20; precipitation was enhanced by another tropical disturbance over the northwestern Caribbean Sea. Portions of <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a> saw their most intense rainfall event on record, with 19.8 in (502 mm) observed in <a href="/wiki/Chetumal" title="Chetumal">Chetumal</a>. This surpassed the previous record of 18 in (450 mm) during Hurricane Wilma in 2005. Flooding affected approximately 1,500 homes in the city, with 150 people seeking refuge in public shelters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[34]</a></sup> Damage in Chetumal was pegged at 1.4 billion <a href="/wiki/Mexican_peso" title="Mexican peso">pesos</a> (US$84.1 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[35]</a></sup> Seven municipalities were declared <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disaster_areas" title="Disaster areas">disaster areas</a> accordingly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[36]</a></sup> In nearby <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">Veracruz</a>, more than 24 in (600 mm) of rain caused widespread flooding; at least 50 municipalities reported damage from the event as multiple rivers over-topped their banks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[37]</a></sup> Flooding in <a href="/wiki/Tabasco" title="Tabasco">Tabasco</a> affected 7,500 ha (19,000 acres) of crops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[38]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Oaxaca" title="Oaxaca">Oaxaca</a>, several roads were washed out by the accompanying floods and mudslides, particularly in the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Norte_de_Oaxaca" title="Sierra Norte de Oaxaca">Sierra Norte region</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[39]</a></sup> Widespread flooding affected large areas of <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guerrero" title="Guerrero">Guerrero</a>, prompting dozens of evacuations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[40]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Tapachula" title="Tapachula">Tapachula</a>, Chiapas, 6.6 in (168 mm) of rain fell in just 90 minutes, triggering flash floods that caught residents by surprise. Hundreds of families were affected and 64 homes were damaged or destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_as_a_tropical_cyclone">Impact as a tropical cyclone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Impact as a tropical cyclone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hurricane Patricia made landfall in Jalisco as a Category 4 hurricane during the evening of October 23. The 15 mi (24 km) wide core of Patricia largely avoided populated centers, passing through areas with a population density less than 30 people per square mile. This, alongside effective evacuations, is credited for the relatively low death toll, despite the hurricane's intensity. Several small communities along the path sustained tremendous damage, however.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WUOct26_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-WUOct26-45">[42]</a></sup> Had the hurricane moved farther east or west, the densely populated areas surrounding <a href="/wiki/Manzanillo,_Colima" title="Manzanillo, Colima">Manzanillo</a> or <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Vallarta" title="Puerto Vallarta">Puerto Vallarta</a> would have suffered a direct hit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTOct24_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOct24-26">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Throughout the affected region, the storm's powerful winds left 261,989 people without electricity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElUniversalDamage_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-ElUniversalDamage-21">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UnivisionOct26_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-UnivisionOct26-23">[20]</a></sup> Twenty-one of the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Mexico" title="States of Mexico">nation's thirty-one states</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_Federal_District" title="Mexican Federal District">Federal District</a>, were affected by the hurricane's rain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[43]</a></sup> Accumulations in Jalisco peaked at 15.09 in (383.2 mm) on <a href="/wiki/Volc%C3%A1n_de_Colima" title="Volcán de Colima">Nevado de Colima</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[44]</a></sup> Roughly 42,000 ha (100,000 acres) of crops were affected across Colima, Jalisco, Michoacán, and Nayarit, with 15,000 ha (37,000 acres) deemed a total loss and 27,000 ha (67,000 acres) partially damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[45]</a></sup> Six deaths occurred due to direct or indirect effects of the hurricane, all in Jalisco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fox_News_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fox_News-49">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zocalo_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-Zocalo-50">[47]</a></sup> Damage assessments indicate total losses around 5.4 billion pesos (US$323.3 million), primarily from agriculture and infrastructure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElUniversalDamage_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-ElUniversalDamage-21">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JaliscoTotal_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-JaliscoTotal-51">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ColimaTotal_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-ColimaTotal-52">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MichoacanDamage1_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-MichoacanDamage1-53">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MichoacanDamage2_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-MichoacanDamage2-54">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Jalisco">Jalisco</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Jalisco">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_(crop).png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1149" data-file-width="1959" height="129" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_%28crop%29.png/220px-Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_%28crop%29.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_%28crop%29.png/330px-Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_%28crop%29.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_%28crop%29.png/440px-Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_%28crop%29.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Patricia_2015-10-23_2035Z_EVL_(crop).png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Hurricane Patricia hours before making landfall in Jalisco</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Striking Jalisco as a strong Category 4 hurricane, Patricia wrought tremendous damage, though the most extreme effects were relatively localized. In the small community of Emiliano Zapata, the hurricane's violent winds tore roofs off homes and business. Countless trees were defoliated, stripped of their branches, snapped, or uprooted. Hillsides along the immediate landfall area were largely stripped of vegetation. Storm chaser Josh Morgerman described the aftermath: "... the tropical landscape was transformed into something barren and wintry."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iCycloneReport_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-iCycloneReport-55">[52]</a></sup> Furthermore, the winds toppled concrete power poles and crumpled <a href="/wiki/Transmission_tower" title="Transmission tower">transmission towers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iCycloneReport_55-1"><a href="#cite_note-iCycloneReport-55">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>Throughout Jalisco, approximately 9,000 homes were damaged or destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[53]</a></sup> The coastal <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(place)" title="Hamlet (place)">hamlet</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chamela" title="Chamela">Chamela</a>, home to 40 families, was completely flattened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[54]</a></sup> More than 24,000 ha (59,000 acres) of crops were affected across the state: 10,684 ha (26,400 acres) sustained total losses while 13,943 ha (34,450 acres) were partially so. Damage from agriculture amounted to roughly 168 million pesos (US$10.1 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JaliscoCrops_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-JaliscoCrops-58">[55]</a></sup> Total damage reached to 1.139 billion pesos (US$68.7 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JaliscoTotal_51-1"><a href="#cite_note-JaliscoTotal-51">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>A total of six deaths in incidents related to Patricia were reported in the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fox_News_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fox_News-49">[46]</a></sup> Two of these deaths occurred when a tree toppled over in the storm's powerful winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zocalo_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-Zocalo-50">[47]</a></sup> A woman was also hospitalized after sustaining injuries in the same incident. Another four people were killed in an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobile_accident" title="Automobile accident">automobile accident</a> in southern Jalisco. Two of the victims died in the wreckage and the other two died after being brought to a hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fox_News_49-2"><a href="#cite_note-Fox_News-49">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSA_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-JSA-59">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>A cargo ship—the 735 ft (224 m) <a href="/wiki/Bulk_carrier" title="Bulk carrier">bulk carrier</a> <i>Los Llanitos</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[57]</a></sup>—was shifted off-course by the hurricane and grounded near <a href="/wiki/Barra_de_Navidad" title="Barra de Navidad">Barra de Navidad</a>, Jalisco. Her 27 crew were unharmed but required rescue by a military helicopter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9news_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-9news-61">[58]</a></sup> The ship was subsequently deemed a total loss and crews began <a href="/wiki/Ship_breaking" title="Ship breaking">scrapping</a> the vessel on-site in February 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[59]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span id="Colima_and_Michoacán"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Colima_and_Michoac.C3.A1n">Colima and Michoacán</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Colima and Michoacán">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima,_Mexico.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3280" data-file-width="4928" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima%2C_Mexico.jpg/300px-Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima%2C_Mexico.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima%2C_Mexico.jpg/450px-Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima%2C_Mexico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima%2C_Mexico.jpg/600px-Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima%2C_Mexico.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Damage_from_Hurricane_Patricia_2015_in_Colima,_Mexico.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Coastal damage from Patricia in Colima</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In nearby Colima, preliminary surveys indicated localized but severe damage. A total of 200 schools, 107 health facilities, 34 sport facilities, and 11,645 ha (28,780 acres) of agriculture was affected by the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LaJordanaColima_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-LaJordanaColima-63">[60]</a></sup> Severe damage was incurred by the banana crops, with losses from the fruit alone estimated at 500 million pesos (US$30.2 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElUniversalDamage_21-3"><a href="#cite_note-ElUniversalDamage-21">[18]</a></sup> Total damage in the state reached 1.7 billion pesos (US$102.6 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ColimaTotal_52-1"><a href="#cite_note-ColimaTotal-52">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although Patricia made landfall farther west, damage across Michoacán was severe. Numerous communities were temporarily isolated as roads were either rendered impassible or washed away altogether by floods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michoac.C3.A1n1_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-Michoac.C3.A1n1-64">[61]</a></sup> A total of 1,512 homes were damaged and another 127 were destroyed across the state,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[62]</a></sup> including 600 in <a href="/wiki/Coahuayana" title="Coahuayana">Coahuayana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michoac.C3.A1n2_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-Michoac.C3.A1n2-66">[63]</a></sup> The storm severely damaged 150 homes in the <a href="/wiki/Arteaga_Municipality,_Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Arteaga Municipality, Michoacán">Arteaga Municipality</a>. A portion of <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Federal_Highway_200" title="Mexican Federal Highway 200">Highway 200</a> was shut down in <a href="/wiki/Aquila,_Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Aquila, Michoacán">Aquila</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michoac.C3.A1n1_64-1"><a href="#cite_note-Michoac.C3.A1n1-64">[61]</a></sup> Agriculture sustained extensive damage with 10,000 ha (25,000 acres) of crops destroyed; losses in the sector amounted to 2.5 billion pesos (US$151.2 million). In Coahuayana alone, 5,600 ha (14,000 acres) of bananas were ruined, the greatest loss of crops in the municipality's history;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MichoacanDamage1_53-1"><a href="#cite_note-MichoacanDamage1-53">[50]</a></sup> thousands of residents lost their jobs as a result of the damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michoac.C3.A1n2_66-1"><a href="#cite_note-Michoac.C3.A1n2-66">[63]</a></sup> Losses to health facilities in the state reached 13.5 million pesos (US$815,000).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MichoacanDamage2_54-1"><a href="#cite_note-MichoacanDamage2-54">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_states">Other states</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Other states">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The hurricane produced large swells along the shores of Guerrero on October 22, causing damage to coastal structures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[64]</a></sup> The remnants of Patricia brought heavy rain to much of northern Mexico on October 24. Up to 7.6 in (193 mm) of rain was observed in <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a>, resulting in flooding. Dozens of structures sustained damage and hundreds were left without power, with areas in <a href="/wiki/Reynosa" title="Reynosa">Reynosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Bravo,_Tamaulipas" title="Río Bravo, Tamaulipas">Río Bravo</a> particularly affected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-InformadorTamaulipas_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-InformadorTamaulipas-68">[65]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Patricia's direct effects in Texas were limited to <a href="/wiki/South_Texas" title="South Texas">southern areas of the state</a>. Moisture associated with the hurricane streamed ahead of its circulation and interacted with a frontal boundary over the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WUOct24_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-WUOct24-69">[66]</a></sup> Antecedent rains saturated the region and produced severe flooding, with cars flooded and a train derailed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[67]</a></sup> Fears arose that the collective effects of these storms would produce deadly floods, similar to those in <a href="/wiki/2015_Texas%E2%80%93Oklahoma_flood_and_tornado_outbreak" title="2015 Texas–Oklahoma flood and tornado outbreak">May earlier in the year</a>; however, no deaths resulted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[68]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" title="Houston, Texas">Houston</a> received 9.38 in (238 mm) of rain during a 24-hour span from October 24–25,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[69]</a></sup> and a brief <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornado</a> caused minor damage near the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[70]</a></sup> Rain fell at rates of 1 to 2 in (25 to 51 mm) per hour in southern <a href="/wiki/Hidalgo_County,_Texas" title="Hidalgo County, Texas">Hidalgo County</a>, leading to damaging flash floods. An estimated 10 to 12 in (250 to 300 mm), or more, accumulated across the <a href="/wiki/Progreso,_Texas" title="Progreso, Texas">Progreso</a>–<a href="/wiki/Weslaco,_Texas" title="Weslaco, Texas">Weslaco</a> area. Several feet of water rendered roads impassible, stranded cars, and affected more than 500 homes. Twenty-six water rescues were conducted in the area. Damage was estimated at $50 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[71]</a></sup> Several roads in and around <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">Corpus Christi</a> were inundated,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[72]</a></sup> including part of <a href="/wiki/Interstate_37" title="Interstate 37">Interstate 37</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[73]</a></sup> Two hours of "blinding rains" flooded the majority of eastern <a href="/wiki/Willacy_County,_Texas" title="Willacy County, Texas">Willacy County</a>, prompting multiple water rescues, and leaving $2.5 million in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[74]</a></sup> Remnant moisture then moved north and northwest through the Great Lakes, Appalachia and Mid-Atlantic states, and Florida Pandhandle before exiting the nation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[75]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[76]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the aftermath of the hurricane, 5,791 Marines from Mexico's <a href="/wiki/Naval_Infantry_Force" title="Naval Infantry Force">Naval Infantry Force</a> were deployed to assist with recovery and rescue efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[77]</a></sup> Rescuers reached some of the hardest-hit areas within a day of Patricia's landfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTOct24_26-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYTOct24-26">[23]</a></sup> Red Cross volunteers began needs assessments on October 24 and distribution of humanitarian aid started the following day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RW1_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-RW1-25">[22]</a></sup> Power was restored to 88 percent of those affected within two days of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UnivisionOct26_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-UnivisionOct26-23">[20]</a></sup> Through the nation's livestock catastrophe fund, 150 million pesos (US$9 million) were allocated for agricultural areas affected by the storm on October 29. Of this total, 76 million pesos (US$4.6 million) were delegated to Jalisco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[78]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretariat_of_Social_Development" title="Secretariat of Social Development">Secretariat of Social Development</a>'s budget allowed for 250 million pesos (US$15.1 million) in relief funds for Jalisco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zocalo_50-2"><a href="#cite_note-Zocalo-50">[47]</a></sup> Of this, 34 million pesos (US$2 million) were allocated for affected persons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[79]</a></sup> On October 27, <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Pacchiano_Alam%C3%A1n" title="Rafael Pacchiano Alamán">Rafael Pacchiano Alamán</a> announced an initial fund of 5.3 million pesos (US$318,000) through the Temporary Employment Program (Programa de Empleo Temporal) to help spur economic recovery in Colima.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[80]</a></sup> On October 28, 15 of Jalisco's <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Jalisco" title="Municipalities of Jalisco">125 municipalities</a> were declared <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disaster_areas" title="Disaster areas">disaster areas</a>; assessments were ongoing in 5–10 other municipalities to potentially be added to this declaration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[81]</a></sup> Of the more than 24,000 ha (59,000 acres) of crops affected by Patricia in Jalisco, only 6,600 ha (16,000 acres) were covered by insurance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JaliscoCrops_58-1"><a href="#cite_note-JaliscoCrops-58">[55]</a></sup> Through late January 2016, <a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Instituto_Nacional_del_Emprendedor" title="es:Instituto Nacional del Emprendedor">Instituto Nacional del Emprendedor</a> provided entrepreneurs with 3.577 million pesos (US$194,000) in funds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[82]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2016, the Michoacán Ministry of Social Development enacted a 10 million peso (US$542,000) rehabilitation program to build 605 homes in affected communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[83]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Patricia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Pacific_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Pacific hurricane names">List of retired Pacific hurricane names</a></div>
<p>Due to the hurricane's extreme intensity, the name <i>Patricia</i> was retired by the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> on April 25, 2016. It will be replaced with <i>Pamela</i> for the 2021 Pacific hurricane season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[84]</a></sup></p>

<p>Other <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Pacific_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Pacific hurricanes">Category 5 Pacific hurricanes</a> that made landfall in Mexico:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Kenna" title="Hurricane Kenna">Hurricane Kenna</a> in <a href="/wiki/2002_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="2002 Pacific hurricane season">2002</a> – Made landfall at Category 4 intensity north of where Patricia made landfall</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Rick_(2009)" title="Hurricane Rick (2009)">Hurricane Rick</a> in <a href="/wiki/2009_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="2009 Pacific hurricane season">2009</a> – Made landfall as a tropical storm, developed around the same time of the year</li>
</ul>
<p>Other record-strength tropical cyclones:</p>
<div class="div-col columns column-count column-count-2" style="-moz-column-count: 2; -webkit-column-count: 2; column-count: 2;">
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Linda_(1997)" title="Hurricane Linda (1997)">Hurricane Linda</a> in <a href="/wiki/1997_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="1997 Pacific hurricane season">1997</a> – Previous record intensity in eastern Pacific basin, during a <a href="/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_El_Ni%C3%B1o_event" title="1997–98 El Niño event">similarly strong</a> <a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o" title="El Niño">El Niño</a> event</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Megi_(2010)" title="Typhoon Megi (2010)">Typhoon Megi</a> in <a href="/wiki/2010_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="2010 Pacific typhoon season">2010</a> – Research reconnaissance observed similarly intense sustained winds</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Haiyan" title="Typhoon Haiyan">Typhoon Haiyan</a> in <a href="/wiki/2013_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="2013 Pacific typhoon season">2013</a> – Most intense landfalling tropical cyclone in terms of wind speed</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Nancy_(1961)" title="Typhoon Nancy (1961)">Typhoon Nancy</a> in <a href="/wiki/1961_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="1961 Pacific typhoon season">1961</a> – Highest winds observed in a tropical cyclone, tied with Patricia, but considered unreliable</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Tip" title="Typhoon Tip">Typhoon Tip</a> in <a href="/wiki/1979_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="1979 Pacific typhoon season">1979</a> – Most intense tropical cyclone recorded in terms of pressure</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Wilma" title="Hurricane Wilma">Hurricane Wilma</a> in <a href="/wiki/2005_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2005 Atlantic hurricane season">2005</a> – Previous record low central pressure in the Western Hemisphere</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Allen" title="Hurricane Allen">Hurricane Allen</a> in <a href="/wiki/1980_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1980 Atlantic hurricane season">1980</a> – Previous record high sustained winds in the Western Hemisphere</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Forrest_(1983)" title="Typhoon Forrest (1983)">Typhoon Forrest</a> in <a href="/wiki/1983_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="1983 Pacific typhoon season">1983</a> – Record-fastest intensification of any tropical cyclone; underwent a 100 mbar (hPa; 2.95 inHg) pressure drop in a day</li>
</ul>
