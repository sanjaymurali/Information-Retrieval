<p>The <b>2017 North Indian Ocean cyclone season</b> is a current event in the annual cycle of <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> formation. It is one of the deadliest seasons that have almost killed 640 people so far.North Indian Ocean cyclone season has no official bounds, but cyclones tend to form between April and December, with the two peaks in May and November. These dates conventionally delimit the period of each year when most tropical cyclones form in the northern <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>.</p>
<p>The scope of this article is limited to the Indian Ocean in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a>, east of the <a href="/wiki/Horn_of_Africa" title="Horn of Africa">Horn of Africa</a> and west of the <a href="/wiki/Malay_Peninsula" title="Malay Peninsula">Malay Peninsula</a>. There are two main <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">seas</a> in the North Indian Ocean — the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">Arabian Sea</a> to the west of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">Indian subcontinent</a>, abbreviated <i>ARB</i> by the <a href="/wiki/India_Meteorological_Department" title="India Meteorological Department">India Meteorological Department</a> (IMD); and the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a> to the east, abbreviated <i>BOB</i> by the IMD. The official <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Centre" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre">Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre</a> in this basin is the IMD, while the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">Joint Typhoon Warning Center</a> (JTWC) releases unofficial advisories. On average, three to four cyclonic storms form in this basin every season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMD_2009_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-IMD_2009-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_summary">Season summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_North_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Season summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<center>
<div class="timeline-wrapper"><map id="timeline_19c4c73d126b7dcc108614caa0497216" name="timeline_19c4c73d126b7dcc108614caa0497216">
<area alt="Cyclone Mora" coords="184,24,242,45" href="/wiki/Cyclone_Mora" shape="rect" title="Cyclone Mora"/>
<area alt="Cyclone Maarutha" coords="55,10,136,31" href="/wiki/Cyclone_Maarutha" shape="rect" title="Cyclone Maarutha"/></map><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/timeline/19c4c73d126b7dcc108614caa0497216.png" usemap="#timeline_19c4c73d126b7dcc108614caa0497216"/></div>
</center>
<p><br/>
The season officially had an early start compared with the last two seasons with the formation of <a href="/wiki/Cyclone_Maarutha" title="Cyclone Maarutha">Cyclone Maarutha</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a> in mid-April.The strongest storm of the season so far was <a href="/wiki/Cyclone_Mora" title="Cyclone Mora">Cyclone Mora</a>, which formed in late May over the Bay of Bengal. The system made landfall in <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a> with three-minute sustained winds of 110 km/h (70 mph), and one-minute sustained winds of 120 km/h (75 mph). At this strength, its peak intensity, it was equivalent to a marginal Category 1 hurricane on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_scale" title="Saffir-Simpson scale">Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale</a>. The cyclone produced severe flooding across <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> , <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a> and caused 15 deaths directly and 203 deaths indirectly. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Floods" title="Floods">floods</a> persisted in Bangladesh since a Deep Depression over Bay of Bengal made landfall and killed 156 people in Bangladesh. A depression formed in northwestern <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a> and produced torrential rainfall. It was followed by a depression over <a href="/wiki/Jharkhand" title="Jharkhand">Jharkhand</a> which killed 70 people in <a href="/wiki/West_Bengal" title="West Bengal">West Bengal</a>. Under the influence of strong <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a> <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">surge</a> a disturbance developed over <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a> travelled westwards and intensified to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Depression_(weather)" title="Depression (weather)">depression</a>. It also affected neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Seasonal_forecast">Seasonal forecast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_North_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Seasonal forecast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Over the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>, is the risk for at least a couple of tropical systems to make landfall this autumn according to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Accuweather" title="Accuweather">Accuweather</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The best chance for tropical depressions or cyclones in the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">Arabian Sea</a> will be during October and November. During <a href="/wiki/September" title="September">September</a>, one or two systems may be declared a depression over the northern Bay of Bengal and could strike eastern India in <a href="/wiki/West_Bengal" title="West Bengal">West Bengal</a> or Orissa coasts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a> Meteorological Department, a cyclone or depression is likely to form in the last week of October in <a href="/wiki/Andaman_Sea" title="Andaman Sea">Andaman Sea</a> and travel towards <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Cyclone_Maarutha" title="Cyclone Maarutha">Cyclone Maarutha</a></div>
<p>On April 13, an area of low pressure formed in the South Bay of Bengal, under the influence of a persistent area of convection, in a span of six hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Under favorable conditions, rapid deepening took place, and the system was classified as a depression on April 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Later on the same day, it further intensified into a Deep Depression, and then into Cyclonic Storm Maarutha.The system moved very fast under the influence of mid-latitude trough in westerlies lying over India in the middle and upper tropospheric levels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> However, strong vertical wind shear and unfavourable MJO inhibited rapid intensification or further intensification of the system. Moving northeastwards, it reached its peak intensity in the early hours of 16th. The system maintained its peak intensity till landfall near Sandoway (Thandwe) in <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a> in the midnight. After landfall, the system weakened into a Deep Depression in early hours of 17th, into a Depression in the morning and well marked low pressure area over central <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a> and neighbourhood in the forenoon of 17th.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Maarutha had already triggered heavy rainfall as a depression in Sri Lanka, as well as the Andaman and Nicobar Islands (India).Due to a heavy swell in the sea around the Andaman Islands, all ferries to Havelock Island from Port Blair were suspended on leaving nearly 1,500 tourists stranded. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maarutha_89_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Maarutha_89-10">[10]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Kyaukpyu" title="Kyaukpyu">Kyaukpyu</a>, Maarutha destroyed more than 81 households and total damages amounted to <a href="/wiki/Burmese_kyat" title="Burmese kyat">Ks</a>31.8 million (US$23,400) as of April 18.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maarutha1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Maarutha1-11">[11]</a></sup> Four people were reported to be killed in the Irrawady division of Myanmar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maarutha2_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Maarutha2-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maarutha_76_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Maarutha_76-13">[13]</a></sup> Maarutha developed as a depression in the first fortnight of April. Climatologically, the formation of tropical cyclones in the Bay of Bengal at this time of the year is rare. Only twelve cyclones have developed over the Bay of Bengal during 1891-2016.</p>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/2017_Sri_Lanka_floods" title="2017 Sri Lanka floods">2017 Sri Lanka floods</a></div>
<p>Under the influence of a persistent area of convection, a low-pressure area formed over the southeast Bay of Bengal on May 26. It rapidly strengthened on May 28 with the IMD classifying it as a Depression and subsequently into a Deep Depression on the same day, designating it <i>BOB 02</i>. In the early hours of May 29, the IMD reported the storm to have reached Cyclonic storm intensity, naming it <i>Mora</i>. The storm followed a north-northeasterly track parallel to Myanmar coast.The system moved fast under the influence of mid-latitude trough in westerlies lying over India in the middle and upper tropospheric levels and the anti-cyclonic cyclonic circulation lying to the northeast of the system. Shortly before landfall, the storm reached its peak intensity as a severe cyclonic storm with winds of 110 km/h and a minimum central pressure of 978 hPa (mbar).The JTWC analysed it having reached Category 1 hurricane strength with winds of 120 km/h on the same day. At peak intensity, the storm made landfall on the southern coast of Bangladesh near Chittagong at IST 6:00 am. After landfall, the storm steadily weakened due to land interaction and dissipated into a well-marked low-pressure area over Nagaland on May 31.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mora0_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mora0-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>A total of 31 people have been killed—9 in <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a> and 1 in <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a> and 19 in <a href="/wiki/Manipur" title="Manipur">Manipur</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mora1_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mora1-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mora2_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mora2-17">[17]</a></sup> Damage throughout Bangladesh reached 44 to 45 crore in taka and in Myanmar it reached to USD 1.5 million. In India it reached to 131 crore (USD 20 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mora7_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mora7-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mora9_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mora9-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mora8_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mora8-20">[20]</a></sup> Two people were reported to be killed in <a href="/wiki/Malda_district" title="Malda district">Malda district</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Bengal" title="West Bengal">West Bengal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mora10_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mora10-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Also, although not directly related to the storm, the precursor low of Mora strengthened the arrival of the monsoon, causing heavy flooding in <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Andaman_Islands" title="Andaman Islands">Andaman Islands</a> that killed 208 people.</p>

<p>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low pressure area</a> formed over northwest <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a> and adjoining westcentral. Road were also damaged and standing crops in over 300 acres were damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The system also caused heavy rainfall in <a href="/wiki/Maharashtra" title="Maharashtra">Maharashtra</a> with the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> received 337 mm of rain in 24 hrs. 15 people were reported to be killed in Mumbai. A total of 35 deaths were reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> The outreach of the system caused heavy rainfall in <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> where up to 122 mm fell on August 30. At least 24 deaths were reported there, mainly due to electrocution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Storm_names">Storm names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_North_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Storm names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within this basin, a tropical cyclone is assigned a name when it is judged to have reached <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales#North_Indian_Ocean" title="Tropical cyclone scales">cyclonic storm</a> intensity with winds of 62 km/h (40 mph). The names were selected by members of the ESCAP/WMO panel on Tropical Cyclones between 2000 and May 2004, before the Regional Specialized Meteorological Center in <a href="/wiki/India_Meteorological_Department" title="India Meteorological Department">New Delhi</a> started to assign names in September 2004. There is no retirement of tropical cyclone names in this basin as the list of names is only scheduled to be used once before a new list of names is drawn up. Should a named tropical cyclone move into the basin from the Western Pacific, then it will retain its original name. The next six available names from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lists_of_tropical_cyclone_names" title="Lists of tropical cyclone names">list of North Indian Ocean storm names</a> are below.</p>
