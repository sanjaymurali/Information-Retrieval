<p><b>Hurricane Lili</b> was the second deadliest and second costliest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">hurricane</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2002_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2002 Atlantic hurricane season">2002 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, being surpassed by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isidore" title="Hurricane Isidore">Hurricane Isidore</a>, which affected the same areas around a week before Lili. Lili was the twelfth named storm, fourth hurricane, and second major hurricane of the 2002 Atlantic hurricane season. The storm developed from a tropical disturbance in the open Atlantic on September 21. It continued westward, affecting the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a> as a tropical storm, then entered the Caribbean. As it moved west, the storm dissipated while being affected by <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> south of <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, and regenerated when the vertical wind shear weakened. It turned to the northwest and strengthened up to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir–Simpson Hurricane Scale">category 2</a> strength on October 1. Lili made two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfalls</a> in western Cuba later that day, and then entered the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. The hurricane rapidly strengthened on October 2, reaching Category 4 strength that afternoon. It weakened rapidly thereafter, and hit <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> as a Category 1 hurricane on October 3. It moved inland and dissipated on October 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lili caused extensive damage through the Caribbean, particularly to crops and poorly built homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caribmud_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-caribmud-2">[2]</a></sup> Mudslides were common on the more mountainous islands, particularly Haiti and Jamaica.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAtoday1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-USAtoday1-3">[3]</a></sup> In the United States, the storm cut off the production of oil within the Gulf of Mexico, and caused severe damage in parts of Louisiana. Lili was also responsible for severe damage to the barrier islands and marshes in the southern portion of the state. Total damage amounted to $925 million (2002 USD), and the storm killed 15 people during its existence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-costliest_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-costliest-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Lili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lili_2002_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Lili_2002_track.png/220px-Lili_2002_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Lili_2002_track.png/330px-Lili_2002_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Lili_2002_track.png/440px-Lili_2002_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lili_2002_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical wave</a> moved off the west coast of Africa on September 16. A low level center of circulation developed from a tropical disturbance spawned by this tropical wave midway between the African coast and the Caribbean on September 20. The next day, the system gained sufficient organization to become a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_Cyclone" title="Tropical Cyclone">tropical depression</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcadv.231_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcadv.231-5">[5]</a></sup> The depression moved westward in excess of 20 mph (32 km/h), and reached <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical storm</a> strength-becoming Tropical Storm Lili as it passed through the <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">Windward Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcadv.232_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcadv.232-6">[6]</a></sup> The cyclone continued to intensify as it moved west through the Caribbean Sea, reaching a peak strength of 70 mph (110 km/h) on the morning of September 24.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcadv.2313_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcadv.2313-7">[7]</a></sup> This was immediately followed by an abrupt weakening, and the storm's <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> dropped to 40 mph (64 km/h) later that day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcadv.2315_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcadv.2315-8">[8]</a></sup> The sudden weakening was attributed to strong southerly vertical <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">shear</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcdis.2315_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcdis.2315-9">[9]</a></sup> The system degenerated into an open tropical wave the next morning, and remained in that state for nearly two days.</p>
<p>Lili regenerated near <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> on the evening of September 26 and gradually turned more to the west-northwest while strengthening.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> The system became a hurricane on September 30, just after passing through the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcadv.2336_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcadv.2336-10">[10]</a></sup> The storm continued on its course while continuing to intensify, and made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> twice the next day, on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isle_of_Youth" title="Isle of Youth">Isle of Youth</a> and near <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo" title="Pinar del Río">Pinar del Río</a> as a Category 2 hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcadv.2340A_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcadv.2340A-11">[11]</a></sup> Lili emerged over the Gulf of Mexico later that day, having lost little strength during its overland passage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcadv.2341_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcadv.2341-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The system turned to the northwest and sped up, becoming a major hurricane on October 2 while 365 miles (587 km) south-southeast of <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcadv.2344_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcadv.2344-13">[13]</a></sup> This intensification continued, aided by warm <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_Surface_Temperature" title="Sea Surface Temperature">sea surface temperatures</a> in the Gulf of Mexico and good outflow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcdisc.2344_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcdisc.2344-14">[14]</a></sup> The system reached its peak strength of category 4 intensity, with winds of 145 mph (233 km/h), during that afternoon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcadv.2345_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcadv.2345-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>This strength was not maintained for long. The storm began to quickly weaken in the early morning hours of October 3,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcadv.2348_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcadv.2348-16">[16]</a></sup> and this rapid weakening continued until the hurricane's final landfall near <a href="/wiki/Intracoastal_City,_Louisiana" title="Intracoastal City, Louisiana">Intracoastal City, Louisiana</a>, due to a combination of vertical wind shear, cool waters just offshore Louisiana,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and slowly encroaching dry air within its southwest quadrant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> By the time of landfall, maximum sustained winds had dropped to 90 mph (145 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcadv.2349_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcadv.2349-19">[19]</a></sup> The weakening was accompanied by a collapse of the inner <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eyewall" title="Eyewall">eyewall</a> before landfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> The system continued inland, curving to the north-northeast, and dissipated when absorbed by an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical_low" title="Extratropical low">extratropical low</a> near the <a href="/wiki/Arkansas" title="Arkansas">Arkansas</a>/<a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a> border on October 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Lili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Tropical storm watches were issued in parts of the Lesser Antilles on September 22. These were upgraded to warnings the next afternoon, and all advisories were dropped late on September 23 once the storm had passed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Over the next week, the islands of <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a>, Jamaica, Cuba, the Caymans, and the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucatán Peninsula">Yucatán Peninsula</a> were all under advisories of some kind at different times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Hurricane and tropical storm watches were issued for the Gulf Coast on October 1, and were upgraded to warnings the next morning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> They were discontinued after the storm moved past the following day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcadv.2349B_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcadv.2349B-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

<p>Because the cyclone affected the islands as a weak tropical storm, preparations were minimal. Two hundred people evacuated their homes in advance of the storm on the islands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_(island)" title="Saint Vincent (island)">St. Vincent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grenadine" title="Grenadine">Grenadine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caribmud_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-caribmud-2">[2]</a></sup> In Jamaica, all schools and universities were closed in advance of the storm, and 17 public shelters were opened on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jamaicamon_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-jamaicamon-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Preparations were extensive in Cuba. Military officials at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guantanamo_Bay,_Cuba" title="Guantanamo Bay, Cuba">Guantanamo Bay, Cuba</a> prepared for the possibility of evacuating their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al_Qaeda" title="Al Qaeda">Al Qaeda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taliban" title="Taliban">Taliban</a> prisoners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guantanamo_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-guantanamo-22">[22]</a></sup> A total of 130,000 Cuban citizens, mainly in western portions of the island, evacuated their homes prior to the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cubaevac_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-cubaevac-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Significant action was taken along the Gulf Coast as the threat the storm posed, predicted to come ashore at Category Four strength, became more urgent. Over a half million people evacuated their homes in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and Louisiana, including everyone in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iberia_Parish" title="Iberia Parish">Iberia Parish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latxevac_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-latxevac-24">[24]</a></sup> A total of 200,000 people evacuated in Louisiana.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latxevac_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-latxevac-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-25">[25]</a></sup> At least 2,000 volunteers staffed 115 Red Cross shelters in Louisiana, Texas, <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redcross2000_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-redcross2000-26">[26]</a></sup> More than 20,000 people stayed in those shelters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-25">[25]</a></sup> The Red Cross also sent over 160,000 meals to the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redcross2000_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-redcross2000-26">[26]</a></sup> A total of 3,000 prison inmates in Texas were evacuated to safer inland locations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latxevac_24-2"><a href="#cite_note-latxevac-24">[24]</a></sup> The launch of <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Atlantis" title="Space Shuttle Atlantis">Space Shuttle Atlantis</a> was delayed for five days when the <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">Kennedy Space Center</a> was threatened by the storm, the first time a launch in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> had been delayed because of weather in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-atlantis_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-atlantis-27">[27]</a></sup> Stores across the warning area were very busy in advance of the storm. In <a href="/wiki/New_Iberia,_Louisiana" title="New Iberia, Louisiana">New Iberia, Louisiana</a>, hardware stores ran out of stock,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hardwareIb_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-hardwareIb-28">[28]</a></sup> and businesses in <a href="/wiki/Lafayette,_Louisiana" title="Lafayette, Louisiana">Lafayette, Louisiana</a> reported similar shortages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lafprep_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-lafprep-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Collegiate activities were also affected by the storm. <a href="/wiki/Southern_University" title="Southern University">Southern University</a> canceled four days of classes because of Lili,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SU_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-SU-30">[30]</a></sup> and 20 <a href="/wiki/Texas_A%26M_University" title="Texas A&amp;M University">Texas A&amp;M University</a> <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">Galveston, Texas</a> students evacuated to the school's <a href="/wiki/College_Station,_Texas" title="College Station, Texas">College Station</a> location.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A.40M_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-A.40M-31">[31]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/University_of_South_Alabama" title="University of South Alabama">University of South Alabama</a> canceled two athletic events in advance of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-USA-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<p>Hurricane Lili was both the second deadliest and the second most devastating hurricane of the 2002 Atlantic hurricane season (<a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isidore" title="Hurricane Isidore">Isidore</a> killed 22 people and damaged $1.3 billion worth of property).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhcyearly_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhcyearly-33">[33]</a></sup> A total of 13 people died in the Caribbean Islands, and 2 more were killed in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Severe damage to crops and livestock occurred through the Lesser Antilles, and damage to buildings and other infrastructure was reported in other Caribbean nations and the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lesser_Antilles">Lesser Antilles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Lili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lesser Antilles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lili affected the islands as a tropical storm. Winds in the area were generally below hurricane force, although some gusts exceeded 74 mph (119 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Rainfall of up to 4 inches (100 mm) caused deadly mudslides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caribmud_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-caribmud-2">[2]</a></sup> The winds, combined with poor construction, tore the roofs off numerous homes and businesses. The majority of the damage was dealt to primarily to the banana crop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relief1_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-relief1-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lucia" title="St. Lucia">St. Lucia</a> lost at least 75 percent of its <a href="/wiki/Banana" title="Banana">banana</a> crop, and hundreds of homes were damaged by the strong winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caribmud_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-caribmud-2">[2]</a></sup> Near total loss of electricity, water, and telephone services occurred, and utility systems were heavily damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relief1_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-relief1-34">[34]</a></sup> Four people were killed on the island, and total damage was estimated at $20 million (2002 USD, $23.5 million 2007 USD)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-13"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relief1_34-2"><a href="#cite_note-relief1-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Over 400 homes were damaged in <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>, and nearly 50 trees were downed by the high gusts. Similar to in St. Lucia, there was significant damage to the nation's banana crop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relief1_34-3"><a href="#cite_note-relief1-34">[34]</a></sup> Extensive loss of electricity and telephone service also occurred. Damage totaled at nearly $200,000 (2002 USD, $235,000 2007 USD)</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">Grenada</a> also experienced moderate damage. A total of 14 homes' roofs were damaged, and one was completely destroyed. The island Medical Centre's roof was also damaged, and 12 landslides were reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relief1_34-4"><a href="#cite_note-relief1-34">[34]</a></sup> There was also mild damage to infrastructure, particularly in St. Patrick's Parish; three bridges were damaged or destroyed, along with seven <a href="/wiki/Utility_pole" title="Utility pole">utility poles</a> and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_main" title="Water main">water main</a>. The entire island was without power at some point, but it was quickly restored in the southern part of the island where damage to the poles themselves was less significant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relief1_34-5"><a href="#cite_note-relief1-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>St. Vincent and the <a href="/wiki/Grenadines" title="Grenadines">Grenadines</a> were heavily damaged, especially compared to other islands in the area. Several hundred homes and two schools were damaged, and the Rose Hall Police Station's roof was lost.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relief1_34-6"><a href="#cite_note-relief1-34">[34]</a></sup> Still, the majority of damage was dealt to the agricultural industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relief1_34-7"><a href="#cite_note-relief1-34">[34]</a></sup> In all, damage to the islands totaled $40 million (2002 USD, $47 million 2007 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-relief1_34-8"><a href="#cite_note-relief1-34">[34]</a></sup></p>

<p>Lili passed offshore of <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> as a dissipating tropical storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-14"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> The storm's major impact was extremely heavy rainfall, in excess of 16 inches (410 mm) near the settlement of Camp-Perrin, Haiti.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-camp-perrin_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-camp-perrin-42">[42]</a></sup> This caused the Ravine du Sud River to overflow, and submerge buildings in the town. Two people died in the mudslides these rains triggered, and two more drowned in the flooding around Camp-Perrin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday3_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday3-43">[43]</a></sup> The floods also seriously damaged crops and infrastructure; over 1700 homes were damaged and 240 were destroyed. Haiti was affected dramatically and many towns and villages submerged into rivers exceeding their bankfull discharge<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday3_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday3-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jamaica">Jamaica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Lili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Jamaica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lili affected Jamaica as a strengthening tropical storm. Wind gusts in excess of 70 mph (110 km/h) and rainfall over 2 feet (0.61 m) resulted in damage to homes, crops, and utility systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-15"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jamaicamon_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-jamaicamon-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Extremely heavy rainfall inundated the island. Cedar Valley recorded the most rainfall, with 23.1 inches (590 mm) measured. This led to prolific flooding that triggered mudslides across the island and killed four people. These floods decimated the island's sugar cane crop, one of the island's principal exports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jamaicamon_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-jamaicamon-21">[21]</a></sup> The resultant flooding caused widespread problems with the infrastructure of the island. All of the island's hospitals had flood damage, and three were also dealt structural damage by the strong winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jamaica2_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-jamaica2-44">[44]</a></sup> The flooding caused latrines and other sewage sources to overflow into the intake sources for the water supply, leading to fear of disease.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jamaica2_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-jamaica2-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuba">Cuba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Lili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Cuba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lili2002overcuba.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Lili2002overcuba.jpg/220px-Lili2002overcuba.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Lili2002overcuba.jpg/330px-Lili2002overcuba.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Lili2002overcuba.jpg/440px-Lili2002overcuba.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lili2002overcuba.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Hurricane Lili over <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> on October 1</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Lili made landfall as a category two hurricane twice in Cuba, on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isle_of_Youth" title="Isle of Youth">Isle of Youth</a> and in the <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo_Province" title="Pinar del Río Province">Pinar del Río Province</a>, on October 1. Wind gusts up to 112 mph (180 km/h) and rainfall amounts reaching 6 inches (150 mm) in some places caused damage to homes, businesses and crops. One person was killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-16"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cuba1_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cuba1-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>Damage to buildings and other infrastructure was significant. The most severely affected provinces were Pinar del Río and La Habana. A total of 48,000 homes were damaged, 16,000 of them lost their roofs. The province Sancti Spiritus was not affected as severely, as only 945 homes were damaged, with 500 losing their roofs. The provinces in Eastern Cuba, including Guantanamo, suffered similar damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cuba2_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cuba2-46">[46]</a></sup> Electricity outages for whole towns lasted weeks in parts of the western provinces. This led to loss of running water due to unpowered pumps, and deliveries of fresh water had to be made to remote villages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cuba1_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cuba1-45">[45]</a></sup> The tobacco and rice crops were badly depleted, but it was difficult to differentiate how much damage was caused by Lili, since Isidore had struck the region just a week earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cuba1_45-2"><a href="#cite_note-Cuba1-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cuba4_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cuba4-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Louisiana">Louisiana</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Lili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Louisiana">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lili_2002_rainfall.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="875" data-file-width="870" height="221" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Lili_2002_rainfall.gif/220px-Lili_2002_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Lili_2002_rainfall.gif/330px-Lili_2002_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Lili_2002_rainfall.gif/440px-Lili_2002_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lili_2002_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lili Storm Total Rainfall in the United States</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Lili made landfall on the morning of October 3 near Intracoastal City, as a weakening category one hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-17"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Wind gusts reaching 120 mph (190 km/h), coupled with over 6 inches (150 mm) of rainfall and a storm surge of 12 feet (3.7 m) caused over $790 million (2002 USD; $1 billion 2007 USD) in damage to Louisiana. A total of 237,000 people lost power, and oil rigs offshore were shut down for up to a week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lili2_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lili2-48">[48]</a></sup> Crops were badly affected, particularly the sugar cane, damage totaled nearly $175 million (2002 USD; $205 million 2007 USD). No direct deaths were reported as early warnings and the compact nature of the storm circumvented major loss of life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lili02_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lili02-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vermillion_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Vermillion Parish, Louisiana">Vermillion Parish</a>, the point of landfall, was hardest hit. Wind gusts in excess of 120 mph (190 km/h), along with a storm surge of 12 feet (3.7 m) dealt major damage to nearly 4000 homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lili2_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lili2-48">[48]</a></sup> The worst storm surge flooding occurred in Intracoastal City, destroying 20 buildings owned by a helicopter company. One person died after the storm, and 20 were hospitalized for carbon monoxide poisoning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lili2_48-2"><a href="#cite_note-Lili2-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lili2002coastalflooding.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="225" data-file-width="302" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Lili2002coastalflooding.jpg/220px-Lili2002coastalflooding.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/fa/Lili2002coastalflooding.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lili2002coastalflooding.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Louisiana coastal flooding associated with Lili</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Acadia_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Acadia Parish, Louisiana">Acadia Parish</a> was also hard hit, recording wind gusts exceeding 110 mph (180 km/h), and 5 tornadoes touched down in the parish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lili2_48-3"><a href="#cite_note-Lili2-48">[48]</a></sup> Thousands of homes were damaged with over 2,500 suffering severe damage. Power across the parish was knocked out, 2 people were injured and one was killed after the storm. Schools in the parish also sustained $1.6 million (2002 USD; $1.9 million 2007 USD) in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lili2_48-4"><a href="#cite_note-Lili2-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mississippi">Mississippi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Lili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Mississippi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lili's outer rainbands dumped large amounts of rain and brought tropical storm force wind gusts to Mississippi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LAlilisummary_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-LAlilisummary-50">[50]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pascagoula,_Mississippi" title="Pascagoula, Mississippi">Pascagoula, Mississippi</a>, recorded wind gusts of 41 mph (66 km/h), and <a href="/wiki/Picayune,_Mississippi" title="Picayune, Mississippi">Picayune, Mississippi</a>, received 4.14 inches (105 mm) of rainfall. Minor power outages occurred, mainly in southern Mississippi, and combined with the flooding of roads and buildings caused $30 million (2002 USD; $35 million 2007 USD) in damage. No deaths occurred in Mississippi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-18"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_areas_in_the_United_States">Other areas in the United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Lili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Other areas in the United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hurricane Lili's remnants brought heavy rainfall, peaking at four inches in <a href="/wiki/Arkansas" title="Arkansas">Arkansas</a>, to the Southeast, before dissipating near the Arkansas-Tennessee border. Lili's remnants also caused minimal rainfall in the <a href="/wiki/Tennessee_Valley" title="Tennessee Valley">Lower Tennessee Valley</a>. No major damage was reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-19"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> In all, Lili caused $820 million in damage to the United States. Lili also caused a little girl to be born in Massachusetts 2 weeks before her due date. Her mother named her after the storm, Lilly. This girl has grown with the fury and intensity of this storm.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Lili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lili2002disasterdeclarationmap.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1275" data-file-width="1650" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Lili2002disasterdeclarationmap.jpg/220px-Lili2002disasterdeclarationmap.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Lili2002disasterdeclarationmap.jpg/330px-Lili2002disasterdeclarationmap.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Lili2002disasterdeclarationmap.jpg/440px-Lili2002disasterdeclarationmap.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lili2002disasterdeclarationmap.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Disaster declarations by parish within Louisiana from Hurricane Lili</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">President Bush</a> declared Louisiana a federal disaster area after the storm, making it eligible for assistance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disdeclaration_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-disdeclaration-51">[51]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">FEMA</a> set up three locations to apply in Mississippi and Louisiana. Applications began pouring in, 153,000 by the time of the deadline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-153k_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-153k-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema35k_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-fema35k-53">[53]</a></sup> Over $311 million in aid was granted to Louisiana.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-311mil_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-311mil-54">[54]</a></sup> A total of $50 million of that money was in the form of low interest loans, and not actual grants.</p>
<p>Over 1,000 power workers from eight different states went to the worst hit areas to help restore power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-power3_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-power3-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arkpower_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-arkpower-56">[56]</a></sup> Seven states sent tree trimmers to help clear debris from power lines and roads to speed the recovery process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cleco_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-cleco-57">[57]</a></sup> In addition, FEMA gave SLEMCO, the state's power company, an $8.6 million grant, which paid for 75% of the damage to the electrical grid there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-power.27sback_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-power.27sback-58">[58]</a></sup> It took up to four weeks to restore power to all customers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-powerbackon_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-powerbackon-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hurricane Lili caused great environmental damage to the marshes and barrier islands in Louisiana. Huge <a href="/wiki/Fish_kill" title="Fish kill">fish kills</a> were observed in marshes near the landfall point, and in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atchafalaya_Swamp" title="Atchafalaya Swamp">Atchafalaya Swamp</a>. The barrier islands to the east of the landfall point, those subjected to the highest surge, were severely eroded. Sand was also deposited behind them into the brackish marshes, burying vegetation. The freshwater marshes were severely damaged by the wind and surge, some of them completely destroyed. The severe erosion created new waterways connecting inland bodies of water with the Gulf of Mexico, which eventually led to further erosion of inland lagoons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-greennowbrown_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-greennowbrown-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Lili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>
<p>The name Lili was retired in the spring of 2003 because of its destruction and deadly Caribbean track, and will never be used again in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_basin" title="Atlantic basin">Atlantic basin</a>. It was replaced with Laura for the <a href="/wiki/2008_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2008 Atlantic hurricane season">2008 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo1_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-wmo1-61">[61]</a></sup> The names Lucy and Lisette were also suggested as possible replacement names for Lili.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo2_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-wmo2-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
