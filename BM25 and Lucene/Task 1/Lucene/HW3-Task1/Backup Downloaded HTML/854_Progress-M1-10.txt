<p><b>Progress M1-10</b>, identified by <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> as <b>Progress 11</b> or <b>11P</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Progress_(spacecraft)" title="Progress (spacecraft)">Progress</a> spacecraft used to resupply the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>. It was a <a href="/wiki/Progress-M1" title="Progress-M1">Progress-M1</a> 11F615A55 spacecraft, with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serial_number" title="Serial number">serial number</a> 259.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSR-LL_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-JSR-LL-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Progress M1-10 was launched by a <a href="/wiki/Soyuz-U" title="Soyuz-U">Soyuz-U</a> carrier rocket from <a href="/wiki/Gagarin%27s_Start" title="Gagarin's Start">Site 1/5</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a>. Launch occurred at 10:34 GMT on 8 June 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSR-LL_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-JSR-LL-1">[1]</a></sup> The spacecraft docked with the <i><a href="/wiki/Pirs_(ISS_module)" title="Pirs (ISS module)">Pirs</a></i> module at 11:14:53 GMT on 11 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKN_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-SKN-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EA_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-EA-3">[3]</a></sup> It remained docked for three months before undocking at 19:41:44 GMT on 4 September<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKN_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-SKN-2">[2]</a></sup> to make way for <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-3" title="Soyuz TMA-3">Soyuz TMA-3</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RSW_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-RSW-4">[4]</a></sup> Following undocking, it remained in orbit for a month, conducting an earth observation mission.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EA_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-EA-3">[3]</a></sup> It was deorbited at 11:26 GMT on 3 October,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKN_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-SKN-2">[2]</a></sup> burning up in the atmosphere over the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, with any remaining debris landing in the ocean at around 12:38:49 GMT.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKN_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-SKN-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSR-SC_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-JSR-SC-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Progress M1-10 carried supplies to the International Space Station, including food, water and oxygen for the crew and equipment for conducting scientific research.</p>
