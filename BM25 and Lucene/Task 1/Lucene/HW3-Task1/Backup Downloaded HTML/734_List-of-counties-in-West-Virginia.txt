<p>The <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a> has 55 <a href="/wiki/County_(United_States)" title="County (United States)">counties</a>. Fifty of them existed at the time of the <a href="/wiki/Wheeling_Convention" title="Wheeling Convention">Wheeling Convention</a> in 1861, before which West Virginia was part of the Commonwealth of <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_County_Founding_Dates_and_Etymology_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-WV_County_Founding_Dates_and_Etymology-1">[1]</a></sup> The remaining five (<a href="/wiki/Grant_County,_West_Virginia" title="Grant County, West Virginia">Grant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mineral_County,_West_Virginia" title="Mineral County, West Virginia">Mineral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_County,_West_Virginia" title="Lincoln County, West Virginia">Lincoln</a>, <a href="/wiki/Summers_County,_West_Virginia" title="Summers County, West Virginia">Summers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mingo_County,_West_Virginia" title="Mingo County, West Virginia">Mingo</a>) were formed within the state<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_County_Founding_Dates_and_Etymology_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-WV_County_Founding_Dates_and_Etymology-1">[1]</a></sup> after its admission to the United States on June 20, 1863.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_Statehood_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-WV_Statehood-2">[2]</a></sup> At that time, <a href="/wiki/Berkeley_County,_West_Virginia" title="Berkeley County, West Virginia">Berkeley County</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_County,_West_Virginia" title="Jefferson County, West Virginia">Jefferson County</a>, the two easternmost counties of West Virginia, refused to recognize their inclusion in the state. In March 1866, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a> passed a joint mandate assenting to their inclusion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_State_shape_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-WV_State_shape-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_West_Virginia" title="Constitution of West Virginia">West Virginia Constitution</a> was ratified in 1872, replacing the state constitution created in 1863 when West Virginia became a state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bastress_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bastress-4">[4]</a></sup> Article 9, Section 8, of the West Virginia Constitution permits the creation of additional counties if a majority of citizens in the proposed new county vote for its creation and the new county has a minimum area of 400 square miles (1,036 km<sup>2</sup>) and a population of at least 6,000. Creation of a new county is prohibited if it would bring another county below these thresholds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_Code_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-WV_Code-5">[5]</a></sup> Three counties (<a href="/wiki/Greenbrier_County,_West_Virginia" title="Greenbrier County, West Virginia">Greenbrier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanawha_County,_West_Virginia" title="Kanawha County, West Virginia">Kanawha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Randolph_County,_West_Virginia" title="Randolph County, West Virginia">Randolph</a>) have sufficient population and land area to allow a new county to be split off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_Code_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-WV_Code-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NACO_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-NACO-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_Census_QuickFacts_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-WV_Census_QuickFacts-7">[7]</a></sup> The remaining counties cannot be split, as either their land area would decrease to under 400 square miles (1,036 km<sup>2</sup>) or their population would decrease to under 6,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_Code_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-WV_Code-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NACO_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-NACO-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_Census_QuickFacts_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-WV_Census_QuickFacts-7">[7]</a></sup> Population figures are based on the 2010 <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census" title="United States Census">United States Census</a>.</p>
<p>The role of counties in local government had been minimized under the 1863 constitution, which vested most local government authority in a system of townships based on the <a href="/wiki/New_England_town" title="New England town">New England model</a>. The authors of the 1872 constitution chose to return to the system used in Virginia, in which each county was governed by a county court with combined authority for executive, legislative and judicial functions of the county government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1880, West Virginia amended its constitution and replaced the county court system with an arrangement that divides county government powers between seven county offices, each of which is independently elected: the county commission, county clerk, circuit clerk, county sheriff, county assessor, county prosecuting attorney, and county surveyor of lands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_County_Functions_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-WV_County_Functions-9">[9]</a></sup> Counties have only those powers that are expressly granted to them by the state Constitution or by state statute. These powers include, but are not limited to, maintaining the infrastructure of the state, funding libraries, maintaining jails and hospitals, and waste disposal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_County_Functions_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-WV_County_Functions-9">[9]</a></sup> Reforming public education became a county function in 1933. In May 1933, a county unit plan was adopted. Under this plan, the state's 398 school districts were consolidated into the current 55 county school systems. This enabled public schools to be funded more economically and saved West Virginia millions of dollars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Randolph County is the largest by area at 1,040 square miles (2,694 km<sup>2</sup>), and <a href="/wiki/Hancock_County,_West_Virginia" title="Hancock County, West Virginia">Hancock County</a> is the smallest at 83 square miles (215 km<sup>2</sup>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_Census_QuickFacts_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-WV_Census_QuickFacts-7">[7]</a></sup> Kanawha County contributed land to the founding of 12 West Virginia counties<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_Culture_and_History_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-WV_Culture_and_History-11">[11]</a></sup> and has the largest population (193,063 in 2010). <a href="/wiki/Wirt_County,_West_Virginia" title="Wirt County, West Virginia">Wirt County</a> has the smallest population (5,717 in 2010).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_Census_QuickFacts_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-WV_Census_QuickFacts-7">[7]</a></sup> The oldest county is <a href="/wiki/Hampshire_County,_West_Virginia" title="Hampshire County, West Virginia">Hampshire</a>, established in 1754, and the newest is Mingo, established in 1895.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WV_County_Founding_Dates_and_Etymology_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-WV_County_Founding_Dates_and_Etymology-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Spruce_Knob" title="Spruce Knob">Spruce Knob</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Pendleton_County,_West_Virginia" title="Pendleton County, West Virginia">Pendleton County</a>, is the state's highest point at 4,863 feet (1,482 m).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pendleton_County_History_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pendleton_County_History-12">[12]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Information_Processing_Standard" title="Federal Information Processing Standard">Federal Information Processing Standard</a> (FIPS) codes, which are used by the United States government to identify counties uniquely, are five-digit numbers. For West Virginia, they start with 54 and end with the three-digit county code (for example, Barbour County has FIPS code 54001). Each county's code is provided in the table below, linked to census data for that county.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIPS_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FIPS-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
