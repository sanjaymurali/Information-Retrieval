<p><b>Hurricane Karen</b> was a hurricane of non-tropical origin that formed in October of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2001 Atlantic hurricane season">2001 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. It developed out of the interaction between a <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_analysis#Cold_front" title="Surface weather analysis">cold front</a> and an upper level <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> on October 10 located to the south of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>, and quickly strengthened as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical" title="Extratropical">extratropical</a> storm. The storm passed near Bermuda on October 12, producing hurricane-force winds on the island. It then organized, becoming a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical cyclone</a> on the 12th and a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> on the 13th. Karen strengthened to reach 80 mph (130 km/h) winds as a Category 1 hurricane on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>, and after weakening over cooler waters, it made landfall on <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> as a tropical storm. It quickly became extratropical.</p>
<p>On Bermuda, winds from the precursor extratropical storm produced moderate damage, primarily to power lines and marine interests. Over 2/3 of the island's power subscribers were left without power during the worst of the storm, and several boats sank or ran aground from the high winds. Damage on Bermuda totaled to over $1.4 million (2001 USD; $1.7 million 2008 USD). In Atlantic Canada, Tropical Storm Karen produced light winds and rain, but caused minimal damage.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Karen_(2001)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Karen_2001_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Karen_2001_track.png/220px-Karen_2001_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Karen_2001_track.png/330px-Karen_2001_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Karen_2001_track.png/440px-Karen_2001_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Karen_2001_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A cold front stalled a couple hundred miles southeast of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> on October 10. During that day, a strong upper-level <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> moved southeastward off the southeast coast of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. Due to several factors, including upward motion and strong diffluence—the rate at which a fluid moves—the area became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baroclinic_instability" title="Baroclinic instability">baroclinically unstable</a>. This caused the interaction between the trough and the front to develop into an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical" title="Extratropical">extratropical low</a> about 345 miles (555 km) southeast of Bermuda on October 11. The low moved quickly northward, then northwestward, strengthening quickly due to the instability of the atmosphere. Late on October 11, the system slowed, and the upper-level circulation became aligned with the low-level circulation. The extratropical storm began to develop <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical characteristics</a> late on October 11, including surface temperatures warmer than the surrounding environment, and vertical wind characteristics of a tropical cyclone. Based on its organization, the system developed into Subtropical Storm One early on October 12 while located about 35 miles (55 km) south of Bermuda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>While passing to the south of Bermuda, the <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical storm</a> maintained winds of 70 mph (110 km/h), with wind gusts on the island surpassing 100 mph (160 km/h). After becoming dissociated from the <a href="/wiki/Westerlies" title="Westerlies">Westerlies</a>, the system turned northward, and began to develop convection over the center. In addition, the frontal characteristics of the subtropical storm continually weakened. On October 13, based on an <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Microwave_Sounding_Unit" title="Advanced Microwave Sounding Unit">Advanced Microwave Sounding Unit</a> observation that stated that a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Mechanics" title="Tropical cyclone">warm core</a> was present throughout the system, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> designated the system as a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Intensity_classifications" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical storm</a>, and gave it the name <i><a href="/wiki/Karen_(name)" title="Karen (name)">Karen</a></i>. At this point, Karen was located 200 miles (320 km) north of Bermuda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Karen slowly strengthened over the warm waters of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-karendisc3_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-karendisc3-2">[2]</a></sup> and the storm intensified to a hurricane later on October 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Convection continued to develop, and organized into a ring around the <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> as Karen reached its peak intensity of 80 mph (130 km/h) on October 14 while located about 400 miles (640 km) south of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_of_Halifax" title="City of Halifax">Halifax, Nova Scotia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-karen7_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-karen7-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

</div>
<p>Karen quickly weakened as it moved over cooler waters,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-karen7_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-karen7-3">[3]</a></sup> and late on October 14 it degenerated back into a tropical storm as it accelerated northward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-karen9_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-karen9-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">Convection</a> gradually decreased,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-karen10_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-karen10-5">[5]</a></sup> and Karen made landfall on southwestern <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> with winds of 45 mph (70 km/h) on October 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Karen retained its tropical characteristics during and after making landfall, based on a research flight out of Halifax intended to study the early stages of extratropical transition. The flight reported arced bands and a warm-core system transitioning into a more typical mid-latitude system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-novanet_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-novanet-6">[6]</a></sup> Under the influence of a mid-latitude system, the storm turned sharply to the northeast, and after losing the remaining of its convection it became extratropical shortly after landfall. Continuing northward, the remnant low quickly weakened, and dissipated as it was absorbed by a larger extratropical storm over the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Saint_Lawrence" title="Gulf of Saint Lawrence">Gulf of Saint Lawrence</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Karen_(2001)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On October 10, as the precursor extratropical storm was forming, the <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_Weather_Service" title="Bermuda Weather Service">Bermuda Weather Service</a> issued a gale and later a storm warning for the <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">island</a>, expecting winds of 50 to 60 mph (60 to 95 km/h). Several radio interviews and television stations issued information on the expected storm. Many residents believed they were insufficiently warned, though it is acknowledged that emergency managers and citizens pay less attention to gale warnings then they do for <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical cyclone warnings</a>. On October 12, as the storm was passing to the south of the island, officials closed all schools and government offices. Many private businesses closed as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-wmo-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the time of Karen's landfall, gale warnings were issued for coastal waters, while inland wind warnings were in effect for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Breton_County,_Nova_Scotia" title="Cape Breton County, Nova Scotia">Cape Breton</a>. In addition, heavy rainfall warnings were issued for large portions of <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Halifax_Regional_Municipality,_Nova_Scotia" title="Halifax Regional Municipality, Nova Scotia">Halifax</a>, southeastern <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fundy_National_Park" title="Fundy National Park">Fundy National Park</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-novanet_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-novanet-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Karen_(2001)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bermuda">Bermuda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Karen_(2001)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bermuda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pre_Karen_ET_storm.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Pre_Karen_ET_storm.jpg/220px-Pre_Karen_ET_storm.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Pre_Karen_ET_storm.jpg/330px-Pre_Karen_ET_storm.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Pre_Karen_ET_storm.jpg/440px-Pre_Karen_ET_storm.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pre_Karen_ET_storm.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Subtropical Storm Karen passing near <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> on October 12</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>While passing to the south of the island, the tight <a href="/wiki/Pressure_gradient" title="Pressure gradient">pressure gradient</a> between the precursor extratropical storm and high pressures resulted in strong winds on the island, including sustained winds of hurricane status at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_George,_Bermuda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fort George, Bermuda (page does not exist)">Fort George</a>. Gusts on the island officially peaked at 100 mph (161 km/h) at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Devonshire_Parish,_Bermuda" title="Devonshire Parish, Bermuda">Devonshire</a>. A cruise ship anchored at harbor reported a wind gust of 118 mph (190 km/h), though it could have been caused by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Downdraft" title="Downdraft">downdraft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The storm also dropped moderate rainfall of just over 3 inches (76 mm),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bws_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-bws-8">[8]</a></sup> resulting in minor flooding of streets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Because the storm developed quickly, wave-induced beach erosion was minor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-wmo-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The strong winds left considerable tree and powerline damage. At the worst of the storm, 23,000 of the island's 30,000 power subscribers were without <a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">electricity</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Damage to power lines totaled to $385,000 (2001 USD, $468,700 2008 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-powerdammage_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-powerdammage-9">[9]</a></sup> The strong winds also caused considerable damage to vegetation. Three cruise ships weathered the storm at <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s,_Bermuda" title="St. George's, Bermuda">Saint George Harbour</a>, where the powerful winds ripped out a post and snapped a mooring line, leaving a ship drifting in the harbor. One crew member was minorly injured. Over a dozen boats broke free from their moorings, resulting in them running aground or sinking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> In all, 87 boats were affected to some degree, with marine damage totaling to about $665,000 (2001 USD, $809,600 2008 USD). The winds also caused minor damage to 175 properties on the island, primarily to houses. Damage to houses amounted to about $425,000 (2001 USD, $517,400 2008 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bfm_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-bfm-10">[10]</a></sup> Overall damage was moderate, totaling to about $1.4 million (2001 USD, $1.7 million 2008 USD). No fatalities were reported, though a few storm-related injuries occurred.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-wmo-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Karen_(2001)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tropical Storm Karen produced light to moderate winds across <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a>, peaking at 47 mph (76 km/h) with a gust of 64 mph (103 km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Cape_George,_Nova_Scotia" title="Cape George, Nova Scotia">Cape George</a> in <a href="/wiki/Antigonish_County,_Nova_Scotia" title="Antigonish County, Nova Scotia">Antigonish County, Nova Scotia</a>, along with a 26 mph (42 km/h) report in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlottetown,_Prince_Edward_Island" title="Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island">Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">Rainbands</a> in the storm dropped light rainfall of up to 1.8 inches (46 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Yarmouth,_Nova_Scotia" title="Yarmouth, Nova Scotia">Yarmouth, Nova Scotia</a> and 1.4 inches (35 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Saint_John,_New_Brunswick" title="Saint John, New Brunswick">Saint John, New Brunswick</a>, most of which fell in a short amount of time. Skewed to the left side of the transitioning storm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-novanet_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-novanet-6">[6]</a></sup> the rainfall was beneficial for the drought-stricken areas of Nova Scotia and New Brunswick. Due to the fast-moving nature of the storm, though, most areas reported only around half an inch of rain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> A buoy in <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Harbour" title="Halifax Harbour">Halifax Harbour</a> reported wave heights of up to 16.7 feet (5.1 m), causing breaking waves at docks white caps along the ocean. Damage in Canada was minor due to the storm, limited to an uprooted tree in <a href="/wiki/New_Glasgow,_Nova_Scotia" title="New Glasgow, Nova Scotia">New Glasgow, Nova Scotia</a> and several other trees with damaged branches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-novanet_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-novanet-6">[6]</a></sup> There were no injuries or fatalities in Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
