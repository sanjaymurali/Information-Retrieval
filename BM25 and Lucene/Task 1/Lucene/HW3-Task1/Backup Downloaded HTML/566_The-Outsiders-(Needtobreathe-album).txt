<p><i><b>The Outsiders</b></i> is the third album from the American <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Needtobreathe" title="Needtobreathe">Needtobreathe</a>, released August 25, 2009 through <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jfh_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-jfh-3">[3]</a></sup> It debuted at No. 20 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, selling 21,000 copies in its first week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard9-28-2011_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboard9-28-2011-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Outsiders_(Needtobreathe_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album was recorded from the end of 2008 through April 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stereo_may8_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-stereo_may8-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>The Outsiders</i> was co-produced by the band along with Jim Scott, <a href="/wiki/John_Alag%C3%ADa" title="John Alagía">John Alagía</a>, and Rick Beato, who produced portions of their previous album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Heat_(Needtobreathe_album)" title="The Heat (Needtobreathe album)">The Heat</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The studios used while recording the album were Plantation Studios in <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston, South Carolina</a>, Black Dog Sound in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, Georgia and Plyrz Studios in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Clarita,_California" title="Santa Clarita, California">Santa Clarita, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liner_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-liner-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Outsiders_(Needtobreathe_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album's first single, "Lay 'Em Down", was released to Christian radio at the beginning of June 2009 and was released on iTunes on June 16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r.26r_chr_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-r.26r_chr-8">[8]</a></sup> The next single from the album, "Something Beautiful", was released in August. The album reached No. 20 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and No. 9 on <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Top Rock Albums chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p><i>The Outsiders</i> garnered critical acclaim from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>. At <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a>, Andrew Leahey rated the album four stars, and suggested that the band "offer[s] up another collection of sweeping, reverent rock songs" on which he alluded to how the album "flirts with touches of roots rock and traditional gospel, though, from the title track's powerful Southern stomp to the sheer power of Bear Rinehart's voice."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-am_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-am-1">[1]</a></sup> Furthermore, Leahey noted the album "covers more ground than records by similar artists, and it does so without alienating any of its secular-minded listeners, too."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-am_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-am-1">[1]</a></sup> Matt Conner of <i><a href="/wiki/CCM_Magazine" title="CCM Magazine">CCM Magazine</a></i> indicated in a positive review that the album "brings even more evidence to the table that Rinehart's rock sensibilities only improve with time", and Conner also mentioned that the album's "second half provides some of the most beautiful moments on the record."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ccm_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ccm-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>At Christian Music Review, Liz Haveman rated the album three-and-a-half stars, and criticized the album because "some of the songs are confusing in their message. They are a little too vague to really grasp what these guys are trying to say."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cmr_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-cmr-11">[11]</a></sup> However, she highlighted how "the music itself is really enjoyable. It has a “<a href="/wiki/Jack_Johnson_(musician)" title="Jack Johnson (musician)">Jack Johnson</a>”-ish quality to its laid back candor. Over all, the album is really enjoyable and I think it is going to satisfy the Needtobreathe fan base."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cmr_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-cmr-11">[11]</a></sup> Ian Webber at <a href="/wiki/Cross_Rhythms" title="Cross Rhythms">Cross Rhythms</a> rated the album nine out of ten squares, and underscored how the band "have crafted an excellent collection of tracks that wrestle with issues of faith and doubt in an honest and accessible way."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cr_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-cr-12">[12]</a></sup> At Indie Vision Music, Joshua Clark rated the album four stars, and felt that the "lyrics seem a lot more ambiguous", yet called the release "near-perfect".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ivm_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-ivm-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>At Jesus Freak Hideout, Jen Rose rated the album four-and-a-half stars, and evoked that the album was "one of those rare gems where every track stands out, some pulling you in with a catchy intro or a clever lyric, others surfacing after spending time with the music."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jfhjfh_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-jfhjfh-14">[14]</a></sup> Pär Winberg of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Melodic.net" title="Melodic.net">Melodic.net</a> rated the album four-and-a-half stars, and underscored that "if you also add really good song writing to that – you all understand that you have a killer album", which he concluded with calling it "a fabulous demonstration how good music should sound like."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-net_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-net-15">[15]</a></sup> At New Release Tuesday, Kevin Davis rated the album a perfect five stars, and emphasized that the album was "the most catchy and meaningful album [I've] heard this year."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrt_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-nrt-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Outsiders_(Needtobreathe_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2010, the album won a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dove_Award" title="Dove Award">Dove Award</a> for Rock/Contemporary Album of the Year at the <a href="/wiki/41st_GMA_Dove_Awards" title="41st GMA Dove Awards">41st GMA Dove Awards</a>. The song "<a href="/wiki/Lay_%27Em_Down" title="Lay 'Em Down">Lay 'Em Down</a>" also won Rock/Contemporary Recorded Song of the Year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
