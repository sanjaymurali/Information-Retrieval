<p>The <b>Greater Antilles</b> is a grouping of the larger islands in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>: <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a> (containing <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>), <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a>.</p>
<p>The Greater Antilles constitute nearly 90% of the land mass of the entire <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> as well as over 90% of its population. The remainder of the land belongs to the archipelago of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>, which is a chain of islands to the east (running north-south and encompassing the eastern edge of the Caribbean Sea where it meets the Atlantic Ocean) and south (running east-west off the northern coast of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>).</p>
<p>The word <i>Antilles</i> originated in the period before the <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> conquest of the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a> - Europeans used the term <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antilia" title="Antilia">Antilia</a></i> as one of the mysterious lands featured on medieval charts, sometimes as an archipelago, sometimes as continuous land of greater or lesser extent, its location fluctuating in mid-ocean between the Canary Islands and Eurasia.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lucayan_Archipelago" title="Lucayan Archipelago">Lucayan Archipelago</a> is not considered to be a part of the Antilles archipelagoes but rather of the North Atlantic.</p>
