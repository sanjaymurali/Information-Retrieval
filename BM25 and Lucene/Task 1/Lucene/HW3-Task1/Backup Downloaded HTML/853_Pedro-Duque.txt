<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">This name uses <a href="/wiki/Spanish_naming_customs" title="Spanish naming customs">Spanish naming customs</a>: the first or paternal <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">family name</a> is <i>Duque</i> and the second or maternal family name is <i>Duque</i>.</div>
<p><b>Pedro Duque Duque</b> (born 14 March 1963) is a Spanish <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">astronaut</a> and a veteran of two space missions.</p>
<p>Duque earned a degree in Aeronautical Engineering from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universidad_Polit%C3%A9cnica_de_Madrid" title="Universidad Politécnica de Madrid">Universidad Politécnica de Madrid</a> (UPM) in 1986. He worked for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GMV,_S.A." title="GMV, S.A.">GMV</a> and for the <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">European Space Agency</a> (ESA) for six years before being selected as an astronaut candidate in 1992. Duque underwent training in both Russia and the United States. His first spaceflight was as a mission specialist aboard <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_shuttle" title="Space shuttle">space shuttle</a> mission <a href="/wiki/STS-95" title="STS-95">STS-95</a>, during which Duque supervised ESA experimental modules. In October 2003, Duque visited the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> for several days during a crew changeover. The scientific program of this visit was called by ESA/Spain <a class="external text" href="http://www.esa.int/Our_Activities/Human_Spaceflight/Cervantes_Mission" rel="nofollow">Misión Cervantes</a>.</p>
<p>He worked at the UPM, in the <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">Escuela Técnica Superior de Ingenieros Aeronáuticos</span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> he used to work at <a class="external text" href="http://www.deimos-imaging.com" rel="nofollow">Deimos Imaging</a>. Currently he is back as an <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">astronaut</a> of ESA, and leads the Flight Operations Office near Munich.</p>
