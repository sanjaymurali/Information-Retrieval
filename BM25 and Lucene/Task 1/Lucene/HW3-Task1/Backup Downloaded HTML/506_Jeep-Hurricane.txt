<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Jeep Hurricane</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Picturejeep_200.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1168" data-file-width="1760" height="232" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Picturejeep_200.jpg/350px-Picturejeep_200.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Picturejeep_200.jpg/525px-Picturejeep_200.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Picturejeep_200.jpg/700px-Picturejeep_200.jpg 2x" width="350"/></a>

<p>The <b>Jeep Hurricane</b> is a bespoke custom <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concept_vehicle" title="Concept vehicle">concept vehicle</a> that was unveiled at the 2005 <a href="/wiki/North_American_International_Auto_Show" title="North American International Auto Show">North American International Auto Show</a> in <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a> by American off-road brand <a href="/wiki/Jeep" title="Jeep">Jeep</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">Hurricane</a>'s one-piece body is composed largely of light-weight structural <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_fiber" title="Carbon fiber">carbon fiber</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jeep_hurricane_info_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jeep_hurricane_info-1">[1]</a></sup> Its <a href="/wiki/Skid_plate" title="Skid plate">skid plate</a> is an aluminum spine that connects the chassis to the underside of the vehicle. The Hurricane doesn’t have side doors or a roof, and there is only seating for two people. The driver and passenger enter the vehicle over bulkheads on each side. The Hurricane features a Chrysler designed and patented four-wheel steering system, which was outsourced to <a href="/wiki/MillenWorks" title="MillenWorks">MillenWorks</a> that allows the Hurricane to move sideways.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jeep_4-wheel_steering_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jeep_4-wheel_steering-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Its principal exterior designer was Aaron Pizzuti.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aaron_Pizzuti_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Aaron_Pizzuti-4">[4]</a></sup> The concept went on to win IDEA Silver Award,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IDEA_Silver_Award_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-IDEA_Silver_Award-5">[5]</a></sup> a Popular Science "Best of what's new" award,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Popular_Science_Award_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Popular_Science_Award-6">[6]</a></sup> and an AutoWeek Editor's Choice award in the "Most Fun" category.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AutoWeek_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-AutoWeek-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>It is not expected to be a production vehicle due to the complexity and cost of the drivetrain, however many patents were secured in the Hurricane’s development.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications.5B8.5D">Specifications<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jeep_Hurricane_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jeep_Hurricane-8">[8]</a></sup></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeep_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Specifications[8]">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Curb Weight (estimated): 3,850 lb (1,746 kg)</li>
<li>Length: 151.8 inches (3,856 mm)</li>
<li>Wheelbase: 108.1 inches (2,746 mm)</li>
<li>Front Overhang: 25.0 inches (635 mm)</li>
<li>Rear Overhang: 18.7 inches (475 mm)</li>
<li>Width: 80.0 inches (2,033 mm)</li>
<li>Height: 68.2 inches (1,732 mm)</li>
<li>Track, Frt/Rr: 67.5/67.5 inches (1,715/1,715 mm)</li>
<li>Engine: Two 5.7-liter, 8-cylinder <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HEMI" title="HEMI">HEMI</a> engines</li>
<li>Power: 670 hp (500 kW) (2 x 335 hp)</li>
<li>Torque: 740 lbf·ft (1000 N·m) (2 x 370 lbf-ft)</li>
<li>Transfer Case: Custom multi-mode with 1:1, 2:1 and 4:1 ratios</li>
<li>Transmission: 5-speed automatic</li>
<li>Front and Rear Suspension: Long-travel, short/long arm independent</li>
<li>Ground Clearance: 14.3 inches (363 mm)</li>
<li>Break-Over Angle: 31.5 degrees</li>
<li>Approach/Depart Angle: 64.0/86.7 degrees</li>
<li>Tire Size: 305/70R20 (all four)</li>
<li>Wheel Size: 20x10 inches (51x25 cm)</li>
<li>0-60 mph (97 km/h): 4.9 seconds</li>
</ul>
