<p><i><b>Hurricane</b></i> was the eighth studio album from <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_Christian_music" title="Contemporary Christian music">contemporary Christian music</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Grant" title="Natalie Grant">Natalie Grant</a>. It was the ninth album for her career, and it was released on October 15, 2013 by <a href="/wiki/Curb_Records" title="Curb Records">Curb Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-am_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-am-1">[1]</a></sup> The album was produced by Bernie Herms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-am_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-am-1">[1]</a></sup> The album has achieved commercial charting successes as well as garnered critical acclaim from music critics.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Natalie_Grant_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Hurricane_(Natalie_Grant_song)" title="Hurricane (Natalie Grant song)">Hurricane</a>" was released as the lead single from the album on June 4, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 27, 2013, "<a href="/wiki/Closer_to_Your_Heart_(Natalie_Grant_song)" title="Closer to Your Heart (Natalie Grant song)">Closer to Your Heart</a>" was released as the album's second single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Burn Bright" was released on January 30, 2015, as the third and final single from the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_and_lyrics">Music and lyrics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Natalie_Grant_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music and lyrics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At New Release Tuesday, Kevin Davis wrote that "Every song is catchy and meaningful", and that this album "seamlessly bringing together all of her elements of infectious melodies, stellar vocals, and worshipful lyrics in a flawlessly produced album."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrt_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-nrt-5">[5]</a></sup> In addition, Davis noted that "This album is not full of Christian clichés, but instead is loaded with fresh offerings of praise that every believer can celebrate."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrt_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-nrt-5">[5]</a></sup> Jonathan Andre of Indie Vision Music said that this album has "some great pop, with a bit of acoustic, country, folk, rock and EDM slotted in between" that was done in a "physical and metaphorical" kind-of way, yet "aren't so great when they suddenly appear in our own lives, sometimes out of warning."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ivm_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ivm-6">[6]</a></sup> At Jesus Freak Hideout, Bert Gangl suggest that this album was in the "dance-pop" genre, which it contains some "country-pop" that he told she should delve deeper into making with her next album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jfhone_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-jfhone-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Laura Chambers of Christian Music Review wrote that "Hurricane takes you into the depths of her mind, heart and soul, offering the same hope she’s found in Christ to anyone facing perilous times."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cmr_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-cmr-8">[8]</a></sup> At Christian Music Zine, Joshua Andre told that the release was "vulnerable, musically diverse, and creative".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cmz_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-cmz-9">[9]</a></sup> Matt Collar writing for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a> said the release has the same "passionate contemporary Christian pop music" for which Grant is known, but did note that "This time around, Grant finds a pleasing balance between her electronic dance music inclinations, piano-driven ballads, and rootsy country sound."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-am_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-am-1">[1]</a></sup> At Louder Than the Music, Jono Davies said that the listener gets taken it with the remarkable vocal abilities of Grant's and stated that this was the reason listeners would smile while hearing the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lttm_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-lttm-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

At <i><a href="/wiki/CCM_Magazine" title="CCM Magazine">CCM Magazine</a></i>, Grace S. Aspinwall called the album "a well-curated collection of jewels, with Grant's signature voice breathing life into these songs [...] proving that Natalie Grant just gets better with time." In addition, Aspinwall noted that "<i>Hurricane</i> is simply a joy."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ccm_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-ccm-11">[11]</a></sup> Kevin Davis of New Release Tuesday evoked that to him "<i>Hurricane</i> represents the best of Natalie's illustrious career", and stated that "they are all standout tracks."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrt_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-nrt-5">[5]</a></sup> At <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a>, Matt Collar called the sound "pleasing".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-am_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-am-1">[1]</a></sup> Founder <a href="/wiki/Tony_Cummings" title="Tony Cummings">Tony Cummings</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cross_Rhythms" title="Cross Rhythms">Cross Rhythms</a> proclaiming that "This album is genuinely uplifting pop music of a high calibre [<i>sic</i>]."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cr_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-cr-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>At Indie Vision Music, Jonathan Andre called this a "treasured" and "an enjoyable album".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ivm_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-ivm-6">[6]</a></sup> Bert Gangl of Jesus Freak Hideout felt that the album "falls just shy of building a convincing enough body of evidence to recommend its purchase" beyond her most avid fans, but suggested that the release was "probably best appreciated by downloading its handful of most impressive tracks".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jfhone_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-jfhone-7">[7]</a></sup> Jesus Freak Hideout's Mark Rice affirmed that "<i>Hurricane</i> might not turn heads, but it is safe to say that Grant has fulfilled her expectations and delivered an overall solid product."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jfhtwo_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-jfhtwo-13">[13]</a></sup> DeWayne Hamby, reviewing the album for <i><a href="/wiki/Charisma_(magazine)" title="Charisma (magazine)">Charisma</a></i>, says, "Though many of the songs are weighty, <i>Hurricane</i> is still upbeat in outlook and tempo."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHARISMA_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-CHARISMA-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>At Christian Music Zine, Joshua Andre proclaimed the release as "her crowning achievement so far in her successful career, mainly because she recorded these songs despite feeling something else entirely", and this was "Due to the busyness and the many things surrounding her life during the recording process; these events alone make Hurricane extra special."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cmz_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-cmz-9">[9]</a></sup> Furthermore, Andre alluded to how the release was "so relatable and brilliant".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cmz_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-cmz-9">[9]</a></sup> Laura Chambers of Christian Music Zine called the release "A brilliant reminder like Hurricane wouldn't hurt." At Louder Than the Music, Jono Davies felt this was "one classy album" because it was "honest, yet has this sweet beauty flowing through all the songs."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lttm_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-lttm-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Natalie_Grant_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> charting week of November 2, 2013, <i>Hurricane</i> was the No. 17 most sold album in the entirety of the United States by the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, and it was the No. 1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Christian_Albums" title="Top Christian Albums">Top Christian Album</a> sold the same week. The album has sold 30,000 copies in the US as of September 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
