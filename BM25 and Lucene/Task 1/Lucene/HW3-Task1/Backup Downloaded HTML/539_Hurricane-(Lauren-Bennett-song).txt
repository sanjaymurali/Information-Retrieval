<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Lauren_Bennett_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bennett's second solo single "Hurricane" was released on 2 May 2016 via <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In an <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> post on 8 May 2016, Bennett explained her inspiration for the single, saying "After G.R.L. ended I had no idea what was going to happen next, everything fell apart pretty fast. That's when this song was written. After seeing my mother suffer with mental struggles for years I then lost a friend to this. It has always effected my life in someway. We all at least know someone who has dealt with it or have yourself. This is a story to show from my eyes what it feels like to suffer from this but also the other perspective to lose someone to this."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was later announced on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> that a portion of the proceeds from the single would be donated to The Campaign to Change Direction,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> an organization dedicated to change the culture of mental health in America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
