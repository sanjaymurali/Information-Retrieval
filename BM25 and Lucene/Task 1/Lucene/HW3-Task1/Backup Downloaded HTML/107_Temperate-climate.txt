<p>In <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geography</a>, <b>temperate</b> or <b>tepid</b> <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitudes</a> of <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> lie between the <a href="/wiki/Tropics" title="Tropics">tropics</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polar_region" title="Polar region">polar regions</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> These regions generally have more variety in <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a> over the course of the year and more distinct changes between <a href="/wiki/Season" title="Season">seasons</a> compared with <a href="/wiki/Tropical_climate" title="Tropical climate">tropical climates</a>, where such variations are often small.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Zones_and_climates">Zones and climates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Zones and climates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>north temperate zone</b> extends from the <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer" title="Tropic of Cancer">Tropic of Cancer</a> (approximately 23.5° north latitude) to the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Circle" title="Arctic Circle">Arctic Circle</a> (approximately 66.5° north latitude). The <b>south temperate zone</b> extends from the <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Capricorn" title="Tropic of Capricorn">Tropic of Capricorn</a> (approximately 23.5° south latitude) to the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Circle" title="Antarctic Circle">Antarctic Circle</a> (at approximately 66.5° south latitude).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In some climate classifications, the temperate zone is often divided into several smaller climate zones, based on latitude. These include <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">Mediterranean climate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">oceanic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Continental_climate" title="Continental climate">continental climate</a>.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subtropical" title="Subtropical">Subtropical</a> climates are generally located between 23.5° and 35.0° north or south latitude on the eastern or leeward sides of landmasses. This climate has long, generally hot, summers and short, mild winters, with annual rainfall often concentrated in the warmest part of the year. These climates may occur in southern Asia, the southeastern United States, parts of eastern Australia, and in eastern coastal South America.</p>
<p>Mediterranean climates occur generally between 30° and 42° north and south latitude, on the western sides of landmasses. This climate has long hot summers and short mild winters; however, seasonal rainfall is the opposite of that of the subtropical humid type, with a winter or cool season rainfall peak being typical. These climates occur near the rimlands of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>, in western Australia, in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, and in the southernmost areas of <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.</p>
<p>The oceanic climates occur in the higher middle latitudes, between 45° and 60° north and south latitude. They are created by the onshore flow from the cool high latitude oceans to their west. This causes the climate to have cool summers and cool (but not cold) winters. These climates are frequently cloudy. Annual rainfall is spread throughout the entire year. Regions with this climate include <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a>, northwestern <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, and parts of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.</p>
<p>The continental climates occur in middle latitudes, between 35° or 40° and 55°. These climates are normally inland or on leeward sides of landmasses. They feature warm to hot summers and cold winters, with a large interseasonal temperature variation. Regions with this climate include northern temperate Asia, the northern United States, southern Canada, and parts of northeastern Europe.</p>
<p>The vast majority of the world's human population resides in temperate zones, especially in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_hemisphere" title="Northern hemisphere">northern hemisphere</a>, due to its greater mass of land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The richest temperate flora in the world is found in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">southern Africa</a>, where some 24,000 taxa (species and infraspecific taxa) have been described.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ger_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ger-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Farming is a large-scale practice in the temperate regions due to the plentiful rainfall and warm summers. because most agricultural activity occurs in the spring and summer, cold winters have a small effect on agricultural production. Extreme winters or summers have a huge impact on the productivity of agriculture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Urbanization">Urbanization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Urbanization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Temperate regions have the majority of the world's population, which leads to large cities. There are a couple factors why the climate of large city landscapes differs from the climate of rural areas. One factor is the strength of the absorption rate of builds and asphalt, which is higher than natural land. The other large factor is the burning of fossil fuels from buildings and vehicles. These factors have led to the average climate of cities to be warmer than surrounding areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
