<p><b>Ocracoke</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/oʊ/: 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/oʊ/: 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span></span>/</a></span></span> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-North_Carolina_Collection_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-North_Carolina_Collection-3">[3]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a> (CDP) and unincorporated town located at the southern end of <b>Ocracoke Island</b>, located entirely within <a href="/wiki/Hyde_County,_North_Carolina" title="Hyde County, North Carolina">Hyde County</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>. The population was 948 as of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_2010" title="United States Census, 2010">2010 Census</a>. As of 2014, Ocracoke's population was 591. It is also the location of the pirate <a href="/wiki/Blackbeard" title="Blackbeard">Blackbeard</a>'s death in November 1718.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackbeard_nps_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-blackbeard_nps-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracokeweb_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracokeweb-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracoke_researchguide_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracoke_researchguide-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackbeard_book1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-blackbeard_book1-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-untold_ocracoke_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-untold_ocracoke-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">Outer Banks</a> area was occasionally visited by <a href="/wiki/Algonquian_languages" title="Algonquian languages">Algonquian</a>-speaking Native Americans, but was never permanently settled. Ocracoke, then called Wokokkon,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> was used as a subsistence hunting and fishing ground for the Hatterask Indians. Yaupon Tea or Black Drink, made from the dried leaves of the indigenous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yaupon" title="Yaupon">yaupon</a>, a native holly, was used ceremonially by the Native Americans in the area. A small village on <a href="/wiki/Hatteras_Island" title="Hatteras Island">Hatteras Island</a> is said<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (August 2011)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> to have had forty fighting men. The area was first described in detail by Giovanni da Verrazzano, an Italian navigator, in 1524. Verrazzano was unable to navigate the tortuous channels leading into the <a href="/wiki/Pamlico_Sound" title="Pamlico Sound">Pamlico Sound</a> and assumed that China lay beyond the Outer Banks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracoke_history1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracoke_history1-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracoke_usgw_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracoke_usgw-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 1585, Sir Walter Raleigh's colony, in their ship the <i>Tiger</i>, in search of Roanoke Island collided with a sand bar in Ocracoke Inlet and were forced to land on the island for repairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>An attempt at an English settlement was tried at <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_Island" title="Roanoke Island">Roanoke Island</a> in the late 16th century, but it failed. This effectively halted European settlement until 1663, when Carolina Colony was chartered by King <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">Charles II</a>. However, remote Ocracoke Island was not permanently settled until 1750, being a pirate haven at times before then. It was a favorite anchorage of <a href="/wiki/Blackbeard" title="Blackbeard">Edward Teach</a>, better known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pirate" title="Pirate">pirate</a> <a href="/wiki/Blackbeard" title="Blackbeard">Blackbeard</a>. He was killed in a fierce battle with troops from <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a> on November 22, 1718.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D._Moore._1997_pp._31_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-D._Moore._1997_pp._31-13">[13]</a></sup> The grounds of what is now the Springer's Point Nature Preserve were said to be the pirate's favored hang-out during those days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-frommers_carolinas_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-frommers_carolinas-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ocracoke_Part_2_121.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Ocracoke_Part_2_121.JPG/220px-Ocracoke_Part_2_121.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Ocracoke_Part_2_121.JPG/330px-Ocracoke_Part_2_121.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Ocracoke_Part_2_121.JPG/440px-Ocracoke_Part_2_121.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Throughout the mid-to-late 18th century, the Island was home to a number of especially skilled schooner pilots, who could get smaller ships through the inlet to the Pamlico Sound. As population increased on the mainland, demand for transshipment of goods from ocean-going vessels increased. The population was listed as 139 in the 1800 census. Warehouses were built to hold goods off-loaded from larger ships offshore and then loaded onto smaller <a href="/wiki/Schooner" title="Schooner">schooners</a> to be delivered to plantations and towns along the mainland rivers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-villagecraftsmen_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-villagecraftsmen-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>By the late 19th century, the shipping business was gone, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Lifesaving_Service" title="United States Lifesaving Service">United States Lifesaving Service</a> became a major source of steady income for local men.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nps_ocracoke2_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-nps_ocracoke2-16">[16]</a></sup> Fishing, including charters for tourists, became more important to the livelihood of the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes_ocracoke_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes_ocracoke-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ocracoke_Historic_District" title="Ocracoke Historic District">Ocracoke Historic District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ocracoke_Light" title="Ocracoke Light">Ocracoke Light Station</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Salter-Battle_Hunting_and_Fishing_Lodge" title="Salter-Battle Hunting and Fishing Lodge">Salter-Battle Hunting and Fishing Lodge</a> are listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-nris-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August and September 1933 and again in September 1944 and August 1949 major hurricanes struck the island. Interestingly the first person accounts of these storms were recorded on the walls of the "Hurricane House."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fort_Ocracoke">Fort Ocracoke</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocracoke,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fort Ocracoke">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Fort Ocracoke, a <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederate</a> fortification constructed at the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, was situated on Beacon Island in Ocracoke Inlet, two miles to the west-southwest of Ocracoke village. The octagonally shaped fort was built on a previous <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a> site. At one point nearly 500 Confederate troops were stationed in and around Ocracoke and the fort. The Confederates abandoned and partially destroyed the fort in August 1861 after <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">Union</a> victories on nearby <a href="/wiki/Hatteras_Island" title="Hatteras Island">Hatteras Island</a>. Union forces razed it a month later on September 17, 1861. Beacon Island and the fort subsided beneath the waves of the inlet after the 1933 hurricanes that struck the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The remnants of Fort Ocracoke were relocated and identified in 1998 by the Surface Interval Diving Company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocracoke,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>The island of Ocracoke is a part of the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">Outer Banks</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>. It is one of the most remote islands in the Outer Banks, as it can only be reached by one of three public <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferries" title="Ferries">ferries</a> (two of which are toll ferries), private boat, or private plane. Other than the village of Ocracoke and a few other areas (a ferry terminal, a pony pen, a small runway), the entire island is part of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras_National_Seashore" title="Cape Hatteras National Seashore">Cape Hatteras National Seashore</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracokeferry_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracokeferry-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracokeferries_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracokeferries-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

<p>The village of Ocracoke is located around a small sheltered <a href="/wiki/Harbor" title="Harbor">harbor</a> called Silver Lake, with a second smaller residential area built around a series of man-made canals called Oyster Creek. The village is located at the widest point of the island, protected from the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sand_dunes" title="Sand dunes">sand dunes</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Salt_marsh" title="Salt marsh">salt marsh</a>. The average height of the island is less than five feet above sea level, and many of the buildings on the island are built on pilings to lift them off the ground. Flooding is a risk during both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">hurricanes</a> and large storms. <a href="/wiki/Ocracoke_Light" title="Ocracoke Light">Ocracoke Lighthouse</a> is situated near Silver Lake and has remained in continuous operation since 1823.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracokeguide_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracokeguide-24">[24]</a></sup></p>

<p>The island is also home to a British cemetery. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, German submarines sank several British ships including the <a href="/wiki/HMT_Bedfordshire" title="HMT Bedfordshire">HMT <i>Bedfordshire</i></a>, and the bodies of British sailors were washed ashore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brit_ocracoke_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-brit_ocracoke-25">[25]</a></sup> They were buried in a cemetery on the island. A lease for the 2,290-square-foot (213 m<sup>2</sup>) plot, where a British flag flies at all times, was given to the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_War_Graves_Commission" title="Commonwealth War Graves Commission">Commonwealth War Graves Commission</a> for as long as the land remained a cemetery, and the small site officially became a British cemetery. The United States Coast Guard station on Ocracoke Island takes care of the property.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> A memorial ceremony is held each year in May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Cemetery_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-British_Cemetery-27">[27]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocracoke,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A single paved two-lane road, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Carolina_State_Highway_12" title="North Carolina State Highway 12">NC 12</a>, runs from the village at the southern end of the island to the ferry dock at the northern tip of the island, where a 1-hour-long free ferry connects to <a href="/wiki/Hatteras_Island" title="Hatteras Island">Hatteras Island</a>. The second ferry dock, located in the village, has toll connections to <a href="/wiki/Swan_Quarter,_North_Carolina" title="Swan Quarter, North Carolina">Swan Quarter, North Carolina</a> on the mainland and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cedar_Island_(North_Carolina)" title="Cedar Island (North Carolina)">Cedar Island</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Atlantic,_North_Carolina" title="Atlantic, North Carolina">Atlantic, North Carolina</a>.</p>
<p>A passenger ferry operates across <a href="/wiki/Ocracoke_Inlet" title="Ocracoke Inlet">Ocracoke Inlet</a> to the deserted village of <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth,_North_Carolina" title="Portsmouth, North Carolina">Portsmouth</a>, at the northern end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Core_Banks" title="Core Banks">Core Banks</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ocracoke_Island_Airport" title="Ocracoke Island Airport">Ocracoke Island Airport</a> (FAA Identifier W95) is located slightly south-east of the village, allowing small aircraft to land. The average commute time on the island is 3 minutes. The National Average is 25 minutes.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocracoke,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocracoke,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ocracoke_Part_3_024.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2112" data-file-width="2816" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Ocracoke_Part_3_024.JPG/220px-Ocracoke_Part_3_024.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Ocracoke_Part_3_024.JPG/330px-Ocracoke_Part_3_024.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Ocracoke_Part_3_024.JPG/440px-Ocracoke_Part_3_024.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ocracoke_Part_3_024.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p>The economy of Ocracoke Island is based almost entirely on <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracokevillage_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracokevillage-32">[32]</a></sup> During the winter, the population shrinks and only a few businesses remain open. During the spring, summer, and early fall, an influx of tourists occupies hotels, campgrounds and weekly rental houses — and day visitors arrive by ferry from <a href="/wiki/Hatteras_Island" title="Hatteras Island">Hatteras Island</a>. Several bars, dozens of restaurants, and many shops, stores and other tourist-based businesses open for the tourist season. Visitors can find many shops that feature local, handmade goods, as well as imported artisanal goods and rare antiques, unusual for such a small island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fishing">Fishing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocracoke,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Fishing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ocracoke_Part_2_007.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Ocracoke_Part_2_007.JPG/220px-Ocracoke_Part_2_007.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Ocracoke_Part_2_007.JPG/330px-Ocracoke_Part_2_007.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Ocracoke_Part_2_007.JPG/440px-Ocracoke_Part_2_007.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ocracoke_Part_2_007.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Ocracoke Island Lighthouse</div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">Commercial fishing</a> contributes to the local economy with chartered sport fishing drawing tourism. With easy access to the <a href="/wiki/Pamlico_Sound" title="Pamlico Sound">Pamlico Sound</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic coast</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>, Ocracoke offers various fishing opportunities, from small Sound fish to <a href="/wiki/Tuna" title="Tuna">tuna</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drum_(fish)" title="Drum (fish)">drum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracoke_watermen_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracoke_watermen-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Records">World Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocracoke,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: World Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On November 4, 1987, the world record 13 pound <a href="/wiki/Spanish_mackerel" title="Spanish mackerel">Spanish mackerel</a> was caught on a boat owned by <a href="/wiki/Woody_Outlaw" title="Woody Outlaw">Woody Outlaw</a> off Ocracoke.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldstein2000_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Goldstein2000-35">[35]</a></sup> The fish that set this world record, exceeded the previous record by almost 20%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldstein2000_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-Goldstein2000-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Winter_economy">Winter economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocracoke,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Winter economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the winter, the island's only main employers are <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction</a>, the NC Department of Transportation and the businesses that support the small population. Many islanders use the winter as time off, since they tend to work between 60 and 80 hours a week during the tourist season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracokerealty_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracokerealty-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracoke-nc_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracoke-nc-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_dialect">Local dialect</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocracoke,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Local dialect">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ocracoke Island historically had its own distinct dialect of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_Language" title="English Language">English</a>, often referred to as a <a href="/wiki/Brogue" title="Brogue">brogue</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The dialect is known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_tider" title="High tider">high tider</a> dialect, after the characteristic phrase "high tide" (pronounced by them somewhat like "hoi toid"). Due to the influx of tourists and greater contact with the mainland in recent years, however, the Ocracoke brogue has been increasingly influenced by outside dialects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocracoke,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2010, there were 948 people residing in the CDP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cityd_ocracoke_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-cityd_ocracoke-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census2010_ocracoke_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-census2010_ocracoke-41">[41]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> as of 2000 was 80.4 people per square mile (31.1/km²). In 2000, There were 784 housing units at an average density of 82.0/sq mi (31.7/km²) in 2000. As of 2010, The racial makeup of the CDP was 96.2% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 1.6% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.6% from two or more races, 0.4% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, and 0.2% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 19.1% of the population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cityd_ocracoke_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-cityd_ocracoke-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census2010_ocracoke_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-census2010_ocracoke-41">[41]</a></sup></p>

<p>There were 370 households out of which 17.6% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 46.8% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 8.9% had a female householder with no husband present, and 40.8% were non-families. 30.8% of all households were composed of individuals and 8.9% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.08 and the average family size was 2.55.</p>
<p>In the CDP, the population was spread out with 13.0% under the age of 18, 6.1% from 18 to 24, 28.3% from 25 to 44, 34.6% from 45 to 64, and 17.9% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 46 years. For every 100 females there were 96.2 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 89.0 males.</p>
<p>The median income for a household in the CDP was $34,315, and the median income for a family was $38,750. Males had a median income of $26,667 versus $25,625 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the CDP was $18,032. About 7.7% of families and 9.3% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 13.8% of those under age 18 and 10.4% of those age 65 or over.</p>

<p>The residents of Ocracoke Island are served by the Ocracoke School (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K-12" title="K-12">K-12</a>), part of the <a href="/wiki/Hyde_County_Schools" title="Hyde County Schools">Hyde County Schools</a>, with a student population of 135 as of 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> The island also has a small airport located southeast of the village on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NC_12" title="NC 12">NC 12</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracoke_airport_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracoke_airport-43">[43]</a></sup> Hyde County maintains the Ocracoke Volunteer Fire Department located on highway 12. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocracokevfd_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocracokevfd-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ocracoke,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Ocracoke, <a href="/wiki/Common_fig" title="Common fig">figs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fig_cake" title="Fig cake">fig cake</a> are a prominent part of the town's cuisine, and the town has an annual fig festival that includes a fig cake contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
