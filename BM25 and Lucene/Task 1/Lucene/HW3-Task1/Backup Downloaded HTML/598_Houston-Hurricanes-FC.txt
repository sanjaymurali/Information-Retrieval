<p><b>Houston Hurricanes FC</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">soccer</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston, Texas</a>. Hurricanes FC was established in December 2012 when the league announced that team owner <a href="/wiki/Brendan_Keyes" title="Brendan Keyes">Brendan Keyes</a> was moving his <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Pirate_SC" title="Galveston Pirate SC">Galveston Pirate SC</a> franchise to Houston.</p>
<p>The Hurricanes' regular kit colors are green and white horizontally striped shirts with white shorts and socks which derive from the iconic <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic F.C.</a> uniform.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Premier_Soccer_League_.28NPSL.29">National Premier Soccer League (NPSL)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Houston_Hurricanes_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: National Premier Soccer League (NPSL)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 24, 2012, the <a href="/wiki/National_Premier_Soccer_League" title="National Premier Soccer League">National Premier Soccer League</a> announced that <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Pirate_SC" title="Galveston Pirate SC">Galveston Pirate SC</a> team owner <a href="/wiki/Brendan_Keyes" title="Brendan Keyes">Brendan Keyes</a> would relocate and rebrand his franchise for the <a href="/wiki/2013_NPSL_season" title="2013 NPSL season">2013 NPSL season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The Pirates, who won the <a href="/wiki/National_Premier_Soccer_League#Teams" title="National Premier Soccer League">South Central Conference</a> crown as an <a href="/wiki/Expansion_team" title="Expansion team">expansion team</a> in <a href="/wiki/2012_NPSL_season" title="2012 NPSL season">2012</a>, will continue to play in local leagues and tournaments in Texas, while the new Houston Hurricanes will take the place of the Galveston team in the NPSL.</p>
<p>In the 1990s Keyes played for the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Hurricanes" title="Houston Hurricanes">Houston Hurricanes</a> (<a href="/wiki/United_Soccer_Leagues" title="United Soccer Leagues">USISL</a>); his boyhood idol <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Lennox" title="Bobby Lennox">Bobby Lennox</a> played for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_American_Soccer_League_(1968%E2%80%931984)" title="North American Soccer League (1968–1984)">NASL</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Houston_Hurricane" title="Houston Hurricane">Hurricane club</a> in 1978-79, two factors that played into Keyes resurrecting the Hurricane brand. The move received the blessing of NPSL Chairman Andy Zorovich and former Hurricane owner Joey Serralta.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the same day of the announcement of their relocation, the club also revealed their first player signing, which was 19-year-old <a href="/wiki/Mercer_University" title="Mercer University">Mercer University</a> player Justin Ross. The Hurricanes then held tryouts in order to fill the rest of their roster before their NPSL debut in May 2013. The Hurricanes finished the 2013 NPSL season in last place in the South Central Conference.</p>
<p>On November 11, 2013, owner Brendan Keyes announced that the club would not be participating in the NPSL the following season, instead focusing on growing the new TPSL.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Texas_Premier_Soccer_League">Texas Premier Soccer League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Houston_Hurricanes_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Texas Premier Soccer League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2013 the Hurricanes joined five other Texas-based clubs (BCS Bearkatz FC, <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Pirate_SC" title="Galveston Pirate SC">Galveston Pirate SC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Houston_Dutch_Lions" title="Houston Dutch Lions">Houston Dutch Lions</a>, Houston Hurricanes FC, Houston Leones FC, Houston <a href="/wiki/Regals_SCA" title="Regals SCA">Regals SCA</a>) forming the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Premier_Soccer_League" title="Texas Premier Soccer League">Texas Premier Soccer League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
