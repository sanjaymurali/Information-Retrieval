<p>The <b>Hurricane Cliffs</b> of southwest <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a> and northwest <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a> are a red, <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a> geographic feature, sets of cliffs along the western, eroded edge of the <a href="/wiki/Kaibab_Limestone" title="Kaibab Limestone">Kaibab Limestone</a>; the cliffs are about 135-mi (217 km) long,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and begin at the north-northwest of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Canyon" title="Grand Canyon">Grand Canyon</a>.</p>

<p>The Hurricane Cliffs are part of the landforms on the southwest perimeter of the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Plateau" title="Colorado Plateau">Colorado Plateau</a>, specifically the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=High_Plateaus_section&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="High Plateaus section (page does not exist)">High Plateaus section</a></i>; the High Plateaus are transitional to the <a href="/wiki/Great_Basin" title="Great Basin">Great Basin</a> northwest, the <a href="/wiki/Mojave_Desert" title="Mojave Desert">Mojave Desert</a> west, and the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_transition_zone" title="Arizona transition zone">Arizona transition zone</a>, southwest and south of the <a href="/wiki/Coconino_Plateau" title="Coconino Plateau">Coconino Plateau</a>.</p>
<p>From Arizona, the cliffs trend north into Utah, but north of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane,_Utah" title="Hurricane, Utah">Hurricane</a>, at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anderson_Junction,_Utah" title="Anderson Junction, Utah">Anderson Junction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pintura,_Utah" title="Pintura, Utah">Pintura</a>, the trendline turns north-northeast, and by <a href="/wiki/Cedar_City,_Utah" title="Cedar City, Utah">Cedar City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Valley_(Iron_County,_Utah)" title="Cedar Valley (Iron County, Utah)">Cedar Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Summit,_Utah" title="Summit, Utah">Summit</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Parowan_Valley" title="Parowan Valley">Parowan Valley</a>, the trend is northeast. At the northeast end of Parowan Valley, which is the <a href="/wiki/Tushar_Mountains" title="Tushar Mountains">Tushar Mountains</a>, the Hurricane Cliffs end.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Interstate_15_in_Utah" title="Interstate 15 in Utah">Interstate 15</a> follows the foothills of the Cliffs from its north terminus, southwest through Parowan Valley, the townsites of <a href="/wiki/Paragonah,_Utah" title="Paragonah, Utah">Paragonah</a>-<a href="/wiki/Parowan,_Utah" title="Parowan, Utah">Parowan</a>, and leaves the cliffs at Anderson Junction and Toquerville.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Spanish_National_Historic_Trail" title="Old Spanish National Historic Trail">Old Spanish National Historic Trail</a> descends from the north <a href="/wiki/Markagunt_Plateau" title="Markagunt Plateau">Markagunt Plateau</a> through the Hurricane Cliffs into Parowan Valley just north of Paragonah. Adjacent south at Summit, and Enoch in northeast Cedar Valley, the <a href="/wiki/Black_Mountains_(Utah)" title="Black Mountains (Utah)">Black Mountains</a> narrow at the pass between the Hurricane Cliffs, and a mountain ridgeline, to only 1-mi;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> the Old Spanish Trail traversed through the pass then crossed west through the center-north of Cedar Valley.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Zion_National_Park" title="Zion National Park">Zion National Park</a> lies 9-mi east<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> of the Hurricane Cliffs at Toquerville; the park contains extensive layers of the Kaibab Limestone.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arizona_section">Arizona section</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Cliffs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Arizona section">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Arizona, the Hurricane Cliffs border the east of north-flowing Hurricane Wash, located in Black Rock Canyon. From there, the Cliffs trend south to Twin Butte, 6,055 feet (1,846 m), then to Mount Trumbull, 5,329 feet (1,624 m),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> (the <a href="/wiki/Mount_Trumbull_Wilderness" title="Mount Trumbull Wilderness">Mount Trumbull Wilderness</a>), adjacent to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sawmill_Mountains&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sawmill Mountains (page does not exist)">Sawmill Mountains</a>, and west flank, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Uinkaret_Mountains&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Uinkaret Mountains (page does not exist)">Uinkaret Mountains</a>. where the Hurricane Cliffs terminate.</p>
<p>The region of the west Uinkaret Mountains, (west Toroweap Valley), is the site of the <a href="/wiki/Toroweap_Overlook" title="Toroweap Overlook">Toroweap Overlook</a>, below Toroweap Point-(above southeast Toroweap Valley), on the ridgeline of <a href="/wiki/Coconino_Sandstone" title="Coconino Sandstone">Coconino Sandstone</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Toroweap_Formation" title="Toroweap Formation">Toroweap Formation</a> rocks above. The Hurricane Cliffs end/begin here, and it is also the connection point of the <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fault" title="Hurricane Fault">Hurricane Fault</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Toroweap_Fault" title="Toroweap Fault">Toroweap Fault</a> which crosses from the Grand Canyon, South Rim, and continues north-(North Rim), northeasterly, striking into south Utah. The region of the two faults also give rise to intrusive lavas through rock fractures, and the region is the southeast section of the <a href="/wiki/Uinkaret_volcanic_field" title="Uinkaret volcanic field">Uinkaret volcanic field</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lava_Falls" title="Lava Falls">Lava Falls</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vulcan%27s_Throne" title="Vulcan's Throne">Vulcan's Throne</a> are two land forms at the intersection point region of the two faults, (at the Uinkaret Mountains–<a href="/wiki/Colorado_River" title="Colorado River">Colorado River</a>), with Lava Falls, flowing into the Grand Canyon, and with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lava_Falls_Rapids&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lava Falls Rapids (page does not exist)">Lava Falls Rapids</a> being one of the largest rapids, blocking the flow of the Colorado.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Aubrey_Cliffs" title="Aubrey Cliffs">Aubrey Cliffs</a> are expressed south of the Colorado River, on the South Rim, and extend due-southwards. They are the west perimeter of the <a href="/wiki/Coconino_Plateau" title="Coconino Plateau">Coconino Plateau</a>, (the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Plateau" title="Colorado Plateau">Colorado Plateau</a>'s southwest).</p>
