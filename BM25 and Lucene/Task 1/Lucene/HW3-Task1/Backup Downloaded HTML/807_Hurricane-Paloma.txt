<p><b>Hurricane Paloma</b> was the third-strongest November <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane" title="Atlantic hurricane">Atlantic hurricane</a> on record. It was the sixteenth tropical storm, eighth hurricane and fifth major hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2008 Atlantic hurricane season">2008 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. A late season hurricane, it set several records for its intensity and formation. Paloma was the third most powerful November hurricane on record in the Atlantic Basin, behind only a 1932 hurricane and 1999's <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Lenny" title="Hurricane Lenny">Lenny</a>. It also marked the first time that at least one major hurricane formed in every month of the hurricane season from July to November, with only June not having a major hurricane this season.</p>
<p>Paloma developed out of a strong tropical disturbance off the eastern coast of <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> and northern coast of <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> on November 5. The disturbance had slowly developed into a tropical depression while hugging the coastline. The depression strengthened into a tropical storm early on November 6, then a hurricane later that day. The next day, Paloma intensified into a Category 2 hurricane then soon a Category 3. Early on November 8, Paloma continued to intensify and reached Category 4 intensity, and then weakened rapidly into a Category 2 before making landfall in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_del_Sur" title="Santa Cruz del Sur">Santa Cruz del Sur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. Paloma weakened into a tropical storm on November 9 while moving over Cuba, where it stalled out. It dissipated later that evening. Hurricane Paloma caused heavy damage in both the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a> and Cuba. Damages in the Caymans amounted to $154.4 million (USD), and damage in Cuba totaled to $300 million, and $100,000 in damage to Jamaica, for a total of $454.5 million, with one fatality.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Paloma_2008_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Paloma_2008_track.png/220px-Paloma_2008_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Paloma_2008_track.png/330px-Paloma_2008_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Paloma_2008_track.png/440px-Paloma_2008_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Paloma_2008_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>

<p>In early November 2008, an area of disturbed weather persisted over the southwestern <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> By November 2, a surface <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> manifested itself, spurring the development of scattered <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_convection" title="Atmospheric convection">convection</a> — shower and thunderstorm activity — across the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> approaching from the east interacted with this trough two days later and enhanced convective organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Steadily consolidating, the system developed into a tropical depression by 18:00 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on November 5 while located 130 mi (215 km) southeast of the <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>/<a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> border. Situated along the southwestern edge of a <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a>, the depressed initially generally to the north-northwest before turning north-northeast within 12 hours of formation. Favorable environmental conditions, including low <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>, allowed for steady intensification following <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">cyclogenesis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Weather_Research_and_Forecasting_model" title="Hurricane Weather Research and Forecasting model">HWRF</a> and <a href="/wiki/Geophysical_Fluid_Dynamics_Laboratory" title="Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory">GFDL</a> <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_forecast_model" title="Tropical cyclone forecast model">forecast models</a> depicted rapid development of the depression into a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_3" title="SSHS">major hurricane</a> within five days before striking <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> the former of which indicated a peak intensity of 131 mph (211 km/h) and 921 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hPa</a>; 27.20 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inches_of_Mercury" title="Inches of Mercury">inHg</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D3_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-D3-5">[5]</a></sup> The system acquired gale-force winds early on November 6, at which time it was <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">assigned the name</a> <i>Paloma</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Aided by good upper-level <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a>, deep convection blossomed over the storm's circulation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D3_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-D3-5">[5]</a></sup> Further development of <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">banding features</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> resulted in Paloma <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapidly intensifying</a> into a hurricane by 00:00 UTC on November 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>The initial phase of rapid intensification temporarily abated on November 7, though slow strengthening continued. Once its eye became defined on visible satellite imagery that evening, rapid strengthening ensued once more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> A localized area of high <a href="/wiki/Ocean_heat_content" title="Ocean heat content">ocean heat content</a> bolstered this phase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIMSS_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIMSS-9">[9]</a></sup> Aided by increasing <a href="/wiki/Divergence" title="Divergence">divergence</a> from an approaching <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">upper-level trough</a>, Paloma's outflow greatly enhanced overnight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> The aforementioned trough imparted a slightly more northeasterly track on the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D11_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-D11-10">[10]</a></sup> Around 09:00 UTC on November 8, the hurricane skirted <a href="/wiki/Little_Cayman" title="Little Cayman">Little Cayman</a> as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_3" title="SSHS">Category 3</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Rapid intensification continued through the first half of the day with Paloma reaching <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_4" title="SSHS">Category 4</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> around 10:30 UTC,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> the first such November system since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Michelle" title="Hurricane Michelle">Hurricane Michelle</a> in <a href="/wiki/2001_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2001 Atlantic hurricane season">2001</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Around this time the storm's center passed over <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Brac" title="Cayman Brac">Cayman Brac</a> and <a href="/wiki/Little_Cayman" title="Little Cayman">Little Cayman</a> shortly thereafter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CaymanReport_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-CaymanReport-13">[13]</a></sup> An unofficial <a href="/wiki/Anemometer" title="Anemometer">anemometer</a> on Cayman Brac at an elevation of 240 ft (73 m) measured sustained winds of 151 mph (243 km/h). Based on data from reconnaissance aircraft, which found flight-level winds 163 mph (262 km/h), Paloma peaked with maximum one-minute sustained winds of 145 mph (230 km/h) at 12:00–18:00 UTC along with a minimum <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of 944 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hPa</a>; 27.88 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inches_of_Mercury" title="Inches of Mercury">inHg</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> This ranked the system as the third-strongest November hurricane on record, only behind the <a href="/wiki/1932_Cuba_hurricane" title="1932 Cuba hurricane">1932 Santa Cruz del Sur hurricane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Lenny" title="Hurricane Lenny">Hurricane Lenny</a> in <a href="/wiki/1999_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1999 Atlantic hurricane season">1999</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-14">[14]</a></sup> Additionally, this marked the first time that major hurricanes developed in five separate months during a single year, with <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Bertha_(2008)" title="Hurricane Bertha (2008)">Bertha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Gustav" title="Hurricane Gustav">Gustav</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ike" title="Hurricane Ike">Ike</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Omar_(2008)" title="Hurricane Omar (2008)">Omar</a> reaching this strength in July, August, September, and October respectively. At its peak, Paloma displayed a well-defined eye surrounded by intense convection estimated at −70 to −80 °C (−94 to −112 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIMSS_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-CIMSS-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Late on November 8, environmental conditions abruptly became hostile as the hurricane approached Cuba. Interaction with land hastened Paloma's weakening with convection diminishing significantly. The northern eyewall moved ashore around 23:00 UTC while the center itself made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> at 01:00 UTC on November 9 near <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_del_Sur" title="Santa Cruz del Sur">Santa Cruz del Sur</a>. Upon moving ashore, Paloma was estimated to have had winds of 100 mph (155 km/h), making it a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_2" title="SSHS">Category 2</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Within hours of moving ashore, the center decoupled from the remaining convection and its forward speed slowed significantly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The system subsequently degraded to a tropical depression by 18:00 UTC, just 24 hours after being a Category 4. With no convection redeveloping as the system drifted northward over Cuba, Paloma degenerated into a remnant low six hours later. The low then briefly moved over the Atlantic Ocean before doubling back to the southwest in response to a building ridge farther north. The system crossed Cuba again and re-emerged over the Caribbean Sea by November 12, changing direction this time to the west and later northwest. After crossing western Cuba early on November 13, the remnants of Paloma entered the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. Accelerating northward the low became decreasingly organized and ultimately dissipated early the next day about 70 mi (110 km) south-southwest of <a href="/wiki/Apalachicola,_Florida" title="Apalachicola, Florida">Apalachicola, Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Later on November 14, the system moved inland over the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panhandle" title="Florida Panhandle">Florida Panhandle</a> where a sudden burst of convective development, akin to that of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supercell_thunderstorm" title="Supercell thunderstorm">supercell thunderstorms</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">Great Plains</a>, took place. Thereafter, energy from Paloma may have contributed to a <a href="/wiki/November_2008_Carolinas_tornado_outbreak" title="November 2008 Carolinas tornado outbreak">deadly tornado outbreak</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Carolinas" title="The Carolinas">The Carolinas</a> on November 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIMSS2_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIMSS2-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cayman_Islands">Cayman Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cayman Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Paloma_IR_nov_7_2008_1109_UTC.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="900" data-file-width="900" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Paloma_IR_nov_7_2008_1109_UTC.gif/220px-Paloma_IR_nov_7_2008_1109_UTC.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Paloma_IR_nov_7_2008_1109_UTC.gif/330px-Paloma_IR_nov_7_2008_1109_UTC.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Paloma_IR_nov_7_2008_1109_UTC.gif/440px-Paloma_IR_nov_7_2008_1109_UTC.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Paloma_IR_nov_7_2008_1109_UTC.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Infrared satellite image of Hurricane Paloma at 11:09 UTC on November 7 as it approached the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a></div>

<p>At 15:00 UTC on November 6, the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_Cayman_Islands" title="Government of the Cayman Islands">Government of the Cayman Islands</a> issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane watch</a> for all of the territory's islands. This was superseded six hours later by a hurricane warning. The warning was eventually discontinued early on November 9 once Paloma cleared the islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> On November 7, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> opened a shelter for possible evacuees while stocks of emergency supplies were checked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Schools, businesses, and government offices closed for November 8.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Shelters opened on Grand Cayman closed their doors by mid-afternoon in advance of Paloma's arrival to "ensure that persons get off the roads."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> At least 40 people sought refuge in these shelters prior to the storm's arrival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AP1_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-AP1-20">[20]</a></sup> The Seaman’s Center, a government office building, was utilized as an unofficial shelter during the storm and housed at least 100 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CaymanReport_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-CaymanReport-13">[13]</a></sup> The Cayman Islands Government requested all hotels to move guests on ground and first floors to higher rooms. As a precautionary measure, water service was turned off during the evening on November 8. The British ship <i><a href="/wiki/RFA_Wave_Ruler_(A390)" title="RFA Wave Ruler (A390)">RFA Wave Ruler</a></i> was sent to Little Cayman and Cayman Brac for humanitarian assistance, on the order of <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_the_Cayman_Islands" title="Governor of the Cayman Islands">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Jack" title="Stuart Jack">Stuart Jack</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AP1_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-AP1-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuba">Cuba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cuba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Paloma_Advisory_13_forecast_and_warnings.GIF"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="716" data-file-width="895" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Hurricane_Paloma_Advisory_13_forecast_and_warnings.GIF/220px-Hurricane_Paloma_Advisory_13_forecast_and_warnings.GIF" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Hurricane_Paloma_Advisory_13_forecast_and_warnings.GIF/330px-Hurricane_Paloma_Advisory_13_forecast_and_warnings.GIF 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Hurricane_Paloma_Advisory_13_forecast_and_warnings.GIF/440px-Hurricane_Paloma_Advisory_13_forecast_and_warnings.GIF 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Paloma_Advisory_13_forecast_and_warnings.GIF" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

</div>
<p>Anticipating hurricane conditions to impact portions of eastern Cuba, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_Cuba" title="Government of Cuba">nation's government</a> issued a hurricane watch for the <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Cuba" title="Provinces of Cuba">provinces</a> of <a href="/wiki/Camag%C3%BCey_Province" title="Camagüey Province">Camagüey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ciego_de_%C3%81vila_Province" title="Ciego de Ávila Province">Ciego de Ávila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Granma_Province" title="Granma Province">Granma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Las_Tunas_Province" title="Las Tunas Province">Las Tunas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sancti_Sp%C3%ADritus_Province" title="Sancti Spíritus Province">Sancti Spíritus</a> at 12:00 UTC on November 7. Six hours later, this was upgraded to a hurricane warning except and by the November 8, warnings covered the entirety of eastern Cuba. These advisories were discontinued following Paloma's rapid dissipation over land on November 9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fearing a "potentially catastrophic" storm with a storm surge of 20 to 25 ft (6.1 to 7.6 m), officials in Cuba scrambled to evacuate nearly half a million people in southern areas of the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CubaEvac2_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-CubaEvac2-21">[21]</a></sup> Coincidentally, Paloma threatened the same area where a <a href="/wiki/1932_Cuba_hurricane" title="1932 Cuba hurricane">devastating hurricane in 1932</a> killed more than 3,000 people almost exactly 76 years prior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cadena1_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cadena1-22">[22]</a></sup> Plans were put in place to evacuate 345,000 in Holguín, 324,000 in Granma, 250,000 people in Las Tunas, and 200,000 in Camagüey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CubaEvac2_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-CubaEvac2-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> An estimated 3,000 tourists in Ciego de Avila were brought to shelters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CubaEvac2_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-CubaEvac2-21">[21]</a></sup> Ultimately, an approximate 1.2 million people, 10.7% of Cuba's entire population, were relocated in advance of the storm's arrival. Of these people, 220,000 sought refuge in 1,448 shelters while the rest stayed with relatives. A total of 927 food processing centers and 72 soup kitchens opened to feed evacuees. Roughly 237,000 animals were moved to safer areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Holguín Province was devastated by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ike" title="Hurricane Ike">Hurricane Ike</a> in September with many residents still homeless at the time of Paloma's approach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> All domestic flights except those around the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isle_of_Youth" title="Isle of Youth">Isle of Youth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cadena1_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cadena1-22">[22]</a></sup> Former <a href="/wiki/President_of_Cuba" title="President of Cuba">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Fidel_Castro" title="Fidel Castro">Fidel Castro</a> urged people to remain positive in the face of yet another hurricane that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> He also issued a written statement to the United States rejecting any aid, citing anger toward the ongoing <a href="/wiki/United_States_embargo_against_Cuba" title="United States embargo against Cuba">embargo of Cuba</a>, before a formal offer was even made.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The non-profit organization <a href="/wiki/Operation_USA" title="Operation USA">Operation USA</a> provided emergency aid to Cuba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">Elsewhere</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the classification of Tropical Depression Seventeen late on November 5, a tropical storm watch was issued for coastal areas between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puerto_Cabezas,_Nicaragua" title="Puerto Cabezas, Nicaragua">Puerto Cabezas, Nicaragua</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Limon,_Honduras" title="Limon, Honduras">Limon, Honduras</a>. This later expired on November 6 as Paloma moved away from the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-12"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Owing to the threat of heavy rains in Honduras, a red alert was issued for <a href="/wiki/Col%C3%B3n_Department_(Honduras)" title="Colón Department (Honduras)">Colón</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gracias_a_Dios_Department" title="Gracias a Dios Department">Gracias a Dios</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/Olancho_Department" title="Olancho Department">Olancho</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Bay_Islands_Department" title="Bay Islands Department">Bay Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although Paloma was not forecast to directly impact Jamaica, officials opened 15 priority shelters in St. James and all agencies were placed under high-alert on November 8.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> A flash flood warning was in place from November 6–9 for the entire island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Early on November 8, a tropical storm watch and later tropical storm warning was issued for the Central <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-13"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> including <a href="/wiki/Cat_Island,_Bahamas" title="Cat Island, Bahamas">Cat Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exuma" title="Exuma">Exuma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Long_Island,_Bahamas" title="Long Island, Bahamas">Long Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rum_Cay" title="Rum Cay">Rum Cay</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador_Island" title="San Salvador Island">San Salvador</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crooked_Island,_Bahamas" title="Crooked Island, Bahamas">Crooked Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acklins" title="Acklins">Acklins</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ragged_Island,_Bahamas" title="Ragged Island, Bahamas">Ragged Island</a>. Accordingly, the National Emergency Operations Centre was partially activated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The warning was allowed to expire the following day as Paloma rapidly weakened over Cuba,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-14"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> and the National Emergency Operations Centre issued an all clear on November 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Honduras_and_Nicaragua">Honduras and Nicaragua</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Honduras and Nicaragua">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/October_2008_Central_America_floods" title="October 2008 Central America floods">October 2008 Central America floods</a></div>
<p>Throughout much of October Central America, was plagued by a series of heavy rain events which resulted in widespread damage and loss of life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">outer bands</a> of then Tropical Storm Paloma exacerbated the situation in parts of Honduras and Nicaragua.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Estimates from the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Rainfall_Measuring_Mission" title="Tropical Rainfall Measuring Mission">Tropical Rainfall Measuring Mission</a> satellites indicated that Paloma dropped upwards of 8 in (200 mm) of rain along coastal areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The overall impacts of flooding since October in Honduras left at least 60 people dead and more than 300,000 in need of assistance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cayman_Islands_2">Cayman Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cayman Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Home_destroyed_by_Paloma_in_CI.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Home_destroyed_by_Paloma_in_CI.jpg/220px-Home_destroyed_by_Paloma_in_CI.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Home_destroyed_by_Paloma_in_CI.jpg/330px-Home_destroyed_by_Paloma_in_CI.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Home_destroyed_by_Paloma_in_CI.jpg/440px-Home_destroyed_by_Paloma_in_CI.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Home_destroyed_by_Paloma_in_CI.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A destroyed home on Cayman Brac</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Passing directly over the small islands Cayman Brac and Little Cayman, with a collective population of 2,695, Hurricane Paloma wrought tremendous damage. Grand Cayman escaped almost entirely unscathed. Throughout the Cayman Islands, Paloma was responsible for $154.4 million in losses of which $124.5 million was incurred from damage. This equates to roughly 7.4 percent of the territory's <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">gross domestic product</a> (GDP). While the overall impact to the islands was offset by a lack of damage on Grand Cayman, the localized losses on Cayman Brac and Little Cayman were tremendous. Discounting the economy of Grand Cayman, the equivalent GDP losses on the two smaller islands was likely similar to that of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ivan" title="Hurricane Ivan">Hurricane Ivan</a> in 2004, which left damage equal to 183 percent of the territory's GDP. The overall per capita impact there reached $57,925. Despite the severity of damage, no loss of life or injuries took place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CaymanReport_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-CaymanReport-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Destructive winds and torrential rains battered both Cayman Brac and Little Cayman, with an unofficial elevated observation station reporting maximum sustained winds of 151 mph (243 km/h) during the height of the storm. Grand Cayman saw lesser winds, with a peak sustained observation of 60 mph (97 km/h) at <a href="/wiki/Owen_Roberts_International_Airport" title="Owen Roberts International Airport">Owen Roberts International Airport</a>. Precipitation on Cayman Brac amounted to 17.77 in (451 mm) and 6.05 in (154 mm) on Grand Cayman. A significant <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> also impacted the islands, reaching 8 ft (2.4 m) on Cayman Brac and 4 ft (1.2 m) on Little Cayman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-15"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Destructive winds were the primary cause of damage across Cayman Brac and Little Cayman, where nearly all homes were affected. Cayman Brac sustained the most severe impact, with 71 homes destroyed and 912 damaged. Although all home on Little Cayman were affected, none experienced major damage. <a href="/wiki/Bodden_Town_(district)" title="Bodden Town (district)">Bodden Town</a> was the only district on Grand Cayman reporting damage, with 11 homes affected. Major damage took place at Cayman Brac's seaport. The port's warehouse and office building were destroyed while anchors and moorings were shifted out of position. The island's fire station and Faith Hospital had major roof damage, with the latter resulting in significant water damage to equipment. The roof of the Seaman’s Center, later determined to have been inadequately built, collapsed during the storm and nearly injured people sheltering within it. All schools on Cayman Brac suffered moderate to major damage while the primary school on Little Cayman experienced minor damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CaymanReport_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-CaymanReport-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The electrical grid sustained major damage, with all residents on Cayman Brac and Little Cayman losing service. Roughly 400 power poles fell during the storm. Telecommunications experienced similar damage, with landline services disrupted for two to three weeks. Six of the ten affected <a href="/wiki/Digicel" title="Digicel">Digicel</a> sites went down. Effects on the water supply was negligible, with only a few pipes damaged by uprooted trees. Major crop damage took place on Grand Cayman, primarily to bananas, plantains, and peppers, due to high winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CaymanReport_13-4"><a href="#cite_note-CaymanReport-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jamaica">Jamaica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Jamaica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Flooding was reported in parts of <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> as a result of the outer bands of Paloma. One person drowned in <a href="/wiki/Clarendon_Parish,_Jamaica" title="Clarendon Parish, Jamaica">Clarendon Parish</a> while crossing a flooded river.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RadioJamaica_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-RadioJamaica-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Severe flooding also destroyed crops in 100 farms, causing over $100,000 in damages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> In St. Catherine, several inches of rain caused flooding in Bog Walk Gorge which inundated several homes and stranded at least 15 people. Numerous vehicles were washed away in the floods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuba_2">Cuba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cuba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Paloma_08-09Nov08_cmw2.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="437" data-file-width="566" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Paloma_08-09Nov08_cmw2.gif/220px-Paloma_08-09Nov08_cmw2.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Paloma_08-09Nov08_cmw2.gif/330px-Paloma_08-09Nov08_cmw2.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Paloma_08-09Nov08_cmw2.gif/440px-Paloma_08-09Nov08_cmw2.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Paloma_08-09Nov08_cmw2.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Radar loop of Hurricane Paloma from peak intensity to landfall in Cuba</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Hurricane Paloma was the final tropical cyclone to impact Cuba during the destructive 2008 season. Preceded by <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Fay_(2008)" title="Tropical Storm Fay (2008)">Fay</a>, Gustav, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hanna_(2008)" title="Hurricane Hanna (2008)">Hanna</a>, and Ike, Paloma compounded damage sustained by the nation. The 2008 season is regarded as the most destructive in Cuban history, primarily due to the effects of Gustav and Ike.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Total damage from hurricanes that year amounted to $9.4 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMOCuba_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-WMOCuba-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cuban utility officials say Paloma's effect on the power grid was not as bad as the destruction caused by Gustav and Ike earlier in the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Paloma did, however knock down power and telephone lines, as well as a major communications tower. The hurricane brought with it a 14-foot (4 meter) storm surge which moved the coastline inland by almost a mile (about 1.5 km) in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_del_Sur" title="Santa Cruz del Sur">Santa Cruz del Sur</a>, doing extensive damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Santa Cruz del Sur where Paloma came ashore, 435 homes were torn to shreds. The sea swept more than a mile inland. The wind and waves left wooden houses in splinters, topped with seaweed. Two of the two-story concrete walls of a factory crumbled into piles of rubble, smashing 57 wooden fishing boats stored inside for safekeeping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cuba_mh_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-cuba_mh-47">[47]</a></sup> An estimated 328 hectares of crops were destroyed by the storm, most of which were in the process of recovery following <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ike" title="Hurricane Ike">Hurricane Ike</a>. A total of 8,000 homes in Santa Cruz were damaged and another 670 in Camaguey and Las Tunas. About 7,000 farmers and 4,700 residences were isolated by floodwaters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Overall damages in Cuba totaled to $300 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMOCuba_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-WMOCuba-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Florida">Florida</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Florida">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Paloma_Florida_landfall.GIF"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="400" height="255" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Paloma_Florida_landfall.GIF/170px-Paloma_Florida_landfall.GIF" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Paloma_Florida_landfall.GIF/255px-Paloma_Florida_landfall.GIF 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Paloma_Florida_landfall.GIF/340px-Paloma_Florida_landfall.GIF 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Paloma_Florida_landfall.GIF" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Infrared satellite image of the remnant of Paloma over the Florida Panhandle</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>After tracking through the Gulf of Mexico, the remnants of Paloma reached the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panhandle" title="Florida Panhandle">Florida Panhandle</a> on November 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-16"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Shortly before crossing the coastline, <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_convection" title="Atmospheric convection">convection</a> suddenly and explosively developed, contributing to a swath of heavy rains. The highest amount was recorded in <a href="/wiki/Bloxham,_Florida" title="Bloxham, Florida">Bloxham</a> at 9.25 inches (235 mm) which contributed to flash flooding. Unofficial radar observations indicated rainfall totals up to 14 inches (360 mm). The torrential rains marked the highest rainfall for November 14 in the state of Florida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIMSS2_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-CIMSS2-16">[16]</a></sup> Flood waters south of <a href="/wiki/Tallahassee,_Florida" title="Tallahassee, Florida">Tallahassee</a> reached 2 feet (0.61 m) in places, stranding vehicles. One person was trapped by flood waters but was rescued without injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>
<p>Owing to the hurricane's destructive effects in the Cayman Islands and Cuba, the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming#Retirement" title="Tropical cyclone naming">retired</a> the name <i>Paloma</i> in the Spring of 2009 and replaced it with <i>Paulette</i>. The name will never again be used for another Atlantic hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cayman_Islands_3">Cayman Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Cayman Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Local groups in the Cayman Islands set up funds following Hurricane Paloma. By March 3, 2009, one group raised $120,000 in relief funds and received another $20,000 in donations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Following the storm, the British Red Cross released £15,000 (US$23,500) in emergency funds. The Government of the Cayman Islands and the Adventist Disaster Response Agency distributed basic relief items such as tarpaulins, hygiene items and buckets to victims of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OCHArpt28_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-OCHArpt28-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>The overall impacts from Hurricane Paloma were reflected in the territory's GDP by a 0.9 percent decrease, worth $33 million, in the expected growth for 2008. Total revenue fell by $11.6 million while expenditures rose by $28.2 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-money_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-money-54">[nb 1]</a></sup> Strain on the economy prompted <a href="/wiki/Inflation" title="Inflation">inflation</a> to rise slightly above 5.2 percent for the year as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CaymanReport_13-5"><a href="#cite_note-CaymanReport-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between November 18 and December 13, 675 tons of debris was collected which covered an area of 21 acres by 12 ft (3.7 m) tall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CaymanReport_13-6"><a href="#cite_note-CaymanReport-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuba_3">Cuba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paloma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Cuba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Already severely impacted by Hurricanes <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Gustav" title="Hurricane Gustav">Gustav</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ike" title="Hurricane Ike">Ike</a>, the European Commission, through its Humanitarian Aid department was already providing €2 million (US$2.7 million) in relief aid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OCHArpt28_52-1"><a href="#cite_note-OCHArpt28-52">[52]</a></sup> By November 21, about 6,000 volunteers from the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_Red_Cross_and_Red_Crescent_Societies" title="International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies">International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies</a> were in Cuba assisting in cleanup efforts and helping victims in shelters. The organization also allocated US$8.8 million in relief funds for 60,000 people affected by Paloma.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[54]</a></sup></p>
