<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Anheuser-Busch brands</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_High_Gravity_Lager&amp;redirect=no" title="Hurricane High Gravity Lager">Hurricane High Gravity Lager</a>)</span></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Anheuser-Busch</b>, a wholly owned subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch_InBev" title="Anheuser-Busch InBev">Anheuser-Busch InBev</a> SA/NV, is the largest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brewing_company" title="Brewing company">brewing company</a> in the United States, with a market share of 45 percent in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company operates 12 breweries in the United States and nearly 20 in other countries, which increased recently since Anheuser-Busch InBev SA/NV acquired <a href="/wiki/SABMiller" title="SABMiller">SABMiller</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Brands include <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Budweiser_(Anheuser-Busch)" title="Budweiser (Anheuser-Busch)">Budweiser</a></i>, <i>Busch</i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michelob" title="Michelob">Michelob</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bud_Light" title="Bud Light">Bud Light</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Natural_Light" title="Natural Light">Natural Light</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Budweiser">Budweiser</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Budweiser">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Budweiser" title="Budweiser">Budweiser</a></div>
<p><i>Budweiser</i> is a 5.0% <a href="/wiki/Alcohol_by_volume" title="Alcohol by volume">ABV</a> Adjunct <a href="/wiki/Pale_lager" title="Pale lager">pale lager</a> introduced in 1876 by <a href="/wiki/Adolphus_Busch" title="Adolphus Busch">Adolphus Busch</a> and has become one of the best selling <a href="/wiki/Beer" title="Beer">beers</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It is made with up to 30% <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a> in addition to <a href="/wiki/Hops" title="Hops">hops</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barley" title="Barley">barley</a> <a href="/wiki/Malt" title="Malt">malt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Protz-CgtwBeer_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Protz-CgtwBeer-4">[4]</a></sup> Budweiser is produced in various <a href="/wiki/Brewery" title="Brewery">breweries</a> located around the United States and the rest of the world. It is a <a href="/wiki/Filtered_beer" title="Filtered beer">filtered beer</a> available in <a href="/wiki/Draught_beer" title="Draught beer">draught</a> and packaged forms. Lower strength versions are distributed in regions with restrictive alcohol laws.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bud_Light">Bud Light</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bud Light">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Introduced in 1982 as <b>Budweiser Light</b>, Budweiser's flagship <a href="/wiki/Light_beer" title="Light beer">light beer</a> with 4.2% ABV and 110 calories per 12 US fl oz (355 mL) serving (1,300 kJ/L). From 1993 to 1998, Bud Light aired 30-second commercials featuring Rob and Laura from the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dick_Van_Dyke_Show" title="The Dick Van Dyke Show">The Dick Van Dyke Show</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bud_Light_Platinum">Bud Light Platinum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bud Light Platinum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A slightly sweeter, higher alcohol version of Bud Light launched in early 2012, with 6% ABV. This product is noted for being packaged in a new translucent blue glass bottle. Bud Light Platinum has 137 calories per 12 ounce serving, 8 fewer than a regular Budweiser.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bud_Light_Apple">Bud Light Apple</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bud Light Apple">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bud Light with apple flavor added. It has 151 calories per 355 ml serving. Released in 2015 with 4% alcohol content by volume.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bud_Light_Lime">Bud Light Lime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Bud Light Lime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bud Light with lime flavor added. It has 116 calories per 12 US fl oz serving (1,370 kJ/L). Released in May 2008 with 4.2% alcohol content, the same alcohol content as Bud Light.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bud_Light_Lime-A-Ritas">Bud Light Lime-A-Ritas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bud Light Lime-A-Ritas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since April 2012, AB has released a line of 8% alcohol by volume flavored malt beverages titled "Bud Light Lime Ritas," with its flagship flavor being the "Lime-a-Rita," a lime flavored beverage. The drinks are available in a twenty-five ounce can, as well as a twelve-pack of eight ounce cans. Since then, AB has released the strawberry-flavored "Straw-Ber-Rita," the mango flavored "Mang-o-Rita," and the raspberry flavored "Raz-Ber-Rita." For the winter 2013 season, AB released the cranberry-flavored "Cran-Brrr-Rita" as well, and wound up extending it through January and February 2014 due to strong sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> After the release of the "Mang-o-Rita" and "Raz-Ber-Rita," A-B released an eighteen-pack case containing six "Lime-a-Ritas," four "Straw-Ber-Ritas," four "Mang-o-Ritas," and four "Raz-Ber-Ritas."</p>
<p>In August 2014, A-B released a new fall seasonal extension for their "Rita" line, "Apple-Ahhh-Rita," an apple-flavored margarita that will be sold until October 2014.</p>
<p>In February 2015, A-B released a new summer seasonal extension for their "Rita" line, "Lemon-Ade-Rita," a lemonade flavored margarita.</p>
<p>In summer 2016, A-B released the newest "rita" flavor of their line named "Water-melon-rita", a watermelon flavored margarita. A-B also released the "Grape-Ahh-Rita," a grape flavored margarita.</p>
<p>In fall 2016, A-B released the new "Cherry-Ahh-Rita," a cherry-flavored margarita.</p>
<p>In summer 2017, A-B released the new "Peach-A-Rita," a peach-flavored margarita. A-B also released the new "Orange-A-Rita," an orange-flavored margarita but only available in MI, OH, TX, FL, and GA. The "Grape-Ahh-Rita" was also renamed "Grape-A-Rita"</p>
<p>In summer 2017, A-B introduced "Splash by Lime-A-Rita," a line of lighter their Lime-A-Ritas with less alcohol, calories, and carbs available in three flavors; the existing "Straw-Ber-Rita" and the two new flavors "Pine-Apple-Rita" and "Coco-Nut-Rita."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Budweiser_Select">Budweiser Select</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Budweiser Select">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Budweiser Select</b>, or <b>Bud Select</b> is a light pale lager that contains 4.3% ABV and 99 calories per 12 US fl oz serving (1,170 kJ/L).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Anheuser-Busch has aggressively promoted Budweiser Select. Its slogan was "The Real Deal". The company hired <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> as a spokesman for the brand.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Budweiser_Select_55">Budweiser Select 55</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Budweiser Select 55">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A version of Budweiser Select that contains 55 calories per 12 US fl oz serving (650 kJ/L) is "a direct counterstrike to Miller's MGD 64" according to Anheuser-Busch officials. Budweiser currently claims that it is the lightest beer in the world. The food energy in both Miller's MGD 64 and Budweiser's Select 55 have been reduced simply by lowering the fermentables content. MGD 64 has only 2.8% alcohol content and some Select 55 states "alcohol content not more than 3.2% by weight / 4% by volume", possibly to allow its sale in areas where that is the limit. The actual alcohol content of "55" is reported to be 2.4% ABV; by comparison, most American lagers have around 5%.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Budweiser_66">Budweiser 66</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Budweiser 66">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Budweiser Brew No. 66 is a 4% alcohol by volume lager that is brewed and distributed in the United Kingdom by <a href="/wiki/InBev" title="InBev">InBev</a> UK Limited. Launched in July 2010, Budweiser 66 has 84 Calories in a 300 ml serving (just over 10 oz).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bud_Ice">Bud Ice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Bud Ice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Budweiser_American_Ale">Budweiser American Ale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Budweiser American Ale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Budweiser_American_Ale" title="Budweiser American Ale">Budweiser American Ale</a> debuted in September 2008. The beer claims to offer complex taste without much bitterness. American Ale has a distinctive hoppier flavor than other Anheuser-Busch beers, in an attempt to capture some of the American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Craft_beer" title="Craft beer">craft beer</a> market, although most American craft beers are hoppier. American Ale is the first beer under the Budweiser name that is brewed with a top fermenting yeast. The beer's darker color is a departure from the other Budweiser brands. Production was discontinued prior to 2015.</p>

It is based on a "Budweiser brewmaster holiday tradition of collecting the richest part of the <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Bud_Dry">Bud Dry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Bud Dry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Bud Dry was introduced nationally in the U.S. in April 1990<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> with the slogan of "Why ask why? Try Bud Dry." It was originally successful in test markets and was expected to be a popular beer with the rise in light lager popularity. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dry_beer#Dry_beer" title="Dry beer">Dry beer</a> is a form of pale lager where the sugars are more fully fermented to give a less sweet beer. It is also known as the Diät-Pils style. However, after the introduction of Bud Ice in 1994, Bud Dry was not heavily marketed. Production was discontinued in December 2010.</p>

<p>Bud Ice Light contains 5.0% ABV and 115 calories per 12 US fl oz serving (1,360 kJ/L). It undergoes <a href="/wiki/Fractional_freezing" title="Fractional freezing">fractional freezing</a>, which Bud Light does not undergo. It was discontinued in 2010.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Bud_Light_Golden_Wheat">Bud Light Golden Wheat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Bud Light Golden Wheat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Bud_Silver">Bud Silver</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Bud Silver">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Michelob">Michelob</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Michelob">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Michelob_ULTRA_1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Michelob_ULTRA_1.jpg/220px-Michelob_ULTRA_1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Michelob_ULTRA_1.jpg/330px-Michelob_ULTRA_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Michelob_ULTRA_1.jpg/440px-Michelob_ULTRA_1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p><i>Michelob</i> is a 4.7% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABV" title="ABV">ABV</a> <a href="/wiki/Pale_lager" title="Pale lager">pale lager</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/Adolphus_Busch" title="Adolphus Busch">Adolphus Busch</a> in 1896 as a "draught beer for connoisseurs".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 1961, Anheuser-Busch produced a <a href="/wiki/Pasteurization" title="Pasteurization">pasteurized</a> version of <i>Michelob</i> which allowed legal shipment of the beer across state lines. Bottled beer began to be shipped soon after, and the brand was introduced in <a href="/wiki/Beverage_can" title="Beverage can">cans</a> in 1966. Bottled <i>Michelob</i> was originally sold in a uniquely shaped bottle named the teardrop bottle because it resembled a water droplet. The teardrop bottle was awarded a medal from the Institute of Design in 1962. Five years later the bottle was redesigned for efficiency in the production line. This bottle was used until 2002 when it was dropped in favor of a traditional bottle. The teardrop bottle was used again from January 2007 to October 2008.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brand_variation">Brand variation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Brand variation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The company introduced <i>Michelob Light</i> in 1978. <i>Michelob Classic Dark</i> was made available in 1981 in kegs, with a bottled version following three years later. In 1991, <i>Michelob Golden Draft</i> was introduced to compete against <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miller_Brewing" title="Miller Brewing">Miller</a> Genuine Draft</i> in the Midwest.</p>
<p>1997 saw the introduction of several specialty beers under the Michelob marquee. These include:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Michelob Honey Lager</i></li>
<li><i>Michelob Pale Ale</i></li>
<li><i>Michelob Marzen</i></li>
<li><i>Michelob Pumpkin Spice Ale</i></li>
<li><i>Michelob Winter's Bourbon Cask Ale</i></li>
</ul>
<p><i>AmberBock</i> is a 5.1% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABV" title="ABV">ABV</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amber_lager" title="Amber lager">amber lager</a> which uses roasted black barley <a href="/wiki/Malt" title="Malt">malt</a> in the ingredients, and which received a World Beer Cup Bronze Medal in 1998.</p>
<p>From the beginning, the specialty beers have had a very limited distribution. The chief outlet has been through a "holiday sampler pack" produced during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_holiday_season" title="Winter holiday season">Christmas holiday season</a>. Other specialty beers that are no longer in production include Michelob Hefeweizen and Michelob Black &amp; Tan. Some (notably <i>Michelob AmberBock</i>) have subsequently gone into larger production, while others have not. The brewery continues to experiment with specialty beers—in 2005 an oak-aged vanilla beer was sold under the Michelob logo, available in single pints. In 2006 Michelob added a chocolate beer to the oak-aged vanilla Celebrate holiday season beer released a year earlier. Michelob also brewed Michelob Bavarian Style Wheat and Michelob Porter for its "holiday sampler pack". In 2007, Michelob launched its Seasonal Specialty Line. These include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Michelob Bavarian Wheat (summer)</li>
<li>Michelob Marzen (fall)</li>
<li>Michelob Porter (winter)</li>
<li>Michelob Pale Ale (spring)</li>
</ul>
<p>The early 21st century saw in the U.S. a demand for diet beer similar to that of the early 1970s, and in 2002 the Michelob line responded with the introduction of Michelob Ultra, advertised as being low in carbohydrates. Later Michelob Ultra Amber, a darker, more flavorful beer, was added to this sub-line.</p>
<p>According to a report by Beer Marketer's Insights and published by <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> on December 9, 2013, sales of Michelob Light declined by nearly 70% between 2007 and 2012. The article listed Michelob Light as one of "nine beers many Americans no longer drink."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Michelob</i> 5% alcohol; <i>Michelob Golden Draft</i> 4.7% alcohol; <i>Michelob Golden Draft Light</i> 4.3% alcohol; <i>Michelob Ultra</i> 4.2% alcohol; <i>Michelob Ultra Amber</i> 4.0% alcohol; <i>Michelob: ULTRA Lime Cactus</i> A fruit-infused light pilsner with natural lime flavor and a floral essence derived from the cactus; <i>Michelob: ULTRA Pomegranate Raspberry</i> A pilsner with a berry aroma, raspberry flavor, and a hint of pomegranate; <i>Michelob: ULTRA Tuscan Orange Grapefruit</i> A pilsner with fresh juicy orange notes and a slight pink-grapefruit finish.</p>
<p>All fruit flavors have the following nutrition content: 107 calories, 6.0g carbs, 0.5g protein and 0.0g fat, per 12 oz bottle.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Advertisements for <i>Michelob Ultra</i> feature people engaged in sporting activities. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michelob_ULTRA_Open_at_Kingsmill" title="Michelob ULTRA Open at Kingsmill">Michelob ULTRA Open at Kingsmill</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michelob_Ultra_Futures_Players_Championship" title="Michelob Ultra Futures Players Championship">Michelob Ultra Futures Players Championship</a>, were sponsored by Michelob Ultra. <i>Michelob Ultra</i> serves as a presenting sponsor of the <a href="/wiki/Tour_of_Missouri" title="Tour of Missouri">Tour of Missouri</a> bicycle race and sponsors the <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Mountains" title="King of the Mountains">King of the Mountains</a> jersey. <i>Michelob</i> also sponsors the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rugby_Super_League_(US)" title="Rugby Super League (US)">Rugby Super League</a>, and many of its teams have shirt sponsorships with its <i>AmberBock</i> brand. <a href="/wiki/PGA_Tour" title="PGA Tour">PGA Tour</a> player <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Garc%C3%ADa" title="Sergio García">Sergio García</a> is sponsored by <i>Michelob</i>. <a href="/wiki/Lance_Armstrong" title="Lance Armstrong">Lance Armstrong</a> signed on October 6, 2009 a three-year agreement to become <i>Michelob Ultra'</i>s new spokesperson and ambassador, but was dropped by the company in 2012 after being accused of using performance-enhancing drugs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Michelob</i> sponsored several episodes of the <a href="/wiki/Diggnation" title="Diggnation">Diggnation</a> podcast. The hosts, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Rose" title="Kevin Rose">Kevin Rose</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Albrecht" title="Alex Albrecht">Alex Albrecht</a>, sampled the beer during the show and several episodes included interviews with the company's head brew-master to discuss the different products that can be found in the sampler packs. Also, an episode of the show was filmed inside the Michelob brewery.</p>
<p>Michelob is most notably famous for its late-1980s TV commercials that used the slogan, "The night belongs to Michelob", which centered on its "night" theme and used songs that had the word "night" or a form of the word "night" in its title, including "<a href="/wiki/Tonight,_Tonight,_Tonight" title="Tonight, Tonight, Tonight">Tonight, Tonight, Tonight</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Genesis_(band)" title="Genesis (band)">Genesis</a> and "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_You_Know_What_the_Night_Can_Do%3F" title="Don't You Know What the Night Can Do?">Don't You Know What the Night Can Do?</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Winwood" title="Steve Winwood">Steve Winwood</a>. In the 1980s and 1990s, Michelob used the slogan "Some days are better than others".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rolling_Rock">Rolling Rock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Rolling Rock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Rock" title="Rolling Rock">Rolling Rock</a></div>
<p><i>Rolling Rock</i> is a 4.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABV" title="ABV">ABV</a> <a href="/wiki/Pale_lager" title="Pale lager">pale lager</a> launched in 1939 by the <a href="/wiki/Latrobe_Brewing_Company" title="Latrobe Brewing Company">Latrobe Brewing Company</a>. In May, 2006, Anheuser-Busch purchased the Rolling Rock brand from <a href="/wiki/InBev" title="InBev">InBev</a> for $82 million and began brewing Rolling Rock at its <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark</a> facility in mid July, 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wsj_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-18">[18]</a></sup> Other pale lagers marketed under the Rolling Rock brand name are <i>Rock Green Light</i>, 3.7% ABV, and <i>Rock Light</i>, 3.5%; the company also produces a 5% ABV amber lager, <i>Rolling Rock Red</i>. Ingredients are pale barley malt, rice, corn and hops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Busch">Busch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Busch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Busch Beer</i>, a 4.3% <a href="/wiki/Alcohol_by_volume" title="Alcohol by volume">ABV</a> economy brand <a href="/wiki/Pale_lager" title="Pale lager">pale lager</a> was introduced in 1955 as <i>Busch Bavarian Beer</i>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> the brand name was changed in 1979 to <i>Busch Beer</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Other beers marketed under the <i>Busch</i> brand name are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Busch_Light_(Anheuser_Busch)" title="Busch Light (Anheuser Busch)">Busch Light</a>, a 4.1% pale lager introduced in 1989, <i>Busch Ice</i>, a 5.9% <a href="/wiki/Ice_beer" title="Ice beer">ice beer</a> introduced in 1995,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and <i>Busch NA</i>, a non-alcoholic brew. Ingredients are a mix of American-grown and imported hops and a combination of malt and corn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> At a slightly lower price point than flagship brand Budweiser, it serves as Anheuser-Busch's second most popular brand.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Shock_Top">Shock Top</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Shock Top">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p><i>Shock Top</i> is a 5.2% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABV" title="ABV">ABV</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_beer#White_or_wheat_beer" title="Belgian beer">Belgian-style wheat ale</a> introduced under the name <i>Spring Heat Spiced Wheat</i> brewed in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Collins,_Colorado" title="Fort Collins, Colorado">Fort Collins, Colorado</a> as a seasonal beer in 2006, then all year from 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The beer is brewed with wheat malt, two-row barley, orange, lemon, lime peel, <a href="/wiki/Coriander" title="Coriander">coriander</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cascade_hops" title="Cascade hops">Cascade</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Willamette_hops" title="Willamette hops">Willamette hops</a>. Entering as the Spring Heat Spiced Wheat, Shock Top Belgian White won gold and bronze medals in the Belgian Wit (White) category at the 2006 and 2007 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_American" title="North American">North American</a> Beer Awards, earning the reputation as America's Beer respectively. The brand now includes some seasonals and specialties that have replaced the Michelob Seasonals. Such varieties include Shock Top Pumpkin Wheat, Shock Top Raspberry Wheat, Shock Top Wheat IPA, Shock Top Lemon Shandy, Shock Top Honeycrisp Apple Wheat, Shock Top Chocolate Wheat, Ginger Wheat, Pretzel Wheat and Shock Top End of the World Midnight Wheat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-26">[26]</a></sup> It competes directly with the <a href="/wiki/MillerCoors" title="MillerCoors">MillerCoors</a> brand <a href="/wiki/Blue_Moon_(beer)" title="Blue Moon (beer)">Blue Moon</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural">Natural</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Natural">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Natural_Light" title="Natural Light">Natural Light</a></i> is an economy brand 4.2% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABV" title="ABV">ABV</a> reduced-calorie <a href="/wiki/Pale_lager" title="Pale lager">pale lager</a> introduced in 1977.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The brand was originally called Anheuser-Busch Natural Light. In 2008 <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i> listed it as the fifth largest selling beer in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> <i>Natural Ice</i> is an economy brand 5.9% ABV <a href="/wiki/Ice_beer" title="Ice beer">ice beer</a>, introduced in 1995. Nearly two decades after the introduction of Natural Ice, a malt liquor, Natty Daddy (8% and 5.9ABV), was added to the market in 2012.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Johnny_Appleseed">Johnny Appleseed</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Johnny Appleseed">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Johnny Appleseed</i> is a 5.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABV" title="ABV">ABV</a> <a href="/wiki/Cider" title="Cider">cider</a> produced by Anheuser-Busch subsidiary Brokenstraw Beverage LLC and introduced in April 2014. Brokenstraw Beverage was created by Anheuser-Busch in 2014 as a corporate identity to manufacture and distribute <i>Johnny Appleseed</i> out of their Baldwinsville, N.Y. brewery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>

<p><i>LandShark Lager</i>, brewed in <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Florida" title="Jacksonville, Florida">Jacksonville</a>, Florida, is a 4.6% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABV" title="ABV">ABV</a> island-style lager launched in 2006 as the house lager for "<a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Buffett%27s_Margaritaville" title="Jimmy Buffett's Margaritaville">Jimmy Buffett's Margaritaville</a>" restaurant chain. Under a sponsorship deal, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dolphin_Stadium" title="Dolphin Stadium">Dolphin Stadium</a>, home of the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Dolphins" title="Miami Dolphins">Miami Dolphins</a>, Florida Marlins and the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Hurricanes" title="Miami Hurricanes">Miami Hurricanes</a>, was renamed "Land Shark Stadium" for the 2009 football season. The contract ended in early 2010, and the stadium has been renamed "Sun Life Stadium" as of January 18, 2010, in time for both the 2010 NFL Pro Bowl and Super Bowl XLIV. The stadium was renamed <a href="/wiki/Hard_Rock_Stadium" title="Hard Rock Stadium">Hard Rock Stadium</a> in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>LandShark also has a Bar &amp; Grill in <a href="/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">Pensacola, Florida</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Beach,_South_Carolina" title="Myrtle Beach, South Carolina">Myrtle Beach, South Carolina</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_City,_New_Jersey" title="Atlantic City, New Jersey">Atlantic City, New Jersey</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and Biloxi, Mississippi.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Goose_Island_Brewery">Goose Island Brewery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Goose Island Brewery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Goose_Island_Brewery" title="Goose Island Brewery">Goose Island Brewery</a></div>
<p><b>Goose Island</b> started in 1988 as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brewpub" title="Brewpub">brewpub</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, and opened a separate bottling plant there in 1995. The brewery and its beers were purchased by <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch_InBev" title="Anheuser-Busch InBev">Anheuser-Busch InBev</a> in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The Chicago brewery continues to produce and sell small batch beers while their national offerings are made in bulk at various Anheuser-Busch facilities.</p>

<p><i>King Cobra</i> is a 6% alcohol by volume <a href="/wiki/Malt_liquor" title="Malt liquor">malt liquor</a> introduced in 1984. It is brewed with a warmer fermentation than used for the company's pale lagers, and the ingredients include barley malt and corn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Shortly after its launch, King Cobra was supported by an advertising campaign featuring actor, martial artist, and former American football player <a href="/wiki/Fred_Williamson" title="Fred Williamson">Fred Williamson</a> and the tag-line "Don't let the smooth taste fool you!"</p>

<p><i>Hurricane High Gravity Lager</i> is an 8.1% alcohol <a href="/wiki/Malt_liquor" title="Malt liquor">malt liquor</a> beverage available primarily in the United States. It is available in 40 ounce bottles, as well as 12, 16, 24, and 25 ounce cans. Recently, Hurricane High Gravity 8.1% has gone from a Black Label to a Silver Label in all of its serving sizes. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spykes">Spykes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=46" title="Edit section: Spykes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The company introduced a flavored 12% ABV <a href="/wiki/Malt_liquor" title="Malt liquor">malt liquor</a> under the name <i>Spykes</i> in 2007. It was sold in colorful, 2-<a href="/wiki/Ounce" title="Ounce">ounce</a> bottles. Available flavors included <a href="/wiki/Mango" title="Mango">mango</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lime_(fruit)" title="Lime (fruit)">lime</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melon" title="Melon">melon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chocolate" title="Chocolate">chocolate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hot_melon_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hot_melon-46">[46]</a></sup> It was withdrawn in the same year after criticism from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alcoholic_beverage" title="Alcoholic beverage">alcohol</a> industry watchdog groups that it was being marketed to underage customers, and the <a href="/wiki/Alcohol_and_Tobacco_Tax_and_Trade_Bureau" title="Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau">Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau</a> found that the labeling of Spykes was illegal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Booze_buzz_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-Booze_buzz-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spykes_labels_illegal_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spykes_labels_illegal-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Minority_ownership_brands">Minority ownership brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anheuser-Busch_brands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=48" title="Edit section: Minority ownership brands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of January 2013, Anheuser-Busch InBev had 32.2% ownership in the <a href="/wiki/Craft_Brew_Alliance" title="Craft Brew Alliance">Craft Brew Alliance</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Beer" title="Beer">beer</a> <a href="/wiki/Brewing" title="Brewing">brewing</a> company<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBA.2C_Inc._Annual_Report_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBA.2C_Inc._Annual_Report-51">[51]</a></sup> that is composed of several beer and cider brands.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Redhook_Ale_Brewery" title="Redhook Ale Brewery">Redhook Ale Brewery</a> founded by Gordon Bowker and Paul Shipman in 1981 in Seattle, Washington;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Widmer_Brothers_Brewery" title="Widmer Brothers Brewery">Widmer Brothers Brewery</a> founded by brothers Kurt and Rob Widmer in 1984 in Portland, Oregon;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kona_Brewing_Company" title="Kona Brewing Company">Kona Brewing Company</a> founded by father and son team Cameron Healy and Spoon Khalsa in 1994 in Kona, Hawaii;</li>
<li>Omission Beer developed internally in 2012 in Portland, Oregon; and</li>
<li>Square Mile Cider, launched in 2013</li>
</ul>
