<p><b>Millersville University of Pennsylvania</b> (commonly known as <b>Millersville University,</b> <b>The Ville,</b>or <b>MU</b>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public university</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Millersville,_Pennsylvania" title="Millersville, Pennsylvania">Millersville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, United States, offering programs embracing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liberal_arts" title="Liberal arts">liberal arts</a> as one of the fourteen schools that comprise the <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_State_System_of_Higher_Education" title="Pennsylvania State System of Higher Education">Pennsylvania State System of Higher Education</a> (PASSHE). Founded in 1855 as the first Normal School in Pennsylvania, Millersville is accredited by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_States_Association_of_Colleges_and_Secondary_Schools" title="Middle States Association of Colleges and Secondary Schools">Middle States Association of Colleges and Secondary Schools</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and the Pennsylvania Department of Education.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>First established in 1854 as the Millersville Academy out of the since demolished Old Main,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> the Academy specialized in a series of workshop-style teacher institutes in response to the 1934 Free School Act of Pennsylvania.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millersville_University_of_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Millersville University was established in 1855 as the <b>Lancaster County Normal School</b>, the first state <a href="/wiki/Normal_school" title="Normal school">normal school</a> in Pennsylvania. It subsequently changed its name to the <b>Millersville State Normal School</b> in 1859 and Millersville later became a state teachers college in 1927. It was renamed <b>Millersville State College</b> in 1959 and officially became Millersville University of Pennsylvania in 1983.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Millersville_University_Old_Main.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="343" data-file-width="419" height="180" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d3/Millersville_University_Old_Main.jpg/220px-Millersville_University_Old_Main.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d3/Millersville_University_Old_Main.jpg/330px-Millersville_University_Old_Main.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/d/d3/Millersville_University_Old_Main.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Millersville_University_Old_Main.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The original Old Main of 1854</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In November 1852, the Lancaster County Educational Association met in Strasburg resolving to form an institute for teacher training. The first institute, which led to the Lancaster County Normal School and received major support from Thomas H. Burrowes, was held in January 1853. While the Association was working to organize, Lewis M. Hobbs, a popular teacher of the Manor district, lobbied heavily in Manor township for a more permanent training facility for teachers. Jacob Shenk, a local farmer, donated a tract of five acres (present-day site of Ganser Library, Biemesderfer Executive Center, and Dutcher Hall) with Hobbs collecting investments from local residents. On April 17, 1855, Lancaster County Normal School opened with <a href="/wiki/James_P._Wickersham" title="James P. Wickersham">James P. Wickersham</a> as Principal and a peak of 147 teachers in attendance. President of the school was Thomas H. Burrowes and Vice President was Lewis M. Hobbs. November 5, 1855 marked the start of the first full session, with a new expansion off of the original Academy building that made 96 rooms available for nearly 200 students and their teachers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Completed in 1894, the <a href="/wiki/Biemesderfer_Executive_Center" title="Biemesderfer Executive Center">Biemesderfer Executive Center</a>, also known as the Old Library, is the centerpiece of Millersville University's campus. The Executive Committee of the Board of Trustees designated $27,500 for the construction of the library in 1891 with the contract awarded to Lancastrian D.H. Rapp, who submitted the lowest bid in a blind auction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Millersville University Library is housed in Ganser Hall. In September 2011, the University closed Ganser Hall for two years in order to complete renovations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On August 26, 2013, the Ganser Library reopened as the McNairy Library and Learning Forum at Ganser Hall.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_facilities">New facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millersville_University_of_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: New facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lombardo_Welcome_Center">Lombardo Welcome Center</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millersville_University_of_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Lombardo Welcome Center">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 29, 2015, local community members Samuel and Dena Lombardo announced a gift to Millersville University of $1.2M for the creation of the University's new Welcome Center and first state-of-the-art Net-Zero energy building on campus. This building, named the Lombardo Welcome Center, will produce as much energy as it consumes and represents the University's largest single-donation to date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On the grounds of former Hull Hall, once complete the Lombardo Welcome Center will house the offices of Admissions, Housing &amp; Residential Life, Marketing and the Office of the Vice President for Enrollment Management.</p>
<h3><span id="Francine_G._McNairy_Library_&amp;_Learning_Forum"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Francine_G._McNairy_Library_.26_Learning_Forum">Francine G. McNairy Library &amp; Learning Forum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millersville_University_of_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Francine G. McNairy Library &amp; Learning Forum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:McNairy_Library_at_Millersville_University.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3264" data-file-width="2448" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/McNairy_Library_at_Millersville_University.jpeg/220px-McNairy_Library_at_Millersville_University.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/McNairy_Library_at_Millersville_University.jpeg/330px-McNairy_Library_at_Millersville_University.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/McNairy_Library_at_Millersville_University.jpeg/440px-McNairy_Library_at_Millersville_University.jpeg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:McNairy_Library_at_Millersville_University.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
McNairy Library at Millersville University</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Originally built from 1965-1967 on the grounds of Old Main, the Helen Ganser Library closed its doors in 2011 for an extensive 2-year renovation project and re-opened in 2013 as the Francine G. McNairy Library &amp; Learning Forum. The entire complex is named after Millersville's 13th President, Dr. Francine McNairy, who began her career at Millersville first as Provost and Vice President of Academic Affairs before becoming President in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Ganser Hall, named for <a href="/wiki/Helen_Ganser" title="Helen Ganser">Helen A. Ganser</a> (1911-1952), librarian and head of the Library Science Department,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> is the 9-story building that houses the University's academic collection. Serving as the academic heart of campus for over 40 years, Ganser Hall's beginning with the two famous "Bookwalks" of 1967.</p>
<p>During the renovation, Library Services was temporarily relocated to Gerhart Hall (22 East Frederick Street). A network of conveyor belts wound around the 9-story stairwell at the beginning of the project so the general collection could be boxed and shipped for temporary storage. Renovation of the building not only brings the academic heart of campus up to ADA Compliance, but also includes redesigned smart classrooms, natural lighting and a café serving Starbucks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On Monday August 26, 2013 the new library finally opened its doors to the Millersville University community as a new, dynamic home for over 500,000 books, 100,000 periodicals, and dozens of other resources, including a brand new reading room as well as a room with garden views of campus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Currently, the Library offers a laptop borrowing service for students, has rooms available for reservation, is home to a 24-hour study room<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and is a part of the EZ-Borrow network, where students, faculty and staff can request a book from another library and arrives in as little as four days to the circulation desk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The Library also hosts several student-worker positions each semester.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Performing_Arts">Performing Arts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millersville_University_of_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Performing Arts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Millersville's Office of Visual and Performing Arts manages two performing arts centers in Lancaster County: <a href="/wiki/The_Ware_Center_of_Millersville_University" title="The Ware Center of Millersville University">The Ware Center</a> and <a href="/wiki/Millersville_University_of_Pennsylvania#Winter_Visual_.26_Performing_Arts_Center" title="Millersville University of Pennsylvania">Winter Visual &amp; Performing Arts Center</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Winter_Visual_&amp;_Performing_Arts_Center"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Winter_Visual_.26_Performing_Arts_Center">Winter Visual &amp; Performing Arts Center</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millersville_University_of_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Winter Visual &amp; Performing Arts Center">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Millersville_University_Music_Students.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="2448" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Millersville_University_Music_Students.jpg/220px-Millersville_University_Music_Students.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Millersville_University_Music_Students.jpg/330px-Millersville_University_Music_Students.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Millersville_University_Music_Students.jpg/440px-Millersville_University_Music_Students.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Built as an expansion of Lyte Auditorium in Alumni Hall,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lancasteronline.com_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-lancasteronline.com-18">[18]</a></sup> the new Charles R. and Anita B Winter Visual and Performing Arts Center has a new entrance off Cottage Avenue in Millersville. The grand opening occurred on Friday, October 12, 2012 after two years of renovations to the original structure, Lyte Auditorium. Named for local philanthropist Charles Winter, whose daughters are both Millersville graduates,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The new Visual and Performing Arts Center, a $26 million construction and renovation project,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> enhances the original 29,041-square-foot building of 700 seats with a 59,452-square-foot addition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Part of the University’s master plan to effectively use and reuse existing land, facilities, and infrastructure, the new Winter Visual &amp; Performing Arts Center houses features a concert hall, recital hall, performance hall, classrooms, a recording studio, piano lab, a music library, faculty offices and more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Known by students as the VPAC (for Visual and Performing Arts Building), other features of the state-of-the-art building are a scenery shop, soundproof classrooms, several sitting areas, a music library and approximately 20 Soundlok rooms, which are modular sound-isolation rooms for practicing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lancasteronline.com_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-lancasteronline.com-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Ware_Center">The Ware Center</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millersville_University_of_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: The Ware Center">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Ware_Center_of_Millersville_University" title="The Ware Center of Millersville University">The Ware Center of Millersville University</a></div>
<p>The Ware Center is a performing arts center in <a href="/wiki/Lancaster,_Pennsylvania" title="Lancaster, Pennsylvania">Lancaster, PA</a> and the Lancaster city campus of Millersville University. Located on North Prince Street at the end of Lancaster's Gallery Row, the Center is part of the Millersville University’s Department of Visual &amp; Performing Arts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MillersvilleU_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-MillersvilleU-24">[24]</a></sup> Originally designed by architect <a href="/wiki/Philip_Johnson" title="Philip Johnson">Philip Johnson</a>, the $32 million building opened in 2008 as the home of the now defunct <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Academy_of_Music" title="Pennsylvania Academy of Music">Pennsylvania Academy of Music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MillersvilleU_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-MillersvilleU-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-:2-26">[26]</a></sup> Since 2010, it has hosted classes for nearly 1,000 Millersville University students during Fall and Spring semesters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Various art exhibits and live performances are held at this venue throughout the year, and the facility can be rented out as a private event venue and banquet hall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
