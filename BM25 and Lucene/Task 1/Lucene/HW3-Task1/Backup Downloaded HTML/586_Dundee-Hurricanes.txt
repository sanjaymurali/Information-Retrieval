<p>The <b>Dundee Hurricanes</b> are an <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Dundee" title="Dundee">Dundee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>. They are members of the National League <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFA" title="BAFA">BAFA</a> recent league restructuring. Since joining the league in 2003, the Hurricanes have qualified for the playoffs in four out of their seasons and won two conference championships.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dundee_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1980–2001"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1980.E2.80.932001">1980–2001</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dundee_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1980–2001">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>American football in Dundee dates back to the 1980s with the founding of the amateur team, the Dundee Whalers. Later a youth <a href="/wiki/Flag_football" title="Flag football">flag football</a> team known as the Dundee Storm was created after its dissolution. They quickly progressed into 9-on-9 youth kitted football and met with great success, winning four consecutive 9-on-9 Scottish Youth Flag Football championships, 1 Scottish Youth Kitted Championship and reaching the semi-finals of the British Youth championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="2002–2003"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2002.E2.80.932003">2002–2003</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dundee_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2002–2003">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following these years of success many of the youth players became old enough to play senior football, and so the Dundee Hurricanes were formed in 2002. They immediately applied to enter the BAFL and were admitted into Division 2 North for the 2003 season. They met with initial success, recording consecutive <a href="/wiki/Shutout" title="Shutout">shutouts</a> in their first two matches against the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tees_Valley_Cougars&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tees Valley Cougars (page does not exist)">Tees Valley Cougars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Wolves" title="Edinburgh Wolves">Edinburgh Wolves</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> After that their form was less consistent but they still finished an impressive fourth out of eleven teams in their debut season, recording a 6–4 record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="2004–2006"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2004.E2.80.932006">2004–2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dundee_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2004–2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The club went on to build on that success during 2004, starting off in the best possible way by recording another shutout in their first match away to <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_Saxons" title="Peterborough Saxons">East Midland Saxons</a>. They lost just twice during the regular season as they finished clear winners of the Scottish Conference. Their good form continued as they beat the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merseyside_Nighthawks" title="Merseyside Nighthawks">Merseyside Nighthawks</a> in their first playoff match, but they were eventually beaten in the semi-final by a single point by the eventual divisional champions <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Mustangs_(American_football)" title="South Yorkshire Mustangs (American football)">Doncaster Mustangs</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In contrast to the previous year, the 2005 season was an inconsistent one. The team began badly, losing their first three games, but that bad start was followed up by five consecutive wins. They eventually finished with a 5-5 record, which was good enough to clinch second place in the Scottish Conference and ensure they qualified for the playoffs again. Unfortunately, they failed to register any points in their quarter-final, going down 0–35 to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coventry_Jets_(American_football)" title="Coventry Jets (American football)">Coventry Jets</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>2006 was a fairly mediocre one for the Hurricanes as they finished with a 3–4 record. However, there were two positives that team were able to take – during their seven games, the team only conceded 38 points, and by virtue of there only being three teams in their conference, they finished second and therefore qualified for the playoffs again. Unfortunately, just as in the previous year they fell at the quarter-final stage again without scoring a single point, losing 0–6 to the Redditch Arrows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="2007–2009"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.932009">2007–2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dundee_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2007–2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Hurricanes finally enjoyed real success in the 2007 season. As part of the divisional reshuffled, the Scottish and Northern conferences were combined into a single six-team conference. After recording a number of heavy wins and finishing with nine wins and just a single defeat, they finished clear winners of their group and so gained a home draw in the playoffs. They made that home advantage count, first defeating <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_Saxons" title="Peterborough Saxons">East Midland Saxons</a> in the quarter-finals, then thrashing the Manchester Titans in the semi-final to book a place at the divisional final against the <a href="/wiki/Norwich_Devils" title="Norwich Devils">Norwich Devils</a>, a team who had gone through the entire season unbeaten and were strongly fancied to win. In a match played at the <a href="/wiki/Don_Valley_Stadium" title="Don Valley Stadium">Don Valley Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a> and broadcast on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Five_(channel)" title="Five (channel)">Five</a>, their opponents scored early and raced into a 20–0 lead at half-time. Despite a second half fightback, the Hurricanes were unable to overturn this deficit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Even though they lost the final, they were still celebrating promotion for the first time in their history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="2010–2012"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.932012">2010–2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dundee_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010–2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
