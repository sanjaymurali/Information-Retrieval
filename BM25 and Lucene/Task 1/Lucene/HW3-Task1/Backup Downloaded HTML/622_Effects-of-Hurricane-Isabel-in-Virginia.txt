<p>The <b>effects of Hurricane Isabel in Virginia</b> proved to be the costliest disaster in the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Virginia" title="History of Virginia">history of Virginia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cws_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-cws-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">Hurricane Isabel</a> formed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> on September 6, 2003 in the tropical <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. It moved northwestward, and within an environment of light wind shear and warm waters it steadily strengthened to reach peak winds of 265 km/h (165 mph) on September 11. After fluctuating in intensity for four days, Isabel gradually weakened and made landfall on the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">Outer Banks</a> of North Carolina with winds of 165 km/h (105 mph) on September 18. It quickly weakened over land as it passed through central Virginia, and Isabel became <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> over western <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> on September 19.</p>
<p>Strong winds from the hurricane affected 99 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_counties_in_Virginia" title="List of counties in Virginia">counties</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Virginia" title="List of cities in Virginia">cities</a> in the state,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cws_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-cws-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> which downed thousands of trees and left about 1.8 million without power. The <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> impacted much of the southeastern portion of the state, peaking at around 9 feet (2.7 m) in <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond</a> along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_River_(Virginia)" title="James River (Virginia)">James River</a>; the surge caused significant damage to homes along riverways. The nationwide maximum rainfall total from the hurricane was 20.2 inches (513 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Sherando,_Virginia" title="Sherando, Virginia">Sherando, Virginia</a>. In the state's mountainous region, heavy rainfall caused severe and damaging <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">flash flooding</a>. The hurricane caused about $1.85 billion (2003 USD, $2.17 billion 2008 USD) in damage and 36 deaths in the state—10 directly from the storm's effects and 26 indirectly related.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vaem_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-vaem-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_Hurricane_Isabel_in_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By four days before Isabel made landfall, most <a href="/wiki/Numerical_weather_prediction" title="Numerical weather prediction">weather models</a> predicted Isabel to make landfall between North Carolina and <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-avila33_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-avila33-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-archive_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-archive-5">[5]</a></sup> Initially, forecasters predicted it to move along the coastline of the <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">Chesapeake Bay</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-avila33_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-avila33-4">[4]</a></sup> though as the hurricane neared land the predicted track was much closer to where it ultimately was. The National Hurricane Center issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane watch</a> from the <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>/Virginia border to <a href="/wiki/Chincoteague,_Virginia" title="Chincoteague, Virginia">Chincoteague</a> near its border with <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a> about 50 hours before Isabel struck land, including the southern portion of the Chesapeake Bay. 18 hours before the hurricane made landfall, the National Hurricane Center upgraded the watch to a hurricane warning for the entire coastline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-6">[6]</a></sup> Additionally, inland hurricane and tropical storm warnings were issued for south-central Virginia. The <a href="/wiki/Wakefield,_Virginia" title="Wakefield, Virginia">Wakefield</a> <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> office issued three <a href="/wiki/Tornado_warning" title="Tornado warning">tornado warnings</a> for four counties, though none became tornadoes. The office also issued two county-wide flood warnings and 43 flood warnings and flood statements for various river basins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Virginia Emergency Management Agency was activated on September 15, about three days prior to Isabel making landfall and entering the state. Officials in <a href="/wiki/Hampton,_Virginia" title="Hampton, Virginia">Hampton</a> issued the first mandatory evacuation in the state on September 17, about 35 hours prior to landfall. Eleven hours later, a mandatory evacuation was issued for some residents in <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake,_Virginia" title="Chesapeake, Virginia">Chesapeake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">Norfolk</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">Virginia Beach</a>, with a recommended evacuation for some residents in the city of <a href="/wiki/Suffolk,_Virginia" title="Suffolk, Virginia">Suffolk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight_County,_Virginia" title="Isle of Wight County, Virginia">Isle of Wight</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northumberland_County,_Virginia" title="Northumberland County, Virginia">Northumberland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richmond_County,_Virginia" title="Richmond County, Virginia">Richmond</a>, and <a href="/wiki/York_County,_Virginia" title="York County, Virginia">York counties</a>. Later on September 17 <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Virginia" title="Governor of Virginia">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Mark_Warner" title="Mark Warner">Mark Warner</a> provided authorization for all recommended evacuations to become mandatory. By the time Isabel entered Virginia late on September 18, evacuations were also issued for <a href="/wiki/Accomack_County,_Virginia" title="Accomack County, Virginia">Accomack County</a>, Chincoteague, <a href="/wiki/Gloucester_County,_Virginia" title="Gloucester County, Virginia">Gloucester County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_County,_Virginia" title="Lancaster County, Virginia">Lancaster County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mathews_County,_Virginia" title="Mathews County, Virginia">Mathews County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newport_News,_Virginia" title="Newport News, Virginia">Newport News</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poquoson,_Virginia" title="Poquoson, Virginia">Poquoson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth,_Virginia" title="Portsmouth, Virginia">Portsmouth</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Westmoreland_County,_Virginia" title="Westmoreland County, Virginia">Westmoreland County</a>. The zones ordered to evacuate included residents along waterfronts, in areas prone to flooding, potentially affected by storm surge from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 2</a> hurricane, low-lying areas, health care facilities, or islands. The tools officials used to determine the evacuation zones included shelter locations, the <a href="/wiki/Sea,_Lake,_and_Overland_Surge_from_Hurricanes" title="Sea, Lake, and Overland Surge from Hurricanes">SLOSH storm surge model</a>, evacuation maps, and clearance times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assess_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-assess-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite the orders, a relatively small number of people evacuated for the hurricane. According to a telephone survey conducted by the United States Department of Commerce, the highest participation rate was for residents in the Northern Neck in areas potentially affected by the storm surge from a Category 2 hurricane, of which 41% in the survey stated they left their houses for a safer location. In <a href="/wiki/Surry,_Virginia" title="Surry, Virginia">Surry</a>, only 9% of those in a Category 2 hurricane storm surge zone left. 30% who participated in the survey along the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Shore_of_Virginia" title="Eastern Shore of Virginia">Eastern Shore</a> left. The primary reasons for the choice whether to evacuate or not were due to the track of Isabel, its strength, or influence from the media. Most participants in the survey stated they did not hear any sort of evacuation notice from public officials in their location, however. Of those who evacuated, about 64% left for the house of a friend or a relative, with about 24% evacuating to a hotel or a motel. Most of those in Hampton and Norfolk left for elsewhere in the state, while the majority of those in the Northern Neck evacuated to destinations in their own neighborhood or community. The evacuation destinations on the Eastern Shore of Virginia were varied, with 23% leaving for Maryland and 46% staying in their own neighborhood or community. The length of the evacuation process varied between a few hours to two days, with the worst evacuation problems being closed or flooded roads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assess_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-assess-8">[8]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Army_National_Guard" title="Virginia Army National Guard">Virginia Army National Guard</a> and State Police troopers assisted in the evacuations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap919_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap919-9">[9]</a></sup> In all, more than 160,000 residents in southeastern Virginia were told to evacuate, including 11,000 in vulnerable locations along the Chesapeake Bay<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap917_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap917-10">[10]</a></sup> and all residents in mobile home parks in Chesapeake and Newport News.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap918_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap918-11">[11]</a></sup> A total of about 16,325 people evacuated to 67 shelters. Some of the reported problems were shortages of supplies, unanticipated medical issues, overcrowding, and lack of security.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assess_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-assess-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p><a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> officials in Norfolk ordered more than 40 destroyers, frigates, and amphibious ships out to sea to avoid any potential damage from the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cruiser_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-cruiser-12">[12]</a></sup> Officials at the <a href="/wiki/Langley_Air_Force_Base" title="Langley Air Force Base">Langley Air Force Base</a> in Hampton ordered about 6,000 workers to evacuate elsewhere, due to its vulnerability to flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap917_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap917-10">[10]</a></sup> About 350 National Guard workers assisted boat owners in the southeastern portion of the state. In Mathews County, two boat owners experienced fatal heart attacks as they worked to protect their boats. Officials distributed sandbags throughout the state for residents in flood-prone areas, including about 10,000 in the city of <a href="/wiki/Alexandria,_Virginia" title="Alexandria, Virginia">Alexandria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rtd919_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-rtd919-13">[13]</a></sup> Prior to the arrival of the hurricane, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay_Bridge_Tunnel" title="Chesapeake Bay Bridge Tunnel">Chesapeake Bay Bridge Tunnel</a> was closed, as were all campgrounds along the <a href="/wiki/Blue_Ridge_Parkway" title="Blue Ridge Parkway">Blue Ridge Parkway</a> and the primary parkway in <a href="/wiki/Roanoke,_Virginia" title="Roanoke, Virginia">Roanoke</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap919_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap919-9">[9]</a></sup> In Chincoteague, the famous <a href="/wiki/Chincoteague_Pony" title="Chincoteague Pony">Chincoteague Ponies</a> were moved by volunteer firefighters to grounds of about 20 feet (6.1 m) higher. Officials closed schools, government offices, and businesses across the eastern portion of the state, leaving usually heavily congested roads as empty streets. Additionally, officials canceled trains along the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Metro" title="Washington Metro">Washington Metro</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Railway_Express" title="Virginia Railway Express">Virginia Railway Express</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Amtrak" title="Amtrak">Amtrak</a> lines, and several flights in and out of the <a href="/wiki/Richmond_International_Airport" title="Richmond International Airport">Richmond International Airport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rtd919_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-rtd919-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_Hurricane_Isabel_in_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Throughout the state, Hurricane Isabel resulted in a damage total of $1.85 billion (2003 USD, $2.17 billion 2008 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vaem_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-vaem-3">[3]</a></sup> The hurricane destroyed more than 1,186 homes and 77 businesses, severely damaged 9,110 homes and 333 businesses, and left 107,908 homes and over 1,000 businesses with minor damage. Across the state, the hurricane generated an estimated 660,000 dump trucks of debris. At least ten people were directly killed by the storm, and hundreds more were injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc1_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc1-14">[14]</a></sup> A total of 1.8 million electrical customers were left without power,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup> with electrical damage totaling $128 million (2003 USD, $150 million 2008 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc1_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc1-14">[14]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dominion_Virginia_Power" title="Dominion Virginia Power">Dominion Virginia Power</a> reported 2,311 broken utility poles, 3,899 snapped crossarms, and 7,363 spans of downed power lines, with 72% of its primary distribution circuits damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iser923_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-iser923-15">[15]</a></sup> The passage of the hurricane resulted in an agricultural damage total of about $117 million (2003 USD, $137 million 2008 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc1_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc1-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hampton_Roads_Metropolitan_Area_and_Delmarva_Peninsula">Hampton Roads Metropolitan Area and Delmarva Peninsula</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_Hurricane_Isabel_in_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hampton Roads Metropolitan Area and Delmarva Peninsula">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Isabel_flood_water.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1680" data-file-width="2859" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Hurricane_Isabel_flood_water.jpg/250px-Hurricane_Isabel_flood_water.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Hurricane_Isabel_flood_water.jpg/375px-Hurricane_Isabel_flood_water.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Hurricane_Isabel_flood_water.jpg/500px-Hurricane_Isabel_flood_water.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Isabel_flood_water.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Flooding at Langley Air Force Base</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Along the Eastern Shore of Virginia, Isabel produced sustained winds reaching 50 mph (80 km/h) with gusts to 62 mph (100 km/h) at <a href="/wiki/Wallops_Island" title="Wallops Island">Wallops Island</a>. Higher unofficial gusts were recorded, including a peak reading of 71 mph (114 km/h) in Chincoteague. Rainfall was fairly light, with 2 inches (51 mm) recorded in <a href="/wiki/Heathsville,_Virginia" title="Heathsville, Virginia">Heathsville</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erhnws_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-erhnws-16">[16]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Fisherman_Island_(Virginia)" title="Fisherman Island (Virginia)">Fisherman Island</a>, the hurricane produced a 4.26-foot (1.3-m) storm surge, flooding much of the island. The combination of the surge and waves resulted in minor beach erosion and overwash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allenoertel_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-allenoertel-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Northampton_County,_Virginia" title="Northampton County, Virginia">Northampton County</a> reported $10 million in crop damage (2003 USD, $12 million 2008 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cws_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-cws-1">[1]</a></sup> One person died in Accomack County when a tree fell on his mobile home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads" title="Hampton Roads">Hampton Roads</a> region, Isabel produced a high storm surge, including reports of 7.5 feet (2.3 m) at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay_Bridge-Tunnel" title="Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel">Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel</a> and a peak of 8.3 feet (2.5 m) at <a href="/wiki/Gloucester_Point,_Virginia" title="Gloucester Point, Virginia">Gloucester Point</a>. Unofficially higher amounts included an estimate of 10.75 feet (3.28 m) at <a href="/wiki/Smithfield,_Virginia" title="Smithfield, Virginia">Smithfield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-6">[6]</a></sup> In some locations, the surge surpassed the previous record set by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1933_Chesapeake-Potomac_Hurricane" title="1933 Chesapeake-Potomac Hurricane">1933 Chesapeake-Potomac Hurricane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erhnws_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-erhnws-16">[16]</a></sup> Despite being classified as a hurricane while crossing the state, sustained winds only peaked at 70 mph (110 km/h) at Gloucester Point, 4 mph (6.4 km/h) less than minimum hurricane intensity. Hurricane-force wind gusts were recorded at multiple locations, with a 91 mph (146 km/h) reading at Gloucester Point and unofficial reports peaking at 107 mph (172 km/h) on <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gwynns_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gwynns Island (page does not exist)">Gwynns Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-6">[6]</a></sup> Additionally, Chesapeake Light, located 12 miles (19 km) east of Virginia Beach, reported a peak wind gust of 107 mph (172 km/h). The hurricane produced heavy rainfall in the Hampton Roads area, reaching 10.6 inches (270 mm) at <a href="/wiki/James_City_County,_Virginia" title="James City County, Virginia">James City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erhnws_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-erhnws-16">[16]</a></sup> The outer <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Physical_structure" title="Tropical cyclone">rainbands</a> of Isabel spawned a 150-foot (46 m) wide <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">F0</a> tornado in <a href="/wiki/Nottoway_County,_Virginia" title="Nottoway County, Virginia">Nottoway County</a> near <a href="/wiki/Crewe,_Virginia" title="Crewe, Virginia">Crewe</a>, the only confirmed tornado during the passage of the hurricane. However, many people in the city of Norfolk reported that there was a tornado in that city as well. The tornado lasted a short amount of time and caused no known damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torn_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-torn-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdctorn_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdctorn-19">[19]</a></sup> Strong waves, which reached 20 feet (6.1 m) in height offshore, caused severe beach erosion in Hampton, Newport News, and other locations along the western shore of the Chesapeake Bay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erhnws_16-3"><a href="#cite_note-erhnws-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Virginia Beach, the 15th Street fishing pier was significantly damaged due to intense wave action. Likewise the historic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ocean_View,_Virginia#Decline.2C_reemergence" title="Ocean View, Virginia">Harrison's Pier</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ocean_View,_Virginia" title="Ocean View, Virginia">Ocean View</a> area of <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a> was completely destroyed. Many private piers and some public ones along the coastline were destroyed or damaged from the waves and storm surge, including a destroyed pier each in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buckroe_Beach,_Virginia" title="Buckroe Beach, Virginia">Buckroe Beach</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lynnhaven" title="Lynnhaven">Lynnhaven</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erhnws_16-4"><a href="#cite_note-erhnws-16">[16]</a></sup> Moderate winds caused light damage to roofs and siding of oceanfront homes and hotels. In Virginia Beach, beach erosion and coastal flooding was minimal, credited due to a $125 million beach expansion project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rtd920_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-rtd920-20">[20]</a></sup> Several bridges in the Hampton Roads area were closed due to the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap919_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-ap919-9">[9]</a></sup> The strong storm surge surpassed the floodgate to the <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Tunnel_(Virginia)" title="Midtown Tunnel (Virginia)">Midtown Tunnel</a> while workers attempted to close the gate. About 44 million US gallons (170,000 m<sup>3</sup>) of water from the <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_River_(Virginia)" title="Elizabeth River (Virginia)">Elizabeth River</a> flooded the tunnel entirely in just 40 minutes, with the workers barely able to escape. The flooding left the tunnel damaged and closed for nearly a month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-isab_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-isab-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Heavy rainfall led to moderate to severe inland flooding, with high waters reported along <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_460" title="U.S. Route 460">U.S. Route 460</a> in <a href="/wiki/Prince_George_County,_Virginia" title="Prince George County, Virginia">Prince George County</a>, <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_17_in_Virginia" title="U.S. Route 17 in Virginia">U.S. Route 17</a>, and several other roads. The rainfall led to river flooding, including minor to moderate flooding along the <a href="/wiki/Rivanna_River" title="Rivanna River">Rivanna</a> and James Rivers, moderate flooding along the <a href="/wiki/Appomattox_River" title="Appomattox River">Appomattox River</a>, and moderate to major flooding along the <a href="/wiki/Meherrin_River" title="Meherrin River">Meherrin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nottoway_River" title="Nottoway River">Nottoway</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Blackwater_River_(Virginia)" title="Blackwater River (Virginia)">Blackwater Rivers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erhnws_16-5"><a href="#cite_note-erhnws-16">[16]</a></sup> In Isle of Wight County, a driver attempted to cross high water on the <a href="/wiki/James_River_Bridge" title="James River Bridge">James River Bridge</a> and was killed when the water washed the car off of the road.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The unusually large wind field uprooted many thousands of trees, downed many power lines, damaged hundreds of houses, and snapped thousands of telephone poles and cross arms. Hundreds of roads, including major highways, were blocked by fallen trees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hrncdc_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-hrncdc-22">[22]</a></sup> An emergency evacuation shelter in Newport News reported minor wind damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assess_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-assess-8">[8]</a></sup> Two people in the region died due to falling trees—one in <a href="/wiki/New_Kent_County,_Virginia" title="New Kent County, Virginia">New Kent County</a> and one in the city of <a href="/wiki/Hopewell,_Virginia" title="Hopewell, Virginia">Hopewell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup> Power outages left most traffic lights not working across Hampton Roads, resulting in multiple minor car accidents. Additionally, most gas stations were closed, due to power outages leaving the pumps unusable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rtd920_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-rtd920-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Old_Dominion_University" title="Old Dominion University">Old Dominion University</a> remained closed for two weeks due to storm damage and power issues, the longest it has ever been closed during a school term. Likewise, <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_State_University" title="Norfolk State University">Norfolk State University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Regent_University" title="Regent University">Regent University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tidewater_Community_College" title="Tidewater Community College">Tidewater Community College</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Virginia_Medical_School" title="Eastern Virginia Medical School">Eastern Virginia Medical School</a> all experienced significant closing times due to both storm damage and a lack of electricity.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern">Northern</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_Hurricane_Isabel_in_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Northern">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Funnel_cloud" title="Funnel cloud">Funnel clouds</a> were reported along the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Neck" title="Northern Neck">Northern Neck</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torn_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-torn-18">[18]</a></sup> The hurricane produced a strong storm surge across northern Virginia, reaching 9.5 feet (2.9 m) in Alexandria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc1_14-3"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc1-14">[14]</a></sup> Rainfall was light across the region, with amounts varying between 1–3 inches (25–75 mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-rain-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The storm surge washed out 160 homes and 60 condominiums in <a href="/wiki/Fairfax_County,_Virginia" title="Fairfax County, Virginia">Fairfax County</a>, with an additional 2,000 units reporting minor to severe damage from the flooding. In <a href="/wiki/Stafford_County,_Virginia" title="Stafford County, Virginia">Stafford County</a>, the surge destroyed five marinas and broke many boats free from their docks, while in Alexandria it flooded numerous businesses and severely impacting marinas. One section of <a href="/wiki/CSX_Transportation" title="CSX Transportation">CSX Transportation</a> railway tracks in <a href="/wiki/Prince_William_County,_Virginia" title="Prince William County, Virginia">Prince William County</a> collapsed into the <a href="/wiki/Potomac_River" title="Potomac River">Potomac River</a> from the surge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc1_14-4"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc1-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gusty winds downed several trees across Alexandria, causing about $2 million in damage (2003 USD, $2.3 million 2008 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc1_14-5"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc1-14">[14]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Arlington_County,_Virginia" title="Arlington County, Virginia">Arlington County</a>, flooding and downed trees destroyed two houses and damaged 192 homes, 46 severely. The storm surge flooded a parking lot at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reagan_National_Airport" title="Reagan National Airport">Reagan National Airport</a>. Damage in the county totaled $2.5 million (2003 USD, $2.9 million 2008 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc1_14-6"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc1-14">[14]</a></sup> Falling trees caused major damage to 15 homes in the city of <a href="/wiki/Fairfax,_Virginia" title="Fairfax, Virginia">Fairfax</a>, and damage throughout Fairfax County totaled $18 million (2003 USD, $21 million 2008 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc1_14-7"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc1-14">[14]</a></sup> Four homes and 20 businesses in <a href="/wiki/King_George_County,_Virginia" title="King George County, Virginia">King George County</a> were severely damaged, with an additional 150–200 reporting lesser damage from winds and falling trees. Gusty winds forced the closure of the <a href="/wiki/Governor_Harry_W._Nice_Memorial_Bridge" title="Governor Harry W. Nice Memorial Bridge">Governor Harry W. Nice Memorial Bridge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc1_14-8"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc1-14">[14]</a></sup> In Prince William County, seven homes were destroyed, with 24 homes and three businesses experiencing major damage. Several roads were closed due to downed trees. The <a href="/wiki/Marine_Corps_Base_Quantico" title="Marine Corps Base Quantico">Marine Corps Base Quantico</a> reported severe damage amounting to $9.5 million (2003 USD, $11 million 2008 USD). Damage included a destroyed marina from flooding, with falling trees damaging buildings and vehicles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc1_14-9"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc1-14">[14]</a></sup> Additionally, fallen trees severely damaged 31 homes and caused minor damage to 68 more in Stafford County.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc1_14-10"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc1-14">[14]</a></sup> The roof of a shelter in Mathews County was partially blown off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assess_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-assess-8">[8]</a></sup> A power outage in Northumberland County caused the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NOAA_Weather_Radio_All_Hazards" title="NOAA Weather Radio All Hazards">NOAA Weather Radio Station</a> in Heathsville to go off the air during the height of the storm, leaving the transmitter out of service for several days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Central_and_southwest">Central and southwest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_Hurricane_Isabel_in_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Central and southwest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Isabel_RVA_tree_split.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Isabel_RVA_tree_split.jpg/200px-Isabel_RVA_tree_split.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Isabel_RVA_tree_split.jpg/300px-Isabel_RVA_tree_split.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Isabel_RVA_tree_split.jpg/400px-Isabel_RVA_tree_split.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Isabel_RVA_tree_split.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Downed tree in Richmond</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A tornado was reported near <a href="/wiki/Emporia,_Virginia" title="Emporia, Virginia">Emporia</a>, though this was not later confirmed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scotsman_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-scotsman-24">[24]</a></sup> The storm surge tracked into the central portion of the state, with a site on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_River_(Virginia)" title="James River (Virginia)">James River</a> in Richmond reporting an estimated surge of about 9 feet (2.7 m), about 103 river miles (166 km) from its confluence with the lower Chesapeake Bay. Additionally, widespread areas of heavy rainfall of over 5 inches (130 mm) led to flooding of rivers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup> Richmond set a new daily rainfall record from the precipitation from Isabel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rtd919_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-rtd919-13">[13]</a></sup> Strong winds were reported throughout the region, and wind gusts reached 73 mph (117 km/h) at Richmond International Airport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-centncdc_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-centncdc-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The storm surge significantly damaged or destroyed many homes along the James River, particularly in the towns of <a href="/wiki/Claremont,_Virginia" title="Claremont, Virginia">Claremont</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Burwells_Bay,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Burwells Bay, Virginia (page does not exist)">Burwells Bay, Virginia</a>. The surges in several tidal rivers caught some residents by surprise, both in height and severity. A man in <a href="/wiki/Henrico_County,_Virginia" title="Henrico County, Virginia">Henrico County</a> drowned after crashing into a flooded creek.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-7"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The winds downed trees throughout the area, some of which hit homes and vehicles. In <a href="/wiki/Amelia_County,_Virginia" title="Amelia County, Virginia">Amelia County</a>, strong winds removed a mobile home from its foundation and destroyed it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torn_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-torn-18">[18]</a></sup> Falling trees killed two in the area—one in <a href="/wiki/Chesterfield_County,_Virginia" title="Chesterfield County, Virginia">Chesterfield County</a> and one in the city of Richmond.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-8"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup> The downed trees snapped many power lines, leaving about 365,000 Dominion Virginia Power customers in the Richmond area without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rtd919_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-rtd919-13">[13]</a></sup> A portion of the roof of <a href="/wiki/The_Diamond_(Richmond,_Virginia)" title="The Diamond (Richmond, Virginia)">The Diamond</a>, home of the <a href="/wiki/Richmond_Braves" title="Richmond Braves">Richmond Braves</a>, was damaged by the winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wet conditions caused many accidents along roadways in central Virginia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rtd919_13-4"><a href="#cite_note-rtd919-13">[13]</a></sup> A motorist on <a href="/wiki/Interstate_95_in_Virginia" title="Interstate 95 in Virginia">Interstate 95</a> in Richmond died when he <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydroplaning_(tires)" title="Hydroplaning (tires)">hydroplaned</a> and crashed his car. In <a href="/wiki/Albemarle_County,_Virginia" title="Albemarle County, Virginia">Albemarle County</a>, a popular forest boardwalk that was part of the <a href="/wiki/Monticello" title="Monticello">Monticello</a> property was destroyed by a falling oak tree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Also in Albemarle, two people were killed when their car drove off a road and crashed into a tree during heavy rainfall. A man in Chesterfield County died when struck by a tree. Three people died when a car in southern <a href="/wiki/Fluvanna_County,_Virginia" title="Fluvanna County, Virginia">Fluvanna County</a> crashed into a tree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap919_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-ap919-9">[9]</a></sup> The deaths were considered indirectly related to the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-9"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup> In south-central Virginia, strong winds produced widespread wind damage, with numerous trees and power lines reported down. In <a href="/wiki/Campbell_County,_Virginia" title="Campbell County, Virginia">Campbell County</a>, two homes were destroyed and ten others suffered minor damage. One residence in <a href="/wiki/Amherst_County,_Virginia" title="Amherst County, Virginia">Amherst County</a> suffered minor damage, and in <a href="/wiki/Appomattox_County,_Virginia" title="Appomattox County, Virginia">Appomattox County</a> one home reported major damage. In <a href="/wiki/Buckingham_County,_Virginia" title="Buckingham County, Virginia">Buckingham County</a>, one home was destroyed, three reported major damage, and one business suffered minor damage, while in <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_County,_Virginia" title="Charlotte County, Virginia">Charlotte County</a>, two homes were destroyed, 30 homes suffered major damage, and five businesses suffered minor damage. The storm destroyed one home and damaged 20, severely damaged three businesses, and caused some crop damage in <a href="/wiki/Pittsylvania_County,_Virginia" title="Pittsylvania County, Virginia">Pittsylvania County</a>. Monetary damage in the region totaled about $3 million (2003 USD; $4 million 2008 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcx_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdcx-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shenandoah_Valley">Shenandoah Valley</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_Hurricane_Isabel_in_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Shenandoah Valley">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Intense rainbands from Isabel produced heavy rainfall across the <a href="/wiki/Shenandoah_Valley" title="Shenandoah Valley">Shenandoah Valley</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shncdc_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-shncdc-29">[29]</a></sup> peaking at 20.2 inches (513 mm) in Upper Sherando in <a href="/wiki/Augusta_County,_Virginia" title="Augusta County, Virginia">Augusta County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-rain-23">[23]</a></sup> Sustained winds in the area ranged from 25–50 mph (40–80 km/h), while wind gusts reached about 60 mph (97 km/h). Strong winds downed numerous trees and power lines, causing some power outages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shncdc2_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-shncdc2-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>The rainfall led to extensive flash flooding and river flooding, including along several <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributaries</a> of the <a href="/wiki/South_River_(South_Fork_Shenandoah_River)" title="South River (South Fork Shenandoah River)">South River</a>. The rainfall also caused extensive <a href="/wiki/Surface_runoff" title="Surface runoff">surface runoff</a> in higher terrains, which led to flow over emergency <a href="/wiki/Spillway" title="Spillway">spillways</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-10"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup> Four spillways to dams flooded, causing damage, though none of them failed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shncdc_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-shncdc-29">[29]</a></sup> At the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mills_Creek_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mills Creek Dam (page does not exist)">Mills Creek Dam</a>, the water reached about 2.5 feet (0.76 m) above the emergency spill way,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-11"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup> causing $125,000 in damage to the dam (2003 USD). Water flowed down the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Creek_(Virginia)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Black Creek (Virginia) (page does not exist)">Black Creek</a> at 60 mph (97 km/h), washing out the bridge and several hundred feet of asphalt along several locations of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Virginia_State_Route_608&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Virginia State Route 608 (page does not exist)">State Route 608</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shncdc_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-shncdc-29">[29]</a></sup> The South River at <a href="/wiki/Waynesboro,_Virginia" title="Waynesboro, Virginia">Waynesboro</a> rapidly flooded and crested at nearly 13.9 feet (4.2 m), destroying four bridges and flooding downtown businesses in 2 feet (0.61 m)–3 feet (0.91 m) of water. There, emergency management personnel evacuated about 300 people due to the ensuing flooding, which caused about $250,000 in property damage (2003 USD; $293,000 2008 USD). In all, about 350 were evacuated in Augusta County, 21 of whom by boat. Damages to public roads and equipment in the area were estimated at $1 million (2003 USD; $1.2 million 2008 USD). 13 homes were destroyed, with three businesses and 64 other residencies reporting major damage—some reported over 1 foot (0.30 m) of mud in their homes. Damage in the Shenandoah Valley totaled about $29 million (2003 USD; $34 million 2008 USD). A man canoeing in high flood waters drowned in <a href="/wiki/Harrisonburg,_Virginia" title="Harrisonburg, Virginia">Harrisonburg</a>, while two others drowned in a <a href="/wiki/Horse_and_buggy" title="Horse and buggy">horse and buggy</a> after crossing a low water bridge in <a href="/wiki/Rockingham_County,_Virginia" title="Rockingham County, Virginia">Rockingham County</a>. The flooding also killed 25–30 head of livestock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-12"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shncdc_29-3"><a href="#cite_note-shncdc-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shncdc3_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-shncdc3-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_Hurricane_Isabel_in_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dec_1491.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1275" data-file-width="1650" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Dec_1491.jpg/220px-Dec_1491.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Dec_1491.jpg/330px-Dec_1491.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Dec_1491.jpg/440px-Dec_1491.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dec_1491.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Counties and cities in Virginia eligible for disaster assistance</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On the day of Isabel moving through the state, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> declared 18 counties and 14 independent cities as disaster areas, making residents and business-owners there eligible for federal funding. Additionally, funds were allocated for state and local governments in the 31 designated jurisdictions to pay 75% of the eligible cost for debris removal and emergency services related to the hurricane, including requested emergency work undertaken by the federal government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema918_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-fema918-32">[32]</a></sup> Many other jurisdictions were added, based on subsequent damage reports, and by September 22, 99 jurisdictions were eligible for disaster assistance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-morejur_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-morejur-33">[33]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">Federal Emergency Management Agency</a> (FEMA) provided hurricane-related bulletins, including safety tips<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-safety_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-safety-34">[34]</a></sup> and the method for removing hazardous materials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hazard_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-hazard-35">[35]</a></sup> By 12 days after the passage of the hurricane, FEMA distributed more than 6.3 million lbs. (2.9 million kg) of ice and 1.4 million US gallons (5,300 m<sup>3</sup>) of water to areas affected by Isabel. Disaster Recovery Centers, which contain information on the aftermath process, were opened in eight locations and received more than 1,850 inquiries. More than 350 FEMA inspectors visited homes to verify damages caused by Isabel, and by the end of September 2003 about 12,000 inspections were completed. In response to the power outages, FEMA installed 28 generators at disaster-affected critical public facilities to support life-sustaining community needs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema930_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-fema930-36">[36]</a></sup> By about four months after the hurricane, 93,139 individuals in the designated areas applied for disaster assistance, while 20,417 people visited Disaster Recovery Centers throughout the state. The <a href="/wiki/Small_Business_Administration" title="Small Business Administration">Small Business Administration</a> approved more than 3,000 low-interest disaster loans from homes and businesses, with the value of the loans totaling $74 million (2003 USD; $87 million 2008 USD). The government provided $105 million (2003 USD; $123 million 2008 USD) for debris removal, emergency protective services, and permanent work, and approved about $25.9 million (2003 USD; $30.3 million 2008 USD) for life-sustaining needs such as water, ice, and generators at critical public facilities. Monetary assistance for temporary rental assistance and minimal home repairs totaled $32 million (2003 USD; $37 million 2008 USD), as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-femfin_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-femfin-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Volunteer agencies arrived in the state to assist in the aftermath of the hurricane, and by about ten days after Isabel volunteers served more than 550,000 meals to affected residents;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema927_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-fema927-38">[38]</a></sup> over 933,000 meals were served during the four-month cleanup operation <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Over 10 volunteer organizations, under the coordination of Virginia's Voluntary Organizations Active in Disaster, worked to help individuals with debris removal across the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-femaclean_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-femaclean-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dominion Virginia Power quickly began to restore the widespread power outages with a workforce of about 11,000, working between 14 and 16 hours per day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-924iser_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-924iser-41">[41]</a></sup> By two days after the storm, about 900,000 remained without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-920iser_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-920iser-42">[42]</a></sup> By five days after the storm, about 584,000 throughout the state were still without power,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iser923_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-iser923-15">[15]</a></sup> and by ten days after Isabel the total dropped to about 160,000, most of whom were in the Hampton Roads area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-928iser_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-928iser-43">[43]</a></sup> Improper use of generators caused three indirect storm deaths due to <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide" title="Carbon monoxide">carbon monoxide</a> poisoning resulting from improper ventilation of homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_7-13"><a href="#cite_note-nws-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a result of polluted runoff from storm surge and heavy rains, the Virginia Department of Health forbade gathering <a href="/wiki/Shellfish" title="Shellfish">shellfish</a> in the Virginia portion of the Chesapeake Bay, as well as all rivers flowing into the bay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-news_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-news-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
