<p><b>Hurricane Paul</b> was a particularly deadly and destructive <a href="/wiki/Pacific_hurricane" title="Pacific hurricane">Pacific hurricane</a> which killed a total of 1,696 people and caused $1.156 billion in damage. The sixteenth <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a> and tenth hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/1982_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="1982 Pacific hurricane season">1982 Pacific hurricane season</a>, Paul developed as a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical depression</a> just offshore Central America on September 18. The depression briefly moved inland two days later just before heading westward out to sea. The storm changed little in strength for several days until September 25, when it slowly intensified into a tropical storm. Two days later, Paul attained hurricane status, and further strengthened to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_2" title="Saffir–Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 2 intensity</a> after turning northward. The hurricane then accelerated toward the northeast, reaching peak winds of 110 mph (175 km/h). Paul made landfall over <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Sur" title="Baja California Sur">Baja California Sur</a> on September 29, and subsequently moved ashore in <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa" title="Sinaloa">Sinaloa</a>, Mexico the next day.</p>
<p>Prior to making landfall near the <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>–<a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> border as a tropical depression, the precursor disturbance dropped heavy rainfall over <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>, which later spread into El Salvador and Guatemala. Many rivers in the region burst their banks after five days of rainfall, causing severe flooding and multiple mudslides. Throughout Central America, at least 1,432 people were killed, with most of the fatalities occurring in El Salvador and Guatemala. Another 225 deaths were attributed to floods from the depression in southern Mexico. In addition, Paul was responsible for moderate damage and 24 fatalities in northwestern Mexico, where it made landfall at hurricane strength.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paul_(1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Paul_1982_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Paul_1982_track.png/220px-Paul_1982_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Paul_1982_track.png/330px-Paul_1982_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Paul_1982_track.png/440px-Paul_1982_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Paul_1982_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The precursor disturbance to Paul originated from an area of <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">low barometric pressure</a> and disorganized <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorms</a>, which was first noted near the Pacific coast of Nicaragua on September 15. Several days later, <a href="/wiki/Weather_satellite" title="Weather satellite">satellite imagery</a> indicated it had developed a center of <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">cyclonic circulation</a>; on 1800 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a> September 20, the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Pacific_Hurricane_Center" title="Eastern Pacific Hurricane Center">Eastern Pacific Hurricane Center</a> initiated advisories on the system and classified it as Tropical Depression Twenty-Two. At that time, it was located 200 mi (320 km) west of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Managua,_Nicaragua" title="Managua, Nicaragua">Managua, Nicaragua</a> and supported winds of 35 mph (50 km/h). The depression turned northward in response to a weak <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Steering_winds" title="Tropical cyclone">steering flow</a> between two <a href="/wiki/High_pressure" title="High pressure">high pressure</a> systems—one near <a href="/wiki/Cabo_San_Lucas" title="Cabo San Lucas">Cabo San Lucas</a> and the other west of Central America. It then moved inland near the El Salvador–Guatemala border, and dissipated overland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Under the influence of a persistent stationary <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> near <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, the remains of the depression retraced westward back over the open waters of the Pacific. Advisories on the system were resumed late on September 20. Though it was reconsidered a tropical cyclone, its wind circulation was poorly defined; the depression again degenerated into an open trough at 0000 UTC September 22. Its forward motion remained relatively unchanged for several days, and by September 24 the system was reclassified as a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a>. After briefly drifting northward, the system began tracking toward the west-northwest. It gradually organized into a tropical storm at 0000 UTC September 25. Since it was then situated over favorable <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperatures</a> between 28 °C (82 °F) and 29 °C (84 °F), Paul underwent a phase of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapid intensification</a>. This allowed it to reach <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_1" title="Saffir–Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 1 hurricane</a> strength on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> (SSHWS) just two days after its naming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Upon becoming a hurricane, Paul turned to the north and continued to develop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup> As the storm neared <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Sur" title="Baja California Sur">Baja California Sur</a>, it reached Category 2 intensity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPAC_HURDAT_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-EPAC_HURDAT-2">[2]</a></sup> An <a href="/wiki/Tropical_upper_tropospheric_trough" title="Tropical upper tropospheric trough">upper-level trough</a> forced the hurricane to accelerate towards the northeast, at which point it had reached peak wind speeds of 110 mph (180 km/h). From 1800 to 2100 UTC on September 29, the <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> of the hurricane made landfall along Baja California Sur, moving ashore less than 100 mi (160 km) south of <a href="/wiki/La_Paz,_Baja_California_Sur" title="La Paz, Baja California Sur">La Paz</a> near San José del Cabo. After weakening slightly inland, Paul briefly reemerged over water and subsequently made its final landfall near <a href="/wiki/Los_Mochis" title="Los Mochis">Los Mochis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa" title="Sinaloa">Sinaloa</a> with winds of 100 mph (165 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup> Tropical cyclone advisories were discontinued shortly thereafter,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPAC_HURDAT_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-EPAC_HURDAT-2">[2]</a></sup> though exact information on the storm after it moved inland is unavailable due to a lack of data completion in the <a href="/wiki/HURDAT" title="HURDAT">hurricane database</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPAC_Book_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-EPAC_Book-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paul_(1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>An <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">alert</a> was issued for the Mexican states of <a href="/wiki/Sonora" title="Sonora">Sonora</a> and Sinaloa and <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Sur" title="Baja California Sur">Baja California Sur</a>; army and navy units were on standby in case of an emergency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPLM_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-HPLM-4">[4]</a></sup> Roughly 50,000 people evacuated to storm shelters<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50.2C000_EAHHM_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-50.2C000_EAHHM-5">[5]</a></sup> and thousands of others sought refuge in public buildings, such as schools and churches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HHMX5D_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-HHMX5D-6">[6]</a></sup> Across <a href="/wiki/La_Paz,_Baja_California_Sur" title="La Paz, Baja California Sur">La Paz</a>, officials evacuated 3,000 families from hurricane-prone areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HHSCOM_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-HHSCOM-7">[7]</a></sup> In the towns of Altata and Guamúchil alone, army officials evacuated 5,000 coastal residents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HC5IMXS_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-HC5IMXS-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>The tropical depression that later became Paul produced the worst <a href="/wiki/Natural_disaster" title="Natural disaster">natural disaster</a> in <a href="/wiki/History_of_El_Salvador" title="History of El Salvador">El Salvador history</a> since 1965.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup> Although the death toll was initially believed to be lower<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESDTH565AMNF_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-ESDTH565AMNF-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5DT_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-5DT-32">[32]</a></sup> it rose to a final toll of 761 after new victims were confirmed on September 28.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Of these deaths, 312 occurred in the capital city of <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador" title="San Salvador">San Salvador</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESDTH565AMNF_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-ESDTH565AMNF-31">[31]</a></sup> which had also sustained the worst damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-TC-34">[34]</a></sup> Another 40 people perished in Actoo, a very small village located 42 mi (68 km) west of San Salvador.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESDTH565AMNF_31-2"><a href="#cite_note-ESDTH565AMNF-31">[31]</a></sup> Rescue workers searched through <a href="/wiki/Rock_(geology)" title="Rock (geology)">rocks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mud" title="Mud">mud</a> to find missing victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DTETH500ISFD_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-DTETH500ISFD-35">[35]</a></sup> About 25,000–30,000 people were left homeless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup> Much of San Salvador was submerged by flood waters of up to 8 ft (2.4 m) high, and even after their recession hundreds of homes remained buried under trees, debris, and 10 ft (3.0 m) of mud.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESDTH565AMNF_31-3"><a href="#cite_note-ESDTH565AMNF-31">[31]</a></sup> In all, property damage from the storm amounted to $100 million (1982 US$) in the country;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESDTH565AMNF_31-4"><a href="#cite_note-ESDTH565AMNF-31">[31]</a></sup> economic losses were estimated at $280 million (1982 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The extensive floods swept through significant amounts of crops, causing destruction and damage worth $250 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5DT_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-5DT-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<p>In Guatemala, widespread catastrophic floods claimed 615 lives and left 668 others missing. More than 10,000 people were left homeless in the wake of the disaster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> According to the highway department, the storm destroyed 16 bridges which left 200 communities isolated from surrounding areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Overall, economic losses of $100 million (1982 USD) were reported in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PW_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-PW-39">[39]</a></sup> In Nicaragua, similar extensive flooding killed 71 people and caused $356 million (1982 USD) in economic losses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Throughout southern Mexico, floods from the precursor depression to Paul killed another 225 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Baja_California_Sur">Baja California Sur</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paul_(1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Baja California Sur">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hurricane Paul produced heavy rainfall along its path through Baja California Sur. At least 85 homes in <a href="/wiki/La_Paz" title="La Paz">La Paz</a> sustained damage, and many telephone lines in the region were down at the height of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HHSCOM_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-HHSCOM-7">[7]</a></sup> Wind gusts estimated at 120 mph (195 km/h) swept through <a href="/wiki/San_Jos%C3%A9_del_Cabo" title="San José del Cabo">San José del Cabo</a>, causing property damage and subsequently leaving 9,000 homeless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup> Despite the damage, no deaths were reported in the wake of Paul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HHBC_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-HHBC-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Northwest_Mexico_and_southwest_United_States">Northwest Mexico and southwest United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paul_(1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Northwest Mexico and southwest United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Upon making its final landfall in <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa" title="Sinaloa">Sinaloa</a>, Paul produced hurricane-force winds recorded at 100 mph (160 km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Los_Mochis" title="Los Mochis">Los Mochis</a>. The winds demolished numerous homes in the region, leaving 140,000 residents homeless and another 400,000 people isolated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MS_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-MS-43">[43]</a></sup> The greatest damage occurred 70 miles (110 km) south of Los Mochis in the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guamuchil" title="Guamuchil">Guamuchil</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup> some houses suffered total destruction, while many other had their roof blown off. A total of 24 people were killed by the storm, although it produced beneficial rains over the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24KFH_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-24KFH-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The worst flooding occurred near the Rio Sinaloa; 7.9 in (200 mm) to 12 in (300 mm) of rain fell in some locations. Over 25,000 homes were damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Agricultural damage was severe in the state of Sinaloa, with up to 40 percent of the <a href="/wiki/Soybean" title="Soybean">soybean</a> crop destroyed. Sugar cane, tomato, and rice crops also sustained damage from the hurricane, and in its wake the state's corn production was down by 26 percent from the previous year. Total storm damage in Mexico amounted to $4.5 billion (1982 <a href="/wiki/Mexican_peso" title="Mexican peso">MXN</a>; $70 million <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USD" title="USD">USD</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The remnants of Paul moved into the United States, producing heavy rainfall in southern <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a> and extreme West <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>. Inclement weather was observed as far inland as the <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">Great Plains</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SHT_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-SHT-45">[45]</a></sup> A combination of rain and snow moved into <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>; 6 in (150 mm) of snow was expected in <a href="/wiki/Wyoming" title="Wyoming">Wyoming</a>, thus <a href="/wiki/Winter_storm_warning" title="Winter storm warning">winter storm warnings</a> were required for parts of the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CWFBHP_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-CWFBHP-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Paul_(1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the aftermath of the storm, the Government of El Salvador was criticized for failing to keep the public well informed. It provided over $300,000 in aid and declared a <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESDTH565AMNF_31-5"><a href="#cite_note-ESDTH565AMNF-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DTETH500ISFD_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-DTETH500ISFD-35">[35]</a></sup> additionally, a state of mourning was declared.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESIM_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-ESIM-47">[47]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations World Food program</a> began distributing food set aside for victims of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Salvador_Civil_War" title="El Salvador Civil War">El Salvador Civil War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DTFFIESR232_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-DTFFIESR232-48">[48]</a></sup> The U.S. Embassy in Guatemala provided $25,000 in aid for the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LRUSDA_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-LRUSDA-49">[49]</a></sup> Mexican authorities rushed to supply food and water to the homeless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
