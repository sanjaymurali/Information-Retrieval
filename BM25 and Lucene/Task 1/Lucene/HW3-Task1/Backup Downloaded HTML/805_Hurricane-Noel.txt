<p><b>Hurricane Noel</b> was the fourteenth named storm and sixth hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2007 Atlantic hurricane season">2007 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. Noel formed on October 27 from the interaction between a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> and an upper-level low in the north-central Caribbean. It strengthened to winds of 60 mph (97 km/h) before making <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> on western <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> and the north coast of eastern Cuba. It turned northward, and on November 1 it attained hurricane status. The hurricane accelerated northeastward after crossing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>, and on November 2 it became an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a>. (The <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hurricane_Centre" title="Canadian Hurricane Centre">Canadian Hurricane Centre</a> classified Noel as a post-tropical storm until 2200 UTC November 4 when it determined that it had lost all tropical characteristics.)</p>
<p>The storm caused at least 163 direct deaths along its path, primarily in <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a>, due to flooding and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mudslide" title="Mudslide">mudslides</a>. It was the deadliest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_hurricane" title="North Atlantic hurricane">North Atlantic hurricane</a> of the 2007 season. After its transition to post-tropical, Noel became a very strong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low</a> pressure system causing flooding and wind damage over <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a> and Eastern Canada. It even dropped snow over some areas of Eastern <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> and <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Noel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Noel_2007_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" data-file-width="1024" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Noel_2007_track.png/220px-Noel_2007_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Noel_2007_track.png/330px-Noel_2007_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Noel_2007_track.png/440px-Noel_2007_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Noel_2007_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> exited the coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> on October 16. It tracked westward, initially without organization, and by October 22 was interacting with a <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> while approaching the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a>. This caused a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low pressure area</a> to develop on October 23 about 175 mi (282 km) east-northeast of the northern Lesser Antilles. The system continued westward with disorganized <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">convection</a>, and was unable to develop further due to strong <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoelTCR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NoelTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Despite the shear, convection increased as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">barometric pressures</a> dropped in association with the organizing system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Turning west-southwestward, the low passed over the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">United States Virgin Islands</a> on October 25 and just southeast of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> the next day. Decreasing wind shear on October 27 allowed the thunderstorms to increase over the center late on October 27. After further organization, the system developed into Tropical Depression Sixteen at 0000 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on October 28, about 215 mi (346 km) south-southeast of <a href="/wiki/Port-au-Prince" title="Port-au-Prince">Port-au-Prince</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoelTCR_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NoelTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Initially, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) forecast the depression to track west-northwestward, brushing southwestern Haiti before moving ashore along south-central <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Upon becoming a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a>, the wave was moving to the west-northwest under the steering currents of a high pressure area over the western Atlantic Ocean.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-disc1-3">[3]</a></sup> Six hours after formation, the center became difficult to locate,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc2-4">[4]</a></sup> though subsequently the depression became much better organized; a large area of convection developed over the center, with several <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Physical_structure" title="Tropical cyclone">rainbands</a> to its south.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc3_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc3-5">[5]</a></sup> Based on reports from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a>, the National Hurricane Center upgraded the depression to Tropical Storm Noel at 1815 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on October 28 while located about 150 miles (240 km) south-southeast of Port-au-Prince, Haiti.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc4_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc4-6">[6]</a></sup> After the center re-developed beneath the thunderstorms, the storm quickly strengthened to winds of 60 mph (97 km/h),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc5_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc5-7">[7]</a></sup> and for several hours the cyclone was drifting to the south of <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a>. Noel began to lose organization as a nearby upper-level low increased wind shear,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc6_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc6-8">[8]</a></sup> with the circulation becoming ill-defined as it further interacted with the mountainous terrain of Haiti.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc7_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc7-9">[9]</a></sup> The storm crossed western Haiti as a disorganized tropical storm on October 29,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc8_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc8-10">[10]</a></sup> and subsequently began moving parallel to the northeastern coast of <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> while moving around the southwestern periphery of a weakening ridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc10_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc10-11">[11]</a></sup> On October 30, Tropical Storm Noel made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> near <a href="/wiki/Gibara" title="Gibara">Gibara</a>, Cuba, with winds of about 60 mph (97 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc11_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc11-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa11_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa11-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The center of Noel remained well-organized as it tracked through eastern Cuba, though its sustained winds quickly diminished to minimal tropical storm force.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc13_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc13-14">[14]</a></sup> Early on October 31 it began drifting to the north-northwest, in response to the approach of a <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">short-wave trough</a> from the west and the eastward shift of the mid-level ridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc14_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc14-15">[15]</a></sup> A large area of strong convective rainbands persisted to its east, though due to increased upper-level wind shear there was little convection near the center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc15_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc15-16">[16]</a></sup> Shortly after 1200 UTC on October 31, Noel emerged off the north coast of Cuba,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa15a_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa15a-17">[17]</a></sup> and subsequently the cyclone became better organized as the circulation became better associated with the convection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc16_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc16-18">[18]</a></sup> While located just off the north coast of Cuba, the motion of Noel was nearly stationary, though forecasters indicated it could have executed a small cyclonic loop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc17_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc17-19">[19]</a></sup> On October 31, the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hurricane_Centre" title="Canadian Hurricane Centre">Canadian Hurricane Centre</a> began tracking the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chc1_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-chc1-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

<p>Convection increased early on November 1, though initially the center remained to the southwest of the large area of thunderstorms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc18_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc18-21">[21]</a></sup> At 1800 UTC that day, the storm passed over the Bahamian island of <a href="/wiki/New_Providence" title="New Providence">New Providence</a> near the city of <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">Nassau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa20a_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa20a-22">[22]</a></sup> Later that day the center tracked beneath the convection,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc20_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc20-23">[23]</a></sup> and Noel accelerated northeastward in the southwesterly flow ahead of an approaching <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a>; as it did so, the center became elongated and distorted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc21_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc21-24">[24]</a></sup> The cyclone maintained a large, round area of deep convection with well-defined <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a>, and an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye feature</a> became evident on satellite imagery. Based on reports from Hurricane Hunters, the National Hurricane Center upgraded Noel to hurricane status early on November 2 about 180 miles (290 km) north-northeast of <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">Nassau, Bahamas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc22_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc22-25">[25]</a></sup> Its convection began to wane as it moved through progressively cooler waters, and as it lost tropical characteristics its wind field expanded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc23_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc23-26">[26]</a></sup> By late on November 2, the inner core had substantially diminished, and the thermodynamical structure of the cyclone had become asymmetrical and <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">frontal</a>; as it began transitioning into an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a>, the National Hurricane Center issued the final advisory on Hurricane Noel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc25_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc25-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Early on November 3, the extratropical cyclone attained winds of 85 mph (137 km/h), and it gradually turned to the north-northeast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chc2_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-chc2-28">[28]</a></sup> As the center of Noel approached <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, its large circulation produced strong winds across all of <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chc3_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-chc3-29">[29]</a></sup> and on November 4 it tracked across Nova Scotia and New Brunswick.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chc4_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-chc4-30">[30]</a></sup> At 6 PM AST (2200 UTC) November 4, as the storm was over <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a>, the Canadian Hurricane Center declared that Noel has lost all tropical characteristics and changed its designation from post-tropical to extratropical. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chc5_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-chc5-31">[31]</a></sup> The extratropical low continued to the northeast and made landfall over southwest <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> on the afternoon of November 5, bringing tropical storm force winds to the area. The center dissipated over Greenland later that night. While its lowest barometric pressure as a tropical system was 980 mb, on November 4, while located near Labrador, it registered a minimum pressure of 966 mb.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Noel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Caribbean_and_Bahamas">Caribbean and Bahamas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Noel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Caribbean and Bahamas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Noel_28_oct_2007_1530Z.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="7800" data-file-width="6000" height="286" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Noel_28_oct_2007_1530Z.jpg/220px-Noel_28_oct_2007_1530Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Noel_28_oct_2007_1530Z.jpg/330px-Noel_28_oct_2007_1530Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Noel_28_oct_2007_1530Z.jpg/440px-Noel_28_oct_2007_1530Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Noel_28_oct_2007_1530Z.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Tropical Storm Noel nearing <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a> on October 28</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan</a> <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> issued widespread flood and flash flood warnings and watches across <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. Due to unsettled conditions, officials advised residents to stay out of harms way and off the roadways.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prnws1_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-prnws1-33">[33]</a></sup> A flash flood warning was issued for the entirety of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">United States Virgin Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carib_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-carib-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Upon being classified as a tropical cyclone, the government of <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical storm warning</a> for the southwestern peninsula from its border with the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> westward to <a href="/wiki/Port-au-Prince" title="Port-au-Prince">Port-au-Prince</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa1_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa1-35">[35]</a></sup> By the time Noel made landfall on Haiti, a tropical storm warning was either recommended or put in place for the southern coast of <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">Hispaniola</a> from <a href="/wiki/Barahona_Province" title="Barahona Province">Barahona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, to Port-au-Prince, Haiti. In Haiti, Health care centers were advised to activate any emergency plans, and medicine stocks were pre-positioned if needed. Residents in flood-prone areas were evacuated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Haiti" title="Departments of Haiti">departments</a> of Sud-Est, Sud, <a href="/wiki/Grand%27Anse_(department)" title="Grand'Anse (department)">Grand'Anse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nippes" title="Nippes">Nippes</a>, Ouest, Centre and Artibonie, a red alert was issued, while an orange alert was issued for <a href="/wiki/Nord-Est_(department)" title="Nord-Est (department)">Nord-Est</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nord_(Haitian_department)" title="Nord (Haitian department)">Nord</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nord-Ouest_(department)" title="Nord-Ouest (department)">Nord-Ouest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> In the Dominican Republic, 14,500 people sought protection in shelters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> About 1,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Incarceration" title="Incarceration">inmates</a> were evacuated from a prison north of the country's capital city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The National Emergency Commission authorized the opening of a number of shelters, and the Dominican Red Cross activated search and rescue teams. The Coordination Centre set up a "crisis room", assigning workers to 24-hour shifts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane watch</a> was issued for southeastern Cuba; a tropical storm warning was in effect for the southeastern <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">Bahamas</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands" title="Turks and Caicos Islands">Turks and Caicos Islands</a>, and a tropical storm watch was put in place for the central Bahamas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa7a_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa7a-41">[41]</a></sup> A tropical storm warning was also issued for the northwestern Bahamas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa11_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-pa11-13">[13]</a></sup> There, most government offices were closed during the passage of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap1031_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap1031-42">[42]</a></sup> On the island of <a href="/wiki/Andros" title="Andros">Andros</a>, residents took extra care in securing their boats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> As the storm tracked further to the northwest, a tropical storm warning was put in place for the Cuban provinces of <a href="/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo_Province" title="Guantánamo Province">Guantánamo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holgu%C3%ADn_Province" title="Holguín Province">Holguín</a>, <a href="/wiki/Las_Tunas_Province" title="Las Tunas Province">Las Tunas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ciego_de_%C3%81vila_Province" title="Ciego de Ávila Province">Ciego de Ávila</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Camag%C3%BCey_Province" title="Camagüey Province">Camagüey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa11_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-pa11-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbc112_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbc112-44">[44]</a></sup> The Cuban Institute of Meteorology advised caution to residents, due to previous heavy rainfall leaving grounds saturated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1031_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp1031-45">[45]</a></sup> Several schools were closed during the storm's onslaught.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap31_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap31-46">[46]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Camag%C3%BCey" title="Camagüey">Camagüey</a>, thousands of <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a> were moved to higher ground, and roughly 3,000 students working to harvest coffee crops were forced to return home. A United States Naval base in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guantanamo_Bay" title="Guantanamo Bay">Guantanamo Bay</a> stacked sandbags to protect structures in areas vulnerable to flooding, namely those in low-lying terrain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_and_Canada">United States and Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Noel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United States and Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Late on October 31, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> issued a tropical storm watch for southeastern <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, as tropical storm force winds were expected to come close to the coastline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc17_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-disc17-19">[19]</a></sup> Coastal flood warnings, high surf advisories, and high wind warnings were posted for the coastline of Florida, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, and <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a> as Noel churned offshore in the Atlantic. Similar advisories were placed into effect from <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">Virginia Beach, Virginia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ocean_City,_Maryland" title="Ocean City, Maryland">Ocean City, Maryland</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Coastal_flood_watch" title="Coastal flood watch">coastal flood watch</a> was issued for parts of <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-nasa-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mianws_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-mianws-49">[49]</a></sup> As the extratropical storm progressed north, wind advisories were posted along the coast of <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-nasa-48">[48]</a></sup> and a <a href="/wiki/Winter_storm_watch" title="Winter storm watch">winter storm watch</a> was issued for northern Maine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Cod_and_Islands" title="Cape Cod and Islands">Cape Cod and Islands</a> division of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> opened a number of shelters on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nantucket" title="Nantucket">Nantucket</a> and along coastal <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>. Stores on Cape Cod were closed; some of them boarded up windows,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Coast_Guard" title="U.S. Coast Guard">U.S. Coast Guard</a> completed preparations and warned sailors of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>Prior to the arrival of Noel into <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a>, heavy rain and strong wind warnings were put in place for many locations in southeastern Canada. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Hurricane_Center" title="Canadian Hurricane Center">Canadian Hurricane Center</a> issued hurricane-force wind warnings for waters to the southwest of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maritimes" title="Maritimes">Maritimes</a> and gale warnings for waters elsewhere off of southeastern Canada. The Halifax and Montreal Storm Prediction Centers of the <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_Service_of_Canada" title="Meteorological Service of Canada">Meteorological Service of Canada</a> issued high winds and heavy rain warning for all the areas of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maritimes" title="Maritimes">Maritimes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a> and Eastern <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, as well as heavy snow warnings for certain regions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chc2_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-chc2-28">[28]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Air_Canada" title="Air Canada">Air Canada</a> canceled 13 of their flights in and out of <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Stanfield_International_Airport" title="Halifax Stanfield International Airport">Halifax</a>. Throughout <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, 500 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> volunteers were put on standby.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>

<p>Heavy rainfall fell across the northern <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">Lesser Antilles</a> for several days in association with the precursor disturbance, unofficially reaching over 6 inches (150 mm) on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Croix" title="Saint Croix">Saint Croix</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carib_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-carib-34">[34]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands">Saint Thomas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-urst_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-urst-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>The precursor disturbance also dropped heavy rainfall across <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> for several days, leaving grounds saturated and causing <a href="/wiki/Surface_runoff" title="Surface runoff">surface runoff</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prnws1_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-prnws1-33">[33]</a></sup> Precipitation peaked at 17.23 inches (438 mm) at <a href="/wiki/Carite_Lake" title="Carite Lake">Carite Lake</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-rain-56">[56]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">Flash flooding</a> was reported in <a href="/wiki/Carolina,_Puerto_Rico" title="Carolina, Puerto Rico">Carolina</a>, and a mudslide occurred in <a href="/wiki/Utuado,_Puerto_Rico" title="Utuado, Puerto Rico">Utuado</a>. Flooding occurred along several rivers, including the <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_la_Plata_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Rio de la Plata (Puerto Rico)">Río de la Plata</a>, with a reading of 8.9 feet (2.7 m) above flood stage at <a href="/wiki/Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico" title="Toa Baja, Puerto Rico">Toa Baja</a> reported late on October 27.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prnws1_33-2"><a href="#cite_note-prnws1-33">[33]</a></sup> In response to high water levels, officials opened dam gates along the Río de la Plata and the Río Carraízo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> There were no official reports of fatalities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoelTCR_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-NoelTCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dominican_Republic">Dominican Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Noel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Dominican Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, the highest rainfall amount reported was 21.65 inches (550 mm) at Padre Las Casas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPCMAX_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-WPCMAX-58">[58]</a></sup> People in the path of the flooding escaped to roof and tree tops;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1031_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp1031-45">[45]</a></sup> more than 50,000 people left their homes. Additionally, about 1,000 prisoners were evacuated due to flooding. For about two hours, the entire island was without power. Two days later, one-third of the population was still without electricity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1031_45-2"><a href="#cite_note-afp1031-45">[45]</a></sup> The heavy rainfall caused extreme flooding throughout the island, which caused mudslides, as well. It is reported that buildings were swept down the sides of mountains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap1031_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap1031-42">[42]</a></sup> The flooding damaged over 24,500 homes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> of which 6,000 were completely destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoelTCR_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-NoelTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Many rivers topped their banks, causing flash flooding in valleys, which killed 10 people in the town of <a href="/wiki/Piedra_Blanca" title="Piedra Blanca">Piedra Blanca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> Many roads and bridges were destroyed, isolating some villages from outside civilization;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> at least 21 bridges in the country were washed away or collapsed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloomberg_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-62">[62]</a></sup> By just days after the storm, 1,522 people were rescued from flood waters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-homeless_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-homeless-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<p>Severe crop losses were reported. The primary agricultural damages were to plantain, banana, tomato and <a href="/wiki/Red_onion" title="Red onion">red onion</a> crops. In some locations, production of such fruits was completely lost.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Crop damage in the country totaled $77.7 million (2007 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">Livestock</a> losses were also reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> There was severe damage to 40% of water distribution, and 60% of 122 <a href="/wiki/Aqueduct_(water_supply)" title="Aqueduct (water supply)">aqueducts</a>. Due to health concerns and the onslaught of disease, some medical centers reached maximum capacity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> In all, the storm caused at least 87 fatalities in the country, and 42 others were reported missing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoelTCR_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-NoelTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Over 65,000 people were left homeless,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-homeless_63-1"><a href="#cite_note-homeless-63">[63]</a></sup> and over 46 bridges were in some way affected by the floods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> However, because many villages were isolated, these totals may not reflect the actual damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-homeless_63-2"><a href="#cite_note-homeless-63">[63]</a></sup></p>

<p>Noel is the second wettest tropical cyclone in Haiti on record, dropping 654.8 mm (25.78 in) at Camp Perrin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoelTCR_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-NoelTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Rainfall across the rest of the country was generally in the 25 to 225 mm (0.98 to 8.86 in) range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> Five days of rainfall caused severe flooding and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mudslide" title="Mudslide">mudslides</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1031reut_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-1031reut-76">[76]</a></sup> affecting about 3,252 families.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Special_report_77-0"><a href="#cite_note-Special_report-77">[77]</a></sup> Rivers and creeks swelled up, forcing communities to evacuate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> In the vulnerable country to flooding, about 500 houses were damaged by the floods' onslaught,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Special_report_77-1"><a href="#cite_note-Special_report-77">[77]</a></sup> of which 400 were completely destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> The cities that were the worst affected by the flooding were Cayes, Cantaloupe, Camp Perrin, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chantal,_Haiti" title="Chantal, Haiti">Chantal</a>, Maniche, Holy louis of the South and Torbeck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-French_report_80-0"><a href="#cite_note-French_report-80">[80]</a></sup> The main road to the country's capital, <a href="/wiki/Port-au-Prince" title="Port-au-Prince">Port-au-Prince</a>, was blocked,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> in addition to other roads throughout the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> The Haitian Civil Protection Office initially attributed 18 deaths to Noel in the country; two additional deaths were reported, one due to flooding and another due to being crushed by a tree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1031reut_76-1"><a href="#cite_note-1031reut-76">[76]</a></sup> In total, 73 deaths were reported in the country, while 17 others remained missing as of December 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoelTCR_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-NoelTCR-1">[1]</a></sup> In addition, 7,500 in Haiti were displaced by the storm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> and 104 were injued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-French_report_80-1"><a href="#cite_note-French_report-80">[80]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="Jamaica,_Cuba,_and_the_Bahamas"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Jamaica.2C_Cuba.2C_and_the_Bahamas">Jamaica, Cuba, and the Bahamas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Noel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Jamaica, Cuba, and the Bahamas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Noel_01_nov_2007_1815Z.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="7200" data-file-width="5600" height="283" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Noel_01_nov_2007_1815Z.jpg/220px-Noel_01_nov_2007_1815Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Noel_01_nov_2007_1815Z.jpg/330px-Noel_01_nov_2007_1815Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Noel_01_nov_2007_1815Z.jpg/440px-Noel_01_nov_2007_1815Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Noel_01_nov_2007_1815Z.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Tropical Storm Noel over <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">the Bahamas</a> on November 1</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Heavy rainfall fell across portions of <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>; the rains triggered a mudslide that caused a house to collapse, killing one person.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1031reut_76-2"><a href="#cite_note-1031reut-76">[76]</a></sup> The eastern end of the island was hit particularly hard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jamaica_rain_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jamaica_rain-84">[84]</a></sup> Several communities were isolated, and roads and bridges were impassable in some areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> The rainfall caused at least 20 mudslides across the island;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloomberg_62-1"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-62">[62]</a></sup> one of them caused a large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pile-up" title="Pile-up">pile-up</a> that persisted for hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jamaica_rain_84-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jamaica_rain-84">[84]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, the storm dropped about 6 inches (150 mm) of rainfall in six hours in the city of <a href="/wiki/Baracoa" title="Baracoa">Baracoa</a>, which overflowed reservoirs in the region. The highest 24 hour total noted was at Mangos de Baraguá, where 364 mm (14.3 in) fell between the mornings of October 29 and October 30.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> Strong winds and rough waves were reported along the northern coastline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1031reut_76-3"><a href="#cite_note-1031reut-76">[76]</a></sup> The storm caused severe flooding and mudslides, which damaged over 21,000 homes in the province of Granma alone,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap31_46-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap31-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> and destroyed about 120 homes in <a href="/wiki/Camag%C3%BCey_Province" title="Camagüey Province">Camagüey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp111_88-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp111-88">[88]</a></sup> Flooding isolated a few areas,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap1031_42-2"><a href="#cite_note-ap1031-42">[42]</a></sup> and destroyed more than 3.9 million lb (1.8 million kg) of crops in <a href="/wiki/Ciego_de_%C3%81vila_Province" title="Ciego de Ávila Province">Ciego de Ávila Province</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbc112_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-cbc112-44">[44]</a></sup> The rainfall contributed to precipitation totals being 300% of the normal rainfall in <a href="/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo_Province" title="Guantánamo Province">Guantánamo Province</a>, which caused a dam in the province to overflow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloomberg_62-2"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-62">[62]</a></sup> In the province of <a href="/wiki/Santiago_de_Cuba_Province" title="Santiago de Cuba Province">Santiago</a>, about 400 km (250 mi) of roads were damaged. At least 98,000 people throughout the country evacuated from the flooding; of which nearly 20,000 did so to escape the <a href="/wiki/Cauto_River" title="Cauto River">Cauto River</a> which topped its banks, and 40,000 were in the province of <a href="/wiki/Granma_Province" title="Granma Province">Granma</a>. Many stayed in shelters. Of Cuba's 239 rivers, at least 137 overflowed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> One fatality was reported in Cuba; total damage is estimated at $500 million (2007 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NoelTCR_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-NoelTCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tropical Storm Noel dropped heavy rainfall across portions of <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">the Bahamas</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1031reut_76-4"><a href="#cite_note-1031reut-76">[76]</a></sup> reaching a record total of 15 inches (380 mm) at one station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbc112_44-2"><a href="#cite_note-cbc112-44">[44]</a></sup> Sustained winds were around 40 mph (64 km/h) throughout the central and northwestern regions of the island chain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup> Extensive flooding was reported, especially on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abaco_Island" title="Abaco Island">Abaco Island</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup> forcing the evacuation of over 700 people. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long_Island_(Bahamas)" title="Long Island (Bahamas)">Long Island</a> was hit the worst, where flood waters reportedly reached 5 ft (1.5 m) deep.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup> Residents of the island deemed the damage "devastating", reporting that flood waters were the highest in 60 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup> In some locations, houses were under several feet of water, while roads throughout the Bahamas suffered damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup> The Deadman's Cay Airport was also flooded, and on the island of <a href="/wiki/Exuma" title="Exuma">Exuma</a>, six of the nine schools received extensive damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Assessment_95-0"><a href="#cite_note-Assessment-95">[95]</a></sup> About 16,000 people were affected by the floods, including 10,000 on Long Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nassau_International_Airport" title="Nassau International Airport">Nassau International Airport</a> was closed due to the storm, and most cruise ships failed to arrive on schedule.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup> One fatality occurred when a man on the island of Exuma abandoned a stalled truck, and was subsequently swept away by flood waters into a nearby pond.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-98"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup> When Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Hubert_Ingraham" title="Hubert Ingraham">Hubert Ingraham</a> visited hard-hit areas to assess the damage, he stated that it would be possible for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Works" title="Public Works">Public Works</a> to "be able to get some pumps in to pump some of the water out." However, he also noted that, "In some areas that will be very difficult because you have ponds on both sides of the road."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Assessment_95-1"><a href="#cite_note-Assessment-95">[95]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Noel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Noel_waves.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1071" data-file-width="1600" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Hurricane_Noel_waves.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Noel_waves.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Hurricane_Noel_waves.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Noel_waves.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Hurricane_Noel_waves.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Noel_waves.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Hurricane Noel made its closest approach to Florida on November 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-99"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup> The interaction of Noel and a <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> to its north produced strong, gusty winds and high waves along the east coast of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._State" title="U.S. State">state</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc14_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-disc14-15">[15]</a></sup> Throughout Palm Beach, Broward and Miami-Dade counties, 5,000 customers lost power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beach_erosion" title="Beach erosion">Beach erosion</a> was significant, amounting to about $4 million (2007 USD) in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Florida_NCDC_101-0"><a href="#cite_note-Florida_NCDC-101">[101]</a></sup> Near <a href="/wiki/West_Palm_Beach,_Florida" title="West Palm Beach, Florida">West Palm Beach</a>, a 20 ft (6.1 m) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sand_dune" title="Sand dune">sand dune</a> was eroded to its foundation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-102"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup> In other areas, large portions of beach were washed away, prompting concerns about properties and structures along the shore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-103"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup> Rainfall was light, with local rainfall amounts up to 2.14 inches (54 mm) as Noel's rain bands moved in from the Atlantic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Florida_Rain_104-0"><a href="#cite_note-Florida_Rain-104">[104]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>As an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a>, Noel interacted with high pressure building in from the northwest, forming a tight pressure gradient that produced moderate winds along the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">Outer Banks</a> of North Carolina, primarily on November 2. At <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Island,_North_Carolina" title="Cedar Island, North Carolina">Cedar Island</a>, sustained winds of 43 mph (69 km/h) were reported. Along the coast from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Lookout,_North_Carolina" title="Cape Lookout, North Carolina">Cape Lookout</a> northward, gusts peaked at around 60 mph (97 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-105"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup> The storm produced strong waves, flooding <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Highway_12" title="North Carolina Highway 12">North Carolina Highway 12</a> with up to 5 ft (1.5 m) of water and sand on November 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NC_NCDC_106-0"><a href="#cite_note-NC_NCDC-106">[106]</a></sup> About 6,000 people lost power after winds knocked down a power line. Eight oceanfront properties were damaged,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NC_impact_107-0"><a href="#cite_note-NC_impact-107">[107]</a></sup> and damage in North Carolina totaled $72,000 (2007 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NC_NCDC_106-1"><a href="#cite_note-NC_NCDC-106">[106]</a></sup> As Noel was tracking northward parallel to the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">U.S. East Coast</a>, high winds affected several states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-108"><a href="#cite_note-108">[108]</a></sup> Beach erosion was reported in New Jersey, in some cases eroding a 10 ft (3.0 m) vertical drop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-109"><a href="#cite_note-109">[109]</a></sup> On Long Island, strong winds knocked down trees and power lines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-110"><a href="#cite_note-110">[110]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 3, the western side of the storm hit eastern <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>, particularly coastal <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>, with hurricane-force wind gusts. Massachusetts reported the highest wind gust of 89 mph (143 km/h), with sustained winds topping out at 59 mph (95 km/h). Throughout <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">Cape Cod</a>, trees and tree limbs were downed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Massachusetts_Impact_111-0"><a href="#cite_note-Massachusetts_Impact-111">[111]</a></sup> Rainfall peaked at 5.03 in (128 mm) at Cutler Rainwise, Maine, while other reports ranged from 2 in (50 mm) to 4 in (100 mm) across the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Eng_112-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Eng-112">[112]</a></sup> In northern Maine, temperatures were cold enough to support 6 in (150 mm) of snowfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-snowfall_113-0"><a href="#cite_note-snowfall-113">[113]</a></sup> Strong surf caused flooding along the coast, with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nantucket" title="Nantucket">Nantucket</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martha%27s_Vineyard" title="Martha's Vineyard">Martha's Vineyard</a> suffering the most;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-114"><a href="#cite_note-114">[114]</a></sup> several beachfront houses were severely damaged. A few roads were closed, mainly due to flooding and downed trees and tree limbs. Old North Wharf in Nantucket was damaged beyond repair, while numerous small boats were washed ashore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nantucket_damage_115-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nantucket_damage-115">[115]</a></sup> Power outages were widespread; about 80,000 customers in Massachusetts, mostly on Cape Cod, and 9,000 in Maine lost electric power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-snowfall_113-1"><a href="#cite_note-snowfall-113">[113]</a></sup> Despite the effects, no fatalities or significant property damage were reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-116"><a href="#cite_note-116">[116]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlantic_Canada_and_Quebec">Atlantic Canada and Quebec</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Noel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Atlantic Canada and Quebec">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:442px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane-Noel-2007-over-Canada_3-4november.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="302" data-file-width="1013" height="131" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Hurricane-Noel-2007-over-Canada_3-4november.png/440px-Hurricane-Noel-2007-over-Canada_3-4november.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Hurricane-Noel-2007-over-Canada_3-4november.png/660px-Hurricane-Noel-2007-over-Canada_3-4november.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Hurricane-Noel-2007-over-Canada_3-4november.png/880px-Hurricane-Noel-2007-over-Canada_3-4november.png 2x" width="440"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane-Noel-2007-over-Canada_3-4november.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rain and snow falls in Canada</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The storm continued on into <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a> with full force in the latter part of November 3 and early on November 4. Many flights were cancelled out of several airports, and around 150,000 people in <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> lost power for several days, due to high winds and "salt fog" disabling the power grid. Flooding was also reported due to the heavy rainfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-117"><a href="#cite_note-117">[117]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a>, 10,000 people lost electricity. <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a> was relatively unaffected, although ferries between it and Nova Scotia were canceled for the entire weekend and winds peaked at 180 km/h (110 mph) in <a href="/wiki/Wreckhouse,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Wreckhouse, Newfoundland and Labrador">Wreckhouse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTV_118-0"><a href="#cite_note-CTV-118">[118]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBC-NS_119-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBC-NS-119">[119]</a></sup> Over 70 mm (2.8 in) of rainfall was received in some areas, and waves of over 12 metres (39 feet) were reported in the Atlantic, off the coast of Nova Scotia. Several coastal roads were washed out, with boulders thrown far on shore by the pounding surf. One fish farm in Nova Scotia took $1,000,000 in damage alone, losing almost all of its catch.</p>
<p>In eastern <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, up to 130 mm (5.1 in) of rain fell in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gasp%C3%A9sie" title="Gaspésie">Gaspésie</a>, Anticosti, and Eastern <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B4te-Nord" title="Côte-Nord">Côte-Nord</a>, causing flooding in several areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lcn_120-0"><a href="#cite_note-lcn-120">[120]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Bas-Saint-Laurent" title="Bas-Saint-Laurent">Bas-Saint-Laurent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sept-%C3%8Eles,_Quebec" title="Sept-Îles, Quebec">Sept-Îles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baie-Comeau" title="Baie-Comeau">Baie-Comeau</a> regions, it was 20 cm (8 in) of snow that fell, causing a bus accident with 10 injuries near <a href="/wiki/Saint-Simon,_Bas-Saint-Laurent,_Quebec" title="Saint-Simon, Bas-Saint-Laurent, Quebec">Saint-Simon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lcn_120-1"><a href="#cite_note-lcn-120">[120]</a></sup> Severe rains flooded the water treatment facility in <a href="/wiki/Perc%C3%A9,_Quebec" title="Percé, Quebec">Percé, Quebec</a>, leaving the city under a <a href="/wiki/Boil-water_advisory" title="Boil-water advisory">boil-water advisory</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-121"><a href="#cite_note-121">[121]</a></sup> the city was also cut from the rest of the province as the heavy rains made a short section of <a href="/wiki/Quebec_Route_132" title="Quebec Route 132">Quebec Route 132</a> unusable. In Quebec, just under 19,000 <a href="/wiki/Hydro-Qu%C3%A9bec" title="Hydro-Québec">Hydro-Québec</a> customers lost power, and activity was affected at several Côte-Nord plants, including plants in the aluminum sector.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-122"><a href="#cite_note-122">[122]</a></sup> A major transmission line in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minganie_Regional_County_Municipality,_Quebec" title="Minganie Regional County Municipality, Quebec">Minganie</a> region was damaged by the heavy wet snow and locally freezing rain, causing blackouts and school closures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-123"><a href="#cite_note-123">[123]</a></sup> One fatality related to the storm was reported in Canada when a boat capsized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-atl.ec.gc.ca_54-1"><a href="#cite_note-atl.ec.gc.ca-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Noel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>
<p>The government of the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> distributed emergency supplies to 145,000 families following the storm, including food, blankets, mattresses, and mosquito nets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1031reut_76-5"><a href="#cite_note-1031reut-76">[76]</a></sup> The relief aid amounted to about $3 million (USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1031_45-3"><a href="#cite_note-afp1031-45">[45]</a></sup> The government requested a $200 million (USD) loan from the <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Development_Bank" title="Inter-American Development Bank">Inter-American Development Bank</a> and the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> on November 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloomberg_62-3"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg-62">[62]</a></sup> Storm flooding led to an outbreak of <a href="/wiki/Leptospirosis" title="Leptospirosis">leptospirosis</a> in the Dominican Republic in the weeks following the passage of Noel, causing 25 deaths and infecting 193 others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-124"><a href="#cite_note-124">[124]</a></sup> The government of <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a> allocated about $1.5 million (USD) for those affected by the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1031_45-4"><a href="#cite_note-afp1031-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>Due to the extensive damage and number of deaths from <i>Noel</i>, the name was officially retired on May 13, 2008 by the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> during its annual meeting in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando, Florida</a>, and will not be used again for any future <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_hurricane" title="North Atlantic hurricane">North Atlantic hurricanes</a>. It was replaced by <i>Nestor</i> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lists_of_tropical_cyclone_names#North_Atlantic" title="Lists of tropical cyclone names">Atlantic hurricane naming lists</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2013_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2013 Atlantic hurricane season">2013 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-125"><a href="#cite_note-125">[125]</a></sup> But because it was not used in that season, it could be used in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2019_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2019 Atlantic hurricane season">2019 season</a>.</p>
