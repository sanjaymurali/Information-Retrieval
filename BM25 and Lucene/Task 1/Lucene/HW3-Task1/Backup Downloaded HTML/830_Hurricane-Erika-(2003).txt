<p><b>Hurricane Erika</b> was a weak <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">hurricane</a> that struck extreme northeastern <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> near the <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>-<a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a> border in mid-August of the <a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">2003 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. Erika was the eighth <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a>, fifth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">tropical storm</a>, and third <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">hurricane</a> of the season. At first, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) operationally did not designate it as a hurricane because initial data suggested winds of 70 mph (115 km/h) at Erika's peak intensity. It was not until later data was analyzed that the NHC revised it to Category 1 intensity in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>. The storm developed from a non-tropical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">area of low pressure</a> that was tracked for five days before developing in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> on August 14. Under the influence of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_pressure_area" title="High pressure area">high pressure system</a>, Erika moved quickly westward and strengthened under <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">favorable conditions</a>. It made landfall as a hurricane on northeastern Mexico on August 16 and rapidly dissipated inland.</p>
<p>While Erika's precursor disturbance was moving across <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, it dropped heavy rainfall. In south Texas, Erika produced moderate winds of 50 to 60 mph (80 to 95 km/h) along with light rain, causing minor and isolated wind damage in the state. In northeastern Mexico, Erika produced moderate amounts of rainfall, resulting in mudslides and flooding. There, two people were killed when their vehicle was swept away by floodwaters.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Erika_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Erika_2003_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Erika_2003_track.png/220px-Erika_2003_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Erika_2003_track.png/330px-Erika_2003_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Erika_2003_track.png/440px-Erika_2003_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Erika_2003_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A weak surface area of low pressure detached from a <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_analysis" title="Surface weather analysis">frontal system</a> on August 8 while 1,150 miles (1,850 km) to the east of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>. It moved southwestward, and on August 9, it generated <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convection</a> as it passed beneath a cold-core <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">upper-level</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low</a>. The surface low and the upper-level low turned westward as it revolved around a common center, and by August 11, the surface low developed into a <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> while 440 miles (700 km) south of Bermuda. As the system rapidly continued westward, much of the convection remained near the center of the upper-level low, preventing development of a closed surface circulation. On August 13, while located near the northwestern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>, a substantial increase in convection resulted in the upper-level low building downwards to the middle levels of the <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">troposphere</a>, coinciding with the development of an upper level <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">anticyclone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>A closed low-level circulation nearly developed on August 14 to the east of <a href="/wiki/Key_Largo,_Florida" title="Key Largo, Florida">Key Largo, Florida</a>, but it weakened due to the deep convection remaining to the north over the mid-level center. The mid-level storm continued westward and moved across Florida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> After crossing Florida, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a> indicated a poorly defined circulation, but with winds exceeding tropical storm strength, and the system was designated as Tropical Storm <i>Erika</i> late on August 14 while located 85 miles (135 km) west of <a href="/wiki/Fort_Myers,_Florida" title="Fort Myers, Florida">Fort Myers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erika1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-erika1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Erika_2003_Radar.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="554" data-file-width="700" height="174" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Hurricane_Erika_2003_Radar.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Erika_2003_Radar.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Hurricane_Erika_2003_Radar.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Erika_2003_Radar.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Hurricane_Erika_2003_Radar.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Erika_2003_Radar.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Erika_2003_Radar.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/NEXRAD" title="NEXRAD">NEXRAD</a> image of Erika making landfall on northeastern Mexico.</div>

<p>With well-established <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a> and low levels of <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erika1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-erika1-2">[2]</a></sup> Erika strengthened as the circulation became better defined. A high pressure system persisted over the south-central <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, forcing the storm to move just south of due west at 25 mph (40 km/h). On August 15, convection organized into <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">bands</a>, and as its winds approached hurricane strength, an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> developed within the storm. Erika turned to the west-southwest on August 16, and attained hurricane status just prior to making landfall near <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Boca_San_Rafael&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Boca San Rafael (page does not exist)">Boca San Rafael</a>, Tamaulipas in northeastern Mexico, or about 40 miles (70 km) south of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States%E2%80%93Mexico_border" title="United States–Mexico border">United States–Mexico border</a>. The storm rapidly weakened over the mountainous <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Oriental" title="Sierra Madre Oriental">Sierra Madre Oriental</a>, and Erika dissipated early on August 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The mid-level circulation maintained integrity as it crossed Mexico, and led to the formation of a tropical disturbance after entering the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_California" title="Gulf of California">Gulf of California</a> on August 18. It turned to the northwest and weakened on August 20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-hpc-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Operationally Erika was never upgraded to hurricane status. Based on a persistent eye feature on radar and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doppler_weather_radar" title="Doppler weather radar">Doppler weather radar</a>-estimated surface winds of 75 mph (120 km/h), the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> posthumously upgraded Erika to a hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Erika_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Erika081503-1945z2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="438" data-file-width="700" height="138" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Erika081503-1945z2.jpg/220px-Erika081503-1945z2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Erika081503-1945z2.jpg/330px-Erika081503-1945z2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Erika081503-1945z2.jpg/440px-Erika081503-1945z2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Erika081503-1945z2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Tropical Storm Erika approaching Mexico on August 15</div>

<p>The threat of Erika's onslaught prompted the evacuation of 51 <a href="/wiki/Oil_platform" title="Oil platform">oil platforms</a> and 3 <a href="/wiki/Oil_platform" title="Oil platform">oil rigs</a> in the western Gulf of Mexico. The lack of production led to a loss of production of 8,708 barrels (1,384.5 m<sup>3</sup>) of oil per day and 173.14 million cubic feet (4,903,000 m<sup>3</sup>) of <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> per day. On the day of its landfall, the lack of production led to 1,979 less barrels of oil for the day, or about 0.12% of the total daily production for the Gulf of Mexico, while the loss of 32 million cubic feet (910,000 m<sup>3</sup>) of gas for the day was equivalent to 0.23% of the total production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oil_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-oil-4">[4]</a></sup> However, due to its rapid motion, the passage of the storm resulted in minimal effects on operations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-planetark_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-planetark-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>While the storm was located in the eastern Gulf of Mexico on August 15, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches#Hurricane_Watch" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">Hurricane Watch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches#Tropical_Storm_Warning" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">Tropical Storm Warning</a> from <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Texas" title="Brownsville, Texas">Brownsville</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baffin_Bay,_Texas" title="Baffin Bay, Texas">Baffin Bay, Texas</a>. The center also recommended a Hurricane Watch spanning from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soto_la_Marina" title="Soto la Marina">Soto la Marina</a>, Tamaulipas to the international border. Late that same day, when strengthening was underway, a Hurricane Warning was either issued or recommended from <a href="/wiki/La_Pesca" title="La Pesca">La Pesca</a>, Mexico to Baffin Bay, Texas, though the warnings for south Texas were dropped when a more southward motion occurred.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Just one month after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Claudette_(2003)" title="Hurricane Claudette (2003)">Hurricane Claudette</a> caused millions in damage in south Texas, the fast movement of Erika caught citizens by surprise as it was forecast to make landfall near Brownsville. Citizens and business owners boarded up for the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jterika_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-jterika-6">[6]</a></sup> About 10,000 were evacuated from northeastern Mexico due to the threat for flooding,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-desdedomin_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-desdedomin-7">[7]</a></sup> including 2,000 in <a href="/wiki/Matamoros,_Tamaulipas" title="Matamoros, Tamaulipas">Matamoros</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-planetark_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-planetark-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Erika_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Erika_2003_rainfall.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="522" data-file-width="1273" height="103" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Erika_2003_rainfall.gif/250px-Erika_2003_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Erika_2003_rainfall.gif/375px-Erika_2003_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Erika_2003_rainfall.gif/500px-Erika_2003_rainfall.gif 2x" width="250"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Erika_2003_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rainfall data for Erika</div>
</div>

<p>The precursor disturbance was expected to bring heavy, yet needed rainfall to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baha_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-baha-8">[8]</a></sup> The precursor disturbance dropped heavy precipitation while moving across Florida, including in <a href="/wiki/Indian_River_County,_Florida" title="Indian River County, Florida">Indian River County</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Florida_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Florida-9">[9]</a></sup> and also produced 6–8 ft (1.8–2.4 m) waves with moderate wind gusts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fltoday_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-fltoday-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Erika produced light rainfall across southern <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, peaking at 3.83 inches (97 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Sabinal,_Texas" title="Sabinal, Texas">Sabinal</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPCMAX_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-WPCMAX-11">[11]</a></sup> though most locations reported less than two inches (50 mm) of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a>. In addition, <a href="/wiki/Weather_radar" title="Weather radar">weather radar</a> estimated isolated accumulations of 4 to 6 inches (100 to 150 mm) of precipitation in <a href="/wiki/Kenedy_County,_Texas" title="Kenedy County, Texas">Kenedy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brooks_County,_Texas" title="Brooks County, Texas">Brooks Counties</a>. Sustained winds from Erika in south Texas peaked at 39 mph (62 km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Texas" title="Brownsville, Texas">Brownsville</a>, where a gust of 47 mph (75 km/h) was also recorded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Strong waves were reported northwards to <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">Corpus Christi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-12">[12]</a></sup> The storm caused minor flooding and beach erosion along <a href="/wiki/South_Padre_Island" title="South Padre Island">South Padre Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Strong wind gusts of up to 60 mph (95 km/h) caused isolated, minor wind damage in south Texas, including in South Padre Island, where the winds damaged the roof of a business. The winds also uprooted a large tree and caused limb damage to several small- to medium-sized trees in Brownsville. In Texas, damage totaled to $10,000 (2003 USD, $13 thousand 2017 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Mexico, Hurricane Erika primarily affected the states of Tamaulipas and <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Nuevo León">Nuevo León</a>, but also had effects on <a href="/wiki/Coahuila" title="Coahuila">Coahuila</a> as well. Rainfall peaked at 6.71 inches (170.5 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Magueyes" title="Magueyes">Magueyes</a> in Tamaulipas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smn_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-smn-14">[14]</a></sup> Several other locations reported over 3 inches (76 mm),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> including 4.02 inches (102 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Cerro_Prieto" title="Cerro Prieto">Cerro Prieto</a>, which was the maximum amount in the state of Nuevo León,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smn_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-smn-14">[14]</a></sup> and 3.42 inches (86.8 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> where 30 people were injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-desdedomin_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-desdedomin-7">[7]</a></sup> Sustained winds peaked at 40 mph (65 km/h) in <a href="/wiki/San_Fernando,_Chiapas" title="San Fernando, Chiapas">San Fernando</a>, where a gust of 65 mph (105 km/h) was also reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The heavy rainfall resulted in severe flooding and mudslides, blocking several highways in northeastern Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smn_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-smn-14">[14]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Matamoros,_Tamaulipas" title="Matamoros, Tamaulipas">Matamoros</a>, the storm damaged roofs and cars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-desdedomin_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-desdedomin-7">[7]</a></sup> Moderate winds snapped tree branches and spread debris across roads, though locals considered the storm minor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-planetark_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-planetark-5">[5]</a></sup> In the Nuevo León city of <a href="/wiki/Montemorelos" title="Montemorelos">Montemorelos</a>, two people died when they were swept away after they drove their truck across a partially flooded bridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Throughout Mexico, 20,000 people were left without power due to the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-desdedomin_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-desdedomin-7">[7]</a></sup> The remnant circulation produced heavy amounts of precipitation in western Mexico and <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Peninsula" title="Baja California Peninsula">Baja California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-hpc-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
