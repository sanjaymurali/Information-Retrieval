<p><b>Warm Jets</b> were a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indie_(music)" title="Indie (music)">indie</a> band, who had two UK top 40 singles and a top 40 album in 1998.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Warm_Jets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The band was formed in 1995 by Louis Jones, Paul Noble (formerly of <a href="/wiki/Eat_(band)" title="Eat (band)">Eat</a>) and Ed Grimshaw, and signed to This Way Up records in early 1996. They recruited former <a href="/wiki/Pale_Saints" title="Pale Saints">Pale Saints</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parachute_Men" title="Parachute Men">Parachute Men</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rialto_(band)" title="Rialto (band)">Rialto</a> member Colleen Browne on <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass guitar</a> 1995-1997. She was replaced by Aki Shibahara. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Strong_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the release of their debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> <i>Autopia</i> the band received positive press and played prestigious support slots and festival appearances, including an arena tour with <a href="/wiki/Blur_(band)" title="Blur (band)">Blur</a>. The band appeared on <a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a>'s annual tour of up and coming bands in early 1998.</p>
<p>Their only album, <i>Future Signs</i>, was released in 1998, mixed by <a href="/wiki/Glyn_Johns" title="Glyn Johns">Glyn Johns</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Strong_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-1">[1]</a></sup> The band had top forty hits in Britain with "Never Never" and "Hurricane".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Strong_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group's name derives from <a href="/wiki/Brian_Eno" title="Brian Eno">Brian Eno</a>'s 1973 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Here_Come_the_Warm_Jets" title="Here Come the Warm Jets">Here Come the Warm Jets</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Strong_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Strong-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band had some tabloid fame when singer Louis Jones had a relationship with DJ <a href="/wiki/Zo%C3%AB_Ball" title="Zoë Ball">Zoë Ball</a>. They were also often namechecked by Jones's friend and media buddy <a href="/wiki/Paul_Kaye" title="Paul Kaye">Paul Kaye</a> under his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dennis_Pennis" title="Dennis Pennis">Dennis Pennis</a> alter ego.</p>
<p>Paul Noble departed from the band. The remaining trio recruited former <a href="/wiki/Strangelove_(band)" title="Strangelove (band)">Strangelove</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_Aeroplanes" title="Blue Aeroplanes">Blue Aeroplanes</a> guitarist <a href="/wiki/Alex_Lee" title="Alex Lee">Alex Lee</a>. The group disbanded shortly after <a href="/wiki/Island_Records" title="Island Records">Island Records</a> dropped them.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
