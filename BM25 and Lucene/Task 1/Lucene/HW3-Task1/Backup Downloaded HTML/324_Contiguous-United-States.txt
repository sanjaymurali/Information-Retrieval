<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Contiguous United States</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<p>The <b>contiguous United States</b> consists of the 48 adjoining <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. states</a> plus <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> (<a href="/wiki/Federal_district" title="Federal district">federal district</a>), on the <a href="/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">continent</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The term excludes the non-<a href="/wiki/Contiguity#Geography" title="Contiguity">contiguous</a> states of <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> and all off-shore <a href="/wiki/Territories_of_the_United_States" title="Territories of the United States">United States territories and possessions</a>, which are <a href="/wiki/American_Samoa" title="American Samoa">American Samoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">Northern Mariana Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">U.S. Virgin Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rhwcd_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rhwcd-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The greatest distance (on a <a href="/wiki/Great_circle" title="Great circle">great circle</a> route) entirely within the 48 contiguous states is 2,802 miles (4,509 km, between <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">state of Washington</a>);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> the greatest north-south line is 1,650 miles (2,660 km).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Together, the 48 contiguous states and Washington, D.C. occupy a combined area of 3,119,884.69 square miles (8,080,464.3 km<sup>2</sup>), which is 1.58% of the total surface area of <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>. Of this area, 2,959,064.44 square miles (7,663,941.7 km<sup>2</sup>) is contiguous land, composing 83.65% of total U.S. land area, similar to the area of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Officially, 160,820.25 square miles (416,522.5 km<sup>2</sup>) of the contiguous United States is water area, composing 62.66% of the nation's total water area.</p>
<p>The contiguous United States would be placed 5th in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_sovereign_states_and_dependencies_by_area" title="List of sovereign states and dependencies by area">list of sovereign states and dependencies by area</a>; the total area of the country, including Alaska and Hawaii, ranks fourth. <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> is the only country that is larger in total area than the contiguous United States, but smaller than the entire United States, while <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> are the only three countries larger than both. The 2010 census population of this area was 306,675,006, comprising 99.33% of the nation's population, and a density of 103.639 inhabitants/sq mi (40.015/km<sup>2</sup>), compared to 87.264/sq mi (33.692/km<sup>2</sup>) for the nation as a whole.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_terms">Other terms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contiguous_United_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Other terms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While <b>conterminous U.S.</b> has the precise meaning of <b>contiguous U.S.</b> (both adjectives meaning "sharing a common boundary"), other terms commonly used to describe the 48 contiguous states have a greater degree of ambiguity.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Continental_United_States">Continental United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contiguous_United_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Continental United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Because Alaska is also on the North American continent, the term <i>continental United States</i> would also include that state, so the term is sometimes qualified with the explicit inclusion or exclusion of Alaska to resolve any ambiguity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rhwcd_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-rhwcd-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The term was in use prior to the admission of Alaska and Hawaii as states of the United States and at that time usually excluded outlying territories of the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> However, even before Alaska became a state, it was sometimes included within the "Continental U.S."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="CONUS_and_OCONUS">CONUS and OCONUS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contiguous_United_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: CONUS and OCONUS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>CONUS</i>, a technical term used by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Defense" title="United States Department of Defense">U.S. Department of Defense</a>, <a href="/wiki/General_Services_Administration" title="General Services Administration">General Services Administration</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration">NOAA/National Weather Service</a>, and others, has been defined both as the continental United States, and as the 48 contiguous states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GSA_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-GSA-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-conus_military_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-conus_military-15">[15]</a></sup> The District of Columbia is not always specifically mentioned as being part of <i>CONUS</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-conus_military_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-conus_military-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>OCONUS</i> is derived from CONUS with <i>O</i> for outside added, thus referring to Outside of Continental United States (OCONUS).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GSA_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-GSA-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id='The_"Lower_48"'></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_.22Lower_48.22">The "Lower 48"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contiguous_United_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title='Edit section: The "Lower 48"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The term "<b>Lower 48</b>" is also used to refer to the conterminous United States. The <i><a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_(magazine)" title="National Geographic (magazine)">National Geographic</a></i> style guide recommends the use of <i>contiguous</i> or <i>conterminous United States</i> instead of <i>lower 48</i> when the 48 states are meant, unless used in the context of Alaska.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id='"Zone_of_the_Interior"'></span><span class="mw-headline" id=".22Zone_of_the_Interior.22">"Zone of the Interior"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contiguous_United_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title='Edit section: "Zone of the Interior"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During World War II, the first four <a href="/wiki/Numbered_Air_Force" title="Numbered Air Force">numbered Air Forces</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USAAF" title="USAAF">USAAF</a> were said to be assigned to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USAAF#Emblems" title="USAAF">"Zone of the Interior"</a> by the American military organizations of the time — the future states of <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>, then each only <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._territory" title="U.S. territory">territories</a> of the Union, were respectively covered by the <a href="/wiki/Eleventh_Air_Force#Origins" title="Eleventh Air Force">Eleventh Air Force</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seventh_Air_Force#Origins" title="Seventh Air Force">Seventh Air Force</a> during WW II.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terms_used_in_the_non-contiguous_states">Terms used in the non-contiguous states</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contiguous_United_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Terms used in the non-contiguous states">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Both Alaskans and Hawaiians have unique labels for the contiguous United States because of their own locations relative to them.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alaska">Alaska</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contiguous_United_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Alaska">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Outside_(Alaska)" title="Outside (Alaska)">Outside (Alaska)</a></div>
<p>Alaska became the 49th state of the United States on January 3, 1959. Alaska is on the northwest end of the North American continent, but separated from the rest of the United States Pacific coast by the <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> province of <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>. In Alaska, given the ambiguity surrounding the usage of <i>continental</i>, the term "continental United States" is almost unheard of when referring to the contiguous 48 states.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Several other terms have been used over the years. The term <i>Lower 48</i> has, for many years, been a common Alaskan equivalent for "contiguous United States";<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> today, more Alaskans use the term <a href="/wiki/Outside_(Alaska)" title="Outside (Alaska)">"Outside"</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> though a few persons may use "Outside" to refer to <i>any</i> location not within Alaska.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hawaii">Hawaii</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contiguous_United_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Hawaii">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hawaii became the 50th state of the United States on August 21, 1959. It is the southernmost and so far, the latest state to join the Union. Not part of any continent, Hawaii is located in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, about 2,200 miles (3,500 km) from North America and almost halfway to <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. In Hawaii and <a href="/wiki/Insular_area" title="Insular area">overseas American territories</a>, for instance, the terms <i>the Mainland</i> or <i>U.S. Mainland</i> are often used to refer to the continental United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-contiguous_areas_within_the_contiguous_United_States">Non-contiguous areas within the contiguous United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contiguous_United_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Non-contiguous areas within the contiguous United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Apart from off-shore <a href="/wiki/List_of_islands_of_the_United_States_by_area" title="List of islands of the United States by area">US islands</a>, a few continental portions of the contiguous US are accessible by road only by traveling through Canada. <a href="/wiki/Point_Roberts,_Washington" title="Point Roberts, Washington">Point Roberts, Washington</a>; <a href="/wiki/Elm_Point,_Minnesota" title="Elm Point, Minnesota">Elm Point, Minnesota</a>; and the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Angle" title="Northwest Angle">Northwest Angle</a> in <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a> are three such places. <a href="/wiki/Alburgh_(town),_Vermont" title="Alburgh (town), Vermont">Alburgh, Vermont</a>, is not directly connected by land, but is accessible by road via bridges from within Vermont and from New York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
