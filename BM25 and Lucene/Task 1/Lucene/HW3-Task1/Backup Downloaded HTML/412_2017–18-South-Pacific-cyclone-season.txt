<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2015-16_South_Pacific_cyclone_season" title="2015-16 South Pacific cyclone season">2015–16</a>, <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_South_Pacific_cyclone_season" title="2016–17 South Pacific cyclone season">2016–17</a>, <b>2017–18</b>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lists_of_tropical_cyclone_names" title="Lists of tropical cyclone names">2018–19</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lists_of_tropical_cyclone_names" title="Lists of tropical cyclone names">2019–20</a></i></td>

<p>The <b>2017–18 South Pacific cyclone season</b> is the period of the year when most <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones" title="Tropical cyclones">tropical cyclones</a> form within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Pacific_Ocean" title="South Pacific Ocean">South Pacific Ocean</a> to the east of 160°E. The season officially runs from November 1, 2017 to April 30, 2018, however a tropical cyclone could form at any time between July 1, 2017 and June 30, 2018 and would count towards the season total. During the season, tropical cyclones will be officially monitored by the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Center" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Center">Regional Specialized Meteorological Center</a> (RSMC) in <a href="/wiki/Fiji_Meteorological_Service" title="Fiji Meteorological Service">Nadi, Fiji</a> and the Tropical Cyclone Warning Centers in <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Meteorology" title="Bureau of Meteorology">Brisbane, Australia</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorological_Service_of_New_Zealand_Limited" title="Meteorological Service of New Zealand Limited">Wellington, New Zealand</a>. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">United States Armed Forces</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">Joint Typhoon Warning Center</a> <small>(JTWC)</small> will also monitor the basin and issue unofficial warnings for American interests. RSMC Nadi attaches a number and an F suffix to tropical disturbances that form in or move into the basin while the JTWC designates significant tropical cyclones with a number and a P suffix. RSMC Nadi, TCWC Wellington and TCWC Brisbane all use the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_Tropical_Cyclone_Intensity_Scale" title="Australian Tropical Cyclone Intensity Scale">Australian Tropical Cyclone Intensity Scale</a> and estimate windspeeds over a period of ten minutes, while the JTWC estimated sustained winds over a 1-minute period, which are subsequently compared to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> <small>(SSHWS)</small>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Storm_names">Storm names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017%E2%80%9318_South_Pacific_cyclone_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Storm names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lists_of_tropical_cyclone_names" title="Lists of tropical cyclone names">Lists of tropical cyclone names</a></div>
<p>Within the Southern Pacific a tropical depression is judged to have reached tropical cyclone intensity should it reach winds of 65 km/h, (40 mph) and it is evident that gales are occurring at least halfway around the center. With tropical depressions intensifying into a tropical cyclone between the Equator and 25°S and between 160°E - 120°W named by the RSMC Nadi. However should a tropical depression intensify to the south of 25°S between 160°E and 120°W it will be named in conjunction with RSMC Nadi by TCWC Wellington. Should a tropical cyclone move out of the basin and into the Australian region it will retain its original name. The next 10 names on the naming list are listed here below.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCOP_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCOP-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
