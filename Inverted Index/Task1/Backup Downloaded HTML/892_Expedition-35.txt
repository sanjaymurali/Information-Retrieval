<p><b>Expedition 35</b> was the 35th long-duration mission to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> (ISS). The expedition started 13 March 2013, and marked the first time a <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> astronaut – Colonel <a href="/wiki/Chris_Hadfield" title="Chris Hadfield">Chris Hadfield</a> – was in command of the station. Expedition 35 was also only the second time an ISS crew is led by neither a <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> astronaut, nor a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federal_Space_Agency" title="Russian Federal Space Agency">Roscosmos</a> cosmonaut, after <a href="/wiki/Expedition_21" title="Expedition 21">Expedition 21</a> in 2009, when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ESA" title="ESA">ESA</a> astronaut <a href="/wiki/Frank_De_Winne" title="Frank De Winne">Frank De Winne</a> was in command.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission_highlights">Mission highlights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_35&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mission highlights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The mission generated considerable media attention and turned Cmdr. Chris Hadfield into a minor celebrity. The expedition made extensive use of social media, and several videos uploaded to YouTube have generated millions of hits. In particular, Cmdr. Hadfield was involved in the "first music video recorded in space", a rendition of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/David_Bowie%27s" title="David Bowie's">David Bowie's</a> 1969 song "<a href="/wiki/Space_Oddity" title="Space Oddity">Space Oddity</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-music_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-music-5">[5]</a></sup> Cmdr. Hadfield was also involved in the reveal of the Bank of Canada's new $5 note, part of the <a href="/wiki/Frontier_Series" title="Frontier Series">Frontier Series</a> of polymer bills released in 2013. The reveal occurred via video on the ISS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-money_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-money-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>During Expedition 35, the <a href="/wiki/SpaceX_CRS-2" title="SpaceX CRS-2">SpaceX CRS-2</a> mission successfully delivered supplies to the station and returned some cargo from space. This was the second of SpaceX's contracted cargo flights to the ISS and the first to use the unpressurized trunk section.</p>
