<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> <b>National Climatic Data Center</b> (<b>NCDC</b>), previously known as the National Weather Records Center (NWRC), in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asheville" title="Asheville">Asheville</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> was the world's largest active <a href="/wiki/Archive" title="Archive">archive</a> of <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">weather</a> <a href="/wiki/Data" title="Data">data</a>. Starting as a tabulation unit in New Orleans, Louisiana in 1934, the climate records were transferred to Asheville in 1951, becoming named the National Weather Records Center (NWRC). It was later renamed the National Climatic Data Center, with relocation occurring in 1993. In 2015, it was merged with the National Geophysical Data Center (NGDC) and the National Oceanic Data Center (NODC) into the <a href="/wiki/National_Centers_for_Environmental_Information" title="National Centers for Environmental Information">National Centers for Environmental Information</a> (NCEI).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncei.noaa.gov_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncei.noaa.gov-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Climatic_Data_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1934, a tabulation unit was established in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Orleans,_Louisiana" title="New Orleans, Louisiana">New Orleans, Louisiana</a> to process past weather records. Climate records and upper air observations were punched onto cards in 1936. This organization was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Asheville,_North_Carolina" title="Asheville, North Carolina">Asheville, North Carolina</a> in 1951, where the National Weather Records Center (NWRC) was established.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It was housed in the <a href="/wiki/Arcade_Building_(Asheville,_North_Carolina)" title="Arcade Building (Asheville, North Carolina)">Grove Arcade Building</a> in Asheville, North Carolina. Processing of the climate data was accomplished at Weather Records Processing Centers at <a href="/wiki/Chattanooga,_Tennessee" title="Chattanooga, Tennessee">Chattanooga, Tennessee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City, Missouri</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>, until January 1, 1963 when it became consolidated with the NWRC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> This name was maintained by the agency through 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The NCDC was then housed at the Veach-Baley Federal Complex in downtown Asheville where it moved after the building's completion in 1995. In 2015, NCDC merged with the National Geophysical Data Center and the National Oceanographic Data Center to form the National Centers for Environmental Information.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncei.noaa.gov_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ncei.noaa.gov-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sources">Sources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Climatic_Data_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sources">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Data were received from a wide variety of sources, including <a href="/wiki/Weather_satellite" title="Weather satellite">weather satellites</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radar" title="Radar">radar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Automated_airport_weather_station" title="Automated airport weather station">automated airport weather stations</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> (NWS) cooperative observers, <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ship" title="Ship">ships</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radiosonde" title="Radiosonde">radiosondes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wind_profiler" title="Wind profiler">wind profilers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rocketsonde" title="Rocketsonde">rocketsondes</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_radiation" title="Solar radiation">solar radiation</a> networks, and NWS Forecast/Warnings/Analyses Products.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_focus">Climate focus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Climatic_Data_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate focus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Center provided historical perspectives on <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climate</a> which were vital to studies on global <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_effect" title="Greenhouse effect">greenhouse effect</a>, and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_issues" title="Environmental issues">environmental issues</a>. The Center stored information essential to <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hydrology" title="Hydrology">hydrology</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transportation" title="Transportation">transportation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Recreation" title="Recreation">recreation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>. These services are still provided by the NCEI.</p>
<p>The NCDC stated:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Evidence is mounting that global climate is changing. The extent to which man is responsible is still under study. Regardless of the causes, it is essential that a baseline of long-term climate data be compiled; therefore, global data must be acquired, quality controlled, and archived. Working with international institutions such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Council_of_Scientific_Unions" title="International Council of Scientific Unions">International Council of Scientific Unions</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Data_Centers" title="World Data Centers">World Data Centers</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>, NCDC develops standards by which data can be exchanged and made accessible.<br/>
<br/>
NCDC provides the historical perspective on climate. Through the use of over a hundred years of weather observations, reference data bases are generated. From this knowledge the clientele of NCDC can learn from the past to prepare for a better tomorrow. Wise use of our most valuable natural resource, climate, is the goal of climate researchers, state and regional climate centers, business, and commerce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Associated_entities">Associated entities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Climatic_Data_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Associated entities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>NCDC also maintained World Data Center for Meteorology, Asheville. The four World Centers (U.S., <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>) have created a free and open situation in which data and dialogue are exchanged.</p>
<p>NCDC maintained the <a href="/wiki/US_Climate_Reference_Network" title="US Climate Reference Network">US Climate Reference Network</a> datasets amongst a vast number of other climate monitoring products.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
