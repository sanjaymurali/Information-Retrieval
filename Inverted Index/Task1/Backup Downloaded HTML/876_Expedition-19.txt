<p><b>Expedition 19</b> was the 19th long-duration flight to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>. This expedition launched on 26 March 2009, at 11:49 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a> aboard the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-14" title="Soyuz TMA-14">Soyuz TMA-14</a> spacecraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Expedition 19 was the final three crew member expedition, before the crew size increased to six crew members with <a href="/wiki/Expedition_20" title="Expedition 20">Expedition 20</a>.</p>
<p>The expedition was commanded by Russian Air Force Colonel <a href="/wiki/Gennady_Padalka" title="Gennady Padalka">Gennady Padalka</a>. On 31 March 2009, Padalka raised an issue concerning shared use of facilities such as exercise equipment and toilet facilities. Padalka claims that initial approval to use exercise equipment owned by the U.S. government was subsequently turned down. Russian and American members of the crew have now been informed to use only their own toilets and not to share rations. The result was a general lowering of morale on the station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
