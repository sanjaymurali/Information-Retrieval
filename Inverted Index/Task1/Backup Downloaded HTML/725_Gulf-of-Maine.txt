<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Gulf of Maine</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p>The <b>Gulf of Maine</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Golfe du Maine</i></span>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Headlands_and_bays" title="Headlands and bays">gulf</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> on the east coast of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>.</p>
<p>It is delineated by <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">Cape Cod</a> at the eastern tip of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> in the southwest and <a href="/wiki/Cape_Sable_Island" title="Cape Sable Island">Cape Sable Island</a> at the southern tip of <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> in the northeast. It includes the entire coastlines of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. states</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> north of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">Cape Cod</a>, and the southern and western coastlines of the <a href="/wiki/Provinces_and_territories_of_Canada" title="Provinces and territories of Canada">Canadian provinces</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, respectively.</p>
<p>Both <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Bay" title="Massachusetts Bay">Massachusetts Bay</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Fundy" title="Bay of Fundy">Bay of Fundy</a> are included within the Gulf of Maine system. As such, the Gulf of Maine is also home to the highest tidal variations on the planet (see <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Fundy" title="Bay of Fundy">Bay of Fundy</a> for further information).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_features_and_biological_importance">Geographic features and biological importance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gulf_of_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geographic features and biological importance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The coastline of the Gulf of Maine is predominantly rocky and scenic; the only significant coastal developments are located in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth,_New_Hampshire" title="Portsmouth, New Hampshire">Portsmouth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Maine" title="Portland, Maine">Portland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint_John,_New_Brunswick" title="Saint John, New Brunswick">Saint John</a> metropolitan areas. The effects of glaciation are responsible for stripping sedimentary soil away from the coastline, therefore the gulf lacks the sandy beaches found to the south along the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">Eastern Seaboard</a>. The underwater features of the seabed sculptured during the lower sea levels of the <a href="/wiki/Ice_age" title="Ice age">ice ages</a> make the gulf a semi-enclosed sea bounded to the south and east by underwater banks. <a href="/wiki/Georges_Bank" title="Georges Bank">Georges Bank</a> in particular, on its southern end, protects the Gulf of Maine waters from the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a>. Gulf of Maine waters are more strongly influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Labrador_Current" title="Labrador Current">Labrador Current</a>, making the gulf waters significantly colder and more nutrient-rich than those found to the south. Undersea valleys in the central basin can reach depths of 1,500 feet (500 m) while undersea mountains rise up 800 feet (266 m) from the sea floor, almost reaching the surface in some locations, or even exceeding it, creating islands.</p>

<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:GulfofMaine2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Major features of the Gulf of Maine</div>

<p>There are three major basins contained within the Gulf of Maine: Wilkinson Basin to the west, Jordan Basin in the northeast, and Georges Basin in the south, which are isolated from each other beneath the 650 foot (200 m) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isobath" title="Isobath">isobath</a>. Georges Basin, just north of Georges Bank, is the deepest of the three at just over 1200 feet (370 m) and generates a pocket at the end of the Northeast Channel, a deep fissure between Georges Bank and Browns Bank, the southwestern edge of the Nova <a href="/wiki/Scotian_Shelf" title="Scotian Shelf">Scotian Shelf</a>. The Northeast Channel is the major channel between the Gulf and the rest of the Northwest Atlantic. A secondary, shallower connection to the rest of the Atlantic is the Great South Channel, located between Georges Bank and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nantucket" title="Nantucket">Nantucket</a> Shoals.</p>
<p>Due to rapid warming of the Gulf of Maine, the water has become too hot for cod. This, along with past overfishing, has helped pushed stocks towards collapse and hampered its recovery despite deep reductions in the number of fish caught, according to a study conducted by the Gulf of Maine Research Institute. Traditional calculations "consistently over-estimated the abundance of cod." From 2004, temperatures rose by more than 0.4 degrees F (0.1 degrees C) per year, culminating in an ocean heat wave in the northwest Atlantic in 2012-13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gulf_of_Maine_watershed">Gulf of Maine watershed</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gulf_of_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Gulf of Maine watershed">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The watershed of the gulf encompasses an area of 69,115 sq mi (179,010 km<sup>2</sup>), including all of Maine, 70% of New Hampshire, 56% of New Brunswick, 41% of Massachusetts, and 36% of Nova Scotia. The watershed also includes a small southern portion (less than 1%) of the Canadian province of <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>. Significant rivers that drain into the Gulf include, from east to west, the <a href="/wiki/Annapolis_River" title="Annapolis River">Annapolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shubenacadie_River" title="Shubenacadie River">Shubenacadie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salmon_River_(Nova_Scotia)" title="Salmon River (Nova Scotia)">Salmon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Petitcodiac_River" title="Petitcodiac River">Petitcodiac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint_John_River_(Bay_of_Fundy)" title="Saint John River (Bay of Fundy)">Saint John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magaguadavic_River" title="Magaguadavic River">Magaguadavic</a>, <a href="/wiki/St._Croix_River_(Maine%E2%80%93New_Brunswick)" title="St. Croix River (Maine–New Brunswick)">St. Croix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Penobscot_River" title="Penobscot River">Penobscot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kennebec_River" title="Kennebec River">Kennebec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saco_River" title="Saco River">Saco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Piscataqua_River" title="Piscataqua River">Piscataqua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Merrimack_River" title="Merrimack River">Merrimack</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_River" title="Charles River">Charles</a> rivers; the Saint John and Penobscot having the greatest discharge.</p>
<p>The cold waters, extreme tidal mixing, and diverse bottom of the Gulf make it one of the most productive marine environments in the North Atlantic, and it furnishes habitat for many diverse species including most notably <a href="/wiki/Haddock" title="Haddock">Haddock</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Acadian_redfish" title="Acadian redfish">Acadian redfish</a> (<i>Sebastes fasciatus</i>), the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_herring" title="Atlantic herring">Atlantic herring</a> (<i>Clupea harengus</i>) and the <a href="/wiki/American_lobster" title="American lobster">American lobster</a> (<i>Homarus americanus</i>), which grows to famously large sizes in the Gulf. The waters of the Gulf of Maine system, particularly at the boundary with the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Fundy" title="Bay of Fundy">Bay of Fundy</a> are also home to the summering grounds for many different <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> and <a href="/wiki/Whale" title="Whale">whale</a> species, most notably the highly endangered <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_right_whale" title="North Atlantic right whale">North Atlantic right whale</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_political_significance">History and political significance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gulf_of_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History and political significance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The gulf's relative proximity to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> made it an early destination for European colonization. <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> settlers founded a settlement on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Croix_Island_(Maine)" title="St. Croix Island (Maine)">St. Croix Island</a> in 1604. <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> settlers founded the <a href="/wiki/Popham_Colony" title="Popham Colony">Popham Colony</a> on an island in the Kennebec River in 1607, the same year as the <a href="/wiki/Jamestown,_Virginia" title="Jamestown, Virginia">Jamestown</a> settlement, followed by the <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Colony" title="Plymouth Colony">Plymouth Colony</a> on the shores of Massachusetts Bay in 1620.</p>
<p>In the 1960s and 1970s there was a dispute between Canada and the United States over fishing and other resource rights in the Gulf of Maine, specifically the <a href="/wiki/Georges_Bank" title="Georges Bank">Georges Bank</a> region. This dispute was taken to the <a href="/wiki/International_Court_of_Justice" title="International Court of Justice">International Court of Justice</a>, which delineated a <a href="/wiki/Maritime_boundary" title="Maritime boundary">maritime boundary</a> through the Gulf in 1984. Canada and the U.S. continue to disagree on the sovereignty of <a href="/wiki/Machias_Seal_Island" title="Machias Seal Island">Machias Seal Island</a> and the waters surrounding it in the northeastern part of the gulf. In recognition of the Gulf's importance to marine habitat, both nations currently maintain complementary embargoes against offshore oil and gas exploration activities on <a href="/wiki/Georges_Bank" title="Georges Bank">Georges Bank</a> in the southern part of the gulf.</p>
