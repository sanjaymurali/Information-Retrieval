<p>"<b>Hurricane</b>" is the debut single of American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Luke_Combs" title="Luke Combs">Luke Combs</a>. The song was released through <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> in 2016 after initially charting in 2015. It is included on his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/This_One%27s_for_You_(Luke_Combs_album)" title="This One's for You (Luke Combs album)">This One's for You</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The song is about comparing an ex-girlfriend to a natural disaster.</p>
<p>"Hurricane" reached number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart. Its second week atop that position gave Combs two distinctions: the first artist to have a multi-week number one debut single chart since <a href="/wiki/Florida_Georgia_Line" title="Florida Georgia Line">Florida Georgia Line</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Cruise_(song)" title="Cruise (song)">Cruise</a>" in 2012 and the first male solo artist to accomplish that since <a href="/wiki/Darius_Rucker" title="Darius Rucker">Darius Rucker</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Think_I_Don%27t_Think_About_It" title="Don't Think I Don't Think About It">Don't Think I Don't Think About It</a>" in 2008. It also peaked at numbers 3 and 31 on both the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a> charts respectively. The song received a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">Gold</a> certification from the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA), and has sold 604,000 copies in the United States as of September 2017. It garnered similar chart success in Canada, reaching number 2 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canada_Country" title="Canada Country">Canada Country</a> chart and number 62 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> chart.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Luke_Combs_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song is about the narrator's former lover, whom he encounters unexpectedly and compares to the damage done by a hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toc_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-toc-2">[2]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> describes the song's arrangement as "an accidental combination of new school and Nineties-era country that freely mixes electronic beats".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stone_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-stone-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Scott Moffatt, of <a href="/wiki/The_Moffatts" title="The Moffatts">The Moffatts</a> produced the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stone_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-stone-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Luke_Combs_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Billy Dukes of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taste_of_Country" title="Taste of Country">Taste of Country</a></i> reviewed the song with favor, saying "The singer proves he’s a strong storyteller, but also a stylist, with a complex chorus that allows a mainstream production to pop from the speakers."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toc_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-toc-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Luke_Combs_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Hurricane" first charted in July 2015 due to strong sales on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>, and was officially sent to radio through <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> Nashville in October 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It reached number one on the Country Airplay chart dated May 27, 2017,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and remained there the following week, making Combs the first artist to chart a multi-week number one debut single since <a href="/wiki/Florida_Georgia_Line" title="Florida Georgia Line">Florida Georgia Line</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Cruise_(song)" title="Cruise (song)">Cruise</a>" spent three weeks at number one in December 2012, and the first solo male artist to do so since <a href="/wiki/Darius_Rucker" title="Darius Rucker">Darius Rucker</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Think_I_Don%27t_Think_About_It" title="Don't Think I Don't Think About It">Don't Think I Don't Think About It</a>" spent two weeks at number one in October 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BCU_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-BCU-6">[6]</a></sup> The song was certified Gold by the RIAA on April 28, 2017, and Platinum on June 8, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-United_StatesLuke_CombsHurricanesingleCertRef_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-United_StatesLuke_CombsHurricanesingleCertRef-7">[7]</a></sup> The song has sold 604,000 copies in the United States as of September 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
