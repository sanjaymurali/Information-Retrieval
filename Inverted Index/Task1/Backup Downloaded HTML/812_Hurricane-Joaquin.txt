<p><b>Hurricane Joaquin</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[xwaˈkin]</a></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><small title="English pronunciation respelling"><i>wah-<b>KEEN</b></i></small></a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was a powerful <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> that devastated several districts of <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">the Bahamas</a> and caused damage in the <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands" title="Turks and Caicos Islands">Turks and Caicos Islands</a>, parts of the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">Greater Antilles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>. It was also the strongest Atlantic hurricane of non-tropical origin in the satellite era.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The tenth <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a>, third hurricane, and second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_hurricane" title="Major hurricane">major hurricane</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2015 Atlantic hurricane season">2015 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, Joaquin evolved from a <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">non-tropical low</a> to become a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" title="Tropical depression">tropical depression</a> on September 28, well southwest of Bermuda. Tempered by unfavorable <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>, the depression drifted southwestward. After becoming a tropical storm the next day, Joaquin underwent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapid intensification</a>, reaching hurricane status on September 30 and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale#Category_4" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale">Category 4 major hurricane</a> strength on October 1. Meandering over the southern Bahamas, Joaquin's <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> passed near or over several islands. On October 3, the hurricane weakened somewhat and accelerated to the northeast. Abrupt re-intensification ensued later that day, and Joaquin acquired <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">sustained winds</a> of 155 mph (250 km/h), just short of Category 5 strength.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">Hurricane warnings</a> were hoisted across most of the Bahamas as the hurricane threatened the country. Battering the nation's southern islands for over two days, Joaquin caused extensive devastation, most notably on <a href="/wiki/Acklins" title="Acklins">Acklins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crooked_Island,_Bahamas" title="Crooked Island, Bahamas">Crooked Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Long_Island,_Bahamas" title="Long Island, Bahamas">Long Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rum_Cay" title="Rum Cay">Rum Cay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador_Island" title="San Salvador Island">San Salvador Island</a>. Severe <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> inundated many communities, trapping hundreds of people in their homes; flooding persisted for days after the hurricane's departure. Prolonged, intense winds brought down trees and powerlines, and unroofed homes throughout the affected region. As airstrips were submerged and heavily damaged, relief workers were limited in their ability to quickly help residents affected by Joaquin, one of the strongest storms on record to affect the nation. Offshore, the American cargo ship <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Faro_(ship)" title="El Faro (ship)"><i>El Faro</i></a> and her 33 members were lost to the hurricane.</p>
<p>Coastal flooding also impacted the nearby Turks and Caicos, washing out roadways, compromising seawalls, and damaging homes. Strong winds and heavy rainfall caused some property damage in eastern <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. One fisherman died when heavy seas capsized a small boat along the coast of <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>. Storm tides resulted in severe flooding in several <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Haiti" title="Departments of Haiti">departments of Haiti</a>, forcing families from their homes and destroying crops. The weakening hurricane passed just west of Bermuda on October 4, attended by strong winds that cut power to 15,000 electric subscribers but caused only minor damage otherwise. After passing near Bermuda, Joaquin ultimately curved northeastward and accelerated, weakening further and becoming extratropical as it entered colder waters.</p>
<p>Collectively, Joaquin killed 34 people and caused $200 million USD in damages. With all 34 deaths attributed to the storm occurring at sea, Joaquin has the highest offshore death toll for any Atlantic hurricane since the <a href="/wiki/1959_Escuminac_disaster" title="1959 Escuminac disaster">Escuminac hurricane</a> in <a href="/wiki/1959_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1959 Atlantic hurricane season">1959</a>, which killed 35 people in the <a href="/wiki/Northumberland_Strait" title="Northumberland Strait">Northumberland Strait</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deadliest_Atlantic_Hurricanes_1492-1996_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Deadliest_Atlantic_Hurricanes_1492-1996-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although Joaquin never directly affected the United States, another large storm system over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southeast_United_States" title="Southeast United States">southeastern states</a> drew tremendous moisture from the hurricane, resulting in <a href="/wiki/October_2015_North_American_storm_complex" title="October 2015 North American storm complex">catastrophic flooding</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Joaquin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Joaquin_2015_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1854" data-file-width="3000" height="185" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Joaquin_2015_track.png/300px-Joaquin_2015_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Joaquin_2015_track.png/450px-Joaquin_2015_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Joaquin_2015_track.png/600px-Joaquin_2015_track.png 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Joaquin_2015_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On September 25, 2015, the US <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) began monitoring an <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">upper-level low</a>, accompanied by a surface <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a>, several hundred miles south-southwest of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> for possible <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">tropical cyclogenesis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The system gradually consolidated as it drifted north-northwest, acquiring a closed <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">surface low</a> late on September 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_convection" title="Atmospheric convection">Convective</a> showers and <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorms</a> steadily increased on September 27, and at 03:00 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> on September 28 the NHC assessed the system to have become a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" title="Tropical depression">tropical depression</a>, situated roughly 405 mi (650 km) southwest of Bermuda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-D1-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Although the depression featured a well-defined low, strong <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> displaced convection and exposed the circulation. A <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> to the north was forecast to steer the system slowly northwest into a region of higher shear; meteorologists at the NHC initially depicted the system dissipating within 96 hours based on <a href="/wiki/Numerical_weather_prediction" title="Numerical weather prediction">modeling depictions</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D1_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-D1-8">[8]</a></sup> Convection developed and persisted closer to the circulation centre throughout September 28,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and early on September 29 <a href="/wiki/Dvorak_technique" title="Dvorak technique">Dvorak satellite classifications</a> indicated the system became a tropical storm. Accordingly, it was <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">assigned the name</a> <i>Joaquin</i>, becoming the tenth named storm of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D5_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-D5-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">

Hurricane Joaquin meandering around the Bahamas on October 2. </div>
</div>
<p>Strengthening of the mid-level ridge prompted a sudden shift in Joaquin's trajectory to the southwest, directing it towards <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">the Bahamas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D5_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-D5-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Forecasters at the NHC noted considerable uncertainty in the future of Joaquin, with forecast models depicting a wide range of possibilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Throughout September 29 the storm steadily intensified as its circulation became embedded within deep convection and upper-level <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a> became increasingly prominent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> High <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperatures</a> and decreasing shear aided strengthening, and early on September 30 the storm achieved <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS" title="SSHS">hurricane status</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rapid_intensification" title="Rapid intensification">Rapid intensification</a> ensued thereafter, with an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> developing within a symmetric <a href="/wiki/Central_dense_overcast" title="Central dense overcast">central dense overcast</a>. Data from aircraft reconnaissance indicated that Joaquin reached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_3" title="SSHS">Category 3</a> status on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir–Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> by 03:00 UTC on October 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Around 12:00 UTC the eye of Joaquin passed over <a href="/wiki/Samana_Cay" title="Samana Cay">Samana Cay</a>, Bahamas, with winds of 130 mph (215 km/h), making it a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_4" title="SSHS">Category 4</a> hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_Joaquin_Report_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-TC_Joaquin_Report-19">[19]</a></sup> Around this same time, its eye contracted from 41 to 27 mi (66 to 43 km) in diameter, representing significant intensification.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> At this time Joaquin was located just 15 mi (25 km) northwest of <a href="/wiki/Crooked_Island,_Bahamas" title="Crooked Island, Bahamas">Crooked Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The storm's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">central pressure</a> bottomed out at 931 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hPa</a>; 27.49 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inches_of_mercury" title="Inches of mercury">inHg</a>) around 00:00 UTC on October 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16a_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-16a-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>As the ridge previously steering Joaquin southwest began retreating north, the hurricane's movement slowed and shifted west, and later north, early on October 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D19_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-D19-25">[25]</a></sup> An <a href="/wiki/Eyewall_replacement_cycle" title="Eyewall replacement cycle">eyewall replacement cycle</a>—a process whereby a second, larger eye develops while the inner eye collapses—began that morning;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D18_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-D18-26">[26]</a></sup> its eye became increasingly ill-defined in satellite imagery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D19_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-D19-25">[25]</a></sup> Slight weakening took place accordingly,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and the hurricane passed over <a href="/wiki/Rum_Cay" title="Rum Cay">Rum Cay</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador_Island" title="San Salvador Island">San Salvador Island</a> around 16:00 UTC and 21:00 UTC as a strong Category 3 hurricane with winds of 125 mph (205 km/h);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_Joaquin_Report_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-TC_Joaquin_Report-19">[19]</a></sup> a pressure near 944 mbar (hPa; 27.88 inHg) was observed on San Salvador Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> An amplifying trough over the <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_United_States" title="Southeastern United States">Southeastern United States</a> enhanced southwesterly flow over Joaquin on October 3 and prompted the hurricane to accelerate northeast away from the Bahamas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Throughout the day the storm's eye became increasingly defined and re-intensification ensued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Aircraft reconnaissance found a considerably stronger system that afternoon; based on flight-level winds of 166 mph (267 km/h), it is estimated that Joaquin attained surface winds of 155 mph (250 km/h)—a high-end Category 4 hurricane—by 16:00 UTC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> This made Joaquin the strongest Atlantic hurricane of non-tropical origin in the satellite era.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_Joaquin_Report_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-TC_Joaquin_Report-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

GOES satellite animation of Hurricane Joaquin from September 28 to October 7</div>

</div>
<p>Shortly after peaking, the hurricane's overall structure began to deteriorate, signalling a weakening trend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> On October 4 the storm curved towards the north-northeast between a large low pressure system to its west and a mid-level <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> to its east.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> As deep convection over its core continued to wane,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Joaquin passed about 70 mi (110 km) west-northwest of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> near 00:00 UTC on October 5, with winds of 85 mph (140 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TC_Joaquin_Report_19-3"><a href="#cite_note-TC_Joaquin_Report-19">[19]</a></sup> The weakening trend slowed that day as the storm's satellite presentation improved slightly, marked by brief reappearances of a distinct eye feature. Joaquin gradually turned northeastward around the periphery of the weak ridge, and subsequently accelerated toward the east-northeast as it entered the <a href="/wiki/Westerlies" title="Westerlies">prevailing westerlies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The system maintained hurricane intensity until 15:00 UTC on October 7, by which point strengthening wind shear and an increasingly colder environment began to take their toll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The cloud pattern became lopsided as colder, drier air infiltrated the circulation, forming the first stages of a <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">frontal structure</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> With its <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical transition</a> well underway, Joaquin lost its identity as a tropical cyclone at 03:00 UTC on October 8, about 850 mi (1,370 km) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Race" title="Cape Race">Cape Race, Newfoundland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> During the next several days, Joaquin's extratropical remnant continued heading eastward across the Atlantic, before reaching <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> on October 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Joaquin_reaches_Portugal_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-Joaquin_reaches_Portugal-39">[39]</a></sup> During the next 5 days, Joaquin's remnant slowly moved southward along the coast of Portugal, until the system was absorbed by another frontal system to the east of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, on October 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Joaquin_absorbed_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-Joaquin_absorbed-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Joaquin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Joaquin_advisory_12_forecast_track.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="716" data-file-width="895" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Hurricane_Joaquin_advisory_12_forecast_track.gif/220px-Hurricane_Joaquin_advisory_12_forecast_track.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Hurricane_Joaquin_advisory_12_forecast_track.gif/330px-Hurricane_Joaquin_advisory_12_forecast_track.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Hurricane_Joaquin_advisory_12_forecast_track.gif/440px-Hurricane_Joaquin_advisory_12_forecast_track.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Joaquin_advisory_12_forecast_track.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The NHC's five-day forecast track for Joaquin at 5:00 p.m. EDT (21:00 UTC) on September 30, depicting landfall over the United States</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_watches_and_warnings" title="Tropical cyclone watches and warnings">Tropical cyclone watches and warnings</a> were posted throughout the Bahamas starting early on September 30 (UTC);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> by October 1, hurricane warnings extended from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Bahama_Island" title="Grand Bahama Island">Grand Bahama Island</a> in the northwest to <a href="/wiki/Mayaguana" title="Mayaguana">Mayaguana</a> in the southeast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PA15_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-PA15-42">[42]</a></sup> As the storm moved away, the last advisories were discontinued by the morning of October 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> All schools on Exuma, Cat Island, San Salvador, and Rum Cay closed on the afternoon of October 1 until further notice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bahamasair" title="Bahamasair">Bahamasair</a> cancelled multiple domestic flights,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> and most airports throughout the island nation were closed, pending post-storm runway inspections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Several cruise ships scheduled to arrive at <a href="/wiki/New_Providence" title="New Providence">New Providence</a> were diverted to other ports on October 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Residents on <a href="/wiki/Mayaguana" title="Mayaguana">Mayaguana</a> were advised to evacuate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABC1_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-ABC1-48">[48]</a></sup> The nation's <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Disaster_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Caribbean Disaster Emergency Management Agency">National Emergency Management Agency</a> (NEMA) activated its Emergency Operations Center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BPMpress_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-BPMpress-49">[49]</a></sup> As conditions worsened, residents in southern islands of the Bahamas criticized the government for providing inadequate warning, with no emergency preparations taking place on Acklins. NEMA refuted the claim and stated people were given ample warning but many residents refused to evacuate. In some instances, police were called in to forcibly move people to shelters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TribuneOct1_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-TribuneOct1-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands" title="Turks and Caicos Islands">Turks and Caicos Islands</a> to the southeast of the Bahamas, the storm forced the closure of schools and government offices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-turks_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-turks-51">[51]</a></sup> Two cruise ships were redirected from the island, and <a href="/wiki/Providenciales_International_Airport" title="Providenciales International Airport">Providenciales International Airport</a> suspended operations for a time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> The islands were placed under a tropical storm warning on October 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PA15_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-PA15-42">[42]</a></sup> On October 2 (UTC), tropical storm warnings were hoisted along coastal <a href="/wiki/Camag%C3%BCey_Province" title="Camagüey Province">Camagüey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Las_Tunas_Province" title="Las Tunas Province">Las Tunas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holgu%C3%ADn_Province" title="Holguín Province">Holguín</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo_Province" title="Guantánamo Province">Guantánamo</a> provinces in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 30, <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Virginia" title="Governor of Virginia">Virginian governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Terry_McAuliffe" title="Terry McAuliffe">Terry McAuliffe</a> declared a <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a> for the entire state owing to heavy rains, unrelated to the hurricane, and the threat of Joaquin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">City of Norfolk</a> also declared an emergency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor_of_Connecticut" title="Governor of Connecticut">Connecticut Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Dannel_Malloy" title="Dannel Malloy">Dannel Malloy</a> advised residents to be prepared for potential impacts from the hurricane,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> as did <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_New_York" title="Governor of New York">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Cuomo" title="Andrew Cuomo">Andrew Cuomo</a> of New York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> On October 1, Governors <a href="/wiki/Larry_Hogan" title="Larry Hogan">Larry Hogan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Christie" title="Chris Christie">Chris Christie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pat_McCrory" title="Pat McCrory">Pat McCrory</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nikki_Haley" title="Nikki Haley">Nikki Haley</a> declared a state of emergency for Maryland, New Jersey, North Carolina, and South Carolina respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ocracoke_Island" title="Ocracoke Island">Ocracoke Island</a>, North Carolina, was placed under a mandatory evacuation at 3:00 p.m. EDT (19:00 UTC).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> In New Jersey, the state of emergency caused the annual <a href="/wiki/Bike_MS:_City_to_Shore_Ride" title="Bike MS: City to Shore Ride">Bike MS: City to Shore Ride</a> to be canceled for the first time in its 35-year history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the afternoon of October 2, a tropical storm watch was issued for <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> and a hurricane warning was in effect late on October 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> As a precaution, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Royal_Bermuda_Regiment" title="The Royal Bermuda Regiment">Royal Bermuda Regiment</a> soldiers were placed on standby, and some emergency equipment was stationed on the east end of <a href="/wiki/The_Causeway,_Bermuda" title="The Causeway, Bermuda">the Causeway</a> to prepare for the possibility of the road becoming impassable;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> officials ultimately closed the Causeway late on October 4, near the height of the storm, and partially reopened it the next morning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> By October 3, two cruise ships had canceled their scheduled stops to Bermuda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Most commercial flights to and from the island on October 4 were canceled, and <a href="/wiki/L.F._Wade_International_Airport" title="L.F. Wade International Airport">L.F. Wade International Airport</a> suspended all operations that afternoon. Public and private schools were scheduled to close on October 5, though one institution was prepared for use as an emergency shelter. The approaching hurricane halted ferry and bus services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_and_aftermath">Impact and aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Joaquin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact and aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bahamas">Bahamas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Joaquin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bahamas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Joaquin_2015_Bahamas_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="922" data-file-width="922" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Joaquin_2015_Bahamas_track.png/220px-Joaquin_2015_Bahamas_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Joaquin_2015_Bahamas_track.png/330px-Joaquin_2015_Bahamas_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Joaquin_2015_Bahamas_track.png/440px-Joaquin_2015_Bahamas_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Joaquin_2015_Bahamas_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Enlarged and annotated track of Joaquin from September 29 – October 3 depicting its path through the Bahamas</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Large <a href="/wiki/Swell_(ocean)" title="Swell (ocean)">swells</a> ahead of the storm's arrival in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> washed out a main road on <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador_Island" title="San Salvador Island">San Salvador Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABC1_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-ABC1-48">[48]</a></sup> Widespread power outages affected several islands as the hurricane closed in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> Reports of flooding and people in need of assistance were received from <a href="/wiki/Acklins" title="Acklins">Acklins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crooked_Island,_Bahamas" title="Crooked Island, Bahamas">Crooked Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exuma" title="Exuma">Exuma</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Long_Island,_Bahamas" title="Long Island, Bahamas">Long Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BPMpress_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-BPMpress-49">[49]</a></sup> Power and communication failures plagued the nation's southeastern islands, leaving several islands effectively isolated in the immediate aftermath of Joaquin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> The hurricane took all 59 of <a href="/wiki/BTC_(Bahamas)" title="BTC (Bahamas)">BTC</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cell_site" title="Cell site">cell sites</a> offline, most of them being returned to service within two weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-followup_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-followup-70">[70]</a></sup> By October 21, the <a href="/wiki/Bahamas_Electricity_Corporation" title="Bahamas Electricity Corporation">Bahamas Electricity Corporation</a> had remedied about 80% of its power outages, aided by crews from New Providence and the Caribbean Association of Electric Utilities group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> Early aerial surveys revealed that Acklins, Rum Cay, Crooked Island, and San Salvador Island were "completely devastated".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-updates_72-0"><a href="#cite_note-updates-72">[72]</a></sup> Throughout the archipelago, flooding from the hurricane trapped over 500 residents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TWCBahamas_73-0"><a href="#cite_note-TWCBahamas-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<p>Floodwaters up to 5 ft (1.5 m) deep submerged at least 70% of nearby Crooked Island, where the storm left widespread structural damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema_74-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdema-74">[74]</a></sup> The hurricane "completely destroyed" a Bahamas Electricity Corporation power plant, where two large diesel tanks were shifted off their bases, allowing more than 10,000 gallons of fuel to leak into the ground.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> In the days following the storm, about 100 evacuees—including 46 from Crooked Island—were flown to New Providence, where several of them sought medical attention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> On Long Island and Crooked Island, septic tank seepage contaminated residential wells, leaving residents without clean drinking water. Both areas still had extensive standing water on October 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema2_78-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdema2-78">[78]</a></sup> Acklins endured severe flooding, with many homes inundated and numerous calls for rescue; the island's sea barrier was breached by 9:00 a.m. local time. Some residents reported the entire island to be under water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TribuneOct1_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-TribuneOct1-50">[50]</a></sup> A bridge in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lovely_Bay,_Bahamas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lovely Bay, Bahamas (page does not exist)">Lovely Bay</a> was completely destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema_74-1"><a href="#cite_note-cdema-74">[74]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Joaquin_GOES-13_Oct_1_2015_1900z.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1152" data-file-width="2048" height="141" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Hurricane_Joaquin_GOES-13_Oct_1_2015_1900z.jpg/250px-Hurricane_Joaquin_GOES-13_Oct_1_2015_1900z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Hurricane_Joaquin_GOES-13_Oct_1_2015_1900z.jpg/375px-Hurricane_Joaquin_GOES-13_Oct_1_2015_1900z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Hurricane_Joaquin_GOES-13_Oct_1_2015_1900z.jpg/500px-Hurricane_Joaquin_GOES-13_Oct_1_2015_1900z.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>

Hurricane Joaquin over the Bahamas on October 1</div>

<p>Long Island was subject to an immense 18 ft (5.5 m) <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> that flooded homes with up to 12 ft (3.7 m) of water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema2_78-1"><a href="#cite_note-cdema2-78">[78]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oct15_79-0"><a href="#cite_note-oct15-79">[79]</a></sup> Southern areas of the island suffered considerable devastation; the surge washed out coastal roadways and drove numerous fishing boats ashore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-turner_80-0"><a href="#cite_note-turner-80">[80]</a></sup> The district's <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_the_Bahamas" title="Politics of the Bahamas">Member of Parliament</a>, Loretta Butler-Turner, estimated that 75% of all fishing vessels there were destroyed. This, combined with heavy losses to farms and crops, threatened the livelihoods of many residents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oct15_79-1"><a href="#cite_note-oct15-79">[79]</a></sup> About 20 individuals required rescue on Long Island, while some hurricane shelters became compromised by water entrance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> The bodies of dead animals were seen floating in the water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TribuneDeaths1_82-0"><a href="#cite_note-TribuneDeaths1-82">[82]</a></sup> Strong winds unroofed dozens of homes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TWCBahamas_73-1"><a href="#cite_note-TWCBahamas-73">[73]</a></sup> and many structures were fully destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nemaoct2_83-0"><a href="#cite_note-nemaoct2-83">[83]</a></sup> Northern parts of the island fared better in comparison.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema2_78-2"><a href="#cite_note-cdema2-78">[78]</a></sup> The winds and flooding took a large toll on native vegetation, even well inland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema2_78-3"><a href="#cite_note-cdema2-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<p>Powerful winds brought down trees and utility poles on Rum Cay, clogging roadways.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nemaoct2_83-1"><a href="#cite_note-nemaoct2-83">[83]</a></sup> A number of homes were damaged or destroyed in the district; two grocery stores and a municipal dock also sustained damage. A church housing 32 evacuees became flooded and structurally compromised, forcing the inhabitants to relocate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-acklinsflood_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-acklinsflood-84">[84]</a></sup> Joaquin also damaged power lines in Exuma, where "extreme" flooding was reported. There was modest structural damage on <a href="/wiki/Mayaguana" title="Mayaguana">Mayaguana</a>, the easternmost island of the Bahamas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw_85-0"><a href="#cite_note-rw-85">[85]</a></sup></p>
<p>Joaquin was one of the strongest known hurricanes to impact the Bahamas,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> and directly affected nearly 7,000 people there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw_85-1"><a href="#cite_note-rw-85">[85]</a></sup> Several weeks after the storm, officials estimated that 836 residences had been destroyed, including 413 on Long Island, 227 on San Salvador, 123 on Acklins, 50 on Crooked Island, and 23 on Rum Cay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-followup_70-1"><a href="#cite_note-followup-70">[70]</a></sup> The storm's effects were considered comparable to the destruction wrought by Hurricane Andrew in 1992, which struck the northwestern Bahamas as a Category 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> Initial claims of numerous casualties throughout the island chain proved unsubstantiated,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-updates_72-1"><a href="#cite_note-updates-72">[72]</a></sup> and although one man died during the storm on Long Island, his death was unrelated to the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> In the aftermath of the hurricane, one person was reported missing on Ragged Island, which escaped with relatively minor effects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema_74-2"><a href="#cite_note-cdema-74">[74]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema2_78-4"><a href="#cite_note-cdema2-78">[78]</a></sup> Initial analysis determined that Joaquin resulted in $120.6 million in damage. However, one year later it was revealed that the storm actually caused $200 million in damage across the Bahamian archepeligo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reliefweb_89-0"><a href="#cite_note-reliefweb-89">[89]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCP38_90-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCP38-90">[90]</a></sup> Due to low insurance penetration, insured losses were expected to account for no more than half of that total.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup></p>
<p>Airports in the hardest-hit districts were damaged, flooded, and carpeted in debris,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema_74-3"><a href="#cite_note-cdema-74">[74]</a></sup> forcing storm victims to rely on helicopters, seaplanes, and watercraft to deliver storm relief.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-turner_80-1"><a href="#cite_note-turner-80">[80]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miami_92-0"><a href="#cite_note-miami-92">[92]</a></sup> Workers gradually cleared runways for emergency use in the days following the storm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema_74-4"><a href="#cite_note-cdema-74">[74]</a></sup> and all airports were open for normal operations by October 9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-followup_70-2"><a href="#cite_note-followup-70">[70]</a></sup> By October 4, the Government of <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Agency_for_International_Development" title="United States Agency for International Development">United States Agency for International Development</a> had donated 50 tonnes and just over 32 tonnes, respectively, of emergency supplies to the Bahamas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup> Private groups, local businesses, and <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organization" title="Non-governmental organization">non-governmental organizations</a>, such as the Bahamas <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a>, started donation drives and began distributing goods to storm victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Disaster_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Caribbean Disaster Emergency Management Agency">Caribbean Disaster Emergency Management Agency</a> arranged for three response teams to evaluate the situation on Acklins, Crooked Island and Long Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema_74-5"><a href="#cite_note-cdema-74">[74]</a></sup> BTC established communication centers on Ragged Island, Inagua, and Long Island, giving affected citizens the opportunity to contact family members for free.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup></p>

<p>A special committee was formed to oversee reconstruction efforts, while government officials began considering new laws to enact stricter building codes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60mil_96-0"><a href="#cite_note-60mil-96">[96]</a></sup> The government pledged to help eligible homeowners rebuild and repair their property.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup> On October 6, <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_Bahamas" title="Prime Minister of the Bahamas">Prime Minister of the Bahamas</a> <a href="/wiki/Perry_Christie" title="Perry Christie">Perry Christie</a> signed an order waiving import duties on materials needed for rebuilding by storm victims and registered charities on 12 islands. Local leaders called for an extension of the three-month exemption period, which many saw as inadequate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-98"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup> In what residents feared to be a major economic setback, storm-related damage forced <a href="/wiki/Club_Med" title="Club Med">Club Med</a> to delay the annual opening of its San Salvador resort—the largest employer on the island—by two months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-99"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup> At the end of October, the National Emergency Management Agency began to shift its focus from emergency relief distribution to permanent rebuilding efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sinking_of_El_Faro">Sinking of <i>El Faro</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Joaquin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sinking of El Faro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Joaquin_2015-10-01_1145z.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1600" data-file-width="1600" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Joaquin_2015-10-01_1145z.png/220px-Joaquin_2015-10-01_1145z.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Joaquin_2015-10-01_1145z.png/330px-Joaquin_2015-10-01_1145z.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Joaquin_2015-10-01_1145z.png/440px-Joaquin_2015-10-01_1145z.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Joaquin_2015-10-01_1145z.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Satellite image at 11:45 UTC (7:45 a.m. EDT) on October 1 depicting the approximate final position of the SS <i>El Faro</i> in relation to Hurricane Joaquin</div>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/SS_El_Faro" title="SS El Faro">SS El Faro</a></div>
<p>An American cargo ship—the 791 ft (241 m) <i>El Faro</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-101"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup>—went missing near Crooked Island with 33 crew members (28 Americans and 5 Poles) amid 20 to 30 ft (6.1 to 9.1 m) seas near the hurricane's eyewall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElFaro1_102-0"><a href="#cite_note-ElFaro1-102">[102]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-103"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABCOct4_104-0"><a href="#cite_note-ABCOct4-104">[104]</a></sup> The vessel was last reported to have lost propulsion and begun to <a href="/wiki/Angle_of_list" title="Angle of list">list</a> around 7:30 a.m. local time on October 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElFaro1_102-1"><a href="#cite_note-ElFaro1-102">[102]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MiamiHerald1_105-0"><a href="#cite_note-MiamiHerald1-105">[105]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a> aircraft investigating the storm flew much lower than normal in an unsuccessful effort to locate the stricken ship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ElFaro1_102-2"><a href="#cite_note-ElFaro1-102">[102]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> conducted searches during the day of October 2 without success; efforts resumed at dawn on October 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-106"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup> On October 3, a lifebuoy from <i>El Faro</i> was recovered about 75 mi (121 km) northeast of the ship's last known position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-updates_72-2"><a href="#cite_note-updates-72">[72]</a></sup> Floating containers and "deck objects" were discovered by the Coast Guard on the next morning, and <a href="/wiki/Oil_spill" title="Oil spill">oil sheen</a> was noted in the area, though it was not conclusively linked to the missing vessel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABCOct4_104-1"><a href="#cite_note-ABCOct4-104">[104]</a></sup> A 225 sq mi (580 km<sup>2</sup>) debris field consisting of wood, cargo, styrofoam, and other objects was discovered that afternoon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-107"><a href="#cite_note-107">[107]</a></sup> The joint mission conducted by the Coast Guard, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">Air Force</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">Navy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Air_National_Guard" title="Air National Guard">Air National Guard</a> covered more than 242,000 sq mi (630,000 km<sup>2</sup>) in search of the vessel and its crew. The Coast Guard called off search operations at sunset on October 7, with the ship and her crew presumed lost.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TOTEOct7_108-0"><a href="#cite_note-TOTEOct7-108">[108]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-109"><a href="#cite_note-109">[109]</a></sup> One body, presumed to be from the <i>El Faro</i>, was recovered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BGOct5_110-0"><a href="#cite_note-BGOct5-110">[110]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-111"><a href="#cite_note-111">[111]</a></sup> A Navy salvage team was requested, at the behest of the <a href="/wiki/National_Transportation_Safety_Board" title="National Transportation Safety Board">National Transportation Safety Board</a>, to search for the wreckage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-112"><a href="#cite_note-112">[112]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greater_Antilles">Greater Antilles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Joaquin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Greater Antilles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Though the storm's center remained north of the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">Greater Antilles</a>, some coastal areas experienced rough winds and high seas. In Cuba, station at <a href="/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo_Bay" title="Guantánamo Bay">Guantánamo Bay</a> recorded gusts of 55 mph (89 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16a_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-16a-23">[23]</a></sup> The storm caused coastal flooding and damage to roofs in <a href="/wiki/Granma_Province" title="Granma Province">Granma Province</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-113"><a href="#cite_note-113">[113]</a></sup> more than 100 homes were affected in the province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-114"><a href="#cite_note-114">[114]</a></sup> Rain from the storm somewhat alleviated conditions from <a href="/wiki/2015_Caribbean_drought" title="2015 Caribbean drought">a record drought</a> in Granma, <a href="/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo_Province" title="Guantánamo Province">Guantánamo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Santiago_de_Cuba_Province" title="Santiago de Cuba Province">Santiago de Cuba</a> provinces, though many reservoirs remained below 30 percent capacity in the latter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-115"><a href="#cite_note-115">[115]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-116"><a href="#cite_note-116">[116]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Niquero" title="Niquero">Niquero</a>, Granma, saw 6.2 in (157 mm) of rain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-117"><a href="#cite_note-117">[117]</a></sup></p>
<p>Along the northern coast of Haiti's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tiburon_Peninsula_(Haiti)" title="Tiburon Peninsula (Haiti)">Tiburon Peninsula</a>, high waves from the hurricane capsized a boat with two occupants, killing one of them. Many communes experienced significant coastal flooding from storm tides and active seas, which drove water up to half a kilometer inland. More than 100 homes in <a href="/wiki/Artibonite_(department)" title="Artibonite (department)">Artibonite</a> were inundated, and the main road to <a href="/wiki/Anse-Rouge" title="Anse-Rouge">Anse-Rouge</a> was impassable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema_74-6"><a href="#cite_note-cdema-74">[74]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema4_118-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdema4-118">[118]</a></sup> Strong winds brought down multiple trees in the commune of <a href="/wiki/Grande-Saline,_Artibonite" title="Grande-Saline, Artibonite">Grand-Saline</a>, where severe flooding was also reported. In <a href="/wiki/Nippes" title="Nippes">Nippes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nord-Ouest_(department)" title="Nord-Ouest (department)">Nord-Ouest</a>, four emergency shelters housed nearly 300 individuals. Joaquin damaged banana and <a href="/wiki/Millet" title="Millet">millet</a> fields, killed a small number of livestock, and triggered several landslides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema_74-7"><a href="#cite_note-cdema-74">[74]</a></sup> In the four hardest-hit departments, about 900 households were directly affected by the storm. The Haitian government distributed nearly 500 hygiene kits to 200 families in Nippes, while evacuees in <a href="/wiki/Port-de-Paix" title="Port-de-Paix">Port-de-Paix</a> received mattresses, bed sheets, hygiene kits, food kits, and clean water; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Action_against_Hunger" title="Action against Hunger">Action against Hunger</a> also donated water purification tablets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema4_118-1"><a href="#cite_note-cdema4-118">[118]</a></sup></p>
<p>North of Haiti, the 212 ft (65 m) cargo vessel <i>Minouche</i> began to sink in heavy weather. The U.S. Coast Guard safely rescued all 12 crew members from a life-raft late on October 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-119"><a href="#cite_note-119">[119]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bermuda">Bermuda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Joaquin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Bermuda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>As conditions worsened throughout October 4, roadways on Bermuda became obstructed by debris and floodwaters, and electric crews combated growing power outages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-120"><a href="#cite_note-120">[120]</a></sup> By the next morning, the hurricane had cut power to over 15,000 customers;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-121"><a href="#cite_note-121">[121]</a></sup> service was returned to the vast majority of households by October 8, despite further inclement weather briefly impeding restoration work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-122"><a href="#cite_note-122">[122]</a></sup> Sustained tropical storm-force winds gusted to 72 mph (116 km/h) at the airport,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-123"><a href="#cite_note-123">[123]</a></sup> with gusts as high as 115 mph (185 km/h) at more exposed and elevated points.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-124"><a href="#cite_note-124">[124]</a></sup> The historic Commissioner's House at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bermuda_Maritime_Museum" title="Bermuda Maritime Museum">Bermuda Maritime Museum</a> lost the last of its original roof, which was heavily damaged by hurricanes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fay_(2014)" title="Hurricane Fay (2014)">Fay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Gonzalo" title="Hurricane Gonzalo">Gonzalo</a> of October 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-125"><a href="#cite_note-125">[125]</a></sup> In general, however, property damage across the island was minor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-126"><a href="#cite_note-126">[126]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">Elsewhere</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Joaquin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/October_2015_North_American_storm_complex" title="October 2015 North American storm complex">October 2015 North American storm complex</a></div>
<p>In the Turks and Caicos Islands, heavy rains and storm surge from Joaquin compromised infrastructure, including roadways, docks, and bulkheads. The seawall along Front Street on <a href="/wiki/Grand_Turk_Island" title="Grand Turk Island">Grand Turk Island</a> was damaged, prompting officials to close part of the road. Multiple homes along the coast faced flooding and leaking roofs. In several areas, pounding surf brought about coastal erosion and deposited large volumes of seaweed. Providenciales International Airport remained closed for two days while its storm-damaged weather station awaited replacement. Additionally, the territory lost fruit and vegetable crops to the storm, especially at the government farm on <a href="/wiki/North_Caicos" title="North Caicos">North Caicos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdema3_127-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdema3-127">[127]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although Joaquin ultimately tracked far to the east of the United States, a <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">non-tropical low</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_United_States" title="Southeastern United States">Southeast</a> tapped into the hurricane's moisture. An <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_river" title="Atmospheric river">atmospheric river</a> developed between the two systems, resulting in <a href="/wiki/October_2015_North_American_storm_complex" title="October 2015 North American storm complex">record-shattering rains and flooding</a> across North and South Carolina. Several areas of South Carolina saw accumulations exceeding the threshold for a <a href="/wiki/Return_period" title="Return period">1-in-1,000-year event</a>. The subsequent floods inundated large areas of the state—with areas around <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston</a> and <a href="/wiki/Columbia,_South_Carolina" title="Columbia, South Carolina">Columbia</a> hardest-hit—and killed 19 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-128"><a href="#cite_note-128">[128]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-129"><a href="#cite_note-129">[129]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Joaquin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>
<p>On April 25, 2016, it was announced by the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> that the name <i>Joaquin</i> would be retired due to the severe damage in the Bahamas and sinking of the SS <i>El Faro.</i> It will be replaced with <i>Julian</i> for the 2021 Atlantic hurricane season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-130"><a href="#cite_note-130">[130]</a></sup></p>
