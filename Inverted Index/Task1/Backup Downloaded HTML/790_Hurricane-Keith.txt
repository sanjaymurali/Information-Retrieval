<p><b>Hurricane Keith</b> was an <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane" title="Atlantic hurricane">Atlantic hurricane</a> in October 2000 that caused extensive damage in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>, especially in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a>. It was the fifteenth <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a>, eleventh named storm, and seventh hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2000 Atlantic hurricane season">that year's Atlantic hurricane season</a>. Keith developed as a tropical depression from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> in the western <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> on September 28. The depression gradually strengthened, and became Tropical Storm Keith on the following day. As the storm tracked westward, it continued to intensify and was upgraded to a hurricane on September 30. Shortly thereafter, Keith began to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapidly deepen</a>, and peaked as a Category 4 hurricane less than 24 hours later. Keith then began to meander erratically offshore of Belize, which significantly weakened the storm due to land interaction. By late on October 2, Keith made landfall in <a href="/wiki/Ambergris_Caye" title="Ambergris Caye">Ambergris Caye</a>, Belize as a minimal hurricane. It quickly weakened to a tropical storm, before another landfall occurred near <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">Belize City</a> early on the following day. While moving inland over the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucatán Peninsula">Yucatán Peninsula</a>, Keith weakened further, and was downgraded to a tropical depression before emerging into the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> on October 4. Once in the Gulf of Mexico, Keith began to re-strengthen and was upgraded to a tropical storm later that day, and a hurricane on the following day. By late on October 5, Keith made its third and final landfall near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tampico,_Tamaulipas" title="Tampico, Tamaulipas">Tampico, Tamaulipas</a>, Mexico as a moderately strong Category 1 hurricane. The storm quickly weakened inland and dissipated as a tropical cyclone by 24 hours after landfall.</p>
<p>Keith brought heavy rainfall to several countries in Central America, which resulted in extensive flooding, especially in Belize and Mexico. In <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>, the storm flooded 10 towns, and caused one fatality. Similarly, one fatality also occurred in <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>, and at least 300 people were affected by flooding in that country. Thirteen communities in <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> were completely isolated after Keith made roads impassable. Twelve deaths were reported in Nicaragua, all of which were flood-related. Five people were presumed dead in <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> after an aircraft disappeared near <a href="/wiki/Roat%C3%A1n" title="Roatán">Roatán</a>; one other fatality occurred due to flooding. The storm brought torrential rainfall to Belize, with many areas reporting at least 10 inches (250 mm) of rain, while highest reported amount of precipitation was greater than 30 inches (760 mm). In Belize, a village reported that only 12 houses remained, while elsewhere in the country, at least 60 homes were destroyed or damaged; several houses in <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">Belize City</a> suffered minor roof damage. At least 19 people were killed in Belize and damages totaled to $280 million (2000 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>). Heavy rainfall also occurred in Mexico, especially in the states of <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Nuevo León">Nuevo León</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a>. Flooding from Keith caused several mudslides and a few rivers reached historic levels. Throughout Mexico, at least 460 houses were damaged or destroyed and other losses in infrastructure occurred. In addition, one person drowned due to heavy rainfall. Damages incurred in associated with Keith in Mexico totaled to approximately $365.9 million (2000 MXN, $38.7 million 2000 USD). Overall, Keith was responsible for 68 deaths and $319 million (2000 USD) in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assessed_damage_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-assessed_damage-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Keith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Keith_2000_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Keith_2000_track.png/220px-Keith_2000_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Keith_2000_track.png/330px-Keith_2000_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Keith_2000_track.png/440px-Keith_2000_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>On September 16, 2000 a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> – an elongated area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low air pressure</a> moving from east to west – exited the west coast of Africa. It moved westward across the Atlantic Ocean without <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">development</a> due to the presence of upper-level <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a>. After moving across the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>, a disturbance along the wave started to become better organized on September 27.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> The next day, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a> flight indicated a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#physical_structure" title="Tropical cyclone">closed circulation</a> and winds of 37 mph (60 km/h); upon receiving the data, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) initiated advisories on Tropical Depression Fifteen at 2100 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc1-3">[3]</a></sup> The NHC later estimated the depression had formed three hours earlier, about 70 mi (110 km) north-northeast of <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Gracias_a_Dios" title="Cabo Gracias a Dios">Cabo Gracias a Dios</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> Upon forming, the depression had good <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a>, although the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">convection</a> was not as organized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-disc1-3">[3]</a></sup> With an <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">anticyclone</a> over the system, the depression was expected to gradually intensify while moving slowly to the west-northwest, a motion caused by weak <a href="/wiki/Prevailing_winds" title="Prevailing winds">steering winds</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Late on September 28, the NHC upgraded the depression to tropical storm status and <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named</a> it Keith,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> after another Hurricane Hunters mission reported flight-level winds of 61 mph (98 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc5_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc5-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>By the time Keith became a tropical storm, it was beginning to undergo <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapid deepening</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> fueled by warm waters, low wind shear, and an organizing cloud pattern.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc5_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-disc5-5">[5]</a></sup> There initially was uncertainty in the storm's future movement, due to a <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_prediction_model" title="Tropical cyclone prediction model">Tropical cyclone prediction models</a> differed on their assessments; three models anticipated a turn to the northeast toward Florida due to a trough, while others predicted a continued slow motion to the west-northwest, eventually reaching the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">Bay of Campeche</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After continued strengthening, Keith became a hurricane on September 30, just 18 hours after becoming a tropical storm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> and making it the seventh such storm of that intensity of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> That day, an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> began developing as the structure became much better organized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In a 13‑hour period beginning at 1808 UTC on September 30, Keith underwent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening#Explosive_intensification" title="Rapid deepening">explosive deepening</a>, as its <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">barometric pressure</a> dropped at a rate of nearly 3 mbar (0.089 inHg) per hour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> The NHC noted that Keith experienced conditions "ideal for strengthening", with the exception of the approaching land interaction with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_peninsula" title="Yucatán peninsula">Yucatán peninsula</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The hurricane slowed until stalling offshore eastern <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a>, caused by the ridge to the north and the precursor to <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Leslie_(2000)" title="Tropical Storm Leslie (2000)">Tropical Storm Leslie</a> forming over Cuba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> By early on October 1, Keith had developed a well-defined eye about 20 mi (32 km) in diameter, with what the NHC described as a "spectacular appearance."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Later that day, Hurricane Hunters estimated a minimum pressure of 939 mbar (27.7 inHg) and deployed a <a href="/wiki/Dropsonde" title="Dropsonde">dropsonde</a> that observed peak winds of 176 mph (283 km/h). Based on the data, the NHC estimated that Keith attained peak <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_winds" title="Maximum sustained winds">winds</a> of 140 mph (220 km/h) at 0700 UTC on October 1, while just offshore eastern Belize. This made it a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_4_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 4 Atlantic hurricanes">Category 4</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shortly after Keith reached peak intensity, the eye had moved over portions of Belize, causing it to lose definition. Outflow became restricted, and the developing disturbance to the northeast increased wind shear. As a result, Keith began to weaken steadily on October 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> While remaining nearly stationary, Keith made a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> on <a href="/wiki/Ambergris_Cay" title="Ambergris Cay">Ambergris Cay</a> late on October 2 as a minimal hurricane. Shortly thereafter, Keith weakened into a tropical storm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> and its convection became limited to the southeastern quadrant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> At 0300 UTC, the storm made its second landfall about 29 mi (47 km) north of <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">Belize City</a>, and within nine hours weakened into a tropical depression.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> The building ridge to the north caused the system to accelerate more to the west-northwest across the Yucatán peninsula.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Early on October 4, Keith emerged into the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">Bay of Campeche</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-10"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> where favorable conditions allowed convection to quickly redevelop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Later that day, it re-intensified into a tropical storm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-11"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> with improved outflow and convective organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On October 5, the Hurricane Hunters reported a circular eye had reformed along with winds supporting an upgrade to hurricane status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Keith intensified further that day, until it made its final landfall 23 mi (37 km) north of <a href="/wiki/Tampico" title="Tampico">Tampico</a> with winds of 90 mph (150 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-12"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> It rapidly weakened over the high terrain of northeastern Mexico,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> dissipating late on October 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-13"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Keith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 29, shortly after Keith formed, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane watch</a> was issued for the Yucatán peninsula from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chetumal,_Quintana_Roo" title="Chetumal, Quintana Roo">Chetumal</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Catoche" title="Cabo Catoche">Cabo Catoche</a> in <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">Quintana Roo</a>. The next day as Keith was nearing hurricane intensity, the watch was upgraded to a warning, and a tropical storm warning and hurricane watch was issued for the Belize coastline from <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">Belize City</a> to the Mexican border. At that time, the trajectory was expected to go more to the northwest, and when the storm turned to a slow westward drift, hurricane warnings were issued for the Belize coast with only about 24 hours of lead time for the offshore islands. The warnings for the Yucatán peninsula were dropped as Keith weakened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-14"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 2, the government of Belize declared a state of emergency and activated the entire <a href="/wiki/Belize_Defence_Force" title="Belize Defence Force">Belize Defence Force</a> due to the storm's threat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha4_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocha4-18">[18]</a></sup> The airport in Belize City was closed during Keith's passage, limiting transportation from the country's mainland to its offshore islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Corozal_Town" title="Corozal Town">Corozal, Belize</a>, authorities evacuated the entire city of 10,000 people to numerous shelters in <a href="/wiki/Orange_Walk_Town" title="Orange Walk Town">Orange Walk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrcdocs_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-ifrcdocs-20">[20]</a></sup> Officials were also ordered evacuations for Belize City, setting up shelters in <a href="/wiki/Belmopan" title="Belmopan">Belmopan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Overall, about 25,000 people evacuate, or about 10% of Belize's population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp103_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp103-22">[22]</a></sup> Three hospitals were evacuated in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In a post-storm assessment, residents on the offshore islands acknowledged they were unprepared for the storm and did not anticipate such intensity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Quintana Roo, 5,000 people from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chetumal,_Mexico" title="Chetumal, Mexico">Chetumal</a> and surrounding low-lying areas were forced to evacuate to 30 shelters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp102_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp102-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dr102_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-dr102-26">[26]</a></sup> Offshore along a coral reef, about 50 fishermen stayed on a coral reef during the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp102_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp102-25">[25]</a></sup> In the Bay of Campeche, <a href="/wiki/Pemex" title="Pemex">Pemex</a> evacuated about 6,300 workers from its offshore oil platforms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Officials in Yucatán and Veracruz declared states of emergency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha4_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-ocha4-18">[18]</a></sup> When Keith reached the Gulf of Mexico and began re-intensifying, a hurricane warning was issued from <a href="/wiki/Tuxpan" title="Tuxpan">Tuxpan</a> to <a href="/wiki/Matamoros,_Tamaulipas" title="Matamoros, Tamaulipas">Matamoros</a> in northeastern Mexico, with a tropical storm watch issued northward to <a href="/wiki/Port_Mansfield,_Texas" title="Port Mansfield, Texas">Port Mansfield, Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-15"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> About 3,000 residents in Tamaulipas evacuated to 250 shelters, and in neighboring Veracruz, about 175 people left their houses before the storm struck. The threat of Hurricane Keith caused Mexican authorities to close most ports along the gulf coast,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reut105_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-reut105-28">[28]</a></sup> and many schools in the area were closed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha7_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocha7-29">[29]</a></sup></p>

<p>Keith's slow and erratic movement caused it to produce strong winds and heavy rainfall in Central America. Belize bore the brunt of the storm, with about $280 million in damage and 19 confirmed fatalities, primarily related to flooding. Several other countries reported significant but less severe flooding, including El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, Mexico, and Nicaragua. Overall, Keith was responsible for 40 deaths,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-20"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allenpress_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-allenpress-30">[30]</a></sup> as well as approximately $319 million in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assessed_damage_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-assessed_damage-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Belize">Belize</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Keith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Belize">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>While Keith was offshore from Belize, northerly winds blew water out of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bay_of_Chetumal" title="Bay of Chetumal">Bay of Chetumal</a>. There were reports of people walking onto the temporarily dry bay floor, despite the potential for the waters to return in the event of shifting winds. At <a href="/wiki/Caye_Caulker" title="Caye Caulker">Caye Caulker</a>, the hurricane produced a 4 ft (1.2 m) <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> from the west. Winds were unofficially estimated to have reached 125 mph (201 km/h) in the offshore islands of Belize. Wind gusts on the mainland reached 61 mph (98 km/h) at <a href="/wiki/Philip_S._W._Goldson_International_Airport" title="Philip S. W. Goldson International Airport">Philip S. W. Goldson International Airport</a>. Due to its slow motion, Keith dropped heavy rainfall, amounting to over 10 in (250 mm) in many parts of the country. The highest recorded precipitation total was 32.67 inches (830 mm) at the international airport in Belize City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-21"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The strong winds destroyed 130 houses in Belize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp103_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp103-22">[22]</a></sup> Two hotel roofs were wrecked, and the winds knocked down trees and power lines. Widespread areas of Belize lost power and telephone service, including Belize City. There, the heavy rainfall flooded streets,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dr102_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-dr102-26">[26]</a></sup> reaching 3 ft (0.91 m) deep.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sa_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-sa-33">[33]</a></sup> Twenty homes were damaged to some degree in the capital, while many more suffered minor roof damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrcdocs_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-ifrcdocs-20">[20]</a></sup> The rains flooded the first floor of the primary hospital in the city,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> causing severe damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha6_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocha6-35">[35]</a></sup> Nationwide, 11 health facilities were damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-paho-36">[36]</a></sup> Elsewhere in the country, the rains increased levels along rivers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha4_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-ocha4-18">[18]</a></sup> including the <a href="/wiki/Belize_River" title="Belize River">Belize River</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hondo_River_(Belize)" title="Hondo River (Belize)">Rio Hondo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_River_(Belize)" title="New River (Belize)">New River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc1-37">[37]</a></sup> The Belize River rose for several days after the storm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fact3_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-fact3-38">[38]</a></sup> reaching record levels of 21 ft (6.6 m) in width,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc1-37">[37]</a></sup> which isolated 15 villages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arc_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-arc-39">[39]</a></sup> Flooding covered <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Highway_(Belize)" title="Northern Highway (Belize)">Northern Highway</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Price_Highway" title="George Price Highway">Western Highway</a> as well as the primary bridge from Belmopan to the rest of <a href="/wiki/Cayo_District" title="Cayo District">Cayo District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha5_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocha5-40">[40]</a></sup> Residual floodwaters persisted due to the low-lying land of the country and poor drainage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha1011_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocha1011-41">[41]</a></sup> Floods contaminated water supply in the northern four districts,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho1010_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-paho1010-42">[42]</a></sup> causing an <a href="/wiki/Escherichia_coli" title="Escherichia coli">E. coli</a> outbreak and food poisoning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho1012_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-paho1012-43">[43]</a></sup> In Belize, Corozal, and Orange Walk districts, about 30% of the houses were severely damaged by flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho1010_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-paho1010-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>Damage was heaviest in the northern offshore islands and in the northern portion of the mainland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> On the offshore islands, downed trees and power lines blocked most roads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho1010_42-2"><a href="#cite_note-paho1010-42">[42]</a></sup> In Caye Caulker and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Pedro,_Belize" title="San Pedro, Belize">San Pedro</a>, Keith damaged the roofs of about 90% of houses and damaged or destroyed 676 houses,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reut105_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-reut105-28">[28]</a></sup> leaving 3,279 people homeless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho1010_42-3"><a href="#cite_note-paho1010-42">[42]</a></sup> About 50% of the houses in Caye Caulker were destroyed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha6_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-ocha6-35">[35]</a></sup> with reports of only five dwellings still standing after the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc105_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc105-45">[45]</a></sup> In San Pedro, about 30% of houses,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha6_35-2"><a href="#cite_note-ocha6-35">[35]</a></sup> or at least 50, were wrecked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc105_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc105-45">[45]</a></sup> On Ambergris Caye, four airplanes were destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho1010_42-4"><a href="#cite_note-paho1010-42">[42]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Turneffe_Atoll" title="Turneffe Atoll">Turneffe Atoll</a>, about 35% of the landmass was affected, with many trees in that section uprooted and numerous tree branches snapped.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>Five individuals died in maritime accidents involving at least one <a href="/wiki/Catamaran" title="Catamaran">catamaran</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-22"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> There was an accident involving a bus carrying evacuees, killing four people on board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha6_35-3"><a href="#cite_note-ocha6-35">[35]</a></sup> Three people died on the offshore Ambergris Caye.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw-kriner_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-rw-kriner-47">[47]</a></sup> Overall, 19 people perished in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allenpress_30-2"><a href="#cite_note-allenpress-30">[30]</a></sup> Several injuries, mainly from flying debris, were reported in <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_Town" title="San Pedro Town">San Pedro</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assessed_damage_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-assessed_damage-1">[1]</a></sup> and there were 142 people who were injured in the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho1010_42-5"><a href="#cite_note-paho1010-42">[42]</a></sup> Throughout the country, approximately 3,279 homes were either damaged or destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw-kriner_47-1"><a href="#cite_note-rw-kriner-47">[47]</a></sup> The damage total from Keith was initially estimated at $200 million, about half of which to tourism facilities; the remainder was roughly split between crop and infrastructure damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cdera3_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-cdera3-48">[48]</a></sup> About 75% of the crops in the country were damaged, including much of the corn harvest that was expected to begin in October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw-kriner_47-2"><a href="#cite_note-rw-kriner-47">[47]</a></sup> The damage total was later estimated at $280 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assessed_damage_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-assessed_damage-1">[1]</a></sup> most of it on the offshore Ambergris Cay and Caye Caulker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-23"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>Hurricane Keith caused damage in three <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Mexico" title="Administrative divisions of Mexico">Mexican states</a> – Quintana Roo along the Yucatán peninsula, <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a> where the hurricane made landfall, and Sonora farther inland. In Quintana Roo, Keith left $2.7 million (2000 MXN, $294,000 2000 USD) in damage, and caused an additional $39.7 million (2000 MXN, $4.2 million 2000 USD) in indirect costs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-2"><a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">[32]</a></sup> While crossing the Yucatán peninsula, Keith dropped heavy rainfall, including over 10 in (250 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Campeche" title="Campeche">Campeche</a> state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-rain-49">[49]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chetumal,_Quintana_Roo" title="Chetumal, Quintana Roo">Chetumal, Quintana Roo</a> reported 9.65 in (245 mm) of precipitation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-24"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Keith made its final landfall, Tampico reported tropical storm force winds, with gusts to 63 mph (101 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-25"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> The winds damaged billboards and some trees in the city, and elsewhere along the coast, Keith knocked down trees and power lines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reut105_28-2"><a href="#cite_note-reut105-28">[28]</a></sup> About 200,000 people in northeastern Mexico lost power, and 100,000 people were left without water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> About 400 houses were damaged in Veracruz, and many roads and bridges were destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crs_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-crs-51">[51]</a></sup> Heavy rainfall occurred in the interior of northeastern Mexico, peaking at 24.55 in (624 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Gonz%C3%A1lez_Municipality,_Tamaulipas" title="González Municipality, Tamaulipas">González, Tamaulipas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-26"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> In a reporting station in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B3mez_Far%C3%ADas,_Tamaulipas" title="Gómez Farías, Tamaulipas">Gómez Farías</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pluviometer" title="Pluviometer">pluviometer</a> reported a 24-hour rain total of 13.24 inches (336 mm), and a storm total of 18.44 inches (468 mm), which broke the precipitation record for that location, which counted with 35 years of data.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-3"><a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">[32]</a></sup> This rain also caused rivers to reach record levels, as the <a href="/wiki/Sabinas_River" title="Sabinas River">Sabinas River</a> rose 10.95 m (35.9 ft) on October 6, to a new historical peak, and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Guayalejo_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Guayalejo River (page does not exist)">Guayalejo River</a> rose 77.46 ft (23.61 m), slightly under the 1976 record. This rainfall caused the Las Ánimas dam to catch 525,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acre_foot" title="Acre foot">acre feet</a> (648,000,000 m<sup>3</sup>) of water between October 5 and October 17; however, some of this water had to be released, as the upstream face of the dam was damaged by debris, and had to be repaired. Overall, the storm produced $117.8 million in damage (2000 MXN, $12.4 million 2000 USD) in the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-4"><a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Nuevo León">Nuevo León</a>, the remnants of Keith interacted with a cold front to produce heavy rainfall,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-rain-49">[49]</a></sup> reaching 14.43 in (367 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Sabinas_Hidalgo,_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Sabinas Hidalgo, Nuevo León">Sabinas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-27"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> The average precipitation in the state was 5 inches (130 mm), and the deluge caused causing mudslides in several cities, including San Pedro, Guadalupe and Escobedo. High rainfall also caused <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">flash flood</a> in mountainous areas near <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a>, forming swift river currents that rose up to 9,000 ft³/s (250 m³/s), and dragged 130 people into local rivers; however, all were rescued. The rivers also dragged 30 vehicles, all of which were moderate to total losses. The storm runoff caused the El Cuchillo dam to receive 105,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acre_foot" title="Acre foot">acre feet</a> (130,000,000 m<sup>3</sup>) of water daily, and the La Boca dam to receive 810 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acre_foot" title="Acre foot">acre feet</a> (1,000,000 m<sup>3</sup>) per hour. About 13,000 residents lost power. About 5,000 people were forced evacuated from several municipalities in the state, including 2,465 from <a href="/wiki/Monterrey_(municipality)" title="Monterrey (municipality)">Monterrey</a> and 1,900 from rural areas, at a cost of $400,000 (2000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MXN" title="MXN">MXN</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-5"><a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-6"><a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">[32]</a></sup> The total damages in the state rose to $115.6 million (2000 <a href="/wiki/Mexican_peso" title="Mexican peso">MXN</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>12.2 million in year 2000), with the damage to urban infrastructure being the most expensive portion of the damage, with $44.2 million (2000 MXN, US$4.6 million in 2000) used to repair damage in eight municipalities; in particular, <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_Garza_Garc%C3%ADa" title="San Pedro Garza García">San Pedro Garza García</a> received approximately three quarters of the infrastructure damage, with $30.8 million (2000 MXN, $3.3 million 2000 USD). Additionally, about 460 homes were damaged or destroyed, and 300 families had to be relocated to safer areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-7"><a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<p>In other states, damage was lighter, but still significant. In <a href="/wiki/Sonora" title="Sonora">Sonora</a>, total damages rose to $64.4 million (2000 MXN, $6.8 million 2000 USD); in <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a>, $25.6 million (2000 MXN, $2.7 million 2000 USD) of property damage were caused by the storm. This brings up the total damage in Mexico to $365.9 million (2000 MXN, $38.7 million 2000 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-8"><a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">[32]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Tabasco" title="Tabasco">Tabasco</a>, flooding damaged 7,896 houses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crs_51-1"><a href="#cite_note-crs-51">[51]</a></sup> Approximately 24,000 residents in the state were impacted by flooding. At the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Villahermosa" title="Villahermosa">Villahermosa</a>, large rivers in the area overflowed, flooding some streets. However, dikes and sandbags along the banks of the rivers prevented further flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-El_Diario_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-El_Diario-52">[52]</a></sup> Overall, about 46,000 people had to evacuate Mexico due to the storm and its flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha7_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-ocha7-29">[29]</a></sup> There was one fatality when a man in Tampico was electrocuted by a downed power line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reut105_28-3"><a href="#cite_note-reut105-28">[28]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Xicot%C3%A9ncatl,_Tamaulipas" title="Xicoténcatl, Tamaulipas">Xicoténcatl, Tamaulipas</a>, a swollen river killed a family of six when their house was flooded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Overall, Keith killed 23 people in Mexico – 13 in Veracruz, 4 in <a href="/wiki/Puebla" title="Puebla">Puebla</a>, and 6 in Tamaulipas. There were six indirect deaths when a plane crashed while attempting to land in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reynosa,_Tamaulipas" title="Reynosa, Tamaulipas">Reynosa, Tamaulipas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1010_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp1010-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">Elsewhere</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Keith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The outerbands of Keith brought intermittent rainfall to several areas of Cuba, though the amounts were very light.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha3_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocha3-54">[54]</a></sup> While Keith was organizing, the storm drew moisture from the eastern Pacific Ocean across Central America, producing heavy rainfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> In Guatemala, the rains caused flooding in ten towns and inundated approximately 500 farms in the <a href="/wiki/Melchor_de_Mencos" title="Melchor de Mencos">Melchor de Mencos</a> municipality with about 4 inches (100 mm) of water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-28"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> Additionally, one fatality was reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-56">[56]</a></sup> Governor of <a href="/wiki/Pet%C3%A9n_Department" title="Petén Department">Petén Department</a> Adán Regalado remarked that, "many communities are cut off by flooded rivers".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-El_Diario_52-1"><a href="#cite_note-El_Diario-52">[52]</a></sup> In El Salvador, a 20-year-old man drowned in a river,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-57">[57]</a></sup> and 200 families had to evacuate after several houses were damaged or destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-act102_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-act102-58">[58]</a></sup> Rainfall in Honduras forced over 200 families to evacuate their houses. One person died when a wall collapsed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> and there were five other fatalities an aircraft disappeared near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roatan_Island" title="Roatan Island">Roatan Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-29"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> A bridge along the <a href="/wiki/Pan-American_Highway" title="Pan-American Highway">Pan-American Highway</a> between Honduras and Nicaragua was destroyed, which had been rebuilt following <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Mitch" title="Hurricane Mitch">Hurricane Mitch</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-act102_58-1"><a href="#cite_note-act102-58">[58]</a></sup> Additionally, a government agency reported that as many as 80,000 people in southern Honduras were left isolated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-El_Diario_52-2"><a href="#cite_note-El_Diario-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Nicaragua, floods from the storm forced 3,962 people to evacuate their houses to 57 shelters,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nic105_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-nic105-60">[60]</a></sup> many of which were schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usaid104_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-usaid104-61">[61]</a></sup> Floods affected 11 of the 17 <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Nicaragua" title="Departments of Nicaragua">Departments of Nicaragua</a>, which began receding on October 5 as Keith exited the region. The floods caused several landslides and covered roads, which isolated communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gon105_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-gon105-62">[62]</a></sup> Keith damaged 436 houses in the country and destroyed another 160.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fact3_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-fact3-38">[38]</a></sup> There were 12 fatalities due to the storm in Nicaragua,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-30"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">[2]</a></sup> one of whom a boy who was swept away by a fast moving flooded river northeast of Managua,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_57-1"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-57">[57]</a></sup> while another death occurred after a man drowned in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Managua" title="Lake Managua">Lake Managua</a>. A man in the western portions of the country died after stepping on a power lines, which had fallen due to strong winds. Fifty communities were isolated, with at least thirteen of them due to impassable roads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-paho-36">[36]</a></sup> Several neighborhoods in <a href="/wiki/Corinto,_Nicaragua" title="Corinto, Nicaragua">Corinto</a> flooded, forcing the Civil Defense to evacuate 9 families. One house collapsed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_%C3%81ngeles,_Regi%C3%B3n_Aut%C3%B3noma_del_Atl%C3%A1ntico_Sur" title="Los Ángeles, Región Autónoma del Atlántico Sur">Los Ángeles</a>, though its ten occupant escape without injury. After rainfall lashed In <a href="/wiki/Villanueva,_Chinandega" title="Villanueva, Chinandega">Villanueva, Chinandega</a>, for six days, the resultant flooding forced 300 families evacuated, while there was significant losses the corn, beans and plantains crops. An additional 20 families left their homes due to flooding in <a href="/wiki/Poneloya,_Nicaragua" title="Poneloya, Nicaragua">Poneloya</a>. About 15 families in the port city of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Sandino" title="Puerto Sandino">Puerto Sandino</a> were evacuated after the El Contrabando river overflowed. Another 18 homes were flooded in the region of Salinas Grandes. About 52 fled Troilo after at least 11 homes flooded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Keith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>
<p>On October 3, a Belize ambassador issued an appeal to the international community for food and money due to the damage from Keith.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp103_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-afp103-22">[22]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Development_Programme" title="United Nations Development Programme">United Nations Development Programme</a> received $30,000 from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_Norway" title="Government of Norway">Government of Norway</a> to give immediate relief to the country of Belize. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Children%27s_Fund" title="United Nations Children's Fund">United Nations Children's Fund</a> also gave out $150,000 for relief in areas not focused on by other relief groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-keith_response_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-keith_response-64">[64]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Peace_Corps" title="Peace Corps">Peace Corps</a>, the <a href="/wiki/International_Red_Cross_and_Red_Crescent_Movement" title="International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement">Red Cross</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/Sikorsky_UH-60_Black_Hawk" title="Sikorsky UH-60 Black Hawk">black hawk helicopters</a> worked together to deliver rice, corn, sugar, salt, cooking oil, toilet paper, and medications to about 700 families in Bermuda Landings and adjacent isolated villages. The <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">American Red Cross</a> donated about $28,500 and 4,080 family hygiene kits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw-kriner_47-3"><a href="#cite_note-rw-kriner-47">[47]</a></sup> Between October and November 2000, the National Society distributed 5,289 food and hygiene parcels to 26,293 people in Belize City, Orange Walk, and Belmopan. Around that time, the <a href="/wiki/Belize_Red_Cross_Society" title="Belize Red Cross Society">Belize Red Cross Society</a> (BRCS) distributed relief items to 4,622 victims in Belize City. The BRCS also shipped two containers with kitchen sets and water buckets for 1,600 families, donated by the <a href="/wiki/German_Red_Cross" title="German Red Cross">German Red Cross</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> A donation account to help the relief effort, titled <i>Hurricane Keith Relief Fund</i>, was set up by the town of <a href="/wiki/Placencia" title="Placencia">Placencia</a>, in the local Atlantic Bank. The Belize Consulate in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> also set up a donation fundraiser. Placencia also delivered a boat full of donated food, clothing, and building supplies to the San Pedro and Caye Caulker area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Belize_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-Belize-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the immediate aftermath, officials enacted a <a href="/wiki/Curfew" title="Curfew">curfew</a> for Belize City, Caye Caulker and San Pedro to curtail looting, and the latter two areas were declared disaster areas. The entirety of the northern three <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Belize" title="Districts of Belize">districts</a> (out of six total) were also declared disaster areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha5_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-ocha5-40">[40]</a></sup> There were initial difficulties in determining the needs in the affected residents, due to cut communications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc105_45-2"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc105-45">[45]</a></sup> The government sent planes with emergency supplies to the most affected offshore islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dr104_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-dr104-67">[67]</a></sup> Several emergency teams were sent to Caye Caulker and San Pedro as soon as weather conditions permitted it. Widespread road repairs also began almost immediately after Keith's occurrence. The Belize International Airport was back online by the morning of October 4, only a single day after the tropical cyclone passed through the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Belize_66-1"><a href="#cite_note-Belize-66">[66]</a></sup> By that time, boat service was re-instated with the most affected areas to pick up stranded tourists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dr104_67-1"><a href="#cite_note-dr104-67">[67]</a></sup> Workers quickly restored power and water in Belize City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho1012_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-paho1012-43">[43]</a></sup> In the days after the storm struck, residual flooding sparked fears for the spread of disease.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha6_35-4"><a href="#cite_note-ocha6-35">[35]</a></sup> In Orange Walk and Cayo District, residents were advised to stay indoors to avoid bites from poisonous snakes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc105_45-3"><a href="#cite_note-ifrc105-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Nicaragua, the United States ambassador declared a disaster, which prompted various United Nations departments to provide $25,000 to the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usaid104_61-1"><a href="#cite_note-usaid104-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>The name Keith was retired in the spring of 2001 and will never again be used for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_hurricane" title="North Atlantic hurricane">North Atlantic hurricane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retired_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-retired-68">[68]</a></sup> The name was replaced with Kirk for the <a href="/wiki/2006_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2006 Atlantic hurricane season">2006 Atlantic hurricane season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> However, since the name Kirk was not used during the course of 2006, it was then first used during the <a href="/wiki/2012_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2012 Atlantic hurricane season">2012 Atlantic hurricane season</a>.</p>
