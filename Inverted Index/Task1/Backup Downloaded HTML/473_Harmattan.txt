<p>The <b>Harmattan</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Season" title="Season">season</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West African</a> subcontinent, which occurs between the end of November and the middle of March. It is characterized by dry<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dove_press_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-dove_press-1">[1]</a></sup> and dusty northeasterly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trade_wind" title="Trade wind">trade wind</a>, of the same name, which blows from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" title="Sahara Desert">Sahara Desert</a> over West Africa into the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BHarmattan_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-BHarmattan-2">[2]</a></sup> The name comes from or is related to <a href="/wiki/Ga_language" title="Ga language">Ga</a> <i>haramata</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It is cold<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dove_press_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-dove_press-1">[1]</a></sup> in most places, but can be hot<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> in some places, depending on circumstances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Harmattan blows during the dry season, which occurs during the lowest-sun months, when the <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">subtropical ridge</a> of high pressure stays over the central Sahara Desert and when the low-pressure <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</a> (ITCZ) stays over the Gulf of Guinea. On its passage over the Sahara, it picks up fine dust and sand particles (between 0.5 and 10 microns).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects">Effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harmattan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Harmattan season differs from <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">winter</a>, because it is characterized by cold, dry, dust-laden wind, and also wide ﬂuctuations in the <a href="/wiki/Room_temperature#Ambient_versus_room_temperature" title="Room temperature">ambient temperatures</a> of the day and night. Temperatures can easily be as low as 9 °C (48 °F) all day, but sometimes in the afternoon the temperature can also soar to as high as 30 °C (86 °F), while the <a href="/wiki/Relative_humidity" title="Relative humidity">relative humidity</a> drops under 10%.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ho,_Ghana.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Ho%2C_Ghana.JPG/220px-Ho%2C_Ghana.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Ho%2C_Ghana.JPG/330px-Ho%2C_Ghana.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Ho%2C_Ghana.JPG/440px-Ho%2C_Ghana.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ho,_Ghana.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Harmattan haze over <a href="/wiki/Ho,_Ghana" title="Ho, Ghana">Ho, Ghana</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The air is particularly dry and desiccating when the Harmattan blows over the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dove_press_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-dove_press-1">[1]</a></sup> The Harmattan brings desert-like weather conditions: it lowers the humidity, dissipates cloud cover, prevents rainfall formation and sometimes creates big clouds of dust which can result in <a href="/wiki/Dust_storm" title="Dust storm">dust storms</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sandstorms" title="Sandstorms">sandstorms</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The wind can increase fire risk<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and cause severe crop damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saharan_wind_stirs_cocoa_market_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Saharan_wind_stirs_cocoa_market-7">[7]</a></sup> The interaction of the Harmattan with <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a> winds can cause <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornadoes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BHarmattan_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-BHarmattan-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Harmattan_haze">Harmattan haze</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harmattan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Harmattan haze">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In some countries in West Africa, the heavy amount of dust in the air can severely limit visibility and block the sun for several days,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> comparable to a heavy <a href="/wiki/Fog" title="Fog">fog</a>. This effect is known as the Harmattan haze. It costs airlines millions of dollars in cancelled and diverted flights each year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Re_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Re-10">[10]</a></sup> When the haze is weak, the skies are clear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The dry air can break the trunks of trees growing in the region.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harmattan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Humidity drops to as low as 15%, which can result in spontaneous nosebleeds for some people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saharan_wind_stirs_cocoa_market_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Saharan_wind_stirs_cocoa_market-7">[7]</a></sup> Other health effects on humans may include conditions of the skin, eyes, and respiratory system, including aggravation of <a href="/wiki/Asthma" title="Asthma">asthma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
