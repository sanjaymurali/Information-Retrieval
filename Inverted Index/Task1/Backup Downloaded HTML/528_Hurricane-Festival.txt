<p>The <b>Hurricane Festival</b>, also just <b>Hurricane</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Music_festival#Rock_Music_festivals" title="Music festival">music festival</a> that takes place in <a href="/wiki/Schee%C3%9Fel" title="Scheeßel">Scheeßel</a> near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bremen_(city)" title="Bremen (city)">Bremen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, usually every June. The festival and its guests are generally associated with the alternative part of mainstream music, although the festival has been becoming more and more mainstream over the years.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Southside_Festival" title="Southside Festival">Southside Festival</a>, often referred to as the "sister" of Hurricane, takes place on the same three days in the very south of Germany (contrary to Hurricane, which is in the north). Together they are a twin festival, since they are on the same days and usually have the same lineup with only few exceptions.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1973, the first festival was held in <a href="/wiki/Schee%C3%9Fel" title="Scheeßel">Scheeßel</a>, the place where today's Hurricane takes place. It was called "Es rockt in der Heide" at that time (literally translated: <i>It's rocking in the <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCneburg_Heath" title="Lüneburg Heath">heath</a></i>) and was attended by 52,000 people.</p>
<p>At the "First Rider Open Air" in 1977, fans caused damage of more than a million <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Mark" title="Deutsche Mark">marks</a> when riots broke out, because only five of the announced 23 bands could perform—a huge scandal was uncovered few days later, forcing the organizing management to discontinue their festivals in Scheeßel.</p>
<p>In 1997 <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> returned to Scheeßel in the form of the Hurricane Festival, with numerous bands performing in front of 20,000 visitors. It has taken place on the <a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eichenring" title="de:Eichenring">Eichenring</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle" title="Motorcycle">motorcycle</a> sand racetrack, ever since. Audience size peaked in 2008 with about 75,000 guests; from 2002 to 2006 between 40,000 and 60,000 people came. In 2003, the festival was extended from two to three days. In 2007 60,000 and in 2008 70.000 tickets were sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2004 <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a> suffered from chest pain. He was flown to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asklepios_Klinik_Altona" title="Asklepios Klinik Altona">AK Altona</a> by helicopter after finishing the show. There he was diagnosed with a heart attack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> This has been his final full concert.</p>
