<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Agatha Christie (band)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Agatha Christie</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Агата Кристи</span>) was a Soviet and Russian rock band, one of the most popular in the country in the middle and in the second half of the 1990s. Founded in 1988 in <a href="/wiki/Yekaterinburg" title="Yekaterinburg">Sverdlovsk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.D0.AD.D0.91.D0.9F_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-.D0.AD.D0.91.D0.9F-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Officially, however, the history of the group is counted from the concert February 20, 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.D0.AD.D0.91.D0.9F_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-.D0.AD.D0.91.D0.9F-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the collapse of <i>Agatha Christie</i> in 2010, the former lead singer Gleb Samoylov, together with keyboardist Konstantin Bekrev and drummer Dmitry Khakimov created the group <a class="extiw" href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Matrixx" title="ru:The Matrixx">The Matrixx</a>.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Vladislav_Surkov" title="Vladislav Surkov">Vladislav Surkov</a>, an advisor to Russian President Vladimir Putin, was credited with penning some of the lyrics for Agatha Christie's songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honors_and_awards">Honors and awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agatha_Christie_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Honors and awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>1991: Grand Prix at the Festival of young European bands Open du Rock (<a href="/wiki/Burgundy" title="Burgundy">Burgundy</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>1996, 1997, 2001: <a href="/wiki/Ovation_(award)" title="Ovation (award)">Ovation</a></li>
<li>1997: <a href="/wiki/Golden_Gramophone_Award" title="Golden Gramophone Award">Golden Gramophone Award</a></li>
<li>1998: <a href="/wiki/World_Music_Awards" title="World Music Awards">World Music Awards</a> in the nomination Successfully selling foreign artist (Monte Carlo)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.D0.AD.D0.91.D0.9F_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-.D0.AD.D0.91.D0.9F-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>2004: <a href="/wiki/Komsomolskaya_Pravda" title="Komsomolskaya Pravda">Komsomolskaya Pravda</a>, according to a survey of readers, called <i>Agatha Christie</i> the fourth most influential group in the history of the national rock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
