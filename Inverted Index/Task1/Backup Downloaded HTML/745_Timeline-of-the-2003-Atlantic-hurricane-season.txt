<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Timeline of the 2003 Atlantic hurricane season</h1>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">2003 Atlantic hurricane season</a> was an active Atlantic hurricane season with tropical activity before and after the official bounds of the season – the first such occurrence in 50 years. The season officially began on June 1, 2003 and ended on November 30. However, a pre-season storm, <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Ana_(2003)" title="Tropical Storm Ana (2003)">Tropical Storm Ana</a>, led to the season starting on April 20, and storm activity continued through December 11. The timeline includes information which was not operationally released, meaning that information from post-storm reviews by the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a>, such as information on a storm that was not operationally warned upon, has been included. This timeline documents <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> formations, strengthening, weakening, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfalls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone#Extratropical_transition" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical transitions</a>, as well as dissipations during the season.</p>
<p>This season had 21 tropical depressions, 16 named storms, 7 hurricanes, and 3 <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">major hurricanes</a>, <i>i.e.</i> those that rank as Category 3 or higher on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-_1-0"><a href="#cite_note--1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fabian" title="Hurricane Fabian">Hurricane Fabian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">Hurricane Isabel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Juan" title="Hurricane Juan">Hurricane Juan</a> had their names retired due to the impact caused in <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>, the eastern <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> respectively. Both Fabian and Juan were the worst hurricanes to hit their respective areas, causing $450 million in damages and 16 fatalities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fabian_TCR_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fabian_TCR-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Juan_TCR_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Juan_TCR-3">[3]</a></sup> Hurricane Isabel was the strongest, deadliest, and costliest storm of the season. Isabel peaked as a Category 5 hurricane over the open waters of the Atlantic; however, it weakened to a Category 2 before impacting the east coast of the United States, causing $3.6 billion in damages and 51 fatalities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Isabel_TCR_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Isabel_TCR-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<li>8 p.m. AST (0000 UTC October 13) – Tropical Depression Nineteen forms 1,030 mi (1,660 km) west-southwest of the Cape Verde islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nicholas_TCR_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nicholas_TCR-22">[21]</a></sup></li>

</dd>

<li>2 p.m. AST (1800 UTC) – Tropical Depression Nineteen strengthens into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Nicholas_(2003)" title="Tropical Storm Nicholas (2003)">Tropical Storm Nicholas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nicholas_TCR_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nicholas_TCR-22">[21]</a></sup></li>

<li>2 p.m. AST (1800 UTC) – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Peter_(2003)" title="Tropical Storm Peter (2003)">Subtropical Storm Peter</a> forms 835 mi (1,340 km) south-southwest of the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Peter_TCR_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Peter_TCR-24">[23]</a></sup></li>
