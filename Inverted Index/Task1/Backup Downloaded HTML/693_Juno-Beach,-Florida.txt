<p><b>Juno Beach</b> is a town in <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_County,_Florida" title="Palm Beach County, Florida">Palm Beach County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, United States. The population was 3,262 at the 2000 census. As of 2004, the population recorded by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Census_Bureau" title="U.S. Census Bureau">U.S. Census Bureau</a> is 3,391.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Juno Beach is home to the headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Florida_Power_%26_Light" title="Florida Power &amp; Light">Florida Power &amp; Light</a>. It was also the original <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> for the area that was then known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dade_County,_Florida" title="Dade County, Florida">Dade County, Florida</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juno_Beach,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Juno Beach was <a href="/wiki/Plat" title="Plat">platted</a> in 1948.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The town was named after <a href="/wiki/Juno_(mythology)" title="Juno (mythology)">Juno</a>, the ancient Roman goddess.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Juno_Beach,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Juno Beach is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Juno_Beach,_Florida&amp;params=26_52_24_N_80_3_15_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">26°52′24″N</span> <span class="longitude">80°3′15″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">26.87333°N 80.05417°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">26.87333; -80.05417</span></span></span></a></span> (26.873404, -80.054063).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the town has a total area of 1.9 square miles (4.9 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 1.4 square miles (3.6 km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 0.5 square miles (1.3 km<sup>2</sup>) (25.13%) is water.</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-3">[3]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 3,262 people, 1,791 households, and 929 families residing in the town. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 2,339.2 inhabitants per square mile (906.1/km²). There were 2,603 housing units at an average density of 1,866.6 per square mile (723.0/km²). The racial makeup of the town was 97.82% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a> (of which 95% were non-Hispanic white<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>), 0.43% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.12% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.61% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.06% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.34% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 0.61% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 3.37% of the population.</p>
<p>There were 1,791 households out of which 9.1% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 47.8% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 3.0% had a female householder with no husband present, and 48.1% were non-families. 42.9% of all households were made up of individuals and 25.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 1.80 and the average family size was 2.42.</p>
<p>In the town, the population was spread out with 10.1% under the age of 18, 2.3% from 18 to 24, 16.2% from 25 to 44, 28.8% from 45 to 64, and 42.6% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 60 years. For every 100 females there were 84.9 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 79.7 males.</p>
<p>The median income for a household in the town was $55,263, and the median income for a family was $68,382. Males had a median income of $50,545 versus $36,842 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the town was $50,344. About 3.9% of families and 4.5% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 5.7% of those under age 18 and 2.7% of those age 65 or over.</p>
<p>As of 2000, speakers of <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> as a <a href="/wiki/First_language" title="First language">first language</a> accounted for 96.34% of all residents, while <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> made up 2.01%, <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> was at 1.00%, and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> accounted for 0.63% of the population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
