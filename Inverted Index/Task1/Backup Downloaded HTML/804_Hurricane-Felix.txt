<p><b>Hurricane Felix</b> was the southernmost landfalling Category 5 hurricane in the Atlantic; surpassing <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Edith_(1971)" title="Hurricane Edith (1971)">Hurricane Edith</a> of 1971. It was also the most recent <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane" title="Atlantic hurricane">Atlantic hurricane</a> to make landfall as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Category_5_hurricane" title="Category 5 hurricane">Category 5</a> until <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irma" title="Hurricane Irma">Hurricane Irma</a> in 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was the sixth named <a href="/wiki/Storm" title="Storm">storm</a>, second hurricane, and second <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">Category 5 hurricane</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2007 Atlantic hurricane season">2007 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. Felix formed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> on August 31, passing through the southern <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">Windward Islands</a> on September 1 before strengthening to attain hurricane status. A day later it rapidly strengthened into a major hurricane, and early on September 3 it was upgraded to Category 5 status; by 2100 UTC, the hurricane was downgraded to Category 4 status, but was upgraded to a Category 5 hurricane for the second and final time by the morning of September 4.</p>
<p>On September 4, Felix made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> just south of the border between <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Mosquito_Coast" title="Mosquito Coast">Mosquito Coast</a> region, as a Category 5 hurricane with 160 mph (260 km/h) winds. Hurricane Felix struck Nicaragua on the same day as <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Henriette_(2007)" title="Hurricane Henriette (2007)">Hurricane Henriette</a> struck the <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Peninsula" title="Baja California Peninsula">Baja California Peninsula</a> in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, which marked the second recorded occurrence that a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_hurricane" title="North Atlantic hurricane">North Atlantic hurricane</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Pacific_hurricane" title="Pacific hurricane">Pacific hurricane</a> made landfall on the same day;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> the previous occurrence was on August 23, 1992, when <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">Hurricane Andrew</a> hit the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> on the same day <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Lester_(1992)" title="Hurricane Lester (1992)">Hurricane Lester</a> hit Mexico's Baja California Peninsula. At least 133 deaths are attributed to Felix.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Felix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Felix_2007_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Felix_2007_track.png/220px-Felix_2007_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Felix_2007_track.png/330px-Felix_2007_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Felix_2007_track.png/440px-Felix_2007_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Felix_2007_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>

<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> moved off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> on August 24, tracking westward at approximately 14 mph (23 km/h) with no associated deep <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convection</a> and a weak low-level inverted-V curvature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Felix_TCR_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Felix_TCR-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-824disc_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-824disc-5">[5]</a></sup> Initially difficult to locate on <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">satellites</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-824disc2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-824disc2-6">[6]</a></sup> the wave axis tracked through a moist environment<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-825disc_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-825disc-7">[7]</a></sup> and developed an area of scattered thunderstorms. By August 25 visible satellite imagery indicated broad cyclonic turning just north of the <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-825disc2_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-825disc2-8">[8]</a></sup> The wave continued to develop moderate to strong convection, and on August 27 a 1012 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hPa</a>; 29.88 <a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">inHg</a>) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low pressure area</a> developed about 830 <a href="/wiki/Mile" title="Mile">mi</a> (1,340 <a href="/wiki/Kilometre" title="Kilometre">km</a>) west-southwest of <a href="/wiki/Praia" title="Praia">Praia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-827disc_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-827disc-9">[9]</a></sup> For the next several days the system failed to organize significantly;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-830two_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-830two-10">[10]</a></sup> however, on August 30 the wave became better defined with more representation of the cyclonic turning into the low.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-830twd_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-830twd-11">[11]</a></sup> Convection increased early the next day,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-831two_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-831two-12">[12]</a></sup> and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a> flight into the system reported the presence of a closed low-level circulation; accordingly, the National Hurricane Center initiated advisories on Tropical Depression Six at 2100 UTC on August 31 while it was located about 180 mi (295 km) east-southeast of the southern Windward Islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc1-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Upon becoming a tropical cyclone, the depression was located to the south of a strong <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a>, resulting in a west-northwest motion. The system maintained curved <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Physical_structure" title="Tropical cyclone">rainbands</a> with expanding <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">outflow</a>, and with light <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> and warm water temperatures the environment favored further development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc1_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-disc1-13">[13]</a></sup> Its deep convection consolidated very near the center, and shortly after passing over the island of <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">Grenada</a> the depression intensified into Tropical Storm Felix early at around 0900 UTC on September 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc3_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc3-14">[14]</a></sup> Felix quickly strengthened as it developed a small inner core of convection with tightly-wound bands around the center,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc4_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc4-15">[15]</a></sup> and by late on September 1 an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye feature</a> was observed on satellite imagery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc5_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc5-16">[16]</a></sup> Based on reports from reconnaissance aircraft, the National Hurricane Center estimated that Felix attained hurricane status by early on September 2 while located about 155 mi (250 km) east-northeast of <a href="/wiki/Bonaire" title="Bonaire">Bonaire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa5a_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa5a-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<p>Tracking through an area of extremely low wind shear and increasingly high <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oceanic_heat_content" title="Oceanic heat content">oceanic heat content</a>, Felix quickly developed a well-defined eye, a symmetric cloud structure, and strong upper-level outflow;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc8_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc8-18">[18]</a></sup> consequently, the hurricane commenced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Explosive_intensification" title="Explosive intensification">explosive intensification</a> shortly afterward. Felix attained major hurricane status at around 1800 UTC on September 2 while located about 490 mi (790 km) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Kingston,_Jamaica" title="Kingston, Jamaica">Kingston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Rapid strengthening continued, and later that day the hurricane attained Category 4 status on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale with the pressure dropping to 957 mbar (hPa; 28.26 inHg); this corresponded to a falling rate of 3.4 mbar (hPa; 0.1 inHg) per hour, which the National Hurricane Center described as "one of the more rapid deepening rates we have observed." A Hurricane Hunters flight reported a <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)#Stadium_effect" title="Eye (cyclone)">stadium effect</a> in the eye, and also reported the eye diameter shrank to 14 mi (22 km).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc9_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc9-20">[20]</a></sup> A subsequent Hurricane Hunters flight experienced flight level winds of 175 mph (280 km/h), with peak surface winds of 163 mph (263 km/h) reported in the southwest quadrant of the eyewall; surface winds of 189 mph (306 km/h) were found in its northeastern quadrant, although the National Hurricane Center reported the peak reading could have been contaminated due to <a href="/wiki/Graupel" title="Graupel">graupel</a> in the clouds. Based on the observations, it is estimated Felix attained winds of 175 mph (280 km/h) by 0000 UTC on September 3 while located about 390 mi (625 km) southeast of Kingston, Jamaica, making Felix a Category 5 hurricane on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale. While investigating the hurricane, Hurricane Hunters encountered extreme turbulence and potentially dangerous graupel, which forced the flight to abort the mission.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc10_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc10-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Continuing quickly westward, the diameter of the eye decreased to 12 mi (19 km),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc12_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc12-22">[22]</a></sup> and the National Hurricane Center estimates the hurricane attained a minimum central pressure of 929 mbar (hPa; 27.43 inHg) about seven hours after it reached Category 5 status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Felix_TCR_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Felix_TCR-4">[4]</a></sup> Initially, Felix was predicted to track west-northwestward, striking <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a> before crossing the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucatán Peninsula">Yucatán Peninsula</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc12_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-disc12-22">[22]</a></sup> however, its path remained nearly due westward. Subsequently, cloud tops near the center started to warm as the eye became less distinct,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc13_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc13-23">[23]</a></sup> and by 1800 UTC on September 3, Felix weakened to Category 4 status with winds of 145 mph (235 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa13a_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa13a-24">[24]</a></sup> On September 4, Felix had completed an <a href="/wiki/Eyewall_replacement_cycle" title="Eyewall replacement cycle">eyewall replacement cycle</a> and began strengthening again during the day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Felix was upgraded to a Category 5 for the second time at 1040 UTC,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and made landfall in extreme north-eastern Nicaragua shortly thereafter with winds of 160 mph (260 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> By nine hours after moving ashore, the system maintained a well-organized cloud pattern and intense rainbands, although the winds rapidly decreased;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc18_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc18-28">[28]</a></sup> early on September 5 Felix weakened to a tropical storm as its upper-level circulation became detached from the low-level portion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc19_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc19-29">[29]</a></sup> The system deteriorated to a tropical depression as it crossed into southern Honduras, and at 0900 UTC the National Hurricane Center issued the last advisory on Felix as it began degenerating into a broad low pressure area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc20_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-disc20-30">[30]</a></sup> The remnants of Felix tracked west-northwestward over other portions of <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>, and eventually dissipated over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Political_divisions_of_Mexico" title="Political divisions of Mexico">Mexican state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tabasco" title="Tabasco">Tabasco</a> on September 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Felix_TCR_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Felix_TCR-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Felix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Upon Felix's becoming a tropical cyclone, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical storm warning</a> was issued for <a href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines" title="Saint Vincent and the Grenadines">Saint Vincent and the Grenadines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tobago" title="Tobago">Tobago</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">Grenada</a> and its dependencies; a tropical storm watch was also issued for the northeastern <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> coast from <a href="/wiki/Cuman%C3%A1" title="Cumaná">Cumaná</a> to <a href="/wiki/Pedernales,_Delta_Amacuro" title="Pedernales, Delta Amacuro">Pedernales</a>, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isla_Margarita" title="Isla Margarita">Isla Margarita</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/ABC_islands_(Lesser_Antilles)" title="ABC islands (Lesser Antilles)">ABC islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa1_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa1-31">[31]</a></sup> The National Emergency Management Agency of <a href="/wiki/Tobago" title="Tobago">Tobago</a> was fully activated, with 79 shelters opened on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trin91_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-trin91-32">[32]</a></sup> Coinciding with the upgrade of Felix to tropical storm status, the watch for the ABC islands was upgraded to a tropical storm warning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa3_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa3-33">[33]</a></sup> A hurricane watch was added to the islands early on September 2,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa6_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa6-34">[34]</a></sup> where several tourists attempted to leave by plane prior to the arrival of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap91_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap91-35">[35]</a></sup> Early on September 2, the government of Jamaica issued a tropical storm watch for the island,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa5a_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-pa5a-17">[17]</a></sup> which was canceled as the hurricane passed far to its south.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa13a_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-pa13a-24">[24]</a></sup> Later on September 2, a tropical storm watch was issued for <a href="/wiki/Grand_Cayman" title="Grand Cayman">Grand Cayman</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa8_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa8-36">[36]</a></sup> and the next day a tropical storm warning was put in place on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Providencia_Island" title="Providencia Island">Providencia Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> A hurricane watch was issued for the Caribbean coast of Guatemala and the entire coastline of <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a> at 1200 UTC on September 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa12a_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa12a-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>At 0300 UTC on September 3, the government of Honduras issued a hurricane watch from <a href="/wiki/Lim%C3%B3n,_Honduras" title="Limón, Honduras">Limón</a> to the Honduras/Nicaragua border,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa11_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa11-39">[39]</a></sup> which was upgraded to a hurricane warning six hours later. At the same time, a hurricane watch was extended westward to the Honduras/<a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> border,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa12_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa12-40">[40]</a></sup> and early on September 4 a tropical storm warning was put in place for the same region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa15_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa15-41">[41]</a></sup> Officials ordered evacuations for citizens in low-lying areas near the coastline; by midday on September 3, around 300 tourists evacuated from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bay_Islands_(department)" title="Bay Islands (department)">Bay Islands</a> department, with an additional 400 preparing to leave by plane;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap93_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap93-42">[42]</a></sup> approximately 2,000 people were evacuated from coastal areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap93a_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap93a-43">[43]</a></sup> By the time the hurricane made landfall, about 20,000 people in Honduras left for safer areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp96_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp96-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>At 1500 UTC on September 3, about 21 hours before its final landfall, the government of Nicaragua issued a hurricane warning from <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Cabezas" title="Puerto Cabezas">Puerto Cabezas</a> northward to the national border.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa13_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa13-45">[45]</a></sup> About 12 hours before landfall, a tropical storm warning was issued from Puerto Cabezas southward to <a href="/wiki/Prinzapolka" title="Prinzapolka">Prinzapolka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa15_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-pa15-41">[41]</a></sup> Prior to the arrival of the hurricane, officials sent about 140,000 <a href="/wiki/Pound_(mass)" title="Pound (mass)">lbs</a> (63,000 <a href="/wiki/Kilogram" title="Kilogram">kg</a>) of meals and emergency supplies to near the landfall location.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap95_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap95-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Felix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Caribbean_Islands">Caribbean Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Felix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Caribbean Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">

Felix passing <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a></div>

<p>At around 1200 UTC on September 1 a wind gust of 46 mph (74 km/h) was recorded on <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>, and around the same time a gust of 44 mph (71 km/h) was observed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_(island)" title="Saint Vincent (island)">Saint Vincent</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa3a_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa3a-47">[47]</a></sup> The storm produced heavy rainfall across the Windward Islands. On <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">Trinidad</a>, heavy precipitation caused mudslides and overflown rivers which destroyed some bridges; moderate winds damaged several buildings on the island. Damage on neighboring <a href="/wiki/Tobago" title="Tobago">Tobago</a> was concentrated on its northern portion, where several mudslides resulted from the rainfall;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbc91_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbc91-48">[48]</a></sup> monetary damage on Tobago was estimated at $250,000 (2007 <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_dollar" title="Trinidad and Tobago dollar">TTD</a>; $40,000 2007 USD, $46.2 thousand 2017 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Felix produced gusty winds on <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">Grenada</a>, which downed several power lines and destroyed the roofs of two houses; rough waves also broke several vessels from their anchors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap91_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap91-35">[35]</a></sup> In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lucia" title="St. Lucia">St. Lucia</a>, winds from the storm damaged the roof of a store in <a href="/wiki/Castries" title="Castries">Castries</a>, which collapsed and destroyed 12 vehicles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbc91_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-cbc91-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/ABC_islands_(Lesser_Antilles)" title="ABC islands (Lesser Antilles)">ABC islands</a>, the hurricane resulted in gusty winds and heavy rainfall. However, little damage was reported in <a href="/wiki/Bonaire" title="Bonaire">Bonaire</a>; the precipitation left several homes submerged under water in <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a>, the winds damaged one house and briefly left a northern town without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap93_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap93-42">[42]</a></sup> Hurricane Felix produced strong winds and 10 ft (3 m) waves along the north coast of <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, which left one person missing in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Cabello" title="Puerto Cabello">Puerto Cabello</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp93_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp93-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Central_America">Central America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Felix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Central America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Felix_04_sept_2007_1610Z.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="7800" data-file-width="6000" height="286" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Felix_04_sept_2007_1610Z.jpg/220px-Felix_04_sept_2007_1610Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Felix_04_sept_2007_1610Z.jpg/330px-Felix_04_sept_2007_1610Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Felix_04_sept_2007_1610Z.jpg/440px-Felix_04_sept_2007_1610Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Felix_04_sept_2007_1610Z.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Hurricane Felix after its landfall in <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> on September 4</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Early reports suggest severe damage in Honduras and Nicaragua after Felix made landfall as a Category 5 hurricane. In Puerto Cabezas, nearly every structure sustained at least roof damage, and many buildings were destroyed. Along the Mosquito Coast, flooding and mudslides were reported, destroying many houses and blocking highways. The government of Nicaragua declared the northern Caribbean coast a <a href="/wiki/Disaster_area" title="Disaster area">disaster area</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes1_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-latimes1-51">[51]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Miskito_Cays" title="Miskito Cays">Miskito Cays</a>, located about 43 mi (69 km) from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bilwi" title="Bilwi">Bilwi</a> on the northeast coast of Nicaragua, was among the strongest hit areas. Hurricane Felix had not yet made landfall but reached maximum force when it passed over the Miskito Cays. The winds of the hurricane, with speeds of up to 160 mph (260 km/h), destroyed the Cays completely. <a href="/wiki/Column" title="Column">Pillars</a> that previously formed the base of the houses were the only remains on the Cays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>At least 133 people were reported dead. At least 130 of them were in Nicaragua.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> While few details have been disclosed, they include at least 25 dead Miskito fishermen swept away, a drowning death on a boat, impact from a fallen tree and at least one indirect death caused by medical complications after birth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes1_51-1"><a href="#cite_note-latimes1-51">[51]</a></sup> There were three deaths reported in Honduras, one of which was caused by a motor vehicle accident caused by heavy rain and landslides, and two caused by flooding in the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Tegucigalpa" title="Tegucigalpa">Tegucigalpa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-physorg1_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-physorg1-54">[54]</a></sup> However, hundreds of others were missing (mostly at sea), and communication was difficult to impossible in many areas. Some survivors were also found on the Mosquito Coast who were initially reported missing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to official information, at least 40,000 people were affected and 9,000 houses destroyed, most of them in the Nicaraguan city of Bilwi (Puerto Cabezas), where a "State of Disaster" was decreed by the government. A total lack of supplies and services were also reported in the area. The response was almost immediate. Help from Venezuela, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, and Honduras was received, and a lot of organizations, such as the Nicaraguan Red Cross, the media and universities organized collections all over the country. Nicaraguan <a href="/wiki/President_of_Nicaragua" title="President of Nicaragua">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ortega" title="Daniel Ortega">Daniel Ortega</a> visited Bilwi on the day after the catastrophe and promised the reconstruction of houses to the people affected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Damage in Nicaragua totaled 13.4 billion (2007 <a href="/wiki/Nicaraguan_c%C3%B3rdoba" title="Nicaraguan córdoba">NIO</a>; $716.3 million 2007 USD, $827 million 2017 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>Inland flooding was also reported in Honduras, particularly near Tegucigalpa and in the northwestern regions where the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ulua_River" title="Ulua River">Ulua River</a> and the Chamelecon River overflowed into an agricultural area. Coastal flooding also damages the town of Izabal in Guatemala where 850 people were evacuated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-physorg1_54-1"><a href="#cite_note-physorg1-54">[54]</a></sup> Crop damage in Honduras amounted to L68.28 million (2007 <a href="/wiki/Honduran_lempira" title="Honduran lempira">HNL</a>; $3.64 million 2007 USD, $4.2 million 2017 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Felix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Felix, over 160,000 people were suffering as many communities were stranded due to the storm. An appeal was given out by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> for over $48 million (2007 USD, $55.4 million 2017 USD) in emergency aid to the hard-hit areas, particularly in Nicaragua.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> The appeal United States federal government delivered over $1.17 million (2007 USD, $1.35 million 2017 USD) in assistance,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> responded with a donation of <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>1 million euros ($1.36 million 2007 USD, $1.57 million 2017 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Agency_for_International_Development" title="United States Agency for International Development">United States Agency for International Development</a> (USAID) and the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Foreign_Disaster_Assistance" title="Office of Foreign Disaster Assistance">Office of Foreign Disaster Assistance</a> (OFDA) airlifted 120 rolls of plastic sheeting to the severely affected areas, which was used to repair the damaged roofs of health clinics and schools. USAID and OFDA also distributed 12,000 ten-liter water containers to Puerto Cabezas. Emergency relief supplies from both USAID and OFDA totaled to $94,190 (2007 USD, $109 thousand 2017 USD). In the aftermath of both Hurricane Dean and Felix, combined, USAID and OFDA donated $25,000 (2007 USD, $28.9 thousand 2017 USD) in aid to Honduras, and $1.2 million (2007 USD, $1.39 million 2017 USD) to Nicaragua. In addition, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Defense" title="United States Department of Defense">United States Department of Defense</a> (USDOD) gave $1.5 million (2007 USD, $1.73 million 2017 USD) in <a href="/wiki/Humanitarian_aid" title="Humanitarian aid">humanitarian assistance</a> to Nicaragua. In collaboration with USAID and OFDA, the USDOD delivered near 27.55 <a href="/wiki/Ton" title="Ton">tons</a> (25 <a href="/wiki/Tonne" title="Tonne">metric tons</a>) of food from the United Nation's World Food Program to the <a href="/wiki/North_Caribbean_Coast_Autonomous_Region" title="North Caribbean Coast Autonomous Region">North Caribbean Coast Autonomous Region</a>, Nicaragua.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-factsheet_.238_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-factsheet_.238-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Felix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>
<p>Due to the extensive damage and high death toll from Felix in Nicaragua, the name was officially retired on May 13, 2008 by the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> during its annual meeting in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando, Florida</a>, and will never be used again for any future Atlantic hurricanes. It was replaced by <i>Fernand</i> on List V of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_naming_lists" title="Atlantic hurricane naming lists">Atlantic hurricane naming lists</a>, which was used in the 2013 Atlantic hurricane season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
