<p>"<b>One If by Clam, Two If by Sea</b>" is the fourth episode of the <a href="/wiki/Family_Guy_(season_3)" title="Family Guy (season 3)">third season</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Animated_cartoon" title="Animated cartoon">animated</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_comedy" title="Television comedy">comedy series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Guy" title="Family Guy">Family Guy</a></i>, another episode produced for Season 2. It originally aired on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> on August 1, 2001. The plot features The Drunken Clam, a tavern, being nearly destroyed by a hurricane, and then bought by a group of wealthy <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">Englishmen</a> who turn the bar into a dignified establishment. <a href="/wiki/Peter_Griffin" title="Peter Griffin">Peter</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joe_Swanson" title="Joe Swanson">Joe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Brown" title="Cleveland Brown">Cleveland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Quagmire" title="Glenn Quagmire">Quagmire</a> then attempt to bring back the Drunken Clam, and send the new owner back to the United Kingdom.</p>
<p>The episode was written by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Bernstein" title="Jim Bernstein">Jim Bernstein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Shipley_(screenwriter)" title="Michael Shipley (screenwriter)">Michael Shipley</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Dan_Povenmire" title="Dan Povenmire">Dan Povenmire</a>. The episode featured guest performances by <a href="/wiki/Ed_Asner" title="Ed Asner">Ed Asner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tara_Strong" title="Tara Strong">Tara Strong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Laurie" title="Hugh Laurie">Hugh Laurie</a> and Alan Shearman, along with several recurring guest voice actors for the series.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=One_If_by_Clam,_Two_If_by_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A hurricane strikes Quahog. Though The Drunken Clam was undamaged, the bar's owner Horace sells it and leaves for Florida. A <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a> man named Nigel Pinchley turns it into a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stereotypical" title="Stereotypical">stereotypically</a> <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a> pub. Upset over the loss of their favorite bar and failing to find another one, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Griffin" title="Peter Griffin">Peter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Quagmire" title="Glenn Quagmire">Quagmire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Brown" title="Cleveland Brown">Cleveland</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joe_Swanson" title="Joe Swanson">Joe</a> try to start their own American revolution at the pub by harassing its staff and patrons. However, the British convince them to leave. Peter and his friends storm a British ship, throwing out its beer like the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Tea_Party" title="Boston Tea Party">Boston Tea Party</a>.</p>
<p>That night, the pub mysteriously burns down and Peter, Quagmire, Cleveland, and Joe are thrown in jail due to an anonymous tip. Steve Bellows, a criminal Joe arrested, plans to kill him and the others at midnight on Saturday. <a href="/wiki/Lois_Griffin" title="Lois Griffin">Lois</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Brown#Loretta_Brown" title="Cleveland Brown">Loretta</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Family_Guy#Bonnie_Swanson" title="List of characters in Family Guy">Bonnie</a> are unable to believe their husbands and Quagmire would burn down the pub. They subsequently discover that Nigel had taken out a large insurance policy the day before the pub burned down and immediately become suspicious. Knowing Nigel is strongly attracted to her, Lois plans to trick Nigel into confessing. Although it was not witnessed by Bonnie and Loretta, Nigel's insurance agent was in his closet and overheard everything.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Stewie_Griffin" title="Stewie Griffin">Stewie</a> tries to teach Eliza, Nigel's daughter, to overcome her "common" <a href="/wiki/Cockney" title="Cockney">Cockney</a> accent and speak "proper" <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. He bets <a href="/wiki/Brian_Griffin" title="Brian Griffin">Brian</a> that she will be a proper lady at her birthday party. After several sessions, Stewie manages to teach Eliza how to speak "properly". In the party, Eliza does, until she <a href="/wiki/Urinary_incontinence" title="Urinary incontinence">wets herself</a> in front of everybody, slipping back to her Cockney accent and making Stewie lose the bet.</p>
<p>On the night Peter, Joe, Quagmire, and Cleveland are supposed to be killed by Steve, they are freed by Lois, Bonnie and Loretta before he could arrive. The men and their wives celebrate their success at The Drunken Clam, which Horace has bought back and returned to normal. Lois states that she hopes that Nigel is punished, and he is hanged at the <a href="/wiki/Tower_of_London" title="Tower of London">Tower of London</a> while Eliza gets sent to an orphanage. She sends Stewie a letter threatening to kill Lois if she gets out.</p>

<p>The episode was written by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Bernstein" title="Jim Bernstein">Jim Bernstein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Shipley_(screenwriter)" title="Michael Shipley (screenwriter)">Michael Shipley</a>, and directed by series regular <a href="/wiki/Dan_Povenmire" title="Dan Povenmire">Dan Povenmire</a> before the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/Family_Guy_(season_3)" title="Family Guy (season 3)">third production season</a>.</p>
<p>In addition to the regular cast, actor <a href="/wiki/Ed_Asner" title="Ed Asner">Ed Asner</a>, actress <a href="/wiki/Tara_Strong" title="Tara Strong">Tara Strong</a>, actor <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Laurie" title="Hugh Laurie">Hugh Laurie</a> and actor Alan Shearman guest starred in the episode. Recurring guest voice actress <a href="/wiki/Lori_Alan" title="Lori Alan">Lori Alan</a>, voice actor <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Brennan" title="Johnny Brennan">Johnny Brennan</a>, writer <a href="/wiki/Danny_Smith_(writer)" title="Danny Smith (writer)">Danny Smith</a> and actress <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Tilly" title="Jennifer Tilly">Jennifer Tilly</a> also made minor appearances.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=One_If_by_Clam,_Two_If_by_Sea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cultural references">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stewie's giving lessons to Eliza to combat her Cockney accent is a direct reference to the musical and film <i><a href="/wiki/My_Fair_Lady" title="My Fair Lady">My Fair Lady</a></i>, in which the girl in question is also named <a href="/wiki/Eliza_Doolittle" title="Eliza Doolittle">Eliza</a>. Also, <a href="/wiki/Seth_MacFarlane" title="Seth MacFarlane">Seth MacFarlane</a> based Stewie's voice on that of <a href="/wiki/Rex_Harrison" title="Rex Harrison">Rex Harrison</a> in the musical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foxnews_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-foxnews-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> A hypothetical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arthouse_film" title="Arthouse film">arthouse film</a>, <i>I Remember Cecil</i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Schwarzenegger" title="Arnold Schwarzenegger">Arnold Schwarzenegger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sylvester_Stallone" title="Sylvester Stallone">Sylvester Stallone</a>, depicts what would happen if there were no more action films. A cutaway to Peter's only defeat shows him as the "green guy" within the film <i><a href="/wiki/Tron" title="Tron">Tron</a></i>. While in jail, Peter, Quagmire, Joe and Cleveland perform a <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_(musical)" title="Chicago (musical)">Chicago</a></i>-style dance number. <a href="/wiki/Demond_Wilson" title="Demond Wilson">Demond Wilson</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Sanford_and_Son" title="Sanford and Son">Sanford and Son</a></i> surprises Lois when she thinks nobody is around. Stewie calls Brian "<a href="/wiki/Dogbert" title="Dogbert">Dogbert</a>" in reference to the comic series <i><a href="/wiki/Dilbert" title="Dilbert">Dilbert</a></i>.</p>
