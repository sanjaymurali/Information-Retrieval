<p><b>Hampton</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Independent_city_(United_States)" title="Independent city (United States)">independent city</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_(U.S._state)" title="Commonwealth (U.S. state)">Commonwealth</a> of <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>. As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>, the population was 137,436.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-QF_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-QF-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>As one of the seven major cities that compose the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads" title="Hampton Roads">Hampton Roads</a> metropolitan area, it is on the southeastern end of the <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Peninsula" title="Virginia Peninsula">Virginia Peninsula</a>.</p>
<p>Hampton traces its history to the city's <a href="/wiki/Old_Point_Comfort" title="Old Point Comfort">Old Point Comfort</a>, the home of <a href="/wiki/Fort_Monroe" title="Fort Monroe">Fort Monroe</a> for almost 400 years, which was named by the 1607 voyagers, led by Captain <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Newport" title="Christopher Newport">Christopher Newport</a>, who first established <a href="/wiki/Jamestown,_Virginia" title="Jamestown, Virginia">Jamestown</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">English</a> colonial settlement. Since consolidation in 1952, Hampton has included the former <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_City_County,_Virginia" title="Elizabeth City County, Virginia">Elizabeth City County</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Incorporated_town" title="Incorporated town">incorporated town</a> of <a href="/wiki/Phoebus,_Virginia" title="Phoebus, Virginia">Phoebus</a>, consolidating by mutual agreement.</p>
<p>After the end of the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, historic <a href="/wiki/Hampton_University" title="Hampton University">Hampton University</a> was established opposite from the town on the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_River" title="Hampton River">Hampton River</a>, providing an education for many newly-freed former slaves and for area Native Americans. In the 20th century, the area became the location of <a href="/wiki/Langley_Air_Force_Base" title="Langley Air Force Base">Langley Air Force Base</a>, <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> <a href="/wiki/Langley_Research_Center" title="Langley Research Center">Langley Research Center</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Air_and_Space_Center" title="Virginia Air and Space Center">Virginia Air and Space Center</a>. Hampton features many miles of waterfront and beaches.</p>
<p>For residents and visitors alike, the city features a wide array of business and industrial enterprises, retail and residential areas, and historical sites. Most recently, the new <a href="/wiki/Peninsula_Town_Center" title="Peninsula Town Center">Peninsula Town Center</a> development opened in May 2010 on the site of the former Coliseum Mall. Located in the area adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Coliseum" title="Hampton Coliseum">Hampton Coliseum</a> and the Convention Center, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_urbanism" title="New urbanism">new urbanism</a>-type project features a wide mix of retail stores, housing and other attractions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Development of new residential development and additional public facilities (such as the new fishing pier recently opened) are underway at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buckroe_Beach" title="Buckroe Beach">Buckroe Beach</a>, long a noted resort area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Located on the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads_Beltway" title="Hampton Roads Beltway">Hampton Roads Beltway</a>, it hosts the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads_Bridge-Tunnel" title="Hampton Roads Bridge-Tunnel">Hampton Roads Bridge-Tunnel</a> (HRBT) on <a href="/wiki/Interstate_64_in_Virginia" title="Interstate 64 in Virginia">Interstate 64</a>. First opened in 1957, it was the world's first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bridge-tunnel" title="Bridge-tunnel">bridge-tunnel</a>, crossing the mouth of the Hampton Roads harbor, which serves as the gateway to the <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">Chesapeake Bay</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> from the eastern United States' largest ice-free harbor and its tributary rivers. Expanded in the 1970s, the HRBT remains Virginia's busiest and deepest such facility.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_Hampton,_Virginia" title="Timeline of Hampton, Virginia">Timeline of Hampton, Virginia</a></div>
<p>In December 1606, three ships carrying men and boys left England on a mission sponsored by a proprietary company. Headed by Captain <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Newport" title="Christopher Newport">Christopher Newport</a>, they sailed across the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> to North America. After a long voyage, they first landed at the entrance to the <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">Chesapeake Bay</a> on the south shore at a place they named <a href="/wiki/Cape_Henry" title="Cape Henry">Cape Henry</a> (for <a href="/wiki/Henry_Frederick,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Henry Frederick, Prince of Wales">Henry Frederick, Prince of Wales</a>, the elder son of their king).</p>
<p>During the first few days of exploration, they identified the site of <a href="/wiki/Old_Point_Comfort" title="Old Point Comfort">Old Point Comfort</a> (which they originally named "Point Comfort") as a strategic defensive location at the entrance to the body of water that became known as <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads" title="Hampton Roads">Hampton Roads</a>. This is formed by the <a href="/wiki/Confluence" title="Confluence">confluence</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_River_(Virginia)" title="Elizabeth River (Virginia)">Elizabeth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nansemond_River" title="Nansemond River">Nansemond</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_River_(Virginia)" title="James River (Virginia)">James</a> rivers. The latter is the longest river in Virginia.</p>
<p>Weeks later, on May 14, 1607, they established the first permanent English settlement in the present-day United States about 25 miles (40 km) further inland from the Bay which became the site of fortifications during the following 200 years.</p>
<p>Slightly south, near the entrance to <a href="/wiki/Hampton_River" title="Hampton River">Hampton River</a>, the colonists seized the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_Americans_of_the_United_States" title="Native Americans of the United States">Native American</a> community of <a href="/wiki/Kecoughtan,_Virginia" title="Kecoughtan, Virginia">Kecoughtan</a> under Virginia's Governor, Sir <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Gates_(governor)" title="Thomas Gates (governor)">Thomas Gates</a>. The colonists established their own small town, with a small Anglican church <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s_Episcopal_Church_(Hampton,_Virginia)" title="St. John's Episcopal Church (Hampton, Virginia)">(known now as St. John's Episcopal Church)</a>, on July 9, 1610. This came to be known as part of Hampton. (Hampton claims to be the oldest continuously occupied English settlement in the United States).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tormey_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-tormey-9">[9]</a></sup> Hampton was named for <a href="/wiki/Henry_Wriothesley,_3rd_Earl_of_Southampton" title="Henry Wriothesley, 3rd Earl of Southampton">Henry Wriothesley, 3rd Earl of Southampton</a>, an important leader of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virginia_Company_of_London" title="Virginia Company of London">Virginia Company of London</a>, for whom the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_River" title="Hampton River">Hampton River</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads" title="Hampton Roads">Hampton Roads</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southampton_County,_Virginia" title="Southampton County, Virginia">Southampton County</a> were also named. The area became part of <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_City_(Virginia_Company)" title="Elizabeth City (Virginia Company)">Elizabeth Cittie</a>  [<i><a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">sic</a></i>] in 1619, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_River_Shire" title="Elizabeth River Shire">Elizabeth River Shire</a> in 1634, and was included in <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_City_County,_Virginia" title="Elizabeth City County, Virginia">Elizabeth City County</a> when it was formed in 1643. By 1680, the settlement was known as Hampton, and it was incorporated as a town in 1705 and became the seat of Elizabeth City County.</p>
<p>In the latter part of August 1619, an English ship flying a Dutch flag, the <i>White Lion</i>, appeared off shore from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Point_Comfort" title="Point Comfort">Point Comfort</a>. Its cargo included 20 plus Africans captured from the <a href="/wiki/Slave_ship" title="Slave ship">slave ship</a> <i>Sao Joao Bautista</i>. These were the first Africans to come ashore on English-occupied land in what would become the United States. <a href="/wiki/John_Rolfe" title="John Rolfe">John Rolfe</a>, the widower of <a href="/wiki/Pocahontas" title="Pocahontas">Pocahontas</a>, wrote in a letter that he was at Point Comfort and witnessed the arrival of the first Africans. Although these first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bantu_people" title="Bantu people">Bantu</a> men from <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a> were considered <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indentured_servants" title="Indentured servants">indentured servants</a>, their arrival marked the beginning of <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a> in North America. Two of the first Africans to arrive at Old Point Comfort in 1619 were Antoney and Isabella. Their child, the first of African descent born in North America, was born baptized January 1624.</p>
<p>Shortly after the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>, the US Army built a more substantial stone facility at Old Point Comfort. It was called <a href="/wiki/Fort_Monroe" title="Fort Monroe">Fort Monroe</a> in honor of President <a href="/wiki/James_Monroe" title="James Monroe">James Monroe</a>. The new installation and adjacent Fort Calhoun (on a man-made island across the channel) were completed in 1834.</p>
<p>Fort Monroe, Hampton and the surrounding area played several important roles during the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> (1861–1865). Although most of Virginia became part of the <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederate States of America</a>, Fort Monroe remained in <a href="/wiki/Union_(American_Civil_War)" title="Union (American Civil War)">Union</a> hands. It became notable as a historic and symbolic site of early freedom for former <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaves</a> under the provisions of <a href="/wiki/Contraband_(American_Civil_War)" title="Contraband (American Civil War)">contraband</a> policies and later the <a href="/wiki/Emancipation_Proclamation" title="Emancipation Proclamation">Emancipation Proclamation</a>. After the War, former Confederate President, <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Davis" title="Jefferson Davis">Jefferson Davis</a> was imprisoned in the area now known as the Casemate Museum on the base.</p>

<p>To the south of Fort Monroe, the Town of Hampton had the misfortune to be burned during both the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>. From the ruins of Hampton left by evacuating Confederates in 1861, <a href="/wiki/Contraband_(American_Civil_War)" title="Contraband (American Civil War)">"Contraband" slaves</a> (formerly owned by Confederates and under a degree of Union protection) built the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Contraband_Camp" title="Grand Contraband Camp">Grand Contraband Camp</a>, the first self-contained <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American" title="African American">African American</a> community in the United States. A number of modern-day Hampton streets retain their names from that community. The large number of contrabands who sought the refuge of Fort Monroe and the Grand Contraband Camp led to educational efforts which eventually included establishment of <a href="/wiki/Hampton_University" title="Hampton University">Hampton University</a>, site of the famous <a href="/wiki/Emancipation_Oak" title="Emancipation Oak">Emancipation Oak</a>.</p>
<p>The original site of the Native American's Kecoughtan Settlement was near the present site of a <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads_Transit" title="Hampton Roads Transit">Hampton Roads Transit</a> facility.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> To the south of present-day Hampton, a small unrelated <a href="/wiki/Incorporated_town" title="Incorporated town">incorporated town</a> also named Kecoughtan many years later and also located in Elizabeth City County was annexed by the <a href="/wiki/Newport_News,_Virginia" title="Newport News, Virginia">city of Newport News</a> in 1927. It is now part of that city's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_End_(Newport_News)" title="East End (Newport News)">East End</a>.</p>
<p>Hampton was incorporated as a city in 1849.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hhistory_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-hhistory-1">[1]</a></sup> On March 30, 1908, Hampton was separated from Elizabeth City County and became an independent city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-historical-county.newberry.org_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-historical-county.newberry.org-11">[11]</a></sup> However, it remained the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> and continued to share many services with the county. On July 1, 1952, following approval of voters of each locality by referendum, the city of Hampton, the <a href="/wiki/Incorporated_town" title="Incorporated town">incorporated town</a> of <a href="/wiki/Phoebus,_Virginia" title="Phoebus, Virginia">Phoebus</a> and Elizabeth City County merged into the independent city of Hampton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-historical-county.newberry.org_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-historical-county.newberry.org-11">[11]</a></sup> It was the first of a series of political consolidations in the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads" title="Hampton Roads">Hampton Roads</a> region during the third quarter of the 20th century.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fort_Monroe">Fort Monroe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fort Monroe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fort Monroe is the largest stone fort in the United States. It is now a national park area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_military_history">Modern military history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Modern military history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hampton has a rich and extensive 20th-century military history — home of Langley Air Force Base, the nation's first military installation dedicated solely to airpower and the home of the U.S. Air Force's 633d Air Base Wing and 1st and 192nd Fighter Wings. Hampton has been a center of military aviation training, research and development for nearly a hundred years, from early prop planes and Zeppelins to rocket parts and advanced fighters. Its proximity to Norfolk means that Hampton has long been home to many Navy families. Together, many Air Force and Navy families in the Hampton area experienced significant losses in war and peacetime due to family members in combat and peacetime military accidents.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Langley_AFB_during_the_Vietnam_War">Langley AFB during the Vietnam War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Langley AFB during the Vietnam War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In particular, during the Vietnam War, Langley Air Force Base was a designated 'waiting base' and thousands of Air Force families were transferred to Hampton from all over the world to wait while their husbands and fathers served in Vietnam. Thousands of Navy families associated with Naval bases in Norfolk next door also waited in Hampton during this era. Vietnam was a very high casualty war for Air Force and Navy pilots (some types of planes experienced a 50% casualty rate), and Naval "river rats" who fought on the rivers of the Mekong Delta experienced high casualties as well. There accumulated over time, in the Hampton area, a high concentration of families of unnaccounted for wartime casualties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jose.2C_Carol.3B_2008_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jose.2C_Carol.3B_2008-13">[13]</a></sup> In many cases Hampton-stationed military families of "Missing in Action" or "Prisoner of War" pilots and sailors spent many years in the area waiting to find out what had happened to their missing or captured airmen and sailors. The service and, in some cases, sacrifice of these pilots, airmen, sailors and their families, despite not having lifelong connections to the area, are a poignant and inspiring part of Hampton's history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jose.2C_Carol.3B_2008_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jose.2C_Carol.3B_2008-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CSPAN_video_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-CSPAN_video-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 136 square miles (350 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 51 square miles (130 km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 85 square miles (220 km<sup>2</sup>) (62.3%) is water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hampton's mild four season climate means outdoor activities can be enjoyed year round. The weather in Hampton is temperate and seasonal. Summers are hot and humid with cool evenings.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The mean annual temperature is 70 °F (21 °C), with an average annual snowfall of 6 inches (150 mm) and an average annual rainfall of 47 inches (1,200 mm). No measurable snow fell in 1999. The wettest seasons are the spring and summer, although rainfall is fairly constant all year round. <span class="citation-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">The highest recorded temperature was 105.0 °F (40.6 °C) in 1980. The lowest recorded temperature was −7.0 °F (−21.7 °C) on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/January_1985_Arctic_outbreak" title="January 1985 Arctic outbreak">January 21, 1985</a>.</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Additionally, the geographic location of the city, with respect to the principal storm tracks, is especially favorable, as it is south of the average path of storms originating in the higher latitudes, and north of the usual tracks of hurricanes and other major tropical storms, with the exception of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">Hurricane Isabel</a> in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Virginia_Hurricane_Climatology_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Virginia_Hurricane_Climatology-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR8_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR8-27">[25]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 137,436 people, 53,887 households, and 35,888 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 2,828.0 people per square mile (1,091.9/km²). There were 57,311 housing units at an average density of 1,106.8 per square mile (427.3/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 49.6% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">Black</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">African American</a>, 2.2% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.1% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 42.7% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>,1.3% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 3.7% from two or more races. 4.5% of the population were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanics_in_the_United_States" title="Hispanics in the United States">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race.</p>
<p>There were 53,887 households out of which 32.5% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 46.2% were married couples living together, 16.4% had a female householder with no husband present, and 33.4% were non-families. 26.6% of all households were made up of individuals and 7.9% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.49 and the average family size was 3.02.</p>
<p>The age distribution is 24.2% under the age of 18, 12.6% from 18 to 24, 32.5% from 25 to 44, 20.4% from 45 to 64, and 10.3% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 34 years. For every 100 females there were 98.3 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 96.6 males.</p>
<p>Population update: estimated population in July 2002: 145,921 (-0.4% change) Males: 72,579 (49.6%), Females: 73,858 (50.4%) <a class="external text" href="http://www.city-data.com/city/Hampton-Virginia.html" rel="nofollow">Source</a></p>
<p>The Census estimate for 2005 shows that the city's population was down slightly to more, 145,579.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-quickfacts.census.gov_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-quickfacts.census.gov-28">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The median income for a household in the city was $39,532, and the median income for a family was $46,110. Males had a median income of $31,666 versus $24,578 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $19,774. About 8.8% of families and 11.3% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 15.9% of those under age 18 and 8.6% of those age 65 or over.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hampton's daily newspaper is the Newport News-based <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Press_(Virginia)" title="Daily Press (Virginia)">Daily Press</a></i>. Other papers include Norfolk's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Virginian-Pilot" title="The Virginian-Pilot">The Virginian-Pilot</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Port_Folio_Weekly" title="Port Folio Weekly">Port Folio Weekly</a></i>, the <i><a href="/wiki/New_Journal_and_Guide" title="New Journal and Guide">New Journal and Guide</a></i>, and the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads_Business_Journal" title="Hampton Roads Business Journal">Hampton Roads Business Journal</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hampton_Roads_News_Links_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hampton_Roads_News_Links-29">[27]</a></sup> <i>Hampton Roads Magazine</i> serves as a bi-monthly regional magazine for Hampton and the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads" title="Hampton Roads">Hampton Roads</a> area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hampton_Roads_Magazine_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hampton_Roads_Magazine-30">[28]</a></sup> <i>Hampton Roads Times</i> serves as an online magazine for all the Hampton Roads cities and counties. Hampton is served by a variety of radio stations on the AM and FM dials, with towers located around the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads" title="Hampton Roads">Hampton Roads</a> area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hampton_Roads_Radio_Links_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hampton_Roads_Radio_Links-31">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hampton is also served by several television stations. The Hampton Roads <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Designated_market_area" title="Designated market area">designated market area</a> (DMA) is the 42nd largest in the U.S. with 712,790 homes (0.64% of the total U.S.).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nielsen_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-nielsen-32">[30]</a></sup> The major network television affiliates are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WTKR-TV" title="WTKR-TV">WTKR-TV</a> 3 (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Columbia_Broadcasting_Service" title="Columbia Broadcasting Service">CBS</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WAVY" title="WAVY">WAVY</a> 10 (<a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WVEC-TV" title="WVEC-TV">WVEC-TV</a> 13 (<a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>), <a href="/wiki/WGNT" title="WGNT">WGNT</a> 27 (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_CW_Television_Network" title="The CW Television Network">CW</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WTVZ" title="WTVZ">WTVZ</a> 33 (<a href="/wiki/MyNetworkTV" title="MyNetworkTV">MyNetworkTV</a>), <a href="/wiki/WVBT" title="WVBT">WVBT</a> 43 (<a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a>), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WPXV" title="WPXV">WPXV</a> 49 (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ION_Television" title="ION Television">ION Television</a>). The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Broadcasting_Service" title="Public Broadcasting Service">Public Broadcasting Service</a> station is <a href="/wiki/WHRO-TV" title="WHRO-TV">WHRO-TV</a> 15. Hampton residents also can receive independent stations, such as <a href="/wiki/WSKY-TV" title="WSKY-TV">WSKY</a> broadcasting on channel 4 from the Outer Banks of North Carolina and <a href="/wiki/WGBS-LD" title="WGBS-LD">WGBS-LD</a> broadcasting on channel 11. Hampton is served by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Verizon_FiOS" title="Verizon FiOS">Verizon FiOS</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cox_Cable" title="Cox Cable">Cox Cable</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Transportation_in_Hampton_Roads" title="Transportation in Hampton Roads">Transportation in Hampton Roads</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roads_and_Highways">Roads and Highways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Roads and Highways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the Hampton Roads region, water crossings are a major issue for land-based transportation. The city is fortunate to have a good network of local streets and bridges to cross the various rivers and creeks. Many smaller bridges, especially those along <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Boulevard" title="Mercury Boulevard">Mercury Boulevard</a>, were named to honor the original NASA astronauts, who had trained extensively at NASA's Langley facilities.</p>
<p>The city is located contiguously to the neighboring independent cities of both Newport News and Poquoson. Many roads and streets are available to travel between them. Likewise, Williamsburg, Yorktown and the counties of James City and York are also located nearby in the Peninsula subregion, and many roads lead to them.</p>
<p>To reach most of its other neighbors in the <a href="/wiki/South_Hampton_Roads" title="South Hampton Roads">South Hampton Roads</a> subregion, it is necessary to cross the harbor and/or the mouth of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_River_(Virginia)" title="James River (Virginia)">James River</a>. There are 3 major motor vehicle crossings. Among these are the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads_Bridge-Tunnel" title="Hampton Roads Bridge-Tunnel">Hampton Roads Bridge-Tunnel</a> (HRBT) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monitor-Merrimac_Memorial_Bridge-Tunnel" title="Monitor-Merrimac Memorial Bridge-Tunnel">Monitor-Merrimac Memorial Bridge-Tunnel</a> (MMMBT), each forming part of the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads_Beltway" title="Hampton Roads Beltway">Hampton Roads Beltway</a>. The HRBT is located on <a href="/wiki/Interstate_64_in_Virginia" title="Interstate 64 in Virginia">Interstate 64</a> near downtown Hampton and the MMMBT is a few miles away on <a href="/wiki/Interstate_664" title="Interstate 664">Interstate 664</a> near downtown Newport News. (These two major interstates converge in Hampton near the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Coliseum" title="Hampton Coliseum">Hampton Coliseum</a>). The third crossing option is the <a href="/wiki/James_River_Bridge" title="James River Bridge">James River Bridge</a>, also in Newport News, which connects to <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight_County,_Virginia" title="Isle of Wight County, Virginia">Isle of Wight County</a> and the town of <a href="/wiki/Smithfield,_Virginia" title="Smithfield, Virginia">Smithfield</a>.</p>
<p>Hampton is also served by several major primary and secondary highways. These notably include <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_17_in_Virginia" title="U.S. Route 17 in Virginia">U.S. Routes 17</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Route_60_(Virginia)" title="Route 60 (Virginia)">60</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Route_258_(Virginia)" title="U.S. Route 258 (Virginia)">258</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Virginia_State_Route_134" title="Virginia State Route 134">Virginia State Routes 134</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virginia_State_Route_143" title="Virginia State Route 143">143</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_and_regional_public_transportation">Local and regional public transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Local and regional public transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Hampton Transit Center, located 2 West Pembroke Boulevard, at the intersection of King Street, close to the downtown area, offers a hub for local and intercity public transportation. It hosts HRT buses, Greyhound/Trailways services and taxicabs.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads_Transit" title="Hampton Roads Transit">Hampton Roads Transit</a> (HRT) is the local provider of transit service within the city, as well offering a regional bus system with routes to and from seven other cities in <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads" title="Hampton Roads">Hampton Roads</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Intercity_bus_service">Intercity bus service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Intercity bus service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Intercity_bus_service" title="Intercity bus service">Intercity bus service</a> is provided by <a href="/wiki/Greyhound_Lines" title="Greyhound Lines">Greyhound Lines</a> and its Carolina Trailways affiliate. The buses serve the Hampton Transit Center. Low cost curbside intercity bus service is also provided by <a href="/wiki/Megabus_(North_America)" title="Megabus (North America)">Megabus</a>, with service to Richmond, Washington, D.C., Baltimore, and Philadelphia.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Amtrak">Amtrak</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Amtrak">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hampton is served by several <a href="/wiki/Amtrak" title="Amtrak">Amtrak</a> trains a day, with direct service from a station in nearby <a href="/wiki/Newport_News,_Virginia" title="Newport News, Virginia">Newport News</a> (on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warwick_Boulevard" title="Warwick Boulevard">Warwick Boulevard</a> just west of Mercury Boulevard) through Williamsburg and <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond</a> to points along the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Corridor" title="Northeast Corridor">Northeast Corridor</a> from Washington DC through Baltimore, Philadelphia, and New York City all the way to Boston. At Richmond, connections can be made for other Amtrak destinations nationwide.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air">Air</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hampton,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Air">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hampton is served by two commercial airports. <a href="/wiki/Newport_News/Williamsburg_International_Airport" title="Newport News/Williamsburg International Airport">Newport News/Williamsburg International Airport</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Air_Transport_Association_airport_code" title="International Air Transport Association airport code">IATA</a>: <b>PHF</b>) is in Newport News, and <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_International_Airport" title="Norfolk International Airport">Norfolk International Airport</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Air_Transport_Association_airport_code" title="International Air Transport Association airport code">IATA</a>: <b>ORF</b>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization_airport_code" title="International Civil Aviation Organization airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>KORF</b>, <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">FAA</a> <a href="/wiki/Location_identifier" title="Location identifier">LID</a>: <b>ORF</b>) is across the harbor in Norfolk. Both are along portions of <a href="/wiki/Interstate_64_in_Virginia" title="Interstate 64 in Virginia">Interstate 64</a>.</p>
<p>The primary airport for the Virginia Peninsula is the Newport News/Williamsburg International Airport in Newport News. Originally known as Patrick Henry Field (hence its airline code letters "PHF"), it was built on the site of Camp Patrick Henry, formerly a World War II facility. It is one of the fastest growing airports in the country, and it reported having served 1,058,839 passengers in 2005. The airport recently added a fourth airline carrier, Frontier Airlines, becoming the first new airline to come to the region in over eight years, despite the economic recession conditions. 2010 was to be the busiest year by passenger count in the airport's history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newport_News.2FWilliamsburg_International_Airport_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-Newport_News.2FWilliamsburg_International_Airport-40">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>The larger <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_International_Airport" title="Norfolk International Airport">Norfolk International Airport</a> (often known locally by its code letters "ORF") also serves the region. The airport is near the Chesapeake Bay, along the city limits between <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">Norfolk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">Virginia Beach</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ORF_History_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-ORF_History-41">[39]</a></sup> Seven airlines provide nonstop services to 25 destinations. During 2006, ORF had 3,703,664 passengers take off or land at its facility and 68,778,934 pounds of cargo were processed through its facilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ORF_Statistics_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-ORF_Statistics-42">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Regional_Airport" title="Chesapeake Regional Airport">Chesapeake Regional Airport</a> provides <a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">general aviation</a> services. It is in <a href="/wiki/South_Hampton_Roads" title="South Hampton Roads">South Hampton Roads</a> in the independent city of Chesapeake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chesapeake_Regional_Airport_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chesapeake_Regional_Airport-43">[41]</a></sup></p>
