<p><b>DJ Hurricane</b> (born <b>Wendell Timothy Fite</b> in <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a> on January 12, 1965)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DJ" title="DJ">DJ</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_producer" title="Music producer">producer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>. He is best known for his work with the <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a>. He was a member of the groups <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Solo_Sounds&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Solo Sounds (page does not exist)">Solo Sounds</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Afros&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Afros (page does not exist)">The Afros</a> and recorded three solo albums, featuring many well-known artists such as <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(band)" title="Public Enemy (band)">Public Enemy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_Thought" title="Black Thought">Black Thought</a>, <a href="/wiki/Papoose_(rapper)" title="Papoose (rapper)">Papoose</a> and <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One of New York's premier hip-hop artists on the turntables, DJ Hurricane fostered his skills alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Run_D.M.C." title="Run D.M.C.">Run D.M.C.</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hollis,_Queens" title="Hollis, Queens">Hollis, Queens</a> area of New York City before eventually hooking up with the Beastie Boys, where he made a name for himself as the group's DJ before going on to release his own albums. Hurricane began rhyming at the age of 11, during hip-hop's infancy, eventually forming a group called the Solo Sounds and later the Afros. While serving as one of Run D.M.C.'s bodyguards on the 1986 Raising Hell tour, he became friends with the Beastie Boys, who were the tour's opening act. It wasn't long before the Beasties offered Hurricane an opportunity to be their exclusive DJ. In 1993, along with the Beastie Boys, he contributed the track "It's The New Style" to the AIDS-benefit album <i><a href="/wiki/No_Alternative" title="No Alternative">No Alternative</a></i> produced by the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Organization" title="Red Hot Organization">Red Hot Organization</a>.</p>
<p>As the Beasties rose in fame with each successive album in the 1990s, Hurricane simultaneously reveled in the spotlight, releasing his first solo album in 1995 on <a href="/wiki/Grand_Royal" title="Grand Royal">Grand Royal</a>, titled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Hurra" title="The Hurra">The Hurra</a></i> (guest artists include the <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Breed" title="MC Breed">MC Breed</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sen_Dog" title="Sen Dog">Sen Dog</a>). He started his own production company, Don't Sleep Productions in 1999.</p>
<p>Five years later in late 2000, after having parted ways with the Beasties prior to their album <i><a href="/wiki/Hello_Nasty" title="Hello Nasty">Hello Nasty</a></i> in 1998, Hurricane released his second album via <a href="/wiki/TVT_Records" title="TVT Records">TVT</a>, <i>Don't Sleep</i>, which found him much more conceptually collected and with a broad scope of guest artists, including <a href="/wiki/Kool_G_Rap" title="Kool G Rap">Kool G Rap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Weiland" title="Scott Weiland">Scott Weiland</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(band)" title="Public Enemy (band)">Public Enemy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rah_Digga" title="Rah Digga">Rah Digga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Talib_Kweli" title="Talib Kweli">Talib Kweli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharoahe_Monch" title="Pharoahe Monch">Pharoahe Monch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ad-Rock" title="Ad-Rock">Ad-Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_Thought" title="Black Thought">Black Thought</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big_Gipp" title="Big Gipp">Big Gipp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hittman" title="Hittman">Hittman</a> among others. The track "Come Get It" featured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Conglomerate_(American_group)" title="The Conglomerate (American group)">Flipmode Squad</a> peaked at No. 73 of <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Billboard Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 2008 to 2012 he was touring DJ for <a href="/wiki/Faith_Evans" title="Faith Evans">Faith Evans</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He's married to Dawn Fite and has four children named Jennifer, Jarell, Ayana and Quran.</p>
