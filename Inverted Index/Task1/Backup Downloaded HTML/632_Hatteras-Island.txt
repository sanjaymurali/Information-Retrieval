<p><b>Hatteras Island</b> (historically, <b>Croatoan Island</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Barrier_island" title="Barrier island">barrier island</a> located off the <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> coast. Dividing the Atlantic Ocean and the <a href="/wiki/Pamlico_Sound" title="Pamlico Sound">Pamlico Sound</a>, it runs parallel to the coast, forming a bend at <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras" title="Cape Hatteras">Cape Hatteras</a>. It is part of North Carolina's <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">Outer Banks</a> and includes the towns of <a href="/wiki/Rodanthe,_North_Carolina" title="Rodanthe, North Carolina">Rodanthe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Waves,_North_Carolina" title="Waves, North Carolina">Waves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salvo,_North_Carolina" title="Salvo, North Carolina">Salvo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Avon,_North_Carolina" title="Avon, North Carolina">Avon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buxton,_North_Carolina" title="Buxton, North Carolina">Buxton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frisco,_North_Carolina" title="Frisco, North Carolina">Frisco</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hatteras,_North_Carolina" title="Hatteras, North Carolina">Hatteras</a>. It contains the largest part of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras_National_Seashore" title="Cape Hatteras National Seashore">Cape Hatteras National Seashore</a>. It is almost entirely in <a href="/wiki/Dare_County,_North_Carolina" title="Dare County, North Carolina">Dare County, North Carolina</a>, but there is a very small sliver of about 45 acres (0.18 km<sup>2</sup>) which extends southwest into <a href="/wiki/Hyde_County,_North_Carolina" title="Hyde County, North Carolina">Hyde County</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The island is one of the longest in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Continental_United_States" title="Continental United States">contiguous United States</a>, measuring 42 miles (68 km) along a straight line from end to end, or roughly 50 miles (80 km) along the curve of the land.</p>
<p>Hatteras Island is known for sport fishing, surfing, windsurfing and <a href="/wiki/Kitesurfing" title="Kitesurfing">kiteboarding</a>, and is known as "the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_blue_marlin" title="Atlantic blue marlin">blue marlin</a> capital of the world".</p>
<p>Accommodations range from hotels and condos to luxurious oceanfront cottages. Hatteras Island is often used for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Destination_wedding" title="Destination wedding">destination weddings</a>, family reunions, and special events.</p>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a> the island has a land area of 85.56 km<sup>2</sup> (33.03 sq mi) and a population of 4,001 as of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_2000" title="United States Census, 2000">2000 census</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It lies in parts of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kinnakeet_Township,_Dare_County,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kinnakeet Township, Dare County, North Carolina (page does not exist)">Kinnakeet Township</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hatteras_Township,_Dare_County,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hatteras Township, Dare County, North Carolina (page does not exist)">Hatteras Township</a> in Dare County, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ocracoke_Township,_Hyde_County,_North_Carolina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ocracoke Township, Hyde County, North Carolina (page does not exist)">Ocracoke Township</a> in Hyde County.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hatteras_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-Colombian_era">Pre-Colombian era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hatteras_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-Colombian era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Hatteras_Indians" title="Hatteras Indians">Hatteras Indians</a> were the first human inhabitants of Hatteras Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harrison11_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-harrison11-2">[2]</a></sup> They established a settlement (village) known as Croatoan on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harrison11_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-harrison11-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Colonial_era">Colonial era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hatteras_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Colonial era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The story of <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_Colony" title="Roanoke Colony">the missing colony</a> began when John White finally returned to Roanoke on a fifth voyage to the colony, a much-delayed re-supply mission arriving in 1590. At that time, the settlement was found abandoned. The only clue to the colonists' whereabouts was the word "Croatoan" found carved into the palisade of the fort. It is logical that the colonists left on Roanoke had gone back to Croatoan, as they had already lived there and had had a strong relationship with the natives, some of whom had visited England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>John White, who made maps showing both Croatoan and Roanoke, wrote in 1590:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>I greatly joyed that I had safely found a certain token of their safe being at Croatoan, which is the place where Manteo was born, and the savages of the island our friends. [<i><a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">sic</a></i>]<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>White had instructed them that if anything happened to them, they should carve a <a href="/wiki/Maltese_cross" title="Maltese cross">Maltese cross</a> on a tree nearby, indicating that their disappearance had been forced. As there was no cross, White took this to mean they had moved to "Croatoan Island" (now known as Hatteras Island). Upon finding the message of CROATOAN carved on the palisade, White also wrote:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>The next morning it was agreed by the captain and myself, with the master and others to weigh anchor and go for the place at Croatoan where our planters were for that then the wind was good for that place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>However, he was unable to conduct a search, as a massive storm was brewing and his men refused to go any further. The next day, they instead left the area without looking further for the colonists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kupperman1984_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kupperman1984-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Communities">Communities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hatteras_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Communities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While there are no incorporated places on Hatteras, there are several resort communities along the length of the island. From north to south these are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Chicamacomico is the older name for a contiguous settlement now divided into three communities:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rodanthe,_North_Carolina" title="Rodanthe, North Carolina">Rodanthe</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Waves,_North_Carolina" title="Waves, North Carolina">Waves</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Salvo,_North_Carolina" title="Salvo, North Carolina">Salvo</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Avon,_North_Carolina" title="Avon, North Carolina">Avon</a>, formerly named Kinnakeet</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Buxton,_North_Carolina" title="Buxton, North Carolina">Buxton</a>, the largest community by population on Hatteras Island, home to the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras_Light" title="Cape Hatteras Light">Cape Hatteras Light</a> as well as the elementary and secondary school that serves all residents of the island</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Frisco,_North_Carolina" title="Frisco, North Carolina">Frisco</a>, location of the largest paved airstrip on the Island at <a href="/wiki/Billy_Mitchell_Airport" title="Billy Mitchell Airport">Billy Mitchell Airport</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hatteras,_North_Carolina" title="Hatteras, North Carolina">Hatteras Village</a>, terminus of the ferry to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ocracoke_Island" title="Ocracoke Island">Ocracoke Island</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_enforcement">Law enforcement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hatteras_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Law enforcement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As there are no incorporated places on Hatteras, the island is patrolled by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dare_County_Sheriff%27s_Office&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dare County Sheriff's Office (page does not exist)">Dare County Sheriff's Office</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Highway_Patrol" title="North Carolina Highway Patrol">North Carolina Highway Patrol</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/National_Park_Service" title="National Park Service">National Park Service</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accessibility">Accessibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hatteras_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Accessibility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For many years, the only ways to reach Hatteras Island was by ferry and footpath.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bridge_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 1963, the Herbert C. Bonner Bridge was completed, connecting Hatteras Island to the northern Outer Banks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bridge_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-7">[7]</a></sup> The bridge's namesake, <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Covington_Bonner" title="Herbert Covington Bonner">Herbert Covington Bonner</a>, was a Democratic congressman who represented the area between 1940 and 1965.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bonner_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-bonner-8">[8]</a></sup> The $4 million to build the bridge was financed by the State of North Carolina, the U.S. Commerce Department, and the National Park Service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bonner_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-bonner-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>From the south, Hatteras is reached by way of a 40-minute ride on the Ocracoke-Hatteras ferry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bridge_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-7">[7]</a></sup> Presently there is no direct route linking Hatteras Island to the Hyde County mainland, resulting in a 2-3 hour commute around the Pamlico Sound.</p>
<p>During a storm on October 26, 1990, a <a href="/wiki/Fishing_dredge" title="Fishing dredge">fishing dredge</a> broke loose from its <a href="/wiki/Mooring_(watercraft)" title="Mooring (watercraft)">mooring</a> and destroyed part of the Herbert C. Bonner Bridge, cutting Hatteras Island's only bridge to the north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hail_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-hail-9">[9]</a></sup> The bridge was repaired by February 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hail_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-hail-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irene_(2011)" title="Hurricane Irene (2011)">Hurricane Irene</a> destroyed part of Highway 12 between <a href="/wiki/Nags_Head,_North_Carolina" title="Nags Head, North Carolina">Nags Head</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rodanthe,_North_Carolina" title="Rodanthe, North Carolina">Rodanthe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bridge_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-bridge-7">[7]</a></sup> Until the construction of a temporary bridge in October 2011, the only way of accessing Hatteras Island was by ferry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap_9_1_2011_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap_9_1_2011-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
