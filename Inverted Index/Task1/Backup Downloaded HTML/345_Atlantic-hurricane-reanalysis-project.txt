<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Atlantic hurricane reanalysis project</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/w/index.php?title=Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis&amp;redirect=no" title="Atlantic hurricane reanalysis">Atlantic hurricane reanalysis</a>)</span></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<p>The <b>Atlantic hurricane reanalysis project</b> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration">National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration</a> seeks to correct and add new information about past <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_hurricane" title="North Atlantic hurricane">North Atlantic hurricanes</a>. It was started around 2000 to update <a href="/wiki/HURDAT" title="HURDAT">HURDAT</a>, the official <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">hurricane</a> database for the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Basin</a>, which has become outdated since its creation due to various systematic errors introduced into the database over time. This effort has involved reanalyses of ship observations from the <a href="/wiki/International_Comprehensive_Ocean-Atmosphere_Data_Set" title="International Comprehensive Ocean-Atmosphere Data Set">International Comprehensive Ocean-Atmosphere Data Set</a> (ICOADS) as well as reanalyses done by other researchers over the years. It has been ongoing as of 2016, and should last another four years.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Inaccuracies_and_omissions_in_existing_data">Inaccuracies and omissions in existing data</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis_project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Inaccuracies and omissions in existing data">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Invalidpreswindrelationship.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="765" data-file-width="750" height="224" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Invalidpreswindrelationship.png/220px-Invalidpreswindrelationship.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Invalidpreswindrelationship.png/330px-Invalidpreswindrelationship.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Invalidpreswindrelationship.png/440px-Invalidpreswindrelationship.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Invalidpreswindrelationship.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Arrows point to storms with unlikely wind/pressure relationships in HURDAT</div>
</div>
</div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Errors">Errors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis_project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Errors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>HURDAT contains a number of errors which need to be corrected, such demonstrated by the outliers in the a pressure vs. wind speed graph of datapoints in the database (right). Some of these errors have existed since the database's creation during <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apollo_Program" title="Apollo Program">Apollo Program</a>, where it was used to help produce probabilities of tropical cyclone-induced winds in critical areas such as Cape Kennedy (now <a href="/wiki/Cape_Canaveral" title="Cape Canaveral">Cape Canaveral</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_information">New information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis_project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: New information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A significant amount of new data for systems between 1851 and 1886 became available after a major basin-wide reanalysis in 1996, a project led by Jose Fernandez-Paratagas with the collaboration of Henry Diaz. The new data was constructed using old newspaper articles and the hemispheric <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_analysis" title="Surface weather analysis">weather map</a> series. Hurricane histories for individual states had been constructed by the 1990s as well, which proposed new storms and increased the knowledge of tropical cyclones already in the database. Due to this profusion of relevant information not included in HURDAT, and evolving definitions for <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical cyclones</a> over the decades, the project was started around 2000 to update the official database.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Main_Project_Page_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Main_Project_Page-2">[2]</a></sup> Since then, the International Comprehensive Ocean-Atmosphere Data Set has been utilized to check for older ship reports which were either not utilized nor available to previous researchers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Incompleteness_of_dataset">Incompleteness of dataset</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis_project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Incompleteness of dataset">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As early as 1957, it was recognized that an increasing trend in the number of tropical cyclones each season in the Atlantic Basin was at least partially tied to increasing observations and better records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> By analyzing the density of ship tracks over time, it has been estimated that between 1900 and 1966, an average of two storms per year are missing from HURDAT. This is mainly due to a lack of satellite imagery and lack of reconnaissance aircraft prior to 1943. An additional storm per year is likely missing prior to the advent of new technologies. These technologies include satellite-derived <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quikscat" title="Quikscat">Quikscat</a> winds, satellite-derived temperature profile information, and Robert Hart's cyclone phase space diagrams, which have led to a recent increase in tropical cyclone detection. Quikscat was launched in 1999, and has been credited with allowing <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Chantal_(2007)" title="Tropical Storm Chantal (2007)">Chantal</a> to be named during the <a href="/wiki/2007_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2007 Atlantic hurricane season">2007 Atlantic hurricane season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The last two help determine whether or not a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low pressure area</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical cyclone</a>, or tropical cyclone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Christopher_Landsea" title="Christopher Landsea">Christopher Landsea</a> noted that the efforts to reanalyze the Atlantic hurricane database</p>
<blockquote>
<p>will not be able to recover observations of open ocean tropical cyclones that were just never taken. Researchers cannot assume that the Atlantic tropical cyclone database presents a complete depiction of frequency of events before the advent of satellite imagery in the mid-1960s. Moreover, newly available advanced tools and techniques are also contributing toward monitoring about one additional Atlantic tropical cyclone per year since 2002. Thus large, long-term 'trends' in tropical cyclone frequency are primarily manifestations of increased monitoring capabilities and likely not related to any real change in the climate in which they develop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Progress">Progress</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis_project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Progress">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HurricaneAndrew.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="828" data-file-width="1024" height="178" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/HurricaneAndrew.jpg/220px-HurricaneAndrew.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/HurricaneAndrew.jpg/330px-HurricaneAndrew.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/HurricaneAndrew.jpg/440px-HurricaneAndrew.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HurricaneAndrew.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">Hurricane Andrew</a> over <a href="/wiki/South_Florida" title="South Florida">South Florida</a> as a Category 5 hurricane</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The project has currently reanalyzed storms from the period 1886 to 1955, and has extended HURDAT back to 1851.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metadata_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-metadata-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2001, data for the years 1851–1885 were added to the official database from the Fernández-Partagás series of publications. Also, a paleotempestology conference was held at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_South_Carolina" title="University of South Carolina">University of South Carolina</a> which proposed to increase the scope of HURDAT from a starting year of 1851 to a start of 1800. The conference also discussed ways of exchanging information for the inclusion of older <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclones</a>, such as via <a href="/wiki/Compact_disc" title="Compact disc">compact disc</a> or a Wikipedia-style website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Prelim-10">[10]</a></sup> While the reanalysis has mostly proceeded sequentially, notable exceptions have been made for the reanalysis of some significant tropical cyclones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metadata_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-metadata-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2002, for the tenth anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">Hurricane Andrew</a>, the reanalysis was completed for the hurricane, which upgraded the cyclone to a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">Category 5</a> hurricane. In 2014, a similar reanalysis was completed for <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Camille" title="Hurricane Camille">Hurricane Camille</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metadata_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-metadata-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Work by Michael Chenoweth, in collaboration with Cary Mock, increased knowledge of tropical cyclones in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> for 1750 to 1786. Chenoweth conducted a basin-wide reassessment for old source material from the 1700 to 1855 period by using <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_observation" title="Surface weather observation">surface weather observations</a> mainly in the form of ship reports, newspapers, and various diaries and journals from the region around the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chenoweth_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chenoweth-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Project_Details_Page_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Project_Details_Page-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_efforts_in_other_basins">Future efforts in other basins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis_project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Future efforts in other basins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Freda_Mar_15_1981_2051Z.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="601" data-file-width="585" height="226" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Freda_Mar_15_1981_2051Z.png/220px-Freda_Mar_15_1981_2051Z.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Freda_Mar_15_1981_2051Z.png/330px-Freda_Mar_15_1981_2051Z.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Freda_Mar_15_1981_2051Z.png/440px-Freda_Mar_15_1981_2051Z.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Freda_Mar_15_1981_2051Z.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Typhoon Freda, in <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">Joint Typhoon Warning Center</a> database as 100 knots (190 km/h) while <a href="/wiki/Dvorak_technique" title="Dvorak technique">Dvorak estimates</a> were 115 knots (213 km/h)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>There is agreement within <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> circles of doing an "Atlantic-style" reanalysis for other ocean basins. Some efforts are underway to start similar reanalyses across the western<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and eastern north <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPAC_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-EPAC-14">[14]</a></sup> but are likely to take longer to complete. This is due to the need for coordination between the multiple <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Centre" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre">Regional Specialized Meteorological Centres</a>, which have the responsibility for tracking and forecasting tropical cyclones across that ocean. These tropical cyclone reanalyses are important, as theories concerning <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> trends hinge upon the quality of these databases.</p>
