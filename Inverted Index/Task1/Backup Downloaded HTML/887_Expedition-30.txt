<p><b>Expedition 30</b> was the 30th long-duration mission to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> (ISS). The expedition's first three crew members – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dan_Burbank" title="Dan Burbank">Dan Burbank</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anton_Shkaplerov" title="Anton Shkaplerov">Anton Shkaplerov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anatoli_Ivanishin" title="Anatoli Ivanishin">Anatoli Ivanishin</a> – arrived on the ISS aboard <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-22" title="Soyuz TMA-22">Soyuz TMA-22</a> on 16 November 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ex29dock2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ex29dock2-3">[3]</a></sup> during the last phase of <a href="/wiki/Expedition_29" title="Expedition 29">Expedition 29</a>. Expedition 30 formally began on 21 November 2011, with the departure from the ISS of the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-02M" title="Soyuz TMA-02M">Soyuz TMA-02M</a> spacecraft. The expedition ended on 27 April 2012, as Burbank, Shkaplerov and Ivanishin departed from the ISS aboard Soyuz TMA-22, marking the beginning of <a href="/wiki/Expedition_31" title="Expedition 31">Expedition 31</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EndApr2012_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-EndApr2012-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission_highlights">Mission highlights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_30&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mission highlights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soyuz_TMA-02M_departure">Soyuz TMA-02M departure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_30&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Soyuz TMA-02M departure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Expedition 30 began with the departure of the <a href="/wiki/Expedition_28" title="Expedition 28">Expedition 28</a>/<a href="/wiki/Expedition_29" title="Expedition 29">29</a> crew on board the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-02M" title="Soyuz TMA-02M">Soyuz TMA-02M</a> spacecraft, on 21 November 2011 at 23:00 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ex28_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ex28-1">[1]</a></sup> This left the Expedition 29/30 crew, who had docked with the ISS in the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-22" title="Soyuz TMA-22">Soyuz TMA-22</a> spacecraft on 16 November 2011 at 05:24 UTC,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ex29dock_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ex29dock-6">[6]</a></sup> on board the station. Soyuz TMA-22 launched on 14 November 2011 at 04:14 UTC, from <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ex29_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ex29-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soyuz_TMA-03M_arrival">Soyuz TMA-03M arrival</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_30&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Soyuz TMA-03M arrival">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The ISS was crewed by the first three Expedition 30 crewmembers for approximately five-and-a-half weeks. They were joined on 23 December 2011 by the Expedition 30/<a href="/wiki/Expedition_31" title="Expedition 31">31</a> crew,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-docking_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-docking-8">[8]</a></sup> who were carried to the ISS by the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-03M" title="Soyuz TMA-03M">Soyuz TMA-03M</a> spacecraft. Soyuz TMA-03M was launched on 21 December 2011 at 13:16 UTC<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ex31_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ex31-9">[9]</a></sup> and docked on 23 December at 15:19 UTC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comet_Lovejoy_observation">Comet Lovejoy observation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_30&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Comet Lovejoy observation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 21 December 2011, Expedition 30 commander <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dan_Burbank" title="Dan Burbank">Dan Burbank</a> observed a pass of the comet <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C/2011_W3" title="C/2011 W3">C/2011 W3 Lovejoy</a>. The comet was initially thought to be in a destructive orbit around the sun, and passed within 140,000 km (87,000 mi) of the sun's surface.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> However, the comet ultimately survived its encounter with the sun.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spacewalk">Spacewalk</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_30&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Spacewalk">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 16 February 2012, Russian crew members <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Kononenko" title="Oleg Kononenko">Oleg Kononenko</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anton_Shkaplerov" title="Anton Shkaplerov">Anton Shkaplerov</a> performed the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spacewalk" title="Spacewalk">spacewalk</a> of 2012, moving one of the station's <a href="/wiki/Strela_(crane)" title="Strela (crane)">Strela</a> cranes from the <a href="/wiki/Pirs_(ISS_module)" title="Pirs (ISS module)"><i>Pirs</i></a> module to the <a href="/wiki/Poisk_(ISS_module)" title="Poisk (ISS module)"><i>Poisk</i></a> module. The astronauts also installed new debris shields and materials experiments on the exterior of the ISS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fiftieth_anniversary_of_Friendship_7">Fiftieth anniversary of <i>Friendship 7</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_30&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Fiftieth anniversary of Friendship 7">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 20 February 2012, the ISS crew commemorated the fiftieth anniversary of <a href="/wiki/John_Glenn" title="John Glenn">John Glenn</a>'s first orbital flight in the <a href="/wiki/Project_Mercury" title="Project Mercury">Project Mercury</a> spacecraft <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friendship_7" title="Friendship 7">Friendship 7</a></i>. The crew surprised the 90-year-old Glenn by speaking to him via video link while he was on-stage with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NASA_Administrator" title="NASA Administrator">NASA Administrator</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlie_Bolden" title="Charlie Bolden">Charlie Bolden</a> at <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_University" title="Ohio State University">Ohio State University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Edoardo_Amaldi_ATV_docking"><i>Edoardo Amaldi</i> ATV docking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_30&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Edoardo Amaldi ATV docking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">European Space Agency</a>'s third <a href="/wiki/Automated_Transfer_Vehicle" title="Automated Transfer Vehicle">Automated Transfer Vehicle</a> (ATV), <a href="/wiki/Edoardo_Amaldi_ATV" title="Edoardo Amaldi ATV"><i>Edoardo Amaldi</i></a>, was launched on 23 March 2012, and docked successfully with the ISS on 28 March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The robotic ATV carried around 6,595 kilograms (14,539 lb) of propellants, water and dry cargo to the Expedition 30 crew, and also helped boost the station's altitude with its thrusters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The ATV ultimately remained docked until September 2012, whereafter it deorbited and burned up in Earth's atmosphere as planned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OverOct4_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-OverOct4-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Progress_M-15M_docking">Progress M-15M docking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_30&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Progress M-15M docking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Progress_M-15M" title="Progress M-15M">Progress M-15M</a>, a Russian unmanned resupply spacecraft, was launched to the ISS from <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a> on 20 April 2012. It successfully docked with the station on 22 April.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In preparation for the spacecraft's arrival, its predecessor, <a href="/wiki/Progress_M-14M" title="Progress M-14M">Progress M-14M</a>, undocked from the ISS on 19 April, having been docked since 28 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soyuz_TMA-22_departure">Soyuz TMA-22 departure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_30&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Soyuz TMA-22 departure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Expedition 30 ended on 27 April 2012 with the departure of Soyuz TMA-22 from the ISS, carrying astronauts Burbank, Shkaplerov and Ivanishin. The three astronauts landed safely in <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> at 11:45 AM (GMT), while Kononenko, Kuipers and Pettit remained aboard the station to begin <a href="/wiki/Expedition_31" title="Expedition 31">Expedition 31</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EndApr2012_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-EndApr2012-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
