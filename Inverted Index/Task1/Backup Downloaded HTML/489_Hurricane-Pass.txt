<p><b>Hurricane Pass</b> is a pedestrian <a href="/wiki/Mountain_pass" title="Mountain pass">mountain pass</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Teton_Range" title="Teton Range">Teton Range</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Teton_National_Park" title="Grand Teton National Park">Grand Teton National Park</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wyoming" title="Wyoming">Wyoming</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-topo_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-topo-2">[2]</a></sup> Situated at approximately 10,338 ft (3,151 m) above sea level, the pass can be accessed from the south by way of the <a href="/wiki/Teton_Crest_Trail" title="Teton Crest Trail">Teton Crest Trail</a> or from the north via the <a href="/wiki/South_Fork_Cascade_Canyon_Trail" title="South Fork Cascade Canyon Trail">South Fork Cascade Canyon Trail</a>. From <a href="/wiki/Jenny_Lake" title="Jenny Lake">Jenny Lake</a> the roundtrip hike is 24.8 mi (39.9 km) with a 4,500 ft (1,400 m) elevation gain. <a href="/wiki/Schoolroom_Glacier" title="Schoolroom Glacier">Schoolroom Glacier</a> is 100 yd (91 m) from the pass.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Views of the west slopes of several of the highest Teton Range peaks as well as <a href="/wiki/Alaska_Basin" title="Alaska Basin">Alaska Basin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battleship_Mountain" title="Battleship Mountain">Battleship Mountain</a> in <a href="/wiki/Caribou-Targhee_National_Forest" title="Caribou-Targhee National Forest">Caribou-Targhee National Forest</a> are available at the pass.</p>
