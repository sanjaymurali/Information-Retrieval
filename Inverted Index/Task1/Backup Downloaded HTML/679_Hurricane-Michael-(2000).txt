<p><b>Hurricane Michael</b> of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2000 Atlantic hurricane season">2000 Atlantic hurricane season</a> caused moderate damage in <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a>. The seventeenth tropical cyclone, thirteenth named storm, and eighth hurricane of the season, Michael developed from a <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">non-tropical</a> system to the southwest of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> on October 15. The system was initially <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical</a>, but rapidly acquired tropical characteristics by October 17. Michael quickly strengthened into a hurricane later that day. By October 20, Michael peaked as a Category 2 hurricane on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale">Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale</a> at a relatively high altitude of <a href="/wiki/44th_parallel_north" title="44th parallel north">44°N</a>. The storm weakened slightly to a Category 1 hurricane and became extratropical shortly before making landfall in Newfoundland on October 20.</p>
<p>As the intensity of Michael was equivalent to a Category 1 hurricane during landfall, high winds were reported throughout Atlantic Canada, especially in Newfoundland. Winds from the storm peaked at 172 km/h (107 mph) in <a href="/wiki/St._Lawrence,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. Lawrence, Newfoundland and Labrador">St. Lawrence, Newfoundland</a>; other locations across the island reported winds of 128 to 150 km/h (80 to 93 mph). High winds caused minor structure damage across Newfoundland, confined to vinyl siding peeled off, shattered windows, and unroofed houses. In addition, sporadic <a href="/wiki/Power_outage" title="Power outage">power outages</a> were also reported from <a href="/wiki/Corner_Brook" title="Corner Brook">Corner Brook</a> to <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador">St. John's</a>. Michael also dropped rainfall across Atlantic Canada and in <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>. The rainfall was generally light, with much of the region reporting between 25 to 51 <a href="/wiki/Millimetre" title="Millimetre">mm</a> (0.98 to 2.01 <a href="/wiki/Inch" title="Inch">in</a>) of precipitation. Michael also produced high tides, accompanied by waves reported as high as 16.9 m (55 ft) by a buoy well offshore of Newfoundland.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michael_(2000)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Michael_2000_track.png"><img alt="The path of a hurricane, it starts about half between Bermuda and the Bahamas, races toward Newfoundland and quickly becomes extratropical" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="712" data-file-width="1024" height="153" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Michael_2000_track.png/220px-Michael_2000_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Michael_2000_track.png/330px-Michael_2000_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Michael_2000_track.png/440px-Michael_2000_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Michael_2000_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The system originated out of a non-tropical <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">cold-core upper level low</a> that drifted southward into the <a href="/wiki/Subtropics" title="Subtropics">subtropics</a>. The low interacted with a <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">cold front</a>, which resulted in the development of a <a href="/wiki/Stationary_front" title="Stationary front">stationary front</a> over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> on October 10. As the cold low drifted southward, a surface low developed on October 12. The surface low remained mostly stationary until early on October 15 as it merged with the upper-level low and deepened while north of the Bahamas and east of the coast of <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>. Later that day, the system gained enough tropical characteristics and became organized enough to be classified as a <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">subtropical depression</a>. The storm remained stationary over warm water, with <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">sea surface temperatures</a> around 28 <a href="/wiki/Celsius" title="Celsius">°C</a> (82 <a href="/wiki/Fahrenheit" title="Fahrenheit">°F</a>), while it continued to gain additional tropical characteristics. Around 0000 UTC on October 16, it strengthened into a subtropical storm after <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">satellite</a> classifications showed a stronger storm. Later that day, the cyclone continued to develop as more <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorms</a> formed and persisted near the low-level circulation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Michael_18_oct_2000_2045Z.jpg"><img alt="A hurricane moving over the warm waters of the Gulf Stream" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1900" data-file-width="1800" height="232" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Hurricane_Michael_18_oct_2000_2045Z.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Michael_18_oct_2000_2045Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Hurricane_Michael_18_oct_2000_2045Z.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Michael_18_oct_2000_2045Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Hurricane_Michael_18_oct_2000_2045Z.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Michael_18_oct_2000_2045Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Michael_18_oct_2000_2045Z.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Hurricane Michael intensifying over the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a> on October 18</div>

<p>By 0000 UTC on October 17, the transition was completed and Michael was reclassified as a tropical storm as supported by satellite classifications via the <a href="/wiki/Dvorak_technique" title="Dvorak technique">Dvorak technique</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Shortly thereafter, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> initiated advisories on Tropical Depression Seventeen;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> post-analysis later discovered it was actually a tropical storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> A well-defined <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> quickly developed early on October 17 as Michael began to strengthen and continued to organize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> That afternoon, Michael rapidly intensified further and was upgraded to a hurricane as <a href="/wiki/Reconnaissance" title="Reconnaissance">Reconnaissance</a> flights found winds of 120 km/h (75 mph). At that point, Michael began to move northward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> While Michael was maintaining minimal hurricane status, the eye remained ragged, yet well-defined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Early on October 19, Michael briefly strengthened to 140 km/h (85 mph) before weakening slightly six hours later. Tracking into cooler water, Michael began its extratropical transition while accelerating to the northeast towards Newfoundland, which prevented significant weakening. The acceleration was caused by a shortwave <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> embedded within strong southwesterly mid-level flow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Unexpectedly, Michael began to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapidly intensify</a> on October 19 while in extratropical transition. Later that day, a <a href="/wiki/Ship" title="Ship">ship</a> report in the eastern eyewall reported a minimum central pressure of 965 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (28.5 <a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">inHg</a>) and maximum sustained winds of 155 km/h (100 mph), making Michael a Category 2 hurricane on the Saffir-Simpson hurricane scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The sudden intensification was determined to have been as a result of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baroclinic" title="Baroclinic">baroclinic</a> effects as it interacted with an incoming trough, which provided additional energy to counter the general weakening of hurricanes while in the high latitudes over sea-surface temperatures around 16 °C (61 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canada_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Canada-7">[7]</a></sup> Michael formed a "hybrid" system combining influences of a hurricane with that of a powerful extratropical cyclone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> At 2300 UTC on October 19, Michael made landfall near <a href="/wiki/Parsons_Harbour,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Parsons Harbour, Newfoundland and Labrador">Parsons Harbour</a>, Newfoundland with winds of 155 km/h (100 mph). Shortly thereafter, Michael transitioned into an extratropical storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The extratropical system gradually weakened as it tracked across Newfoundland on October 20, and slowed down considerably with the presence of the trough. The storm was absorbed by another extratropical low just north of Newfoundland that afternoon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michael_(2000)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Because it was unknown that Michael became extratropical before landfall, it was the initially considered the first tropical cyclone to strike Newfoundland since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Luis" title="Hurricane Luis">Hurricane Luis</a> in 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHC_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-CHC-9">[9]</a></sup> Furthermore, Michael was also considered the first hurricane to make landfall in Canada since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hortense" title="Hurricane Hortense">Hurricane Hortense</a> in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> When the <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_Service_of_Canada" title="Meteorological Service of Canada">Meteorological Service of Canada</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Research_Council_(Canada)" title="National Research Council (Canada)">National Research Council</a> flew a <a href="/wiki/Convair" title="Convair">Convair</a> research flight into Michael on October 19, it was the first successful research flight made into a tropical cyclone by those agencies. Subsequent flights have been made into <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Karen_(2001)" title="Hurricane Karen (2001)">Tropical Storm Karen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">Hurricane Isabel</a> (while inland), <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Juan" title="Hurricane Juan">Hurricane Juan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ophelia_(2005)" title="Hurricane Ophelia (2005)">Hurricane Ophelia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newfoundland">Newfoundland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michael_(2000)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Newfoundland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Radar_image_from_Hurricane_Michael_over_Newfoundland_(October_19,_2000).gif"><img alt="Radar image of a storm moving over Newfoundland" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="508" height="208" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6b/Radar_image_from_Hurricane_Michael_over_Newfoundland_%28October_19%2C_2000%29.gif/220px-Radar_image_from_Hurricane_Michael_over_Newfoundland_%28October_19%2C_2000%29.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6b/Radar_image_from_Hurricane_Michael_over_Newfoundland_%28October_19%2C_2000%29.gif/330px-Radar_image_from_Hurricane_Michael_over_Newfoundland_%28October_19%2C_2000%29.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6b/Radar_image_from_Hurricane_Michael_over_Newfoundland_%28October_19%2C_2000%29.gif/440px-Radar_image_from_Hurricane_Michael_over_Newfoundland_%28October_19%2C_2000%29.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>In advance of the storm, the Newfoundland Weather Centre posted wind warnings, gale warnings, storm warnings, and hurricane warnings throughout Newfoundland. Rainfall advisories were also issued in portions of the East Coast, Central Newfoundland, the South Coast, and the West Coast. Offshore, storm force wind warnings were declared on October 19 for Newfoundland waters. Several Special Weather Statements were also posted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canada_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Canada-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Light to moderate damage was reported throughout Newfoundland, primarily as a result of the strong winds. The entire region was affected by Michael, though the majority of the damage was confined to small communities east of where the storm made landfall, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaultois,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Gaultois, Newfoundland and Labrador">Gaultois</a>, Harbour Breton, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hermitage,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Hermitage, Newfoundland and Labrador">Hermitage</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_Harbour_West,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="English Harbour West, Newfoundland and Labrador">English Harbour West</a>. These areas reported significant <a href="/wiki/Power_outage" title="Power outage">power outages</a> and minor structural damage; vinyl siding was peeled off, windows were shattered, and roof shingles were torn off in some localized locations. The most severe damage was in Gaultois, where several roofs were torn off and trees were uprooted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canada_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Canada-7">[7]</a></sup> Wind damage also occurred in the <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s_Metropolitan_Area" title="St. John's Metropolitan Area">St. John's Metropolitan Area</a>, where a <a href="/wiki/Sobeys" title="Sobeys">Sobeys</a> store in <a href="/wiki/Conception_Bay_South" title="Conception Bay South">Conception Bay South</a> had broken windows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EC_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-EC-13">[13]</a></sup> Sporadic power outages were reported throughout the island, as far east as St. John’s, and as far west as Corner Brook. As the storm struck at low tide, <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> was insignificant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canada_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-Canada-7">[7]</a></sup> However, just prior to landfall, waves on the coast of Newfoundland reached heights of 7–8 m (23–26 ft).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EC_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-EC-13">[13]</a></sup> Rainfall near the storm's track was generally around 30 mm (1.2 in), though further inland, precipitation reached 75 mm (3.0 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canada_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-Canada-7">[7]</a></sup> Rainfall from the hurricane amounted to 77 mm (3.0 in) at <a href="/wiki/Englee" title="Englee">Englee</a>, a small fishing community on the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_Peninsula" title="Great Northern Peninsula">Great Northern Peninsula</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EC_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-EC-13">[13]</a></sup> Wind gusts peaked at 172 km/h (107 mph) at <a href="/wiki/St._Lawrence,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. Lawrence, Newfoundland and Labrador">St. Lawrence</a>, with other reports varying between 128 km/h (80 mph) and 150 km/h (93 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHC_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-CHC-9">[9]</a></sup> Despite severe winds, damage was relatively light due to high construction standards in Newfoundland, where <a href="/wiki/Winter_storm" title="Winter storm">winter storms</a> routinely result in hurricane-force winds, as well as the sparse population of the area affected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canada_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-Canada-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tug" title="Tug">tug</a> <i>Atlantic Elm</i> lost its tow to the unmanned <a href="/wiki/Barge" title="Barge">barge</a> <i>Portland Star</i> on October 19. The barge was carrying over 18,000 tonnes of <a href="/wiki/Cement" title="Cement">cement</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diesel_fuel" title="Diesel fuel">diesel fuel</a> and was later reported to have sunk in the <a href="/wiki/Fortune_Bay" title="Fortune Bay">Fortune Bay</a>. Damage was reported to numerous pleasure craft around the bay due to the waves;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canada_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-Canada-7">[7]</a></sup> two sailboats were toppled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbc_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbc-14">[14]</a></sup> Also, ferry service between Newfoundland and <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> was disrupted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canada_7-7"><a href="#cite_note-Canada-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Several offshore buoys and ships reported high winds and low barometric pressure,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> as well as waves of up to 16.9 m (55 ft).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHC_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-CHC-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">Elsewhere</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michael_(2000)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 14 and October 15, the <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">frontal boundary</a> associated with the hurricane's precursor dropped heavy rainfall on Bermuda, amounting to 76 mm (3.0 in). However, as a tropical cyclone, Michael remained far from the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Impact outside Newfoundland was generally light. However, rainfall was greater in some areas of Nova Scotia than in Newfoundland, where precipitation reached 95.7 mm (3.77 in) over <a href="/wiki/North_Mountain_(Nova_Scotia)" title="North Mountain (Nova Scotia)">North Mountain</a>. Light rain was also reported in <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>. Effects from the storm in the United States were minimal, limited to light rainfall in the state of Maine which totaled less than 51 mm (2.0 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MichaelRain_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-MichaelRain-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
