<p><b>The Fabulous Hurricanes</b> (formerly The Hurricanes) are a <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_and_blues" title="Rhythm and blues">rhythm &amp; blues</a> group originally from Edinburgh, now based in London.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="About">About</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: About">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The four-piece band consists of a drummer, bassist, guitarist and keyboardist, with three members of the group singing. The band play 1950s <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock and roll</a> and 1960s rhythm and blues with a modern twist covering various artists including <a href="/wiki/Ray_Charles" title="Ray Charles">Ray Charles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dr._Feelgood_(band)" title="Dr. Feelgood (band)">Dr. Feelgood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Lee_Lewis" title="Jerry Lee Lewis">Jerry Lee Lewis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Coasters" title="The Coasters">The Coasters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muddy_Waters" title="Muddy Waters">Muddy Waters</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Progression">Progression</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Progression">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After all meeting at school in 2004, the group began performing around Scotland, building up a following. What followed was national radio plays on XFM Scotland (now rebranded as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galaxy_Scotland" title="Galaxy Scotland">Galaxy Scotland</a>) and <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Scotland" title="BBC Radio Scotland">BBC Radio Scotland</a> and articles have appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_List_(magazine)" title="The List (magazine)">The List</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fest_Magazine" title="Fest Magazine">Edinburgh Festival Magazine</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FestHurricanes_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FestHurricanes-1">[1]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Evening_News" title="Edinburgh Evening News">Edinburgh Evening News</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Scotsman" title="The Scotsman">The Scotsman</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheListHurricanes_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheListHurricanes-2">[2]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Skinny_(magazine)" title="The Skinny (magazine)">The Skinny</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WinterDanceParty_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-WinterDanceParty-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2007 and 2008, The Fabulous Hurricanes played a string of shows most notably supporting <a href="/wiki/Neils_Children" title="Neils Children">Neils Children</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bombay_Bicycle_Club" title="Bombay Bicycle Club">Bombay Bicycle Club</a>, and they were voted number 4 on the list of <a class="external text" href="http://living.scotsman.com/features/Best-unsigned-band.3632119.jp" rel="nofollow">'Scotland's Best Unsigned Bands'</a> by <i>The Scotsman</i>. In 2008, the band were sent a letter by <a href="/wiki/Paul_McCartney" title="Paul McCartney">Paul McCartney</a> inviting them to his Liverpool concert after he heard their music, and in 2010 the band performed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitty_Daisy_%26_Lewis" title="Kitty Daisy &amp; Lewis">Kitty Daisy &amp; Lewis</a> to a sold-out crowd in Edinburgh. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheHurricanesWebsite_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheHurricanesWebsite-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
