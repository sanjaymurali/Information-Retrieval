<p><b>Expedition 18</b> was the 18th permanent crew of the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> (ISS). The first two crew members, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Fincke" title="Michael Fincke">Michael Fincke</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuri_Lonchakov" title="Yuri Lonchakov">Yuri Lonchakov</a> were launched on 12 October 2008, aboard <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-13" title="Soyuz TMA-13">Soyuz TMA-13</a>. With them was astronaut <a href="/wiki/Sandra_Magnus" title="Sandra Magnus">Sandra Magnus</a>, who joined the Expedition 18 crew after launching on <a href="/wiki/STS-126" title="STS-126">STS-126</a> and remained until departing on <a href="/wiki/STS-119" title="STS-119">STS-119</a> on 25 March 2009. She was replaced by <a href="/wiki/JAXA" title="JAXA">JAXA</a> astronaut <a href="/wiki/Koichi_Wakata" title="Koichi Wakata">Koichi Wakata</a>, who arrived at the ISS on <a href="/wiki/STS-119" title="STS-119">STS-119</a> on 17 March 2009. <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Chamitoff" title="Gregory Chamitoff">Gregory Chamitoff</a>, who joined Expedition 18 after <a href="/wiki/Expedition_17" title="Expedition 17">Expedition 17</a> left the station,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasaexp_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nasaexp-1">[1]</a></sup> ended his stay aboard ISS and returned to Earth with the STS-126 crew.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="March_2009_debris_incident">March 2009 debris incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_18&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: March 2009 debris incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 12 March 2009, a piece of <a href="/wiki/Space_debris" title="Space debris">debris</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Payload_Assist_Module" title="Payload Assist Module">upper stage</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Delta_II" title="Delta II">Delta II</a> rocket used to launch a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GPS_satellite" title="GPS satellite">GPS satellite</a> in 1993, passed close to the ISS. The conjunction between the debris and the Space Station was not detected until it was too late to perform a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collision_avoidance_manoeuvre" title="Collision avoidance manoeuvre">collision avoidance manoeuvre</a>. The crew prepared to evacuate the station by closing hatches between modules, and boarding the Soyuz spacecraft that was docked to provide emergency crew escape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nearmiss_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nearmiss-3">[3]</a></sup> The debris did not hit the station, instead it passed by at 16:38 UTC, and the crew were cleared to resume operations about five minutes later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nearmiss_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-nearmiss-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
