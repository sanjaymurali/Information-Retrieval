<p>The <b>Lethbridge Hurricanes</b> are a Canadian <a href="/wiki/Junior_ice_hockey#Major_junior" title="Junior ice hockey">major junior</a> <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> team currently members of the Eastern Conference (Central Division) of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hockey_League" title="Western Hockey League">Western Hockey League</a> (WHL). The team is based in <a href="/wiki/Lethbridge" title="Lethbridge">Lethbridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, and play their home games at the <a href="/wiki/ENMAX_Centre" title="ENMAX Centre">ENMAX Centre</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lethbridge_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the <a href="/wiki/Lethbridge_Broncos" title="Lethbridge Broncos">Lethbridge Broncos</a> returned to their original home in <a href="/wiki/Swift_Current" title="Swift Current">Swift Current</a> following the 1985–86 season, hockey fans in Lethbridge did not have to wait long for a new team; after just one year out of the WHL, Lethbridge returned to the WHL in 1987–88 when the <a href="/wiki/Calgary_Wranglers" title="Calgary Wranglers">Calgary Wranglers</a> moved south to become the Hurricanes.</p>
<p>The team's crowning achievement came in 1996–97, when the Hurricanes captured their first, and to date only, WHL Championship. The Hurricanes then finished as <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Cup" title="Memorial Cup">Memorial Cup</a> runners-up when they lost the <a href="/wiki/1997_Memorial_Cup" title="1997 Memorial Cup">title game</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hull_Olympiques" title="Hull Olympiques">Hull Olympiques</a>. That same year, they also won their division title (only done twice before, in 1989–90 and 1990–91) and the regular season title. In the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_WHL_season" title="2007–08 WHL season">2007–08 season</a>, the Hurricanes won the Eastern Conference Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The team changed its logo for the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_WHL_season" title="2013–14 WHL season">2013–14 season</a> per requests from the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a> (NHL)'s Washington Capitals, who claim the former Hurricanes’ logo was too similar to theirs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Despite the optimism going into the season under new Head Coach <a href="/wiki/Drake_Berehowsky" title="Drake Berehowsky">Drake Berehowsky</a>, who replaced the fired <a href="/wiki/Rich_Preston" title="Rich Preston">Rich Preston</a>, the 2013–14 season would be a record-setting one, but in the wrong categories; the team stumbled out of the gate and dealt with turmoil amongst the players and coaching staff. Some notable occurrences saw veteran forwards Sam McKechnie and Jaimen Yakuboski sent home until both players were dealt to the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Thunderbirds" title="Seattle Thunderbirds">Seattle Thunderbirds</a> in October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A week later, third year defenseman Ryan Pilon requested a trade and left the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Pilon got his wish and was dealt to the <a href="/wiki/Brandon_Wheat_Kings" title="Brandon Wheat Kings">Brandon Wheat Kings</a> in a multiplayer deal shortly afterwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In addition to two more players requesting trades, the team endured a public relations nightmare when Assistant Coach <a href="/wiki/Brad_Lukowich" title="Brad Lukowich">Brad Lukowich</a> walked out on the team following a 3–2 victory over the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Albert_Raiders" title="Prince Albert Raiders">Prince Albert Raiders</a>. Lukowich was terminated "with cause" days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The team hit new lows by scoring a franchise-low 171 goals, allowing 358 goals and earned notoriety by losing two games by a combined score of 22–0; the first humiliation was a 10–0 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Giants" title="Vancouver Giants">Vancouver Giants</a> on January 24, 2014, followed by a 12–0 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Edmonton_Oil_Kings" title="Edmonton Oil Kings">Edmonton Oil Kings</a> on February 17. The team capped off the season on a 15-game losing streak, finishing the year at 12–55–2–3 with 29 points, the League's lowest point total, placing them in last place in the entire WHL. The 12 wins and 29 points also set records for fewest wins and fewest points in the 26-year history of the Lethbridge Hurricanes, and the 46-year history of the franchise that began as the Winnipeg Jets.</p>
<p>In recent years, the community-owned franchise has faced serious financial problems, which came to light during the 2013–14 season. The team lost upwards of $1.25 million in a two-year period<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and has gone as far as having to scale back on their marketing campaigns and player accommodations on road trips. In March 2014, the team revealed it had to take out a <a href="/wiki/Line_of_credit" title="Line of credit">line of credit</a> in order to meet financial goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The financial situation of the team has led to internet rumours of the team possibly being sold to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/True_North_Sports_and_Entertainment" title="True North Sports and Entertainment">True North Sports and Entertainment</a> and relocated to <a href="/wiki/Winnipeg" title="Winnipeg">Winnipeg</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> while former Hurricanes forward and Lethbridge native <a href="/wiki/Kris_Versteeg" title="Kris Versteeg">Kris Versteeg</a> has publicly stated his desire to purchase the team and keep it in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> As the losses continued to pile up and the fan interest waning, the team fired Head Coach Drake Berehowsky on December 9 and General Manager Brad Robson on December 10, and hired former Prince Albert Raiders Head Coach Peter Anholt to both positions that day.</p>
<p>Anholt stepped down as coach, but stayed on as general manager, and hired 33 year old <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brent_Kisio" title="Brent Kisio">Brent Kisio</a> away from the <a href="/wiki/Calgary_Hitmen" title="Calgary Hitmen">Calgary Hitmen</a> as the team's new head coach.</p>
