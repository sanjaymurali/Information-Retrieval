<p><i><b>The Truth Is...</b></i> is the fourth studio album by rock music group <a href="/wiki/Theory_of_a_Deadman" title="Theory of a Deadman">Theory of a Deadman</a>, released on July 12, 2011. The first single is "Lowlife", which was released to radio stations on May 17, 2011. It went to #1 on the US rock charts. It was co-written by Christine Danielle Connolly.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Truth_Is..._(Theory_of_a_Deadman_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the United States, the album debuted at #8 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart selling 38,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In Canada, the album debuted at #2 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Albums_Chart" title="Canadian Albums Chart">Canadian Albums Chart</a>, selling 8,500 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> This is an increase in sales from their previous album, <i><a href="/wiki/Scars_%26_Souvenirs" title="Scars &amp; Souvenirs">Scars &amp; Souvenirs</a>,</i> which sold 6,000 copies in its first week.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
