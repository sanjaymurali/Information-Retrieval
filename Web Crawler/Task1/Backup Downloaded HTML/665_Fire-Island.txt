<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Fire Island</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Fire Island</b> is the large center island of the <a href="/wiki/Outer_barrier" title="Outer barrier">outer barrier islands</a> parallel to the south shore of <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Sandy" title="Hurricane Sandy">Hurricane Sandy</a> once again divided Fire Island into two islands. Together, these two islands are approximately 31 miles (50 km) long and vary between 520 and 1,310 feet (160 and 400 m) wide. Fire Island is part of <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_County,_New_York" title="Suffolk County, New York">Suffolk County</a>. It lies within the towns of <a href="/wiki/Babylon,_New_York" title="Babylon, New York">Babylon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Islip,_New_York" title="Islip, New York">Islip</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brookhaven,_New_York" title="Brookhaven, New York">Brookhaven</a>, containing two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Village_(New_York)" title="Village (New York)">villages</a> and a number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamlet_(New_York)" title="Hamlet (New York)">hamlets</a>. All parts of the island not within village limits are part of the Fire Island <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a> (CDP), which had a permanent population of 292 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_United_States_census" title="2010 United States census">2010 census</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-1">[1]</a></sup> though that expands to thousands of residents and tourists during the summer months.</p>
<p>The land area of Fire Island is 9.6 square miles (24.9 square kilometres).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_area_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census_area-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>Fire Island lies an average of 3.9 miles (6.2 kilometres) off the south shore of Long Island, but nearly touches it along the east end. It is separated from Long Island by <a href="/wiki/Great_South_Bay" title="Great South Bay">Great South Bay</a>, which spans interconnected bays along Long Island: <a href="/wiki/Patchogue_Bay" title="Patchogue Bay">Patchogue Bay</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bellport_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bellport Bay (page does not exist)">Bellport Bay</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Narrow_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Narrow Bay (page does not exist)">Narrow Bay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Moriches_Bay" title="Moriches Bay">Moriches Bay</a>. The island is accessible by automobile near each end: via <a href="/wiki/Robert_Moses_Causeway" title="Robert Moses Causeway">Robert Moses Causeway</a> on its western end, and by William Floyd Parkway (<a href="/wiki/County_Route_46_(Suffolk_County,_New_York)" title="County Route 46 (Suffolk County, New York)">Suffolk County Road 46</a>) near its eastern end. Cross-bay ferries connect to over 10 points in between. Motor vehicles are not permitted on the rest of the island, except for utility, construction and emergency access and with limited beach-driving permits in winter. The island and its resort towns are accessible by boat, seaplane and a number of <a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">ferries</a>, which depart from <a href="/wiki/Bay_Shore,_New_York" title="Bay Shore, New York">Bay Shore</a> (<a href="/wiki/Fire_Island_Ferries" title="Fire Island Ferries">Fire Island Ferries</a>), <a href="/wiki/Sayville,_New_York" title="Sayville, New York">Sayville</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Patchogue,_New_York" title="Patchogue, New York">Patchogue</a>.</p>
<p>Fire Island is located at 40°39'35" north, 73°5'23" west (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Fire_Island&amp;params=40.653_N_-73.126_E_type:isle_region:US-NY"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">40°39′11″N</span> <span class="longitude">73°07′34″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">40.653°N 73.126°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">40.653; -73.126</span></span></span></a></span><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Fire_Island&amp;params=40.653_N_-73.126_E_type:isle_region:US-NY"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">40°39′11″N</span> <span class="longitude">73°07′34″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">40.653°N 73.126°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">40.653; -73.126</span></span></span></a></span></span></span>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-3">[3]</a></sup> According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, Fire Island has a land area of 9.6 square miles (24.9 km<sup>2</sup>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_area_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census_area-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_modifications">Historical modifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Historical modifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The physical attributes of the island have changed over time and they continue to change. At one point it stretched more than 60 miles (97 km) from <a href="/wiki/Jones_Beach_Island" title="Jones Beach Island">Jones Beach Island</a> to <a href="/wiki/Southampton_(village),_New_York" title="Southampton (village), New York">Southampton</a>.</p>
<p>Around 1683, <a href="/wiki/Fire_Island_Inlet" title="Fire Island Inlet">Fire Island Inlet</a> broke through, separating it from Jones Beach Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thompson_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-thompson-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Fire Island Inlet grew to 9 miles (14 km) in width before receding. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_Island_Light" title="Fire Island Light">Fire Island Lighthouse</a> was built in 1858, right on the inlet, but Fire Island's western terminus at Democrat Point has steadily moved west so that the lighthouse today is 6 miles (10 km) from the inlet.</p>
<p>Fire Island separated from Southampton in a 1931 <a href="/wiki/Nor%27easter" title="Nor'easter">Nor'easter</a> when <a href="/wiki/Moriches_Inlet" title="Moriches Inlet">Moriches Inlet</a> broke through. However, this was expected. The inlet widened on September 21, 1938. Moriches Inlet and efforts by local communities east of Fire Island to protect their beach front with <a href="/wiki/Groyne" title="Groyne">jetties</a> have led to an interruption in the <a href="/wiki/Longshore_drift" title="Longshore drift">longshore drift</a> of sand going from east to west and is blamed for <a href="/wiki/Coastal_erosion" title="Coastal erosion">erosion</a> of the Fire Island beachfront.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Between these major breaks there have been reports over the years of at least six <a href="/wiki/Inlet" title="Inlet">inlets</a> that broke through the island but have since disappeared.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fire_Island_Ferry.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="640" data-file-width="480" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Fire_Island_Ferry.JPG/220px-Fire_Island_Ferry.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Fire_Island_Ferry.JPG/330px-Fire_Island_Ferry.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Fire_Island_Ferry.JPG/440px-Fire_Island_Ferry.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fire_Island_Ferry.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Entrance to board the ferry to Cherry Grove, Fire Island</div>

<p>The origin of Fire Island's name is not certain. It is believed its Native American name was <i>Sictem Hackey</i>, which translated to "Land of the Secatogues". The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secatogue" title="Secatogue">Secatogues</a> were a tribe in the area of the current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Town_(New_York)" title="Town (New York)">town</a> of <a href="/wiki/Islip,_New_York" title="Islip, New York">Islip</a>. It was part of what was also called the "Seal Islands".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The name of Fire Island first appeared on a deed in 1789.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nps_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-nps-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Historian <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Bayles&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Richard Bayles (page does not exist)">Richard Bayles</a> suggested that the name derives from a misinterpretation or corruption of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> word <i>vijf</i> ("five"), or in another version <i>vier</i> ("four"), referring to the number of islands near the Fire Island inlet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> At times histories have referred to it in the plural, as "Fire Islands", because of the inlet breaks.</p>
<p>Other versions say the island derived its name from fires built on the sea's edge by Native Americans or by pirates to lure unsuspecting ships into the sandbars. Some say it is how portions of the island look to be on fire from sea in autumn. Yet another version says it comes from the rash caused by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poison_ivy" title="Poison ivy">poison ivy</a> on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thompson_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-thompson-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>While the western portion of the island was referred to as Fire Island for many years, the eastern portion was referred to as Great South Beach until 1920, when widespread development caused the whole land mass to be called Fire Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nps_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-nps-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Settlement">Settlement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Settlement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/William_%22Tangier%22_Smith" title='William "Tangier" Smith'>William "Tangier" Smith</a> held title to the entire island in the 17th century, under a royal patent from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thomas_Dongan" title="Thomas Dongan">Thomas Dongan</a>. The remnants of Smith's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manor_of_St._George" title="Manor of St. George">Manor of St. George</a> are open to the public in <a href="/wiki/Shirley,_New_York" title="Shirley, New York">Shirley, New York</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li>The first large house was built in 1795 in Cherry Grove by Jeremiah Smith. Smith was said to have lured ships to their doom and killed the crews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>In the early 19th century when <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a> was still legal in New York, slave runners built stockades on the island by the Fire Island Inlet.</li>
<li>The first Fire Island Lighthouse was built in 1825 and was replaced by the current lighthouse in 1858.</li>
<li>In 1855, David S.S. Sammis bought 120 acres (0.49 km<sup>2</sup>) near the Fire Island Lighthouse and built the Surf Hotel at what today is Kismet. Sammis operated the hotel until 1892, when the state took it over. In 1908, it became the first state park on Long Island.</li>
<li>In 1868, Archer and Elizabeth Perkinson bought the land around Cherry Grove and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_Island_Pines" title="Fire Island Pines">Fire Island Pines</a>. They built a hotel in 1880.</li>
<li>In 1887, the Coast Guard established 11 manned lifesaving stations on the island.</li>
<li>In 1892, troops were called out to suppress a potential riot at Democrat Point over a cholera panic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>In 1908, Ocean Beach was established, followed by Saltaire in 1910.</li>
<li>In 1921, the Perkinsons sold the land around Cherry Grove in small lots. Bungalows from the newly closed <a href="/wiki/Camp_Upton" title="Camp Upton">Camp Upton</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yaphank,_New_York" title="Yaphank, New York">Yaphank</a> were ferried over the Great South Bay to build the new community. Duffy's Hotel was built in 1930.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Hurricane_of_1938" title="Great Hurricane of 1938">Great Hurricane of 1938</a> devastated much of the island and made it appear undesirable to many. However, Duffy's Hotel remained relatively undamaged. According to legend, the <a href="/wiki/Gay" title="Gay">gay</a> population began to concentrate in Cherry Grove at Duffy's Hotel with <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Isherwood" title="Christopher Isherwood">Christopher Isherwood</a> and <a href="/wiki/W._H._Auden" title="W. H. Auden">W. H. Auden</a> dressed as <a href="/wiki/Dionysus" title="Dionysus">Dionysus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ganymede_(mythology)" title="Ganymede (mythology)">Ganymede</a> and carried aloft on a gilded <a href="/wiki/Litter_(vehicle)" title="Litter (vehicle)">litter</a> by a group of singing followers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The gay influence was continued in the 1960s when male model <a href="/wiki/John_B._Whyte" title="John B. Whyte">John B. Whyte</a> developed <a href="/wiki/Fire_Island_Pines,_New_York" title="Fire Island Pines, New York">Fire Island Pines</a>. The Pines currently has some of the most expensive property on the island and accounts for two-thirds of the island's swimming pools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li>In 1964, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Moses" title="Robert Moses">Robert Moses</a> built the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Captree_Causeway" title="Captree Causeway">Captree Causeway</a> to the western end of the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Opponents, fearing that this was the beginning of plans for the continuation of <a href="/wiki/Ocean_Parkway_(Long_Island)" title="Ocean Parkway (Long Island)">Ocean Parkway</a>, which would have run down the middle of the island, organized and eventually stopped the parkway.</li>
<li>In September 1964, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lyndon_Johnson" title="Lyndon Johnson">Lyndon Johnson</a> signed a bill creating Fire Island National Seashore.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009:_Beach_renourishment">2009: Beach renourishment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2009: Beach renourishment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A 2009 beach renourishment program was credited with saving the island from the full effects of Hurricane Sandy in 2012.</p>
<p>In the winter and spring of 2009, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beach_renourishment" title="Beach renourishment">beach renourishment</a> project was undertaken on Fire Island, with the cooperation of the <a href="/wiki/National_Park_Service" title="National Park Service">National Park Service</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="U.S. Army Corps of Engineers">U.S. Army Corps of Engineers</a>, the Towns of Brookhaven and Islip, and Fire Island residents. The renourishment program involved dredging sand from an offshore borrow area, pumping it onto the beach and shaping the sand into an approved beach face and dune template in front of the communities of Corneille Estates, Davis Park, Dunewood, Fair Harbor, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_Island_Pines" title="Fire Island Pines">Fire Island Pines</a>, Fire Island Summer Club, Lonelyville, Ocean Bay Park, <a href="/wiki/Ocean_Beach,_New_York" title="Ocean Beach, New York">Ocean Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saltaire,_New_York" title="Saltaire, New York">Saltaire</a>, and Seaview. Fire Islanders agreed to a significant <a href="/wiki/Property_tax" title="Property tax">property tax</a> increase to help pay for the project, which was estimated to cost between $23 and $25 million ($6,020 per housing unit), including the cost of environmental monitoring, and was expected to add 1,400,000 cubic meters (1,800,000 cubic yards) of sand in front of the participating communities. The Towns of Brookhaven and Islip, in which the communities are located, issued bonds to pay for the project, backed by the new taxes levied by community Erosion Control Taxing Districts.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012:_Hurricane_Sandy">2012: Hurricane Sandy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2012: Hurricane Sandy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The island was heavily damaged in the high tides associated with <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Sandy" title="Hurricane Sandy">Hurricane Sandy</a> in 2012 including three breaches around Smith Point County Park on the sparsely populated east end of the island. The biggest breach (and politically most difficult one to deal with because it is in a wilderness area) is at Old Inlet in the Otis Pike Wilderness Area just west of Smith Point County Park. Old Inlet is at the site of previous breaches (which have come and gone on their own) was 108 feet wide after the storm on the south end and 1,171 feet on February 28, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Officials have been debating whether to close the breach and let nature take its course as it has been flushing out the Great South Bay and improving water quality. However residents of the bay front communities noted increased flooding after the storm. This flooding was later found to be the result of several nor'easters and unrelated to the breaches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> As of 2015<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fire_Island&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the breach remained open. Officials have moved to close the other two breaches which are on either side of <a href="/wiki/Moriches_Inlet" title="Moriches Inlet">Moriches Inlet</a>—one in Cupsogue County Park and the other one being in Smith Point County Park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reports indicated that 80 percent of the homes particularly those on the east end were flooded and 90 homes were completely destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The storm also tore away about 75 feet of the dune coastline. But Fire Island was not hit as hard as other areas and most of the 4,500 homes on the island survived even if damaged. Officials credited the dune replenishment program with helping to spare the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<p>Except for the western <span class="frac nowrap">4<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> miles (7.2 km) of the island, the island is protected as part of <a href="/wiki/Fire_Island_National_Seashore" title="Fire Island National Seashore">Fire Island National Seashore</a>. <a href="/wiki/Robert_Moses_State_Park_(Long_Island)" title="Robert Moses State Park (Long Island)">Robert Moses State Park</a>, occupying the remaining western portion of the island, is one of the popular recreational destinations in the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> area. The <a href="/wiki/Fire_Island_Lighthouse" title="Fire Island Lighthouse">Fire Island Lighthouse</a> stands just east of Robert Moses State Park.</p>
<p>A memorial to <a href="/wiki/TWA_Flight_800" title="TWA Flight 800">TWA 800</a> is located on the eastern end at <a href="/wiki/Smith_Point_County_Park" title="Smith Point County Park">Smith Point County Park</a> near where the airplane crashed at sea.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Inhabitants">Inhabitants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Inhabitants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fire Island's population varies seasonally. There are few residents in winter months, with the population rising in the late spring to early fall.</p>
<p>Housing is mostly <a href="/wiki/Stick_style" title="Stick style">stick-built</a> <a href="/wiki/Bungalow" title="Bungalow">bungalow</a>-style with generous helpings of <a href="/wiki/Bamboo" title="Bamboo">bamboo</a>. Some are beachfront, built on the dunes of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>, while others are on boardwalks or concrete walks, like a miniaturized city. For year-round residents, there are schools, churches, shops and even a school bus service to Long Island via an off-road modified school bus.</p>
<p>The quiet villages on Fire Island provide solitude, while the larger towns like Ocean Beach and Cherry Grove provide a more social atmosphere with clubs, bars and open air dining. Two of these hamlets, <a href="/wiki/Fire_Island_Pines,_New_York" title="Fire Island Pines, New York">Fire Island Pines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cherry_Grove,_New_York" title="Cherry Grove, New York">Cherry Grove</a>, are destinations for <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a> vacationers.</p>
<p>The incorporated villages of Ocean Beach and <a href="/wiki/Saltaire,_New_York" title="Saltaire, New York">Saltaire</a> within Fire Island National Seashore are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auto-free_zone" title="Auto-free zone">car-free</a> during the summer tourist season (<a href="/wiki/Memorial_Day" title="Memorial Day">Memorial Day</a> through <a href="/wiki/Labor_Day" title="Labor Day">Labor Day</a>) and permit only pedestrian and bicycle traffic (during certain hours only in Ocean Beach). For off-season use, there are a limited number of driving permits for year-round residents and contractors. The hamlet of <a href="/wiki/Davis_Park,_New_York" title="Davis Park, New York">Davis Park</a> allows no vehicles or bicycles year-round.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-23">[23]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 491 people, 138 households, and 77 families residing on Fire Island. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 52.82/mi<sup>2</sup> (21.82/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 4,153 housing units, at an average density of 478.1/mi<sup>2</sup> (184.6/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the town was 96.77% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.65% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.32% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.65% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.61% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 2.90% of the population.</p>
<p>There were 138 households on Fire Island, out of which 25.4% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 48.6% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 2.2% had a female householder with no husband present, and 44.2% were non-families. 34.8% of all households were made up of individuals and 10.1% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.20 and the average family size was 2.90.</p>
<p>Fire Island's population was spread out with 20.6% under the age of 18, 6.5% from 18 to 24, 29.0% from 25 to 44, 33.5% from 45 to 64, and 10.3% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 42 years. For every 100 females there were 133.1 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 143.6 males.</p>
<p>The median income for a household on Fire Island was $73,281, and the median income for a family was $83,672. Males had a median income of $46,875 versus $41,429 for females. The per capita income for Fire Island was $43,681. 0.0% of families and 3.1% of individuals were below the poverty line, including 0.0% of those under age 18 and 8.6% of those age 65 or over.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Emergency_services">Emergency services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fire_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Emergency services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Urgent_Care_on_Fire_Island.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="640" data-file-width="516" height="273" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Urgent_Care_on_Fire_Island.JPG/220px-Urgent_Care_on_Fire_Island.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Urgent_Care_on_Fire_Island.JPG/330px-Urgent_Care_on_Fire_Island.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Urgent_Care_on_Fire_Island.JPG/440px-Urgent_Care_on_Fire_Island.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Fire Island's unique location and constantly changing geography play a major role in the protection of its citizens. Although it is served by nine fire departments and two police departments,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> the seasonal residency and remote driving distance are a challenge to the public safety community. Because there are no roads on inhabited Fire Island, fire department vehicles are heavily modified <a href="/wiki/Four-wheel_drive" title="Four-wheel drive">four-wheel drive</a> with <a href="/wiki/Suspension_lift" title="Suspension lift">suspension lifts</a>, large diameter off-road tires and recovery equipment, which allow them to traverse the sometimes washed-out, loose sand.</p>
<p>Until 1986, there was no ambulance service on Fire Island,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> prompting the village of Saltaire to form its rescue company, later followed by Ocean Beach, and then in 2004 with <a href="/wiki/Fair_Harbor,_New_York" title="Fair Harbor, New York">Fair Harbor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Due to relatively close distances, fire departments on Fire Island are obliged to provide <a href="/wiki/Mutual_aid_(emergency_services)" title="Mutual aid (emergency services)">mutual aid</a> to neighboring communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Some coastal fire departments on Long Island have fully equipped marine rescue and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_boat" title="Fire boat">fire boat</a> units which can cross the <a href="/wiki/Great_South_Bay" title="Great South Bay">Great South Bay</a> to provide necessary assistance.</p>
<p>Fire Island's corps of off-road-capable fire apparatus and the firefighters' training to use them effectively provide much-needed support in the event of a <a href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">wildfire</a>, as was illustrated in the <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Central_Pine_Barrens" title="Long Island Central Pine Barrens">Long Island Central Pine Barrens</a> fires of 1995.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Northwell_Health" title="Northwell Health">Northwell Health</a> recently opened two urgent care facilities on Fire Island (one in <a href="/wiki/Ocean_Beach,_New_York" title="Ocean Beach, New York">Ocean Beach</a> and the other in <a href="/wiki/Cherry_Grove,_New_York" title="Cherry Grove, New York">Cherry Grove</a>). These centers operate during the summer from Memorial Day through Labor Day, and are open for walk-in patient visits seven days a week from 9 to 11 AM and 4 to 6 PM. If patients require medical treatment outside of those hours, the Immediate Care Center’s physician can be reached on call by contacting local police.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>Good Samaritan Hospital Medical Center, Southside Hospital, and Brookhaven Memorial Hospital Medical Center are located directly across the Great South Bay from Fire Island in the Long Island hamlets of <a href="/wiki/West_Islip,_New_York" title="West Islip, New York">West Islip</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bay_Shore,_New_York" title="Bay Shore, New York">Bay Shore</a>, and the village of <a href="/wiki/Patchogue,_New_York" title="Patchogue, New York">Patchogue</a>, respectively. A heliport for medevac helicopter use is adjacent to Good Samaritan Hospital Medical Center. Specially equipped boats provided by the <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_County_Police_Department" title="Suffolk County Police Department">Suffolk County Police Department</a> Marine Bureau docked at the various communities on Fire Island provide emergency transportation to individuals in need of dire medical care. In many cases, Long Island based ambulances will meet the boats once they cross the Bay (roughly 4.5 miles) and then drive individuals the short distance to one of the three hospitals.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_County_Police_Department" title="Suffolk County Police Department">Suffolk County Police Department</a> Marine Bureau is the primary law enforcement agency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ocean_Beach,_New_York" title="Ocean Beach, New York">Ocean Beach</a> also has a dedicated police department of its own.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Criminal proceedings are handled by Suffolk District Court and subjects that are arrested will go to the 3rd, 1st or 5th precinct, or to one of the <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_County_Sheriff%27s_Office" title="Suffolk County Sheriff's Office">Suffolk County Sheriff's Office</a>-run jails. Small claims and property matters are usually handled by the individual village of case origin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> It is common practice for police to write tickets then send unruly visitors off the island via <a href="/wiki/Water_taxi" title="Water taxi">water taxi</a>, at the offender's expense.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_County_Park_Police" title="Suffolk County Park Police">Suffolk County Park Police</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_Park_Police" title="New York State Park Police">New York State Park Police</a> patrol the <a href="/wiki/Robert_Moses_State_Park_(Long_Island)" title="Robert Moses State Park (Long Island)">Robert Moses State Park</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/National_Park_Service" title="National Park Service">National Park Service</a> is stationed at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_Island_Light" title="Fire Island Light">Fire Island Light</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fire_Island_National_Seashore" title="Fire Island National Seashore">Fire Island National Seashore</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> has a base on Fire Island and provides aerial and nautical patrols to the Fire Island National Seashore as well as all beaches in the area. One of the oldest Coast Guard stations in America, Station #25 has been in uninterrupted operation since 1849.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>

<li>The American writer <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Nell_Warren" title="Patricia Nell Warren">Patricia Nell Warren</a>, known as "the mother of <a href="/wiki/Frontrunners" title="Frontrunners">Frontrunners</a>" — the <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">Gay, Lesbian, Bisexual, and Transgender</a> running/walking clubs — locates parts of her 1974 '<a href="/wiki/The_Front_Runner" title="The Front Runner">The Front Runner</a><i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> as well as of</i> Harlan's Race<i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> a 1994 sequel, on Fire Island.</i></li>
<li>Fire Island is featured prominently in <a href="/wiki/Ann_Brashares" title="Ann Brashares">Ann Brashares</a>'s 2008 novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Summer_(of_You_and_Me)" title="The Last Summer (of You and Me)">The Last Summer (of You and Me)</a></i>, about two sisters and a friend who grow up together, vacationing on the island every summer.</li>
<li>Fire Island serves dual meanings as both a vacation destination and a <a href="/wiki/Homoeroticism" title="Homoeroticism">homoerotic</a> euphemism in <i><a href="/wiki/Dress_Your_Family_in_Corduroy_and_Denim" title="Dress Your Family in Corduroy and Denim">Dress Your Family in Corduroy and Denim</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/David_Sedaris" title="David Sedaris">David Sedaris</a>.</li>
<li>The novel <i><a href="/wiki/Auntie_Mame" title="Auntie Mame">Auntie Mame</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Dennis" title="Patrick Dennis">Patrick Dennis</a> uses a Fire Island reference to emphasize that the eponymous character keeps "queer" company.</li>
<li>Fire Island is repeatedly referenced on the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Will_%26_Grace" title="Will &amp; Grace">Will &amp; Grace</a></i>.</li>
<li><i>When Ocean Meets Sky</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> a 2003 documentary detailing the 50-year history of the <a href="/wiki/Fire_Island_Pines,_New_York" title="Fire Island Pines, New York">Fire Island Pines</a> community, had its television premiere on June 10, 2006. The film includes much previously unseen archival footage.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Mockumentary" title="Mockumentary">mockumentary</a> <i>Beach Comber</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> was filmed on Fire Island in 2004.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>'s reality show <i><a href="/wiki/One_Ocean_View" title="One Ocean View">One Ocean View</a></i> (2006) was shot on Fire Island.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Frank_Perry" title="Frank Perry">Frank Perry</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Summer" title="Last Summer">Last Summer</a></i> (1969), adapted by Eleanor Perry from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evan_Hunter" title="Evan Hunter">Evan Hunter</a>'s novel about a summer of sexual discovery on Fire Island, brought an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Oscar</a> nomination for actress <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Burns" title="Catherine Burns">Catherine Burns</a>.</li>
<li>A feature entitled "<a href="/wiki/Shel_Silverstein" title="Shel Silverstein">Shel Silverstein</a> on Fire Island" appeared in the August 1965 <i><a href="/wiki/Playboy" title="Playboy">Playboy</a></i> magazine, with humorous quips about the <a href="/wiki/Gay" title="Gay">gay</a> club scene there.</li>
<li>Fire Island is also the setting of <a href="/wiki/Terrence_McNally" title="Terrence McNally">Terrence McNally</a>'s play <i><a href="/wiki/Lips_Together,_Teeth_Apart" title="Lips Together, Teeth Apart">Lips Together, Teeth Apart</a></i>.</li>
<li>In their song "Raw Deal" <a href="/wiki/Judas_Priest" title="Judas Priest">Judas Priest</a> refer to Fire Island and a gay club called "Spike".</li>
<li>Fire Island is mentioned in the book <i>Kramer Vs. Kramer</i> by novelist Avery Corman.</li>
<li>Family Guy's episode "The Father, the Son and the Holy Fonz" (2005), there is a cutaway gag of "Jaws 5: Fire Island." It shows two presumably gay men swimming in the ocean, with a large shark looking up at them from below. The shark's thoughts can be heard, as he ponders in an effeminate voice whether or not he should eat the swimmers due to the calories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Fire_Island_(TV_series)" title="Fire Island (TV series)">Fire Island</a> series airing in April 2017 is about a group of young gay guys living in a beachfront share house on Fire Island for the summer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
