<p><b>Cochrane District, Ontario</b> is a <a href="/wiki/District" title="District">district</a> and <a href="/wiki/Census_division" title="Census division">census division</a> in <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_Ontario" title="Northeastern Ontario">Northeastern Ontario</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_province" title="Canadian province">Canadian province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>. It was created in 1921 from parts of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timiskaming_District,_Ontario" title="Timiskaming District, Ontario">Timiskaming</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thunder_Bay_District,_Ontario" title="Thunder Bay District, Ontario">Thunder Bay</a> districts.</p>
<p>In 2016, the population was 79,682. The land area of this district is 141,270.41 square kilometres (54,544.81 sq mi),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cp2011_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-cp2011-1">[1]</a></sup> making it slightly smaller than the US State of <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a> and the second largest district in Ontario after <a href="/wiki/Kenora_District" title="Kenora District">Kenora District</a>. The district seat is <a href="/wiki/Cochrane,_Ontario" title="Cochrane, Ontario">Cochrane</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bennet_Lake_Esker_Kame_Complex_Conservation_Reserve" title="Bennet Lake Esker Kame Complex Conservation Reserve">Bennet Lake Esker Kame Complex Conservation Reserve</a> is located in Cochrane District.</p>
