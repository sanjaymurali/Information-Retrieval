<p><i><b>Addicted Romantic</b></i> is the debut <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> by <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Faker_(band)" title="Faker (band)">Faker</a>. <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">Record label</a> <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a> released the album on 13 June 2005. Four singles were released from the album; "Quarter to Three", "The Familiar"/"Enough", "Love for Sale", and "Hurricane". Of these, "Hurricane" was the most successful; it reached number 21 on the <a href="/wiki/Triple_J_Hottest_100" title="Triple J Hottest 100">Triple J Hottest 100</a>, <a href="/wiki/Triple_J_Hottest_100,_2005" title="Triple J Hottest 100, 2005">2005</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The album spent one week on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ARIA_Albums_Chart" title="ARIA Albums Chart">ARIA Albums Chart</a> at number 44, on 26 June 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album was recorded by Faker with Lindsay Gravina, of <a href="/wiki/Magic_Dirt" title="Magic Dirt">Magic Dirt</a> fame, at <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>'s Birdland Studios.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JAward_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-JAward-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMO_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMO-4">[4]</a></sup> The inspiration for "Enough", the first song written from the album, came to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lead_singer" title="Lead singer">lead singer</a> Nathan Hudson from a <a href="/wiki/Patti_Smith" title="Patti Smith">Patti Smith</a> song in 1997. Eight years later, the band worked with Gravina to produce "the winning sound" on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMO_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-AMO-4">[4]</a></sup> He said the biggest challenge in recording was objectivity; choosing which songs to use, as well as improving their studio recording technique. Hudson told <a href="/wiki/Australian_Music_Online" title="Australian Music Online">Australian Music Online</a> that the band wanted people to "draw from their own experiences" when listening to <i>Addicted Romantic</i>, which they hoped contained music people would relate to.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMO_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-AMO-4">[4]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Age" title="The Age">The Age</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Andrew Murfett said the album was influenced by <a href="/wiki/Joy_Division" title="Joy Division">Joy Division</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Cure" title="The Cure">The Cure</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Smiths" title="The Smiths">The Smiths</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Echo_%26_the_Bunnymen" title="Echo &amp; the Bunnymen">Echo &amp; the Bunnymen</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheAge_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheAge-5">[5]</a></sup> while Guy Blackman drew comparisons to <a href="/wiki/Franz_Ferdinand_(band)" title="Franz Ferdinand (band)">Franz Ferdinand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bloc_Party" title="Bloc Party">Bloc Party</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Age2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Age2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Triple_J" title="Triple J">Triple J</a>'s review of the album called it "a dapper slice of post-mod power pop".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Courier_Mail" title="The Courier Mail">The Courier Mail</a></i> said it featured "love-struck schoolboy lyrics and passion",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Love4Real_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Love4Real-8">[8]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Advertiser_(Adelaide)" title="The Advertiser (Adelaide)">The Advertiser</a></i> called it the best Australian album of 2005, praising "urgent vocals and angular guitars".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Guy Blackman of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Age" title="The Age">The Age</a></i> called the Hudson's vocals "unashamedly accented" but said it was "almost refreshing to hear an Australian sing in a thick British brogue rather than the usual faux-American twang".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Age2_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Age2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the release of <i>Addicted Romantic</i>, Faker toured around Australia, supporting <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a> band <a href="/wiki/The_Bravery" title="The Bravery">The Bravery</a>. The concert saw them "blow... [The Bravery] off stage" according to <i>The Courier Mail</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Patrick Lion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Love4Real_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Love4Real-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
