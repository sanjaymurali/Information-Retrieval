<p>The <b>effects of Hurricane Isabel in Pennsylvania</b> were primarily related to tropical storm force winds across much of the state. <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">Hurricane Isabel</a> formed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> on September 6, 2003 in the tropical <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. It moved northwestward, and within an environment of light wind shear and warm waters it steadily strengthened to reach peak winds of 165 mph (265 km/h) on September 11. After fluctuating in intensity for four days, Isabel gradually weakened and made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">Outer Banks</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> with winds of 105 mph (165 km/h) on September 18. It quickly weakened over land and became <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical</a> over western <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> the next day.</p>
<p>On September 19, Tropical Storm Isabel passed through southwestern Pennsylvania, though its large circulation produced tropical storm force winds throughout much of the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The passage of Hurricane Isabel resulted in $160 million in damage (2003 USD, $187 million 2008 USD) and two indirect deaths in Pennsylvania.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> One person suffered from <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide_poisoning" title="Carbon monoxide poisoning">carbon monoxide poisoning</a>, believed to be caused due to improperly ventilated generators in an area affected by the power outages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mono_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mono-2">[2]</a></sup> Moderate winds left about 1.4 million customers without power across the state as a result of trees falling into power lines, with dozens of houses and cars damaged by the trees.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_Hurricane_Isabel_in_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While over the western Atlantic Ocean as a major hurricane, forecasters predicted Isabel would pass through the central portion of the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-franklin39_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-franklin39-3">[3]</a></sup> Successive forecasts shifted the track slightly further to the west, though its track into <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> was not anticipated. Hours before it entered the state, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> continued to predict Isabel would traverse the entire length of the state from south to north and later entering <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> before crossing <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">Lake Ontario</a> into <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-archive_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-archive-4">[4]</a></sup> Around 3% of those who evacuated in North Carolina and in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Shore_of_Virginia" title="Eastern Shore of Virginia">Eastern Shore of Virginia</a> stayed in Pennsylvania during the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assess_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-assess-5">[5]</a></sup> The threat of the hurricane canceled some flights in and out of the state. To compensate, <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a> allowed those flying to or from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Allentown,_Pennsylvania" title="Allentown, Pennsylvania">Allentown</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Harrisburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Harrisburg, Pennsylvania">Harrisburg</a> to reschedule to a later date. <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines" title="American Airlines">American Airlines</a> offered a similar option. <a href="/wiki/United_Airlines" title="United Airlines">United Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_Express" title="United Express">United Express</a> opted to waive charging fees for travelers in and out of the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnn2-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Prior to the arrival of Isabel, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Governors_of_Pennsylvania" title="List of Governors of Pennsylvania">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Ed_Rendell" title="Ed Rendell">Ed Rendell</a> declared a <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a> for the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-7">[7]</a></sup> The Pennsylvania Emergency Management Agency activated a support team to assist Urban Search and Rescue operations as part of the threat from the storm. The state's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_National_Guard" title="United States National Guard">National Guard</a> placed 2,990 guardsmen on Emergency Condition 5 status to be deployed anywhere in the state for emergency support, with other guardsmen readying equipment such as generators, heavy trucks, water trailers, and engineer equipment for deployment. State police officers were readied for deployment, while the state health department contacted hospitals to ensure generators were in working condition. The state Environmental Protection Agency prepared for the storm by monitoring the status of all dams, water treatment facilities, and nuclear plants. In addition, the <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Turnpike" title="Pennsylvania Turnpike">Pennsylvania Turnpike</a> Commission stationed extra workers to patrol the highways in poor drainage areas, with extra equipment prepared for quick response for potential road blockage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pema_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-pema-8">[8]</a></sup> Prior to the arrival of the storm, officials from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PECO_Energy" title="PECO Energy">PECO Energy</a> prepared its largest workforce in its history with 1,500 workers, including employees from <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Edison" title="Commonwealth Edison">Commonwealth Edison</a> in <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Detroit_Edison" title="Detroit Edison">Detroit Edison</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prsm_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-prsm-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_Hurricane_Isabel_in_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Upon making landfall, Isabel produced a <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> that tracked up the <a href="/wiki/Delaware_River" title="Delaware River">Delaware River</a>, with a station along the river in Philadelphia reporting a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Storm_tide" title="Storm tide">storm tide</a> of 9.47 feet (2.89 m). There, the surge flooded low-lying areas along the river.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nwsmh_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-nwsmh-10">[10]</a></sup> The large circulation of the hurricane produced moderate winds throughout the state, with wind gusts in the southeastern portion of the state reaching 60 mph (95 km/h) in <a href="/wiki/Forks_Township,_Northampton_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Forks Township, Northampton County, Pennsylvania">Forks Township</a>. Philadelphia, Pennsylvania also recorded a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wind_gust" title="Wind gust">wind gust</a> of 49 mph (79 km/h),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sencdc_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-sencdc-11">[11]</a></sup> which contributed to severe delays at the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_International_Airport" title="Philadelphia International Airport">Philadelphia International Airport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pia_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-pia-12">[12]</a></sup> The moderate wind gust downed thousands of trees, tree limbs, and power lines. PECO energy reported their worst power outage on record with about 572,425 customers losing electricity. The company estimated it would cost at least $20 million (2003 USD, $23 million 2008 USD) for it to install about 81 miles (130 km) of new cable and install about 7,600 new fuses and circuit breakers. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Edison" title="Metropolitan Edison">Metropolitan Edison</a> power company reported 300,000 customers without power, with 500,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PPL_(utility)" title="PPL (utility)">Pennsylvania Power and Light</a> customers losing electricity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sencdc_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-sencdc-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>Wind gusts reached 60 mph (95 km/h) in northeastern Pennsylvania, resulting in scattered reports of downed trees and power lines. Tens of thousands were left without power. A few downed trees hit cars and houses, with damage totaling about $350,000 (2003 USD, $410,000 in 2008 USD). The fallen trees also closed some roads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nencdc_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-nencdc-13">[13]</a></sup> In the central portion of the state, the storm dropped moderate precipitation of over 3 inches (75 mm), while wind gusts reached 73 mph (117 km/h) at a station in <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Lancaster County, Pennsylvania">Lancaster County</a>. The combination of the wind gusts and moist grounds from previous rainfalls resulted in hundreds of downed tree limbs,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cencdc_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-cencdc-14">[14]</a></sup> primarily in <a href="/wiki/Dauphin_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Dauphin County, Pennsylvania">Dauphin</a> and Lancaster Counties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nwscen_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-nwscen-15">[15]</a></sup> Lancaster County reportedly suffered the worst wind damage since <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hazel" title="Hurricane Hazel">Hurricane Hazel</a> in 1954.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The falling trees downed power lines and poles, causing power outages and some property damage due to the trees and poles hitting houses. One indirect fatality occurred when a motorist drove into a downed tree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cencdc_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-cencdc-14">[14]</a></sup> Two other vehicles in <a href="/wiki/Franklin_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Franklin County, Pennsylvania">Franklin County</a> and a fire truck in <a href="/wiki/York,_Pennsylvania" title="York, Pennsylvania">York</a> were hit and greatly damaged by trees. Downed wires set a <a href="/wiki/Family_Dollar" title="Family Dollar">Family Dollar</a> on fire in <a href="/wiki/Paradise_Township,_York_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Paradise Township, York County, Pennsylvania">Paradise Township</a> and was believed to have started a fire in a church in <a href="/wiki/Ringtown,_Pennsylvania" title="Ringtown, Pennsylvania">Ringtown</a>. Falling trees hit at least eleven houses, with one tree severely damaging a mobile home in <a href="/wiki/Bellwood,_Pennsylvania" title="Bellwood, Pennsylvania">Bellwood</a>. Damage directly from the wind was generally minor, and included a house losing a chimney in <a href="/wiki/Camp_Hill,_Pennsylvania" title="Camp Hill, Pennsylvania">Camp Hill</a>, a carport being blown over in <a href="/wiki/New_Oxford,_Pennsylvania" title="New Oxford, Pennsylvania">New Oxford</a>, and at least four houses reporting roof or siding damage. The hurricane also damaged corn fields near <a href="/wiki/Lancaster,_Pennsylvania" title="Lancaster, Pennsylvania">Lancaster</a>. Downed trees closed or disrupted traffic on at least 56 roads in the region, including <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_6" title="U.S. Route 6">U.S. Routes 6</a> and <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_322" title="U.S. Route 322">322</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Interstate_83" title="Interstate 83">Interstate 83</a> in <a href="/wiki/York_County,_Pennsylvania" title="York County, Pennsylvania">York County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nwscen_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-nwscen-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania" title="Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania">Pittsburgh</a> reported 1.24 inches (31 mm) of precipitation from Isabel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pitts_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-pitts-17">[17]</a></sup> The storm produced wind gusts of up to 60 mph (95 km/h) across the southwestern portion of the state, knocking down trees and power lines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swncdc_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-swncdc-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Allegheny_Energy" title="Allegheny Energy">Allegheny Energy</a> reported 31,184 customers in the southwest portion of the state lost power, primarily in <a href="/wiki/McConnellsburg,_Pennsylvania" title="McConnellsburg, Pennsylvania">McConnellsburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/State_College,_Pennsylvania" title="State College, Pennsylvania">State College</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Waynesboro,_Pennsylvania" title="Waynesboro, Pennsylvania">Waynesboro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iser2_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-iser2-19">[19]</a></sup> Damage in southeastern Pennsylvania totaled $32.2 million (2003 USD; $37.7 million 2008 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sencdc_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-sencdc-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Effects_of_Hurricane_Isabel_in_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Power workers immediately began repairing the power failures by clearing tree branches and replacing fuses and circuit breakers. Peco energy restored power to 72% of the affected customers by two days after the storm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iser2_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-iser2-19">[19]</a></sup> with 85% restored by two nights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iser3_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-iser3-20">[20]</a></sup> By two days after the storm, Pennsylvania Power and Light restored power to about 80% of its impacted customers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iser2_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-iser2-19">[19]</a></sup> with about 93% restored by two nights after the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iser3_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-iser3-20">[20]</a></sup> By five days after Isabel, most power outages in southeastern Pennsylvania were repaired,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iser4_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-iser4-21">[21]</a></sup> with all outages restored by a week after the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iser5_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-iser5-22">[22]</a></sup> Allegheny Power restored power to about 20% of its customers by two days after the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iser2_19-3"><a href="#cite_note-iser2-19">[19]</a></sup> Most power outages for the company were restored by five days after the hurricane,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iser4_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-iser4-21">[21]</a></sup> with all power completely restored by a week after Isabel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iser5_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-iser5-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 26, President Bush declared <a href="/wiki/Chester_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Chester County, Pennsylvania">Chester County</a> as a disaster area following the damage of previous <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Henri_(2003)" title="Tropical Storm Henri (2003)">Henri</a>, Isabel, and severe flooding unrelated to either tropical cyclone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paid_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-paid-23">[23]</a></sup> Officials opened a disaster recovery center in <a href="/wiki/West_Chester,_Pennsylvania" title="West Chester, Pennsylvania">West Chester</a> to provide additional information to disaster victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pima_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-pima-24">[24]</a></sup> By a month after the declaration, 342 homeowners and business owners applied for disaster aid, totaling to around $600,000 (2003 USD, $703,000 2008 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paaid_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-paaid-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
