<p>The <b>Saarland Hurricanes</b> are an <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> team from <a href="/wiki/Saarbr%C3%BCcken" title="Saarbrücken">Saarbrücken</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Saarbrücken is the capital of the federal German state of <a href="/wiki/Saarland" title="Saarland">Saarland</a>, which the club is named after. The club was formed in 1996 in a merger of the two local sides Saarbrücken Wölfe and Dillingen Steelhawks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clubhist_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Clubhist-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As its greatest success, the team has won promotion to the <a href="/wiki/German_Football_League" title="German Football League">German Football League</a> twice, in 2000 and 2010. In this league, it qualified for the play-offs once, in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fhist_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fhist-2">[2]</a></sup> On junior level the club earned its greatest success in 2013 when it won the <a href="/wiki/German_Junior_Bowl" title="German Junior Bowl">German Junior Bowl</a> for the first time.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saarland_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Predecessor_clubs">Predecessor clubs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saarland_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Predecessor clubs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The earliest club the current <i>Hurricanes</i> link their past to are the Saarlouis Hurrikans, a team from <a href="/wiki/Saarlouis" title="Saarlouis">Saarlouis</a>, which was formed in 1982. This team played in the <i>2nd Bundesliga</i> from 1983 onwards, but relocated to <a href="/wiki/Dillingen,_Saarland" title="Dillingen, Saarland">Dillingen</a> in 1984 and became the Dillingen Hurrikanes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clubhist_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Clubhist-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fhist_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fhist-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In between, in 1983, another American football club was formed, the Saarlouis Wölfe, but never reached above local league level and folded in 1989, with its players joining the Dillingen Hurrikanes. Meanwhile, the Dillingen Hurrikanes continued playing in the <i>2nd Bundesliga</i> until 1986, when its division folded and the club had to step a level down. In 1989, after the <i>Wölfe</i> had folded, the club was renamed Dillingen Steelhawks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clubhist_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Clubhist-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Also in 1989, the Saarbrücken Ghostbusters were formed as another American football club in the Saarland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clubhist_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Clubhist-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Dillingen Steelhawks continued competitive league football in the tier-three <i>Regionalliga</i>, at times alongside the <i>Ghostbusters</i>. The team remained at local level until 1993, when it won promotion to the central division of the <i>2nd Bundesliga</i>. At this level, it lasted for only one season, but bounced back in 1995 and returned to the second level for 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fhist_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Fhist-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Saarbrücken Ghostbusters were renamed Saarbrücken Wölfe in 1991 but were unable to rise above the third tier of the league system. By the end of 1996, both the Saarbrücken and the Dillingen club had come to the conclusion that, as individual clubs, no further improvement was possible and agreed on a merger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clubhist_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Clubhist-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saarland_Hurricanes">Saarland Hurricanes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saarland_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Saarland Hurricanes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The team, now as the Saarland Hurricanes and still playing in the <i>2nd Bundesliga</i>, had some instant success, winning its division in 1997 and 2000. In 1997, the <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart_Scorpions" title="Stuttgart Scorpions">Stuttgart Scorpions</a> proved too strong, winning both promotion/relegation matches and barring the <i>Hurricanes</i> from the elite league. In its second attempt, in 2000, the side overcame the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Landsberg_Express&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Landsberg Express (page does not exist)">Landsberg Express</a> in the first game of the promotion round 40-22. Landsberg did not field in the second game and the <i>Hurricanes</i> were qualified for the <a href="/wiki/German_Football_League" title="German Football League">German Football League</a> for the 2001 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fhist_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Fhist-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Saarland Hurricanes were not a strong side in their following time in the GFL, only winning a game each in three of their first four seasons. However, they still managed to finish fifth and away from the relegation rank. The 2005 season was to be the club's best, finishing with a balanced win-loss record and qualifying for the post season for the first time. It then lost to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Braunschweig_Lions" title="Braunschweig Lions">Braunschweig Lions</a> 17-32 in the quarter finals and was eliminated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fhist_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Fhist-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After this one-off success, the club quickly declined again and finished last in its division in 2007. Having to enter the relegation round, the team lost both games to the <a href="/wiki/Munich_Cowboys" title="Munich Cowboys">Munich Cowboys</a> and found itself back in the <i>2nd Bundesliga</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fhist_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-Fhist-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In this league, soon to be renamed <a href="/wiki/German_Football_League_2" title="German Football League 2">German Football League 2</a>, the Saarland Hurricanes took two seasons to rebuild before another division title in 2010 meant direct promotion back to the GFL, courtesy to a league expansion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fhist_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-Fhist-2">[2]</a></sup> The club was only able to win two of their regular season games in 2011 and finished second-last in the GFL South, avoiding relegation because of an even worse performance by the <a href="/wiki/Plattling_Black_Hawks" title="Plattling Black Hawks">Plattling Black Hawks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eleven_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eleven-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, the club came seventh in the southern division of the GFL and thereby failed to qualify for the play-off. It improved to fifth place in 2013 but again did not qualify for the play-offs. The 2014 season saw another sixth-place finish and the club once again missed out on the play-offs. It finished fourth in the GFL south in 2015 and qualified for the play-offs for the second time in the clubs history, losing to the <a href="/wiki/New_Yorker_Lions" title="New Yorker Lions">New Yorker Lions</a> in the quarter finals.</p>
