<p><b>1970 Bhola Cyclone</b> was a devastating <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> that struck <a href="/wiki/East_Pakistan" title="East Pakistan">East Pakistan</a> (present-day <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>) and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>'s <a href="/wiki/West_Bengal" title="West Bengal">West Bengal</a> on November 12, 1970. It remains the deadliest tropical cyclone ever recorded and one of the deadliest <a href="/wiki/Natural_disaster" title="Natural disaster">natural disasters</a>. Up to 500,000 people lost their lives in the storm, primarily as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> that flooded much of the low-lying islands of the <a href="/wiki/Ganges_Delta" title="Ganges Delta">Ganges Delta</a>. This cyclone was the sixth cyclonic storm of the <a href="/wiki/1970_North_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_season" title="1970 North Indian Ocean cyclone season">1970 North Indian Ocean cyclone season</a>, and also the season's strongest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The cyclone formed over the central <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a> on November 8 and traveled north, intensifying as it did so. It reached its peak with winds of 185 km/h (115 mph) on November 11, and made landfall on the coast of East Pakistan (now Bangladesh) the following afternoon. The storm surge devastated many of the offshore islands, wiping out villages and destroying crops throughout the region. In the most severely affected <a href="/wiki/Upazilas_of_Bangladesh" title="Upazilas of Bangladesh">upazila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tazumuddin_Upazila" title="Tazumuddin Upazila">Tazumuddin</a>, over 45% of the population of 167,000 was killed by the storm.</p>
<p>The Pakistani government led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_junta" title="Military junta">junta</a> leader General <a href="/wiki/Yahya_Khan" title="Yahya Khan">Yahya Khan</a> was criticized for its delayed handling of the relief operations following the storm, both by local political leaders in <a href="/wiki/East_Pakistan" title="East Pakistan">East Pakistan</a> and in the international media. During the <a href="/wiki/Pakistani_general_election,_1970" title="Pakistani general election, 1970">election that took place a month later</a>, the opposition <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Awami_League" title="Awami League">Awami League</a> gained a <a href="/wiki/Landslide_victory" title="Landslide victory">landslide victory</a> in the province, and continuing unrest between East Pakistan and the central government triggered the <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_Liberation_War" title="Bangladesh Liberation War">Bangladesh Liberation War</a>, which led to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1971_Bangladesh_atrocities" title="1971 Bangladesh atrocities">widespread atrocities</a> and eventually concluded with the creation of the country of <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>. This storm as well as the Bangladesh Liberation War and <a href="/wiki/1971_Bangladesh_genocide" title="1971 Bangladesh genocide">1971 Bangladesh genocide</a> and the subsequent refugees led ex-<a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">Beatle</a> <a href="/wiki/George_Harrison" title="George Harrison">George Harrison</a> and <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Bengal" title="Music of Bengal">Bengali musician</a> <a href="/wiki/Ravi_Shankar" title="Ravi Shankar">Ravi Shankar</a> to organize <a href="/wiki/The_Concert_for_Bangladesh" title="The Concert for Bangladesh">The Concert for Bangladesh</a> in 1971 in <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Bhola_cyclone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1970_Bhola_cyclone_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/1970_Bhola_cyclone_track.png/220px-1970_Bhola_cyclone_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/1970_Bhola_cyclone_track.png/330px-1970_Bhola_cyclone_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/1970_Bhola_cyclone_track.png/440px-1970_Bhola_cyclone_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1970_Bhola_cyclone_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The remnants of <a href="/wiki/1970_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="1970 Pacific typhoon season">Tropical Storm Nora</a> from the Pacific, which had lasted for two days in the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a>, moved west over the <a href="/wiki/Malay_Peninsula" title="Malay Peninsula">Malay Peninsula</a> on November 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ATCR_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ATCR-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMS-7">[7]</a></sup> The remnants of this system contributed to the development of a new depression in the central <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">Bay of Bengal</a> on the morning of November 8. The depression intensified as it moved slowly northward, and the <a href="/wiki/India_Meteorological_Department" title="India Meteorological Department">India Meteorological Department</a> upgraded it to a cyclonic storm the next day. No country in the region had ever <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named</a> tropical cyclones during this time, so no new identity was given.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMD_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-IMD-8">[8]</a></sup> The storm became nearly stationary that evening near 14.5° N, 87° E, but began to accelerate to the north on November 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMD_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-IMD-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The storm further intensified into a severe cyclonic storm on November 11 and began to turn towards the northeast as it approached the head of the bay. It developed a clear <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a>, and reached its peak later that day with 3-minute sustained winds of 185 km/h (115 mph) and a central pressure of 966 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HPa" title="HPa">hPa</a>. The cyclone made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> on the <a href="/wiki/East_Pakistan" title="East Pakistan">East Pakistan</a> coastline during the evening of November 12, around the same time as the local <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_tide" title="High tide">high tide</a>. Once over land, the system began to weaken; it degraded to a cyclonic storm on November 13 when it was about 100 km (62 mi) south-southeast of <a href="/wiki/Agartala" title="Agartala">Agartala</a>. The storm then rapidly weakened into a remnant low over southern <a href="/wiki/Assam" title="Assam">Assam</a> that evening.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMD_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-IMD-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Bhola_cyclone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Indian government received many ship reports from the Bay of Bengal giving meteorological information on the cyclone, but as <a href="/wiki/India%E2%80%93Pakistan_relations" title="India–Pakistan relations">Indo–Pakistani relations</a> were generally hostile, the information was not passed on to the Pakistani government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lowell-Jan_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lowell-Jan-9">[9]</a></sup> A large part of the population was reportedly taken by surprise by the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1-10">[10]</a></sup> There were indications that the storm warning system that existed in East Pakistan was not used properly, which probably cost tens of thousands of lives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2-11">[11]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Meteorological_Department" title="Pakistan Meteorological Department">Pakistan Meteorological Department</a> issued a report calling for "danger preparedness" in the coastal regions in danger during the day on November 12. As the storm neared the coast, a "great danger signal" was broadcast on Pakistan Radio. Survivors later said that this meant little to them, but that they had recognised a No. 1 warning signal as representing the greatest possible threat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stars-and-Stripes_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stars-and-Stripes-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following two previously <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pre-1980_North_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_seasons#1960_East_Pakistan_I_cyclone" title="Pre-1980 North Indian Ocean cyclone seasons">destructive cyclones</a> in October 1960 which killed at least 16,000 people in East Pakistan,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR-1961_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-MWR-1961-13">[13]</a></sup> the Pakistani government contacted the American government for assistance in developing a system to avert future disasters. Gordon Dunn, the director of the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> at the time, carried out a detailed study and submitted his report in 1961. However, the government did not carry out all of the recommendations Dunn had listed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lowell-Jan_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lowell-Jan-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Bhola_cyclone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the North Indian Ocean is the least active of the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basins" title="Tropical cyclone basins">tropical cyclone basins</a>, the coast of the Bay of Bengal is particularly vulnerable to the effects of tropical cyclones. The exact <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Death_toll" title="Death toll">death toll</a> will never be known, but it is estimated to be 300,000 to 500,000 people in total.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-AMS-7">[7]</a></sup> The Bhola cyclone was not the most powerful of these, however; the <a href="/wiki/1991_Bangladesh_cyclone" title="1991 Bangladesh cyclone">1991 Bangladesh cyclone</a> was significantly stronger when it made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> in the same general area as a Category 5-equivalent cyclone with 260 km/h (160 mph) winds.</p>
<p>The Bhola cyclone is nonetheless the deadliest <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> on record and is one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_natural_disasters_by_death_toll" title="List of natural disasters by death toll">deadliest</a> <a href="/wiki/Natural_disaster" title="Natural disaster">natural disasters</a> in recent history. A comparable number of people died as a result of the <a href="/wiki/1976_Tangshan_earthquake" title="1976 Tangshan earthquake">1976 Tangshan earthquake</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_Indian_Ocean_earthquake" title="2004 Indian Ocean earthquake">2004 Indian Ocean earthquake</a>, but because of uncertainty in the number of deaths in all three disasters, it may never be known which one was the deadliest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="East_Pakistan">East Pakistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Bhola_cyclone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: East Pakistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The meteorological station in <a href="/wiki/Chittagong" title="Chittagong">Chittagong</a>, 95 km (59 mi) to the east of where the storm made landfall, recorded winds of 144 km/h (89 mph) before its <a href="/wiki/Anemometer" title="Anemometer">anemometer</a> was blown off at about 2200 UTC. A ship anchored in the port in the same area recorded a peak gust of 222 km/h (138 mph) about 45 minutes later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-AMS-7">[7]</a></sup> As the storm made landfall, it caused a 10-metre (33 ft) high <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Ganges_Delta" title="Ganges Delta">Ganges Delta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IWM_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-IWM-15">[15]</a></sup> In the port at Chittagong, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Storm_tide" title="Storm tide">storm tide</a> peaked at about 4 m (13 ft) above the average sea level, 1.2 m (3.9 ft) of which was the storm surge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-AMS-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pakistani radio reported that there were no survivors on the 13 islands near Chittagong. A flight over the area showed the devastation was complete throughout the southern half of Bhola Island, and the rice crops of <a href="/wiki/Bhola_Island" title="Bhola Island">Bhola Island</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hatia_Island" title="Hatia Island">Hatia Island</a> and the nearby mainland coastline were destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-3_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-3-16">[16]</a></sup> Several seagoing vessels in the ports of Chittagong and <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Mongla" title="Port of Mongla">Mongla</a> were reported damaged, and the airports at Chittagong and <a href="/wiki/Cox%27s_Bazar" title="Cox's Bazar">Cox's Bazar</a> were under 1 m (3.3 ft) of water for several hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-4_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-4-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Over 3.6 million people were directly affected by the cyclone, and the total damage from the storm was estimated at $86.4 million (1970 USD, $450 Million 2006 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The survivors claimed that approximately 85% of homes in the area were destroyed or severely damaged, with the greatest destruction occurring along the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lancet_p._9_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lancet_p._9-19">[19]</a></sup> Ninety percent of marine fishermen in the region suffered heavy losses, including the destruction of 9,000 offshore fishing boats. Of the 77,000 onshore fishermen, 46,000 were killed by the cyclone, and 40% of the survivors were affected severely. In total, approximately 65% of the fishing capacity of the coastal region was destroyed by the storm, in a region where about 80% of the protein consumed comes from fish. Agricultural damage was similarly severe with the loss of $63 million worth of crops and 280,000 cattle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-AMS-7">[7]</a></sup> Three months after the storm, 75% of the population was receiving food from relief workers, and over 150,000 relied upon aid for half of their food.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lancet_p._11_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lancet_p._11-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Bhola_cyclone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The cyclone brought widespread rain to the <a href="/wiki/Andaman_and_Nicobar_Islands" title="Andaman and Nicobar Islands">Andaman and Nicobar Islands</a>, with very heavy rain falling in places on November 8 and 9. <a href="/wiki/Port_Blair" title="Port Blair">Port Blair</a> recorded 130 mm (5.1 in) of rain on November 8, and there were a number of floods on the islands. The MV <i>Mahajagmitra</i>, a 5,500-ton freighter en route from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calcutta" title="Calcutta">Calcutta</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>, was sunk by the storm on November 12, with the loss of all 50 people on board. The ship sent out a distress signal and reported experiencing hurricane-force winds before it sank.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMD_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-IMD-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> There was also widespread rain in <a href="/wiki/West_Bengal" title="West Bengal">West Bengal</a> and southern <a href="/wiki/Assam" title="Assam">Assam</a>. The rain caused damage to housing and crops in both Indian states, with the worst damage occurring in the southernmost districts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMD_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-IMD-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_toll">Death toll</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Bhola_cyclone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Death toll">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two medical relief surveys were carried out by the Pakistan-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SEATO" title="SEATO">SEATO</a> Cholera Research Laboratory: the first in November and the second in February and March. The purpose of the first survey was to establish the immediate medical needs in the affected regions, and the second, more detailed, survey was designed as the basis for long-term relief and recovery planning. In the second survey, approximately 1.4% of the area's population was studied.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lancet_p._6_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lancet_p._6-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first survey concluded that the surface water in most of the affected regions had a comparable salt content to that drawn from wells, except in <a href="/wiki/Noakhali_Sadar_Upazila" title="Noakhali Sadar Upazila">Sudharam</a>, where the water was almost undrinkable with a salt content of up to 0.5%. The <a href="/wiki/Mortality_rate" title="Mortality rate">mortality</a> was estimated at 14.2%—equivalent to a death toll of 240,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lancet_p._5_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lancet_p._5-23">[23]</a></sup> Cyclone-related <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Morbidity" title="Morbidity">morbidity</a> was generally restricted to minor injuries, but a phenomenon dubbed "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_syndrome&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cyclone syndrome (page does not exist)">cyclone syndrome</a>" was observed. This consisted of severe <a href="/wiki/Abrasion_(medical)" title="Abrasion (medical)">abrasions</a> on the limbs and chest caused by survivors clinging to trees to withstand the storm surge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lancet_p._5_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lancet_p._5-23">[23]</a></sup> Initially, there were fears of an outbreak of <a href="/wiki/Cholera" title="Cholera">cholera</a> and <a href="/wiki/Typhoid_fever" title="Typhoid fever">typhoid fever</a> in the weeks following the storm,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-5_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-5-24">[24]</a></sup> but the survey found no evidence of an epidemic of <a href="/wiki/Cholera" title="Cholera">cholera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smallpox" title="Smallpox">smallpox</a> or any other disease in the region affected by the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lancet_p._5_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-Lancet_p._5-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The totals from the second survey were likely a considerable underestimate as several groups were not included. The 100,000 migrant workers who were collecting the rice harvest, families who were completely wiped out by the storm and those who had migrated out of the region in the three months were not included, and by excluding these groups, the risk of hearsay and exaggeration was reduced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lancet_p._6_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lancet_p._6-22">[22]</a></sup> The survey concluded that the overall death toll was, at minimum, 224,000. The worst effects were felt in <a href="/wiki/Tazumuddin_Upazila" title="Tazumuddin Upazila">Tazumuddin</a>, where the mortality was 46.3%, corresponding to approximately 77,000 deaths in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Upazila" title="Upazila">Thana</a> alone. The mean mortality throughout the affected region was 16.5%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lancet_p._7_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lancet_p._7-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The results showed that the highest survival rate was for adult males aged 15–49, while more than half the deaths were children under ten, who only formed a third of the pre-cyclone population. This suggests that the young, old and sick were at highest risk of perishing in the cyclone and its surge. In the months after the storm, the mortality of the middle-aged was lower in the cyclone area than in the <a href="/wiki/Scientific_control" title="Scientific control">control</a> region, near <a href="/wiki/Dhaka" title="Dhaka">Dhaka</a>. This reflected the storm's toll on the less healthy individuals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lancet_p._7-8_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lancet_p._7-8-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Bhola_cyclone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_response">Government response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Bhola_cyclone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Government response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:40%; padding: 10px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>There have been mistakes, there have been delays, but by and large I'm very satisfied that everything is being done and will be done.</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agha_Muhammad_Yahya_Khan" title="Agha Muhammad Yahya Khan">Agha Muhammad Yahya Khan</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-6_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-6-27">[27]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>The day after the storm struck the coast, three Pakistani gunboats and a hospital ship carrying medical personnel and supplies left <a href="/wiki/Chittagong" title="Chittagong">Chittagong</a> for the islands of <a href="/wiki/Hatia" title="Hatia">Hatia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sandwip" title="Sandwip">Sandwip</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kutubdia" title="Kutubdia">Kutubdia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-4_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-4-17">[17]</a></sup> Teams from the Pakistani army reached many of the stricken areas in the two days following the landfall of the cyclone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-7_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-7-28">[28]</a></sup> Pakistani President <a href="/wiki/Yahya_Khan" title="Yahya Khan">Yahya Khan</a> returned from a state visit to China and overflew the disaster area on November 16. The president ordered "no effort to be spared" to relieve the victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stars-and-Stripes_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stars-and-Stripes-12">[12]</a></sup> He also ordered that all flags should be flown at <a href="/wiki/Half-mast" title="Half-mast">half-mast</a> and announced a day of national mourning on November 21, a week after the cyclone struck land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-8_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-8-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the ten days following the cyclone, one military transport aircraft and three crop-dusting aircraft were assigned to relief work by the Pakistani government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-9_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-9-30">[30]</a></sup> The Pakistani government said it was unable to transfer military helicopters from West Pakistan as the Indian government did not grant clearance to cross the intervening Indian territory, a charge the Indian government denied.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-5_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-5-24">[24]</a></sup> By November 24, the Pakistani government had allocated a further $116 million to finance relief operations in the disaster area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-10_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-10-31">[31]</a></sup> Yahya Khan arrived in <a href="/wiki/Dhaka" title="Dhaka">Dhaka</a> to take charge of the relief operations on November 24. The governor of East Pakistan, Vice Admiral <a href="/wiki/Syed_Mohammad_Ahsan" title="Syed Mohammad Ahsan">S. M. Ahsan</a>, denied charges that the armed forces had not acted quickly enough and said supplies were reaching all parts of the disaster area except for some small pockets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-11_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-11-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>A week after the cyclone's landfall, President Khan conceded that his government had made "slips" and "mistakes" in its handling of the relief efforts. He said there was a lack of understanding of the magnitude of the disaster. He also said that the general election slated for December 7 would take place on time, although eight or nine of the worst affected districts might experience delays, denying rumours that the election would be postponed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-6_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-6-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>As the conflict between East and West Pakistan developed in March, the Dhaka offices of the two government organisations directly involved in relief efforts were closed for at least two weeks, first by a <a href="/wiki/General_strike" title="General strike">general strike</a> and then by a ban on government work in East Pakistan by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Awami_League" title="Awami League">Awami League</a>. Relief work continued in the field, but the long-term planning was curtailed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-12_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-12-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism_of_government_response">Criticism of government response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Bhola_cyclone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Criticism of government response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; padding: 10px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>We have a large army, but it is left to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Marines" title="Royal Marines">British Marines</a> to bury our dead.</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;"><a href="/wiki/Sheikh_Mujibur_Rahman" title="Sheikh Mujibur Rahman">Sheikh Mujibur Rahman</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>Political leaders in East Pakistan were deeply critical of the central government's initial response to the disaster. A statement released by eleven political leaders in <a href="/wiki/East_Pakistan" title="East Pakistan">East Pakistan</a> ten days after the cyclone hit charged the government with 'gross neglect, callous indifference and utter indifference'. They also accused the president of playing down the news coverage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-10_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-10-31">[31]</a></sup> On November 19, students held a march in Dhaka in protest of the speed of the government response,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-13_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-13-35">[35]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maulana_Abdul_Hamid_Khan_Bhashani" title="Maulana Abdul Hamid Khan Bhashani">Maulana Abdul Hamid Khan Bhashani</a> addressed a rally of 50,000 people on November 24, when he accused the president of inefficiency and demanded his resignation. The president's political opponents accused him of bungling the efforts and some demanded his resignation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-11_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-11-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pakistan_Red_Crescent" title="Pakistan Red Crescent">Pakistan Red Crescent</a> began to operate independently of the government as the result of a dispute that arose after the Red Crescent took possession of 20 rafts donated by the <a href="/wiki/British_Red_Cross" title="British Red Cross">British Red Cross</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DailyTribune_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-DailyTribune-36">[36]</a></sup> A pesticide company had to wait two days before it received permission for two of its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crop_duster" title="Crop duster">crop dusters</a>, which were already in the country, to carry out supply drops in the affected regions. The Pakistani government only deployed a single helicopter to relief operations, with <a href="/wiki/Yahya_Khan" title="Yahya Khan">Yahya Khan</a> later stating that there was no point deploying any helicopters from West Pakistan as they were unable to carry supplies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stars-and-Stripes_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Stars-and-Stripes-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>A reporter for the <i><a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Observer" title="Pakistan Observer">Pakistan Observer</a></i> spent a week in the worst hit areas in early January and saw none of the tents supplied by relief agencies being used to house survivors and commented that the grants for building new houses were insufficient. The <i>Pakistan Observer</i> regularly carried front page stories with headlines like "No Relief Coordination", whilst publishing government statements saying "Relief operations are going smoothly." In January, the coldest period of the year in East Pakistan, the National Relief and Rehabilitation Committee, headed by the editor of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Ittefaq" title="The Daily Ittefaq">Ittefaq</a></i>, said thousands of survivors from the storm were "passing their days under [the] open sky". A spokesman said families who were made homeless by the cyclone were receiving up to 250 <a href="/wiki/Pakistani_rupee" title="Pakistani rupee">rupees</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USD" title="USD">US$</a>55 in 1971 dollars; $325 in 2017) to rebuild, but that resources were scarce and he feared the survivors would "eat the cash".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Long-Beach_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-Long-Beach-37">[37]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Awami_League" title="Awami League">Awami League</a>, headed by <a href="/wiki/Sheikh_Mujibur_Rahman" title="Sheikh Mujibur Rahman">Sheikh Mujibur Rahman</a>, swept to a landslide victory in the national elections in December 1970, in part because of dissatisfaction over failure of the relief efforts by the national government. The elections for nine national assembly and eighteen provincial assembly seats had to be postponed until January 18 as a result of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Story-Pak_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-Story-Pak-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>The government's handling of the relief efforts helped exacerbate the bitterness felt in East Pakistan, swelling the resistance movement there. Funds only slowly got through, and transport was slow in bringing supplies to the devastated regions. As tensions increased in March, foreign personnel evacuated because of fears of violence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-12_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-12-33">[33]</a></sup> The situation deteriorated further and developed into the <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_Liberation_War" title="Bangladesh Liberation War">Bangladesh Liberation War</a> in March. This conflict widened into the <a href="/wiki/Indo-Pakistani_War_of_1971" title="Indo-Pakistani War of 1971">Indo-Pakistani War of 1971</a> in December and concluded with the creation of <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>. This was one of the first times that a natural event helped to trigger a civil war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAID_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-USAID-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_response">International response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Bhola_cyclone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: International response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> became one of the first nations to offer aid to Pakistan, despite the generally poor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indo-Pakistani_relations" title="Indo-Pakistani relations">relations</a> between the two countries, and by the end of November had pledged $1.3 million (1970 USD, $6.9 million 2007 USD) of assistance for the relief efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-14_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-14-40">[40]</a></sup> The Pakistani government refused to allow the Indians to send supplies into East Pakistan by air, forcing them to be transported slowly by road instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-15_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-15-41">[41]</a></sup> The Indian government also said that the Pakistanis refused an offer of military aircraft, helicopters and boats from <a href="/wiki/West_Bengal" title="West Bengal">West Bengal</a> to assist in the relief operation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-16_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-16-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>US President <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">Richard Nixon</a> allocated a $10 million ($62 million in 2017) grant to provide food and other essential relief to the survivors of the storm, and the U.S. ambassador to Pakistan pledged that he would "assist the East Pakistan government in every way feasible."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DailyRepublic_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-DailyRepublic-43">[43]</a></sup> The American government also sent a number of blankets, tents and other supplies. Six helicopters, two helicopters at an aid mission in <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a> and four from the United States, were sent to East Pakistan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-17_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-17-44">[44]</a></sup> Some 200,000 tons of wheat were shipped from the United States to the stricken region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Long-Beach_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-Long-Beach-37">[37]</a></sup> By the end of November, there were 38 helicopters operating in the disaster area, ten of which were British and ten American. The Americans had provided about 50 small boats and the British 70 for supply distribution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-14_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-14-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CARE_(relief)" title="CARE (relief)">CARE</a> halted aid shipments to the country the week after the cyclone hit, because of unwillingness to let the Pakistani government handle distribution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DailyTribune_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-DailyTribune-36">[36]</a></sup> However, by January, they had reached an agreement to construct 24,000 cement brick houses at a cost of about $1.2 million ($7 million in 2017).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Long-Beach_37-2"><a href="#cite_note-Long-Beach-37">[37]</a></sup> American concerns about delays by the Pakistani government in determining how the relief should be used meant that $7.5 million ($46 million in 2017) of relief granted by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Congress" title="US Congress">US Congress</a> had not been handed over in March. Much of the money was earmarked to be spent on constructing cyclone shelters and rebuilding housing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-12_33-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-12-33">[33]</a></sup> The American <a href="/wiki/Peace_Corps" title="Peace Corps">Peace Corps</a> offered to send volunteers but were rebuffed by the Pakistani government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-14_40-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-14-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/Task_force" title="Task force">task force</a>, centred on <i><a href="/wiki/HMS_Intrepid_(L11)" title="HMS Intrepid (L11)">HMS Intrepid</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/HMS_Triumph_(R16)" title="HMS Triumph (R16)">HMS Triumph</a></i>, left Singapore for the Bay of Bengal to assist with the relief efforts. They carried eight helicopters and eight landing craft, as well as rescue teams and supplies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DailyRepublic_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-DailyRepublic-43">[43]</a></sup> Fifty soldiers and two helicopters were flown in ahead of the ships to survey the disaster area and bring relief work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-18_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-18-45">[45]</a></sup> The British task force arrived off the Pakistan coast on November 24, and the 650 troops aboard the ships immediately began using <a href="/wiki/Landing_craft" title="Landing craft">landing craft</a> to deliver supplies to offshore islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-11_32-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-11-32">[32]</a></sup> An appeal by the British <a href="/wiki/Disasters_Emergency_Committee" title="Disasters Emergency Committee">Disasters Emergency Committee</a> raised about £1.5 million (£21 million in 2017) for disaster relief in East Pakistan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-14_40-3"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-14-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DEC_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-DEC-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Canadian government pledged $2 million of assistance. France and <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a> both sent helicopters and various supplies worth $1.3 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-14_40-4"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-14-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-18_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-18-45">[45]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pope_Paul_VI" title="Pope Paul VI">Pope Paul VI</a> announced that he would visit Dhaka during a visit to the Far East and urged people to pray for the victims of the disaster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-19_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-19-47">[47]</a></sup> The Vatican later contributed $100,000 to the relief efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-14_40-5"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-14-40">[40]</a></sup> By the start of 1971, four Soviet helicopters were still operating in the region transporting essential supplies to hard-hit areas. The Soviet aircraft, which had drawn criticism from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bengali_people" title="Bengali people">Bengalis</a>, replaced the British and American helicopters that had operated immediately after the cyclone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Long-Beach_37-3"><a href="#cite_note-Long-Beach-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>The government of Singapore sent a military medical mission to East Pakistan which arrived at Chittagong on December 1, 1971. They were then deployed to <a href="/wiki/Sandwip" title="Sandwip">Sandwip</a> where they treated nearly 27,000 people and carried out a <a href="/wiki/Smallpox" title="Smallpox">smallpox</a> vaccination effort. The mission returned to Singapore on December 22, after bringing about $50,000 worth of medical supplies and 15 tons of food for the victims of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MINDEF_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-MINDEF-48">[48]</a></sup> The Japanese cabinet approved a total of $1.65 million of relief funds in December. The Japanese government had previously drawn criticism for only donating a small amount to relief work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-20_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-20-49">[49]</a></sup> The first shipment of Chinese supplies to East Pakistan was a planeload of 500,000 doses of cholera vaccine, which was not necessary as the country had adequate stocks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-15_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-15-41">[41]</a></sup> The Chinese government sent $1.2 million in cash to Pakistan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-14_40-6"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-14-40">[40]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Reza_Pahlavi" title="Mohammad Reza Pahlavi">Mohammad Reza Pahlavi</a> declared that the disaster was also an Iranian one and responded by sending two planeloads of supplies within a few days of the cyclone striking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-13_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-13-35">[35]</a></sup> Many smaller, poorer Asian nations sent nominal amounts of aid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-14_40-7"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-14-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>The United Nations donated $2.1 million in food and cash, whilst <a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a> began a drive to raise a further million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-14_40-8"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-14-40">[40]</a></sup> UNICEF helped to re-establish water supplies in the wake of the storm, repairing over 11,000 wells in the months following the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UNICEF_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-UNICEF-50">[50]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UN_Secretary-General" title="UN Secretary-General">UN Secretary-General</a> <a href="/wiki/U_Thant" title="U Thant">U Thant</a> made appeals for aid for the victims of the cyclone and the civil war in August, in two separate relief programs. He said only about $4 million had been contributed towards immediate needs, well short of the target of $29.2 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-21_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-21-51">[51]</a></sup> By the end of November, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/League_of_Red_Cross_Societies" title="League of Red Cross Societies">League of Red Cross Societies</a> had collected $3.5 million to supply aid to the victims of the disaster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-14_40-9"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-14-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> estimated that it would cost $185 million to reconstruct the area devastated by the storm. The bank drew up a comprehensive recovery plan for the Pakistani government. The plan included restoring housing, water supplies and infrastructure to their pre-storm state. It was designed to combine with a much larger ongoing flood-control and development program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-22_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-22-52">[52]</a></sup> The Bank provided $25 million of credit to help rebuild the East Pakistan economy and to construct protective shelters in the region. This was the first time that the <a href="/wiki/International_Development_Association" title="International Development Association">IDA</a> had provided credit for reconstruction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-World-Bank_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-World-Bank-53">[53]</a></sup> By the start of December, nearly $40 million had been raised for the relief efforts by the governments of 41 countries, organisations and private groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-22_52-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-22-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Concert_for_Bangladesh">The Concert for Bangladesh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Bhola_cyclone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: The Concert for Bangladesh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Concert_for_Bangladesh" title="The Concert for Bangladesh">The Concert for Bangladesh</a></div>
<p>In 1971, ex-<a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">Beatle</a> <a href="/wiki/George_Harrison" title="George Harrison">George Harrison</a> was inspired to organize <a href="/wiki/The_Concert_for_Bangladesh" title="The Concert for Bangladesh">The Concert for Bangladesh</a>, in part from the 1970 Bhola Cyclone, and from the <a href="/wiki/1971_Bangladesh_genocide" title="1971 Bangladesh genocide">1971 Bangladesh Genocide</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_Liberation_War" title="Bangladesh Liberation War">Bangladesh Liberation War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> Although it was the first <a href="/wiki/Benefit_concert" title="Benefit concert">benefit concert</a> of its type, it was extremely successful in raising money, aid and awareness for the region's plight.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_impact">After the impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Bhola_cyclone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: After the impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December, the League of Red Cross Societies drafted a plan for immediate use should a comparable event to the cyclone hit other "disaster prone countries". A Red Cross official stated some of the relief workers sent to East Pakistan were poorly trained, and the organisation would compile a list of specialists. The UN General Assembly adopted a proposal to improve its ability to provide aid to disaster-stricken countries. In 1966, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Crescent" title="Red Crescent">Red Crescent</a> had begun to support the development of a cyclone warning system, which developed into a Cyclone Preparedness Programme in 1972, today run by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Bangladesh" title="Government of Bangladesh">Government of Bangladesh</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_Red_Crescent_Society" title="Bangladesh Red Crescent Society">Bangladesh Red Crescent Society</a>. The programme's objectives are to raise public awareness of the risks of cyclones and to provide training to emergency personnel in the coastal regions of Bangladesh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 30 years after the 1970 cyclone, over 200 cyclone shelters were constructed in the coastal regions of Bangladesh. When the <a href="/wiki/1991_Bangladesh_cyclone" title="1991 Bangladesh cyclone">next destructive cyclone</a> approached the country in 1991, volunteers from the Cyclone Preparedness Programme warned people of the cyclone two to three days before it struck land. Over 350,000 people fled their homes to shelters and other brick structures, whilst others sought high ground. While the 1991 cyclone killed over 138,000 people, this was significantly less than the 1970 storm, partly because of the warnings sent out by the Cyclone Preparedness Programme. However, the 1991 storm was significantly more destructive, causing 1.5 billion dollars in damage (2 billion inflation-adjusted) compared to the 1970 storm's 86.4 million dollars in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
