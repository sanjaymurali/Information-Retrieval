<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Satellite Catalog Number</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Satellite Catalog Number</b> (also known as <b>NORAD Catalog Number</b>, <b>NASA catalog number</b>, <b>USSPACECOM object number</b> or simply <b>Catalog number</b> and similar variants) is a sequential 5-digit number assigned by USSPACECOM (<a href="/wiki/United_States_Space_Command" title="United States Space Command">United States Space Command</a>) to all Earth orbiting <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">satellites</a> in order of identification. Before USSPACECOM, the catalog was maintained by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NORAD" title="NORAD">NORAD</a>. The first catalogued object, catalog number 1957-001A, is the final stage of the <a href="/wiki/Sputnik_(rocket)" title="Sputnik (rocket)">rocket</a> which launched <a href="/wiki/Sputnik_1" title="Sputnik 1">Sputnik 1</a> and remained in orbit for almost two months. (Sputnik itself is 1957-001B and lasted in orbit an additional month.)</p>
<p>As of August 2017<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Satellite_Catalog_Number&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Space_Science_Data_Center" title="National Space Science Data Center">National Space Science Data Center</a> Master Catalog listed more than 42,900 tracked objects including more than 7,800 satellites launched into orbit since 1957.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
