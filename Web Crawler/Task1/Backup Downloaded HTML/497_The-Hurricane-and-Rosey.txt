<p><b><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Helms" title="Gregory Helms">The Hurricane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rosey_(wrestler)" title="Rosey (wrestler)">Rosey</a></b> (also known unofficially as <b>The Super Heroes</b>) were a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a> <a href="/wiki/Tag_team" title="Tag team">tag team</a> in <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">World Wrestling Entertainment</a> (WWE) based on the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_Brand_Extension" title="WWE Brand Extension">brand</a>. While in the WWE, their <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Gimmick" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">ring personas</a> were those of a duo of <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">superheroes</a>.</p>
<p>In mid-2003, The Hurricane "discovered" Rosey and from there on they wrestled together as a team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-August_4.2C_2003_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-August_4.2C_2003-1">[1]</a></sup> Two years later, they won the <a href="/wiki/World_Tag_Team_Championship_(WWE)" title="World Tag Team Championship (WWE)">World Tag Team Championship</a>. During that time, The Hurricane and Rosey joined forces with <a href="/wiki/Stacy_Keibler" title="Stacy Keibler">Stacy Keibler</a>, who adopted a superhero ring persona and the name Super Stacy. After losing the World Tag Team title, the team disbanded.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hurricane_and_Rosey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shortly after Rosey's old tag team partner <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eddie_Fatu" title="Eddie Fatu">Jamal</a> was released from WWE in 2003,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rosey.C2.A0.E2.80.94_slam_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rosey.C2.A0.E2.80.94_slam-2">[2]</a></sup> The Hurricane "discovered" Rosey's potential as a superhero, and christened him Rosey, the Super Hero in Training (or the S.H.I.T.).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-August_4.2C_2003_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-August_4.2C_2003-1">[1]</a></sup> Throughout this time, Rosey came to the ring clad in makeshift superhero costumes, with each clearly depicting the S.H.I.T. reference on the front, and <a href="/wiki/Sketch_comedy" title="Sketch comedy">skits</a> aired weekly showing Rosey training to be a superhero.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Hurricane and Rosey began teaming together, and competed sporadically on <i>Raw</i>, making more regular appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Heat" title="WWE Heat">Heat</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hemlsOWOW_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-hemlsOWOW-4">[4]</a></sup> They faced the teams of <a href="/wiki/Chris_Jericho" title="Chris Jericho">Chris Jericho</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jason_Reso" title="Jason Reso">Christian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Evolution_(professional_wrestling)" title="Evolution (professional wrestling)">Evolution</a>, before beginning a long-running <a href="/wiki/Feud_(professional_wrestling)" title="Feud (professional wrestling)">feud</a> (scripted rivalry) with <a href="/wiki/La_R%C3%A9sistance_(professional_wrestling)" title="La Résistance (professional wrestling)">La Résistance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The feud continued for most of 2004, and The Hurricane and Rosey faced La Résistance several times on <i>Raw</i> and pay-per-view, including at <a href="/wiki/Backlash_(2004)" title="Backlash (2004)">Backlash</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On the July 19, 2004 episode of <i>Raw</i>, Rosey appeared with a new costume, showing that he seemingly graduated to a full superhero.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The Hurricane and Rosey finished out the year competing against teams including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhino_(wrestler)" title="Rhino (wrestler)">Rhyno</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yoshihiro_Tajiri" title="Yoshihiro Tajiri">Tajiri</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sean_Morley" title="Sean Morley">Val Venis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Richards" title="Stevie Richards">Steven Richards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hemlsOWOW_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-hemlsOWOW-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Tag_Team_Champions">World Tag Team Champions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hurricane_and_Rosey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: World Tag Team Champions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In early 2005 at May's <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> event <a href="/wiki/Backlash_(2005)" title="Backlash (2005)">Backlash</a>, The Hurricane and Rosey won the <a href="/wiki/World_Tag_Team_Championship_(WWE)" title="World Tag Team Championship (WWE)">World Tag Team Championship</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types#Gauntlet_match" title="Professional wrestling match types">Tag Team Turmoil match</a> after defeating then-champions <a href="/wiki/Yoshihiro_Tajiri" title="Yoshihiro Tajiri">Tajiri</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_Regal" title="William Regal">William Regal</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Heart_Throbs" title="The Heart Throbs">The Heart Throbs</a> (<a href="/wiki/Romeo_Roselli" title="Romeo Roselli">Romeo</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tom_Matera" title="Tom Matera">Antonio</a>), <a href="/wiki/Mike_Bucci" title="Mike Bucci">Simon Dean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maven_Huffman" title="Maven Huffman">Maven</a> and La Résistance (<a href="/wiki/Rob_Conway" title="Rob Conway">Robért Conway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sylvain_Grenier" title="Sylvain Grenier">Sylvain Grenier</a>) by last eliminating La Résistance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> During this time, the team successfully retained the championship against various teams, including La Résistance, Simon Dean and Maven, and The Heart Throbs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>While on <i>Raw</i>, <a href="/wiki/Stacy_Keibler" title="Stacy Keibler">Stacy Keibler</a> joined forces with The Hurricane and Rosey, acting as their <a href="/wiki/Manager_(professional_wrestling)" title="Manager (professional wrestling)">valet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> She accompanied them to the ring wearing a mask and superhero costume of her own, calling herself "Super Stacy"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> until she was traded to the <a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown!</a> brand on August 22.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Smackdown8-25-05_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Smackdown8-25-05-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the September 5, 2005 episode of <i>Raw</i>, The Hurricane and Rosey were defeated by the team of <a href="/wiki/Lance_Cade_and_Trevor_Murdoch" title="Lance Cade and Trevor Murdoch">Lance Cade and Trevor Murdoch</a> in Cade and Murdoch's debut match for the brand. Although this was a non-title match, the pair earned themselves a title match at the September pay-per-view, <a href="/wiki/Unforgiven_(2005)" title="Unforgiven (2005)">Unforgiven</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> During the title match at the event, Murdoch delivered an <a href="/wiki/DDT_(professional_wrestling)#Elevated_DDT" title="DDT (professional wrestling)">elevated DDT</a> on The Hurricane outside the ring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The DDT caused The Hurricane to suffer a <a href="/wiki/Kayfabe" title="Kayfabe">storyline</a> "<a href="/wiki/Stinger_(medicine)" title="Stinger (medicine)">stinger</a>" and allowed Cade and Murdoch to beat the injured The Hurricane later in the match to win the World Tag Team Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Split_and_Aftermath">Split and Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Hurricane_and_Rosey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Split and Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The loss of the World Tag Team Championship signalled the end of the team; in the following weeks, the pair began a losing streak, mainly caused by The Hurricane's injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> During the October 17 episode of <i>Raw</i>, The Hurricane was assaulted by <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Angle" title="Kurt Angle">Kurt Angle</a> at the request of <a href="/wiki/Vince_McMahon" title="Vince McMahon">Vince McMahon</a>. After the beating, footage was shown of The Hurricane ripping off his mask and striking Rosey (who had come to the ring to his aid).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The following week, The Hurricane <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#No-show" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">did not show up</a> in a World Tag Team title match leaving Rosey to face the champions alone. During the match, The Hurricane (out of costume) appeared at the top of the entrance ramp, now going by his real name, Gregory Helms, and watched as Rosey was double teamed and defeated. After the match, Helms announced that he was fed up with being funny for the crowd and that he had been carrying Rosey as a tag team partner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Helms requested a match with Rosey, and defeated him with a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_attacks#Shining_Wizard" title="Professional wrestling attacks">shining wizard</a> to end their partnership permanently.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> This was one of Rosey's last matches with WWE, as he was released shortly thereafter while Helms went on to win his third <a href="/wiki/WWE_Cruiserweight_Championship_(1991%E2%80%932007)" title="WWE Cruiserweight Championship (1991–2007)">Cruiserweight Championship</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble_(2006)" title="Royal Rumble (2006)">2006 Royal Rumble</a> in January, resulting in Helms returning to the SmackDown! roster and subsequently establishing himself outside of The Hurricane identity, while Stacy Keibler left WWE in early 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cruise2_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-cruise2-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 19, 2017, Rosey died from congestive heart failure at the age of 47, thus ending any possibility of a reunion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
