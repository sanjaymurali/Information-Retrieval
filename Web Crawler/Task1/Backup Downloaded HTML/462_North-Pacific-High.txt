<p>The <b>North Pacific High</b> is a semi-permanent, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subtropic" title="Subtropic">subtropical</a> <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">anticyclone</a> located in the northeastern portion of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, located northeast of <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> and west of <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. It is strongest during the northern hemisphere summer and shifts towards the equator during the winter, when the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Low" title="Aleutian Low">Aleutian Low</a> becomes more active. It is responsible for California's typically dry <a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">summer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Autumn" title="Autumn">fall</a> and typically wet <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">winter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spring_(season)" title="Spring (season)">spring</a>, as well as Hawaii's year-round <a href="/wiki/Trade_winds" title="Trade winds">trade winds</a>.</p>
