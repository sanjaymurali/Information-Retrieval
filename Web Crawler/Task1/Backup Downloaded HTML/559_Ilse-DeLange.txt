<p><b>Ilse Annoeska de Lange</b> (born 13 May 1977) is a <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>, better known as <b>Ilse DeLange</b> (<a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> spelling).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> As a member of <a href="/wiki/The_Common_Linnets" title="The Common Linnets">The Common Linnets</a>, she finished in second place at the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2014" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2014">Eurovision Song Contest 2014</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilse_DeLange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ilse Annoeska de Lange was born on May 13, 1977 in <a href="/wiki/Almelo" title="Almelo">Almelo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. She started her career at the age of 8 as a lip-synching artist, which won her several contests. DeLange drew greater attention after switching to a live repertoire in both regional and national talent shows in <a href="/wiki/Hilversum" title="Hilversum">Hilversum</a> (Dutch city where most TV-programs are recorded). This helped her book some live-performances on national television. She formed a duo with guitarist <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joop_van_Liefland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joop van Liefland (page does not exist)">Joop van Liefland</a>, who introduced her to <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a>.</p>
<p>In 1994, DeLange performed at the Dutch Country Music Awards. Despite positive reviews, nothing happened with her career. She did get in touch with representatives of <a href="/wiki/Warner_Music_Group" title="Warner Music Group">Warner Music</a> but it took several years of negotiation before they decided to offer her a contract. That same year, she participated in the "Zangfestival der Onbekenden" ("Song Contest of the Unknown") in the Dutch city of <a href="/wiki/Eindhoven" title="Eindhoven">Eindhoven</a>. With her implementation of <i>Daddy's Hands</i> by <a href="/wiki/Holly_Dunn" title="Holly Dunn">Holly Dunn</a> and <i>The Song Remembers When</i> by <a href="/wiki/Trisha_Yearwood" title="Trisha Yearwood">Trisha Yearwood</a>, she won the contest and got the opportunity to record a demo. In 1996, DeLange drew the attention from the A&amp;R-manager of Dutch record company BMI Music, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Henk-Jan_Smits&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Henk-Jan Smits (page does not exist)">Henk-Jan Smits</a>. He persuaded her to join the occasional popgroup <i>Wij</i> and they released the single "De Oorlog Meegemaakt" ("Experienced The War"). However the single did not chart the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Top_40" title="Dutch Top 40">Dutch Top 40</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1998-1999:_Breakthrough_with_World_of_Hurt_and_Dear_John">1998-1999: Breakthrough with <i>World of Hurt</i> and <i>Dear John</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilse_DeLange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1998-1999: Breakthrough with World of Hurt and Dear John">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1998 while being a member of the group Cash On Delivery, she traveled to <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S.</a> to record her debut album <i>World Of Hurt</i> with top producer <a href="/wiki/Barry_Beckett" title="Barry Beckett">Barry Beckett</a>. She became well known in the Netherlands, because her album was recorded in the capital of <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a>. Partly because of that, her debut single <i>I'm Not So Tough</i> charted and peaked at #35 in the Dutch <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">Top 40</a>. Her album received big success in her home country, going 5x Platinum. In the Netherlands, Ilse received a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TMF_Award" title="TMF Award">TMF Award</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Edison_Award" title="Edison Award">Edison Award</a> for her musical success in 1999. Although the album was a huge success, next singles "World of Hurt<i>,</i> I'd Be Yours <i>and</i> When We Don't Talk <i>didn't chart. Unfortunately</i> World of Hurt <i>wasn't released in the States, despite some interest due to guest vocals from <a href="/wiki/Vince_Gill" title="Vince Gill">Vince Gill</a>, and Ilse's cover of <a href="/wiki/Beth_Nielsen_Chapman" title="Beth Nielsen Chapman">Beth Nielsen Chapman</a>'s song "World of Hurt", and <a href="/wiki/Patty_Larkin" title="Patty Larkin">Patty Larkin</a>'s song "Lonely Too".</i></p>
<p>A year after DeLange released live-album <i>Dear John</i>, an album full of covers by <a href="/wiki/John_Hiatt" title="John Hiatt">John Hiatt</a>, after a very successful performance at the "Marlboro Flashbacks". Tobacco brand <a href="/wiki/Marlboro_(cigarette)" title="Marlboro (cigarette)">Marlboro</a> organised the tour in a manner of promotion and asked famous Dutch singers to cover their favourite artist. She even went on a mini-tour in support of the album, which lasted several months during 1999. The album went platinum in the Netherlands (with sales of 80,000 copies).</p>
<h3><span id="2000-2002:_Livin'_on_Love_and_fatigue"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000-2002:_Livin.27_on_Love_and_fatigue">2000-2002: <i>Livin' on Love</i> and fatigue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilse_DeLange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000-2002: Livin' on Love and fatigue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rumours of a new album were heard mid-2000. The first single of the album, <i>Livin' on Love</i> (written by <a href="/wiki/Craig_Fuller" title="Craig Fuller">Craig Fuller</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gary_Nicholson_(singer)" title="Gary Nicholson (singer)">Gary Nicholson</a>), was released in October and peaked at #37 in the Dutch <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">Top 40</a>. November saw the release of her sophomore studio album <i>Livin' on Love</i>, which was a musical departure from her debut, leaning more toward pop/rock. The country music-scene wasn't as excited by this album. Although there was a good amount of promotion for the album, it could not compete with the success of <i>World of Hurt</i>, hitting its peak at #5 but still going platinum. Second single <i>I Still Cry</i> didn't chart. In 2001 DeLange started her <i>Livin' on Love</i> Tour, performing in many theaters and concert halls throughout her home country.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, Warner Music attempted to promote this release as her "breakthrough" release in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. Although <i>"World Of Hurt"</i> was planned to be released in the States, the record labels in America thought the album was too old-fashioned for the zero's. Ilse set her hopes on the release of <i>"Livin' on Love"</i>, but once again the album was not released for unknown reasons. There were rumours that the American label didn't like the pop/rock-sound of the album. Due to all the business problems and a heavy tour schedule, DeLange became physically exhausted. During a performance in Paradiso, (<a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>), she experienced vocal problems and emotionally told her fans that she couldn't continue the performance. On doctor's advice she took a few weeks rest.</p>

<p>In 2002, DeLange and her partner <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bart_Vergoossen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bart Vergoossen (page does not exist)">Bart Vergoossen</a>, who is the drummer of her live band, went to America for nine months to work on a new album. During the recording sessions DeLange took a greater creative role in her music, including receiving songwriting credit for the entire album. Her writing on was autobiographical. In April 2003 she released <i>Clean Up</i>, her third studio album. This album continued with the pop/rock-sound she experimented with on <i>Livin' on Love</i>. The album debuted at #1 in the charts and got certified gold. Although of the success, the first single <i>No Reason To Be Shy</i> was a commercial failure and the record company decided not to release any further singles, but rather focus on a compilation album. This album was releases in October of that same year and was called <i>Here I Am</i>. The album spawned two singles, <i>Wouldn't That Be Something</i> and <i>All The Answers</i>, which both were a commercial failure.</p>
<h3><span id="2004–2007:_Search_for_new_record_company_and_The_Great_Escape"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2004.E2.80.932007:_Search_for_new_record_company_and_The_Great_Escape">2004–2007: Search for new record company and <i>The Great Escape</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilse_DeLange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2004–2007: Search for new record company and The Great Escape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the beginning of 2004, the Dutch section of record company <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Music" title="Warner Music">Warner Music</a> was disbanded and Ilse lost her contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> A few months later, she collaborated on the song <i>Blue</i> with Italian singer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zucchero" title="Zucchero">Zucchero</a>. The song was a moderate success in the Netherlands, reaching #23 in the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Top_40" title="Dutch Top 40">Dutch Top 40</a>. There was no information about a new record deal, but Ilse stated that she was working on her future.</p>
<p>In the end of 2005 she went on tour, without releasing a new album or even having a record company. Every concert sold out. She began working with producer <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Leonard" title="Patrick Leonard">Patrick Leonard</a> on a new album, which stirred up the fire that she signed a new record deal. Later that year, it was confirmed Ilse signed a new contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Music" title="Universal Music">Universal Music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her first release on the new label was the single <i>The Great Escape</i>, peaking at #11 in the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Top_40" title="Dutch Top 40">Dutch Top 40</a>. Her fourth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_Escape_(Ilse_DeLange_album)" title="The Great Escape (Ilse DeLange album)">The Great Escape</a></i>, was released on June 16, 2006. It went gold only a week after its release and went 2x Platinum only months later. In September the second single <i>The Lonely One</i> was released, which peaked at #12 in the <a href="/wiki/Single_Top_100" title="Single Top 100">Single Top 100</a>. Third single, <i>I Love You</i>, was released in February 2007 and peaked at #23 in the Dutch Top 40. The song was included on the soundtrack for the Dutch movie adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Steel_Magnolias" title="Steel Magnolias">Steel Magnolias</a></i>. Then the song <i>Reach For The Light</i> was released as a promo-only single, nevertheless it charted at #51. In October 2007 she released a live album, simply called <i>Live</i>.</p>
<h3><span id="2008–2010:_Incredible_and_Next_To_Me_EP"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.932010:_Incredible_and_Next_To_Me_EP">2008–2010: <i>Incredible</i> and <i>Next To Me EP</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilse_DeLange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2008–2010: Incredible and Next To Me EP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2008 Ilse stated she was writing and recording a new album in Swedish city <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg" title="Gothenburg">Gothenburg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Before that, she went to England to work with the same songwriters which were working on the debut album of <a href="/wiki/James_Morrison_(singer)" title="James Morrison (singer)">James Morrison</a>. The first single of her forthcoming album was <i>So Incredible</i>, which peaked at #6. The album <i>Incredible</i> was released at October 17, 2008 and went platinum in less than a month. It eventually got 5x Platinum. Next single <i>Miracle</i> came to be her biggest hit to date, reaching the top spot of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Top_40" title="Dutch Top 40">Dutch Top 40</a>. It was written as the title track for the Dutch movie <a href="/wiki/Bride_Flight" title="Bride Flight">Bride Flight</a>. Third single <i>Puzzle Me</i> peaked at #5 and radio-only release <i>We're Alright</i> peaked at #21, in spite of it not being released as a single.</p>
<p>After releasing another live album, Ilse went on and started working on new material. The single <i>Next To Me</i> was released in July, 2010 and reached a #9-spot. Her first EP <i><a href="/wiki/Next_to_Me_(album)" title="Next to Me (album)">Next To Me</a></i>, was released at August 27, 2010. The album only included eight tracks, Ilse explaining that "...she wanted to release new material more often, but due to a heavy touring schedule and contractual obligations didn't have the time to record a whole album".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Second single <i>Beautiful Distraction</i> charted at #18 and third single <i>Carousel</i> peaked at #32. In early 2011, <i>Next To Me</i> has been certified 2x Platinum.</p>
<h3><span id="2011–2012:_Shelved_album_DoLuv2LuvU_and_Eye_Of_The_Hurricane"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.932012:_Shelved_album_DoLuv2LuvU_and_Eye_Of_The_Hurricane">2011–2012: Shelved album <i>DoLuv2LuvU</i> and <i>Eye Of The Hurricane</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilse_DeLange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2011–2012: Shelved album DoLuv2LuvU and Eye Of The Hurricane">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In November 2011 DeLange released her new single <i>DoLuv2LuvU</i>. The song was the theme song of <a href="/wiki/Serious_Request" title="Serious Request">Serious Request</a> 2011, a big charity-event held yearly by Dutch radio station <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3FM" title="3FM">3FM</a>. While promoting the single, DeLane heard her father was incurably ill. She immediately stopped all kinds of promotion and postponed the recording process of her new album <i>DoLuv2LuvU</i>. Though the song wasn't promoted, it still charted at #14. A day after her father's death, at January 10, 2012, she posted an acoustic version of her song <i>Without You</i> on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>, which featured on her 2007 live album. Later that year, Ilse revealed that the <i>DoLuv2LuvU</i>-album was shelved and she started recording a new album. This is because she didn't feel the flow of the album anymore after her father had died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She doesn't rule out the possibility it's going to be released in the future. In June 2012, the first single from her sophomore album, <i>Hurricane</i> was released. It charted at #23 in the Dutch <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">Top 40</a>. Her seventh album <i>Eye Of The Hurricane</i>, was released September 14, 2012 and got Platinum in only a few weeks. Second single <i>Winter of Love</i> rose to #20 and third single <i>We Are One</i> got to #33.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013-present:_The_Voice_of_Holland_and_The_Common_Linnets">2013-present: <i>The Voice of Holland</i> and <i>The Common Linnets</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilse_DeLange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2013-present: The Voice of Holland and The Common Linnets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, DeLange replaced <a href="/wiki/Nick_%26_Simon" title="Nick &amp; Simon">Nick &amp; Simon</a> as a coach on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice_of_Holland" title="The Voice of Holland">The Voice of Holland</a></i> for its <a href="/wiki/The_Voice_of_Holland_(season_4)" title="The Voice of Holland (season 4)">fourth season</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Marco_Borsato" title="Marco Borsato">Marco Borsato</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trijntje_Oosterhuis" title="Trijntje Oosterhuis">Trijntje Oosterhuis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ali_B" title="Ali B">Ali B</a> (who replaced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roel_van_Velzen" title="Roel van Velzen">Roel van Velzen</a>). When the live shows started, DeLange released her second compilation album, called <i>After The Hurricane</i>. The first single <i>Blue Bittersweet</i> was written as a soundtrack for the Dutch movie "Het Diner" ("Dinner") and charted to #8.</p>
<p>DeLange <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2014" title="Netherlands in the Eurovision Song Contest 2014">represented the Netherlands</a>, with singer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waylon_(Dutch_singer)" title="Waylon (Dutch singer)">Waylon</a> as <a href="/wiki/The_Common_Linnets" title="The Common Linnets">The Common Linnets</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2014" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2014">Eurovision Song Contest 2014</a> in Copenhagen, Denmark. They took second place after the winner <a href="/wiki/Conchita_Wurst" title="Conchita Wurst">Conchita Wurst</a> from <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>. She returned to <i>The Voice of Holland</i> as a coach for its <a href="/wiki/The_Voice_of_Holland_(season_5)" title="The Voice of Holland (season 5)">fifth season</a> in August. On January 15, 2015, it was announced that DeLange would be replaced as a coach for the sixth season of <i>The Voice of Holland</i> by <a href="/wiki/Miss_Montreal" title="Miss Montreal">Miss Montreal</a>. DeLange decided she wanted to spend more time on her international career with <a href="/wiki/The_Common_Linnets" title="The Common Linnets">The Common Linnets</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> However, she was announced as the new coach for the fifth season of The Voice Kids, replacing Angela Groothuizen.</p>
