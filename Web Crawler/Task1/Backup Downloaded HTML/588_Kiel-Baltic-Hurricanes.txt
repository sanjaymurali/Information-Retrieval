<p>The <b>Kiel Baltic Hurricanes</b> are an <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Kiel" title="Kiel">Kiel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.</p>
<p>The club has been, of recent, a strong force in the sport in Germany, playing in five consecutive <a href="/wiki/German_Bowl" title="German Bowl">German Bowls</a> from 2008 to 2012, losing the first two, winning the third, and losing the fourth and fifth.</p>
<p>The club is part of the American Sports Club Kiel e.V.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kiel_Baltic_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Founded in 1988, the Kiel Baltic Hurricanes first took part in competitive football in 1989, when they entered the tier-four <i>Landesliga Nord</i>. The club had some quick success, reaching the tier-two <i>2nd Bundesliga Nord</i> by 1993. After back-to-back championships in this league, the Hussicanes played in the <i>1st Bundesliga Nord</i> from 1996. This league would eventually become the <a href="/wiki/German_Football_League" title="German Football League">German Football League</a>. The Hurricanes reached the play-offs from 1997 to 2000, making it to the semi finals once, in 1999, but were relegated in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clubhist_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Clubhist-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Restarting in the tier-three <i><a href="/wiki/American_football_Regionalliga" title="American football Regionalliga">Regionalliga Nordost</a></i>, the club quickly recovered, playing in the GFL once more from 2007 onwards, now with much greater success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clubhist_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Clubhist-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The club came third in its division in 2007 and was knocked-out in the play-off quarter finals, but managed to win its division the following year. The Hurricanes managed to reach their first <a href="/wiki/German_Bowl" title="German Bowl">German Bowl</a> that season, but lost to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Braunschweig_Lions" title="Braunschweig Lions">Braunschweig Lions</a>, who made their last of twelve consecutive German Bowl appearances, ending the golden era of the later club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fhist_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fhist-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kiel came second in 2009, on equal points with the Berlin Adler. The two teams met in the German Bowl, but unlike 2008, when Kiel had defeated Berlin in the semi finals, this time the club from the German capital triumphed. In 2010, Kiel once more topped its division and advanced to the German Bowl, once more against the Berlin Adler. This time, the Kiel Baltic Hurricanes proved the stronger and managed to win their first national German title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fhist_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fhist-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>Kiel won its division again in 2011, defeated the <a href="/wiki/Munich_Cowboys" title="Munich Cowboys">Munich Cowboys</a> in the quarter finals and the <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf_Panther" title="Düsseldorf Panther">Düsseldorf Panther</a> in the semi finals of the play-offs and advanced to the German Bowl again where they met the <a href="/wiki/Schw%C3%A4bisch_Hall_Unicorns" title="Schwäbisch Hall Unicorns">Schwäbisch Hall Unicorns</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eleven_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eleven-4">[4]</a></sup> The 33rd edition was the highest scoring German Bowl so far, ending with a 48-44 victory for the Unicorns, who thereby ended an 18 year title drought for the south. It was also the highest score achieved by any team losing a German Bowl at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, the club once more won its division, only losing one game during the regular season away at Düsseldorf Panther, and reaching the German Bowl again against Schwäbisch Hall Unicorns, losing 53–56 in the championship game and topping the previous year's score. In 2013, the team came in on third place and was knocked out in the semi-finals of the playoffs by Braunschweig.</p>

<p>The club participated in the 2014 European Football League and won the inaugural EFL Bowl when it defeated the <a href="/wiki/Badalona_Dracs" title="Badalona Dracs">Badalona Dracs</a> 40–0 in the final. In the GFL the club came fourth in the northern division in 2014 but lost 24–50 to the Schwäbisch Hall Unicorns in the quarterfinals of the playoffs.</p>
<p>In 2015, the Kiel Baltic Hurricanes competed in the EFL tournament and defended their title in the final against the <a href="/wiki/Allg%C3%A4u_Comets" title="Allgäu Comets">Allgäu Comets</a>. The team finished third in the northern division of the GFL and lost to the Allgäu Comets in the quarter finals of the playoffs.</p>
<p>Despite missing the EFL Bowl in 2016, the Hurricanes GFL season was more successful than the previous years'. The team again finished third in the northern division of the GFL, thus securing a playoff spot for the tenth year in a row. With a huge defensive effort the Canes won the first round (quarterfinal) against Frankfurt Universe 10:3 and advanced to the semifinal for the first time since 2013. Here, they lost 21:38 to the New Yorker Lions. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
