<p><b>Hurricane Isidore</b> was the ninth <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a> and the second hurricane in the <a href="/wiki/2002_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2002 Atlantic hurricane season">2002 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. Isidore was the fifth of eight named storms to occur in September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> peaked as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 3 hurricane</a>, causing damage as well as four fatalities in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, and the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LA_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-LA-2">[2]</a></sup> Isidore is noted for threatening to strike the northern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast" title="Gulf Coast">Gulf Coast</a> as a Category 4 hurricane, but instead striking as a moderate tropical storm due to a track change that brought the storm over the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucatán Peninsula">Yucatán Peninsula</a> for over a day, which significantly weakened the <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">cyclone</a>. Its primary impact was the heavy rainfall which fell across southeast Mexico and from the central United States Gulf coast into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ohio_Valley" title="Ohio Valley">Ohio Valley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DR_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-DR-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Isidore_2002_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="890" data-file-width="1024" height="191" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Isidore_2002_track.png/220px-Isidore_2002_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Isidore_2002_track.png/330px-Isidore_2002_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Isidore_2002_track.png/440px-Isidore_2002_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Isidore_2002_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On September 9, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> moved off the coast of Africa. Despite moving through a dry environment, its convection became organized as an anticyclone developed over the wave. On September 14, convection was well-organized enough around a small closed wind circulation to classify the system as Tropical Depression Ten near <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LA_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-LA-2">[2]</a></sup> Land interaction from <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> later weakened the depression back to a tropical wave. The wave continued west-northwestward through the Caribbean, and redeveloped into a tropical depression on September 17 while 140 miles (230 km) south of Jamaica.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Embedded within a weak steering current, it drifted to the northwest, and strengthened into <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">Tropical Storm</a> Isidore on September 18 near Jamaica.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-5">[5]</a></sup> After grazing the island, it quickly intensified to a hurricane late on September 19 while south of Cuba. Isidore's <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">maximum sustained winds</a> reached 100 miles per hour (160 km/h) near the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isle_of_Youth" title="Isle of Youth">Isle of Youth</a>, and weakened slightly to an 85 miles per hour (137 km/h) hurricane as it made landfall on Cabo Frances late on September 20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">[1]</a></sup> The hurricane crossed the island, then slowed as it moved westward across the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. Favorable conditions aloft with warm water temperatures allowed Isidore to quickly intensify to a peak of 125 miles per hour (201 km/h) winds on September 21.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite <a href="/wiki/Dvorak_technique" title="Dvorak technique">Dvorak</a> satellite estimates of 145 miles per hour (233 km/h), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">reconnaissance aircraft</a> indicated Isidore remained a 125 miles per hour (201 km/h) Category 3 hurricane, with pressures dropping to 934 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (27.6 <a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">inHg</a>), typical of a Category 4 hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It is the lowest minimum pressure ever observed for an Atlantic hurricane which did not exceed Category 3 intensity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">[1]</a></sup> With nearly ideal conditions for development, Isidore was forecast to reach winds of 150 miles per hour (240 km/h) over the northern Gulf of Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> High pressures to its north forced it southward, and Isidore made landfall at <a href="/wiki/Telchac_Puerto" title="Telchac Puerto">Telchac Puerto</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_(state)" title="Yucatán (state)">Yucatán</a> as a major hurricane on September 22. It weakened rapidly as it nearly stalled over Yucatán, and was only a minimal tropical storm after being inland for 30 hours.</p>

Tropical Storm Isidore (left), <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Lili" title="Hurricane Lili">Lili</a> (middle) and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Kyle_(2002)" title="Hurricane Kyle (2002)">Kyle</a> on September 25</div>

<p>The inner core of convection collapsed while over southeast Mexico, and upon moving northward and reaching the Gulf of Mexico again, it was a large but weak tropical storm. Conditions favored significant strengthening, but Isidore did not redevelop central convection until reaching the northern Gulf of Mexico. The strengthening system hit <a href="/wiki/Grand_Isle,_Louisiana" title="Grand Isle, Louisiana">Grand Isle, Louisiana</a> on September 26 as a 65 miles per hour (105 km/h) tropical storm, but weakened quickly into a tropical depression after moving inland. The system raced to the northeast, and became an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> on September 27 before becoming absorbed in a frontal zone over <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> that night.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>Before Isidore became a hurricane, there were fears that the storm would end up being a significant threat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LA_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-LA-2">[2]</a></sup> The upper level environment ahead of the storm was very favorable, and the oceanic heat content was very high.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Just ten months after the destructive <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Michelle" title="Hurricane Michelle">Hurricane Michelle</a>, Hurricane Isidore threatened to cause similar effects in Cuba. In preparation for the storm, about 292,000 people and thousands of farm animals were evacuated in the <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo" title="Pinar del Río">Pinar del Río</a> province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WFP_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-WFP-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cuba_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cuba-12">[12]</a></sup> Hurricane Warnings were posted about 48 hours before landfall, leaving ample time to prepare for the storm.</p>
<p>Once in the Gulf of Mexico, Hurricane Isidore became a major <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 3</a> storm with winds of 125 miles per hour (201 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">[1]</a></sup> The forecasts then predicted Isidore to move westward in the Gulf of Mexico for a few days, before turning northward towards the Gulf Coast as what was predicted by then to be a strong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Category 4</a> hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Initially expected to remain north of the peninsula, hurricane warnings were issued just over a day prior to landfall, with no watch preceding Isidore's passage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> More than 70,000 people were evacuated,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MASON_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-MASON-16">[16]</a></sup> and ports on the Gulf coast of Yucatán were closed. Mexican authorities declared a state of emergency prior to the arrival of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Due to Isidore's unexpected southward turn into the Yucatán Peninsula, Isidore weakened significantly to a tropical storm which limited potential damage along the United States Gulf Coast. In preparation for the hurricane, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> mobilized its members across <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> days before Isidore's expected landfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MASON_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-MASON-16">[16]</a></sup> Volunteers were sent in the days prior to ensure families had a family disaster plan and a disaster supplies kit in their household. Hurricane watches raised for the United States Gulf coast between <a href="/wiki/Cameron,_Louisiana" title="Cameron, Louisiana">Cameron, Louisiana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pascagoula,_Mississippi" title="Pascagoula, Mississippi">Pascagoula, Mississippi</a> late on the morning of September 24 were discontinued early on the morning of September 25, when the storm was no longer expected to restrengthen into a hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Caribbean_islands">Caribbean islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Caribbean islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">Windward Islands</a>, Isidore was a weak tropical depression that caused no reported damage or casualties. The Cayman Islands reported tropical storm force winds, and likely experienced flooding to some degree, but Isidore caused no reported damage or casualties. Isidore brought heavy rains to Jamaica, totalling 27.2 inches (690 mm) at Cotton Tree Gully.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuba">Cuba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cuba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="9600" data-file-width="7200" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Isidore_19_sept_2002_1845Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

</div>

<p>Hurricane Isidore brought torrential rainfall, with many areas reporting over 1 foot (300 mm) of rain. The maximum reported was 21.7 inches (550 mm) at Isabel Rubio in western Cuba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-5">[5]</a></sup> The rainfall, combined with a 12 feet (3.7 m) storm surge, destroyed 77 houses,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WFP_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-WFP-11">[11]</a></sup> caused power outages, uprooted trees and blew off roofs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RW_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-RW-18">[18]</a></sup> Over 130 tobacco drying houses were affected to some degree, damaging valuable tobacco stock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cuba_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cuba-12">[12]</a></sup> A total of 24,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metric_ton" title="Metric ton">metric tons</a> of <a href="/wiki/Citrus" title="Citrus">citrus</a> and 132 metric tons of <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> were lost from the storm. Thousands of livestock perished.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RW_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-RW-18">[18]</a></sup> No deaths were reported.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Southeast_Mexico">Southeast Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Southeast Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="915" data-file-width="861" height="234" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif/220px-Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif/330px-Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif/440px-Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Isidore_2002_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rainfall totals in Mexico and the United States</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Hurricane Isidore was one of only four storms to hit the north side of the Yucatán since records have been kept. It was the first tropical cyclone to hit the area since Tropical Depression Greta in <a href="/wiki/1970_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1970 Atlantic hurricane season">1970</a>, the first tropical storm since <a href="/wiki/1935_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1935 Atlantic hurricane season">1935</a>, the first hurricane since <a href="/wiki/1888_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1888 Atlantic hurricane season">1888</a>, and only major hurricane to ever hit the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The storm shut down oil production of the Mexican state-run oil company, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PEMEX" title="PEMEX">PEMEX</a>. Drilling operations, as well as exports from Mexico, stopped for one week which cost the oil company US$108.7 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SourceMex_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-SourceMex-19">[19]</a></sup> The Gulf Coast island of Isla Arena was nearly covered by Isidore's storm surge. Its residents evacuated prior to the storm's passage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-USA-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hurricane Isidore hit near <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9rida,_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="Mérida, Yucatán">Mérida, Yucatán</a>, as a 125 miles per hour (201 km/h) hurricane, and remained over the area for around 36 hours. Over 30 inches (760 mm) of rainfall fell, with the maximum recorded near Campeche.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DR_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-DR-3">[3]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> reached as high as 6 metres (20 ft) over southern portions of Yucatán state. Nearly one-third of all fishing vessels were damaged by Isidore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWH_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-MWH-21">[21]</a></sup> Nearly 36,500 houses were destroyed in Yucatán from the intense winds,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWH_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-MWH-21">[21]</a></sup> 83,000 homes damaged, and 500,000 were left homeless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SourceMex_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-SourceMex-19">[19]</a></sup> Downed trees and power lines were common over much of the northern Yucatán Peninsula,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and 75% of barns and warehouses were either severely damaged or destroyed. Damage to crops and livestock was extreme; 2,000 square kilometres of maize and 400 square kilometres of fruit trees were damaged across the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> A total of 70% of the poultry production and livestock were lost during the storm in Yucatán. Damage to Mexico's power grid reached US$19.76 million. Over one-fourth of the roads across the peninsula were moderately or severely damaged, which resulted in losses of US$9.88 million to rural areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SourceMex_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-SourceMex-19">[19]</a></sup> Farming in Campeche experienced significant damage, with 30,000 cattle and 100,000 acres (400 km²) of agricultural land lost.</p>
<p>Throughout the Yucatán Peninsula, there were 17 deaths were attributed to Hurricane Isidore, two of which were indirectly related to the storm. Damage in the country totaled $950 million (2002 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-USA-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Central_America">Central America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Central America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Isidore caused heavy rains in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> due to upslope flow from the Pacific Ocean, which led to landslides and flooding across southern and western portions of the country. The rains led to the drowning of two people when they tried to cross a rain-swollen river. Three hundred and fifty households reported losses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RW_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-RW-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-USA-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eastern_United_States">Eastern United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Eastern United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the open Gulf of Mexico, Hurricane Isidore and later <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Lili" title="Hurricane Lili">Hurricane Lili</a> contributed to the shutdown of offshore oil and gas platforms. The effects of back-to-back storms resulted in the loss of production of 14.4 million barrels (2,290,000 m<sup>3</sup>) of oil and 88.9 billion cubic feet (2.52<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>9</sup> m<sup>3</sup>) of natural gas. A storm surge of 8.3 feet (2.5 m) was measured at Rigoletes, Louisiana and at <a href="/wiki/Gulfport,_Mississippi" title="Gulfport, Mississippi">Gulfport Harbor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-5">[5]</a></sup> Hurricane Isidore brought widespread heavy rainfall from the central Gulf coast into the Ohio Valley, with a maximum of 15.97 inches (406 mm) at <a href="/wiki/Metairie,_Louisiana" title="Metairie, Louisiana">Metairie, Louisiana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DR_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-DR-3">[3]</a></sup> The flooding was responsible for moderate crop damage, with a total of $330 million in damage (2002 USD, 360 million 2006 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Isidore claimed five lives in the U.S.: four direct and one indirect. The indirect death was from a man that went into <a href="/wiki/Cardiac_arrest" title="Cardiac arrest">cardiac arrest</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>, whereas the other four were drowning deaths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Isidore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>
<p>The name Isidore was retired in the spring of 2003 and will never be used for an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a> again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> It was replaced with <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ike" title="Hurricane Ike">Ike</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2008_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2008 Atlantic hurricane season">2008 season</a>, which was itself retired after the latter season.</p>
