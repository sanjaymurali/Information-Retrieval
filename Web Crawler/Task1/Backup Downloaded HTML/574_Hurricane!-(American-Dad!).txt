<p>"<b>Hurricane!</b>" is the second episode of the <a href="/wiki/American_Dad!_(season_8)" title="American Dad! (season 8)">eighth season</a> of the animated comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/American_Dad!" title="American Dad!">American Dad!</a></i>, an episode produced for season 7. It first aired on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> in the United States on October 2, 2011. The episode plot mainly revolves around the Smith family, who prepare for evacuation in response to an oncoming <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">hurricane</a>. Reluctant to leave his home, Stan encourages his family to ride out the storm in their house with him. The hurricane sends a flood to the city of Langley Falls, which puts the entire family in danger.</p>
<p>"Hurricane!" is the final part of the <a href="/wiki/Night_of_the_Hurricane" title="Night of the Hurricane">Night of the Hurricane block</a> with <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Guy" title="Family Guy">Family Guy</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cleveland_Show" title="The Cleveland Show">The Cleveland Show</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Reilly_(executive)" title="Kevin Reilly (executive)">Kevin Reilly</a>, the president of the entertainment division of Fox Broadcasting Company, introduced the idea of a crossover to <a href="/wiki/Seth_MacFarlane" title="Seth MacFarlane">Seth MacFarlane</a>, the creator of all three shows. The episode was first announced at the 2010 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con_International" title="San Diego Comic-Con International">San Diego Comic-Con International</a>. The episode plot is loosely modeled to that of the disaster film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Poseidon_Adventure_(1972_film)" title="The Poseidon Adventure (1972 film)">The Poseidon Adventure</a></i>. Originally scheduled to air on May 1, 2011, as the season finale for the seventh season, "Hurricane!" was postponed due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/April_25%E2%80%9328,_2011_tornado_outbreak" title="April 25–28, 2011 tornado outbreak">tornado outbreak that occurred from April 25–28</a>. Other factors even included the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">March 11, 2011 Japanese tsunami</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The episode was well received by television critics, who praised its humor and cultural references. It was viewed by 5.72 million viewers upon its initial airing, while also receiving a 2.6/6 rating in the 18–49 demographic, according to <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_ratings" title="Nielsen ratings">Nielsen ratings</a>. "Hurricane!" was written by Erik Sommers, and directed by Tim Parsons. It featured the guest voices of <a href="/wiki/Mike_Barker_(producer)" title="Mike Barker (producer)">Mike Barker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Edelstein" title="Lisa Edelstein">Lisa Edelstein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Fischer_(actor)" title="Jeff Fischer (actor)">Jeff Fischer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Henry_(voice_actor)" title="Mike Henry (voice actor)">Mike Henry</a>, Matt McKenna, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Michael_Richardson" title="Kevin Michael Richardson">Kevin Michael Richardson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kristen_Schaal" title="Kristen Schaal">Kristen Schaal</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane!_(American_Dad!)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When a hurricane arrives at Langley Falls, <a href="/wiki/List_of_American_Dad!_characters#Main_characters" title="List of American Dad! characters">the Smiths</a> prepare to evacuate their home with their neighbor <a href="/wiki/List_of_American_Dad!_characters#Around_the_neighborhood" title="List of American Dad! characters">Buckle</a> in his amphibious raft. <a href="/wiki/Stan_Smith_(American_Dad!)" title="Stan Smith (American Dad!)">Stan</a>, however, recommends that the family stay home during the storm, believing it is the best way for everyone to be safe. <a href="/wiki/Hayley_Smith_(American_Dad!)" title="Hayley Smith (American Dad!)">Hayley</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeff_Fischer_(American_Dad!)" title="Jeff Fischer (American Dad!)">Jeff</a> also wish to stay to help the animals that have been left behind in the zoo. <a href="/wiki/Francine_Smith" title="Francine Smith">Francine</a> disagrees with Stan's notion, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Smith_(American_Dad!)" title="Steve Smith (American Dad!)">Steve</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roger_(American_Dad!)" title="Roger (American Dad!)">Roger</a> supporting her, as she points out Stan's history with making terrible decisions during crisis situations. But before the debate can be resolved, the seawall breaks and the ocean floods down the street, removing Buckle and his raft out of its path and trapping the Smiths in their home nevertheless. The neighborhood is now flooded, and while the smiths thinks it's too late to escape, the house is drifting along the floodwater's current. In an attempt to save the family from floating into any potential dangerous areas, Stan anchors the home; however the anchoring system also acts as a <a href="/wiki/Hinge" title="Hinge">hinge</a> and rotates the house upside-down, partially submerging it underwater.</p>

<p>The family becomes increasingly frightened and distrustful of him, especially when his latest advice leads Hayley to be attacked by a <a href="/wiki/Shark" title="Shark">shark</a>. With the shark loose in the house, the family fights for survival. Stan loses hope and admits Francine's suggestions are better than his own, until <a href="/wiki/Klaus_Heissler" title="Klaus Heissler">Klaus</a> gives him an encouragement speech which inspires him to prove himself reliable in a crisis once more. Stan obtains a <a href="/wiki/Bear" title="Bear">bear</a> from the zoo to battle the shark, which only further aggravates the situation when the two animals work together to kill the family. Refusing to accept Francine's pleas to just get help, Stan instead throws a javelin that hits his wife in her shoulder instead of the bear. Buckle enters the house with a tranquilizer gun and sedates the two predators before also shooting Stan, who later tells Buckle that he made the right call. Despite the whole ordeal, Stan is no longer convinced that he should stay out of a future crisis.</p>
<p>After the storm clears, Stan looks out to scope the damage done by the storm, only to find himself in a stand-off with <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Brown" title="Cleveland Brown">Cleveland Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Griffin" title="Peter Griffin">Peter Griffin</a>, whose houses have ended up on both sides of the Smiths' house, since the <a href="/wiki/Night_of_the_Hurricane" title="Night of the Hurricane">same hurricane</a> hit their <a href="/wiki/The_Hurricane!" title="The Hurricane!">respective</a> <a href="/wiki/Seahorse_Seashell_Party" title="Seahorse Seashell Party">towns</a> earlier. When Francine comes out the front door, Stan accidentally shoots her, which Peter claims to be "classic <i>American Dad!</i>" due to its unpredictability.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane!_(American_Dad!)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Night_of_the_Hurricane" title="Night of the Hurricane">Night of the Hurricane</a></div>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:30em; padding: 10px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 85%; background-color: #c6dbf7;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>"There were talks initially of doing a crossover with all the characters from all the shows but it became a little bit of a conundrum because you have three different staffs, each of whom is used to writing three different sets of characters. This idea of an outside force that sweeps its way through all three shows seemed like a cool way to accomplish that.</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: right;">—Seth MacFarlane, in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-KR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>The episode was first announced in July 2010 by series creator <a href="/wiki/Seth_MacFarlane" title="Seth MacFarlane">Seth MacFarlane</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con_International" title="San Diego Comic-Con International">Comic-Con International</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego,_California" title="San Diego, California">San Diego, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOX_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOX-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVSquad_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVSquad-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Reilly_(executive)" title="Kevin Reilly (executive)">Kevin Reilly</a>, the entertainment president of the Fox Broadcasting Company, originally pitched the idea for the crossover, which was inspired by theme nights of comedy shows from the 1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KR_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-KR-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CST_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-CST-5">[5]</a></sup> MacFarlane described the crossover event to be an "enormous challenge" and a "substantial undertaking"; he chose to do one central story line so that each writing staff would not have to write stories for unfamiliar characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KR_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-KR-1">[1]</a></sup> He was also willing to do another crossover event if this one received successful ratings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KR_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-KR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Much of the episode plot in "Hurricane!" was modeled after the <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a> <a href="/wiki/Adventure_film" title="Adventure film">adventure</a> <a href="/wiki/Disaster_film" title="Disaster film">disaster film</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Poseidon_Adventure_(1972_film)" title="The Poseidon Adventure (1972 film)">The Poseidon Adventure</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVSquad_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-TVSquad-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVG_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVG-6">[6]</a></sup> In his interview on the plot of the final part of the crossover episode, Mike Barker, a producer for <i>American Dad</i>, stated that "[they] end up flooding the house and turning it upside down." He continued: "Ultimately, the house drifts and ends up in the same neighborhood as the Griffin and Brown homes." The actual crossing over of the event occurs at the end of this episode when Stan gets involved in a stand off with <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Brown" title="Cleveland Brown">Cleveland Brown</a> of <i>The Cleveland Show</i> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Griffin" title="Peter Griffin">Peter Griffin</a> of <i>Family Guy</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVG_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-TVG-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2011, executives of the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox Broadcasting Company</a> officially announced that "Hurricane!" would air on May 1 as part of the <i><a href="/wiki/Night_of_the_Hurricane" title="Night of the Hurricane">Night of the Hurricane</a></i> crossover, along with <i>The Cleveland Show</i> episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Hurricane!" title="The Hurricane!">The Hurricane!</a>" and <i>Family Guy</i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Seahorse_Seashell_Party" title="Seahorse Seashell Party">Seahorse Seashell Party</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVG_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-TVG-6">[6]</a></sup> However, on April 29, it was announced that the crossover event would be removed from the schedule, in response to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/April_25%E2%80%9328,_2011_tornado_outbreak" title="April 25–28, 2011 tornado outbreak">series of tornadoes that killed nearly 300 people in the Southern United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CST_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-CST-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THR_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-THR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-11">[11]</a></sup> The crossover was subsequently replaced by repeats of "<a href="/wiki/I_Am_the_Walrus_(American_Dad!)" title="I Am the Walrus (American Dad!)">I Am the Walrus</a>" from <i>American Dad!</i>, "<a href="/wiki/Brian_Writes_a_Bestseller" title="Brian Writes a Bestseller">Brian Writes a Bestseller</a>" from <i>Family Guy</i>, and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ain%27t_Nothin%27_But_Mutton_Bustin%27" title="Ain't Nothin' But Mutton Bustin'">Ain't Nothin' But Mutton Bustin'</a>" from <i>The Cleveland Show</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THR_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-THR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Futic_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Futic-12">[12]</a></sup> MacFarlane later agreed with the decisions after being consulted with the executives of Fox, and a spokeswoman for the company later announced that the episode would air the succeeding season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THR_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-THR-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>"Hurricane!" was directed by Tim Parsons, in his first directing credit of the season. This episode marked the first time Parsons has directed an <i>American Dad!</i> episode since the <a href="/wiki/American_Dad!_(season_7)" title="American Dad! (season 7)">seventh season</a> episode "<a href="/wiki/I_Am_the_Walrus_(American_Dad!)" title="I Am the Walrus (American Dad!)">I Am the Walrus</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-episode_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-episode-13">[13]</a></sup> It was written by Erik Sommers, which also served as his first writing credit for the season. Sommers' last production credit was the sixth season episode "<a href="/wiki/You_Debt_Your_Life" title="You Debt Your Life">You Debt Your Life</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-episode_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-episode-13">[13]</a></sup> In addition to the regular cast, voice actor <a href="/wiki/Mike_Henry_(voice_actor)" title="Mike Henry (voice actor)">Mike Henry</a>, actress <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Edelstein" title="Lisa Edelstein">Lisa Edelstein</a> and voice actress <a href="/wiki/Kristen_Schaal" title="Kristen Schaal">Kristen Schaal</a> guest starred in the episode as Cleveland Brown, Sharri Rothberg, and Roger's One Night Stand respectively. Recurring voice actors <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Fischer_(actor)" title="Jeff Fischer (actor)">Jeff Fischer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Barker_(producer)" title="Mike Barker (producer)">Mike Barker</a>, Matt McKenna, and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Michael_Richardson" title="Kevin Michael Richardson">Kevin Michael Richardson</a> reprised their roles of Jeff Fischer, Terry Bates, Buckle, and Principal Brian Lewis respectively. In addition to his <i>American Dad!</i> roles of Stan, Roger, and Greg Corbin, MacFarlane reprised his <i>Family Guy</i> role of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Griffin" title="Peter Griffin">Peter Griffin</a>, while Henry guest starred as his role of <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Brown" title="Cleveland Brown">Cleveland Brown</a>. This episode marked the first time Henry made a guest appearance on <i>American Dad</i> since the season four episode "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stan%27s_Night_Out" title="Stan's Night Out">Stan's Night Out</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Edelstein and McKenna reprised their roles of Shari and Buckle, having last done so in the season seven episode "<a href="/wiki/Best_Little_Horror_House_in_Langley_Falls" title="Best Little Horror House in Langley Falls">Best Little Horror House in Langley Falls</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane!_(American_Dad!)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ratings">Ratings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane!_(American_Dad!)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ratings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"Hurricane!" first aired in the United States on October 2, 2011 as part of the animation television night on Fox. It was preceded by episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">The Simpsons</a></i>, and its sister shows <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cleveland_Show" title="The Cleveland Show">The Cleveland Show</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Guy" title="Family Guy">Family Guy</a></i>. It was viewed by 5.71 million viewers upon its initial airing, despite simultaneously airing with <i><a href="/wiki/Desperate_Housewives" title="Desperate Housewives">Desperate Housewives</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazing_Race" title="Amazing Race">Amazing Race</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>, and a match between the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Ravens" title="Baltimore Ravens">Baltimore Ravens</a> and the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Jets" title="New York Jets">New York Jets</a> on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ratings_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-ratings-16">[16]</a></sup> Total viewership for the episode was the third highest of the animation television block on Fox, having moderately higher ratings than that of <i>The Cleveland Show</i> but lower ratings than that of <i>The Simpsons</i> and <i>Family Guy</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ratings_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-ratings-16">[16]</a></sup> "Hurricane!" garnered a 2.6/6 rating in the 18–49 demographic, according to <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_ratings" title="Nielsen ratings">Nielsen ratings</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ratings_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-ratings-16">[16]</a></sup> Total viewership and ratings were slightly down from the previous episode, "<a href="/wiki/Hot_Water_(American_Dad!)" title="Hot Water (American Dad!)">Hot Water</a>", which was watched by 5.83 million viewers and garnered a 3.0/7 rating in the 18–49 demographic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_reviews">Television reviews</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane!_(American_Dad!)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Television reviews">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Critics generally praised the episode, with many deeming "Hurricane!" as the best part of the crossover. Terron Moore of <i><a href="/wiki/Ology_(website)" title="Ology (website)">Ology</a></i> praised the episode, giving it an 8.5 out of ten rating. Points of acclaim went to the humor of the episode, to which Moore opined that it "was packed with a lot of funny lines, a lot of ridiculous Stan antics [...], and a pretty funny arc involving Roger and his girlfriend".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ology_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-ology-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rowan Kaiser of <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> was polarized with the episode. Although she stated that the episode was not bad, Kaiser stated that it "didn't quite work". Kaiser went on to give the episode a 'B-' grade, scoring higher than the <i>Family Guy</i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Seahorse_Seashell_Party" title="Seahorse Seashell Party">Seahorse Seashell Party</a>", but lower than <i>The Simpsons</i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Bart_Stops_to_Smell_the_Roosevelts" title="Bart Stops to Smell the Roosevelts">Bart Stops to Smell the Roosevelts</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-av_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-av-19">[19]</a></sup> Another writer felt that the interactions between Stan and Francine were inferior to previous episodes of the series and wrote, "It breaks away from Francine and Stan's fun relationship and veers into the more conventional cartoon relationship, where the husband is a buffoon and the wife has to keep him check."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-av_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-av-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
