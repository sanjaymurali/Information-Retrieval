<td class="hlist noprint nowrap" colspan="2" style="text-align:center;border-top: 1px solid #aaa; padding-top: 3px;"><a href="/wiki/Australian_region_tropical_cyclone" title="Australian region tropical cyclone">Australian region tropical cyclone seasons</a><br/>
<a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Australian_region_cyclone_season" title="2015–16 Australian region cyclone season">2015–16</a>, <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_Australian_region_cyclone_season" title="2016–17 Australian region cyclone season">2016–17</a>, <b>2017–18</b>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lists_of_tropical_cyclone_names" title="Lists of tropical cyclone names">2018–19</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lists_of_tropical_cyclone_names" title="Lists of tropical cyclone names">2019–20</a></i></td>

<p>The <b>2017–18 Australian region cyclone season</b> is the period of the year when most <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones" title="Tropical cyclones">tropical cyclones</a> form in the Southern <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Oceans</a> between 90°E and 160°E. The season will officially run from 1 November 2017 to 30 April 2018; however, tropical cyclones can form at any time of the year, as demonstrated by the first tropical low of the season in early August. Any tropical system that forms between 1 July 2017 and 30 June 2018 will count towards the season total. During the season, tropical cyclones will be officially monitored by one of the five tropical cyclone warning centres (TCWCs) that operate in this region. Three of the five centres are operated by the Australian <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Meteorology" title="Bureau of Meteorology">Bureau of Meteorology</a> (BOM) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perth,_Western_Australia" title="Perth, Western Australia">Perth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Darwin,_Northern_Territory" title="Darwin, Northern Territory">Darwin</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brisbane,_Queensland" title="Brisbane, Queensland">Brisbane</a>, while the other two are operated by the National Weather Service of <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a> in <a href="/wiki/Port_Moresby" title="Port Moresby">Port Moresby</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_Agency_for_Meteorology,_Climatology_and_Geophysics" title="Indonesian Agency for Meteorology, Climatology and Geophysics">Indonesian Agency for Meteorology, Climatology and Geophysics</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jakarta,_Indonesia" title="Jakarta, Indonesia">Jakarta</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">Joint Typhoon Warning Center</a> (JTWC) of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and other national meteorological services including <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9t%C3%A9o-France" title="Météo-France">Météo-France</a> at <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">Réunion</a> will also monitor the basin during the season.</p>

<p>On 8 August, a low-pressure system that had been meandering in the Indian Ocean for a few days<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was upgraded by the Bureau of Meteorology to a tropical low.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> The system was located approximately 850 km (530 mi) to the west-northwest of the <a href="/wiki/Cocos_(Keeling)_Islands" title="Cocos (Keeling) Islands">Cocos Islands</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/90th_meridian_east" title="90th meridian east">90th meridian east</a>—the western edge of the BOM's area of responsibility.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The tropical low moved in a west-southwesterly direction<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> and attained 35 km/h (25 mph) sustained winds south of the circulation centre (as assessed by <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9t%C3%A9o-France" title="Météo-France">Météo-France</a> at <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">Réunion</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and a minimum barometric pressure of 1005 hPa (29.68 inHg).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">[5]</a></sup> The storm moved out of the Australian region the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="TCWC_Port_Moresby">TCWC Port Moresby</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017%E2%80%9318_Australian_region_cyclone_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: TCWC Port Moresby">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tropical cyclones that develop north of 11°S between 151°E and 160°E are assigned names by the TCWC <a href="/wiki/Port_Moresby" title="Port Moresby">Port Moresby</a>. Tropical cyclone formation in this area is rare, with no cyclones being named in it since <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007-08_Australian_region_cyclone_season#Tropical_Cyclone_Guba" title="2007-08 Australian region cyclone season">2007</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Padgett_October_07_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Padgett_October_07-7">[7]</a></sup> As names are assigned in a random order, the whole list is shown below.</p>
