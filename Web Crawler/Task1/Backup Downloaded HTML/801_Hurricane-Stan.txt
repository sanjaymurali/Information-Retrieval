<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Hurricane Stan</h1>

<p><b>Hurricane Stan</b> was a rather weak but a very deadly and destructive <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> that affected areas of Central America in early October 2005. The eighteenth <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a> and eleventh <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson hurricane scale">hurricane</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2005 Atlantic hurricane season">2005 Atlantic hurricane season</a>, Stan formed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> on October 1 after it had moved into the western <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. The depression slowly intensified, and reached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">tropical storm</a> intensity the following day, before subsequently making its first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucatán Peninsula">Yucatán Peninsula</a>. Traversing the peninsula, the tropical storm weakened, but was able to re-intensify once it entered the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">Bay of Campeche</a>. Under favorable conditions for <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">tropical development</a>, Stan attained hurricane strength on October 4, and later reached peak intensity with winds of 80 mph (130 km/h) and a minimum <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of 977 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hectopascals" title="Hectopascals">hPa</a>; 28.85 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inches_of_mercury" title="Inches of mercury">inHg</a>). The hurricane maintained this intensity until landfall near Punta Roca Partida, Mexico later the same day. Once over the mountainous terrain of Mexico, however, Stan quickly weakened, and dissipated on October 5.</p>
<p>Due to Stan's position within a large area of <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convective</a> activity and thunderstorms, the hurricane's effects were far-reaching and widespread across Central America. <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">Flash floods</a> generated by the hurricane caused severe crop losses, particularly to <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> crops. Overall, Stan caused at least 1,668 deaths across six countries, with many others unaccounted for. Most of these fatalities occurred in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>, and were mostly caused by mudslides triggered by torrential rainfall. The floods in Guatemala destroyed entire towns and disrupted exportation of <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>. In Mexico, the heavy rains triggered additional mudslides and caused rivers to overflow, flooding nearby villages. Despite being relatively far from Stan as opposed to other countries, <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a> was also severely affected by the hurricane. The <a href="/wiki/Santa_Ana_Volcano" title="Santa Ana Volcano">Santa Ana Volcano</a> erupted while Stan was producing heavy rains in the country, which contributed to the damage already wrought by mudslides. Transportation in the country was disrupted. Across the region, Stan caused $3.9 billion in damages, primarily due to torrential rainfall.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Stan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stan_2005_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Stan_2005_track.png/220px-Stan_2005_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Stan_2005_track.png/330px-Stan_2005_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Stan_2005_track.png/440px-Stan_2005_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stan_2005_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>

<p>Hurricane Stan originated from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> that was first identified by the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) off the west coast of Africa on September 17, 2005. Traveling westward, the wave remained weak, barely being notable. By September 22 <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_convection" title="Atmospheric convection">convection</a> began to increase; however, <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> in the region prevented this development from continuing. The system entered the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> several days later, a region with more favorable conditions for cyclonic development. Intermittent convection formed around the wave as it moved west-northwestward before the development maintained itself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> On October 1, the system had become sufficiently organized for the NHC to classify it as Tropical Depression Twenty,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> with the center of circulation situated roughly 135 mi (215 km) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Cozumel" title="Cozumel">Cozumel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Located to the south of a low to mid-level <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">tropospheric</a> <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a>, the depression tracked towards the west-northwest. Within a few hours of being warned upon, the system further intensified into Tropical Storm Stan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> This followed the development of a strong <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">convective banding feature</a> to the southeast of the storm's center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Around 1000 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> (5 a.m. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Daylight_Time" title="Central Daylight Time">CDT</a>) on October 2, Stan made its first landfall near <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Punta_Hualaxtoc&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Punta Hualaxtoc (page does not exist)">Punta Hualaxtoc</a>, Mexico, roughly 35 mi (55 km) south of <a href="/wiki/Tulum" title="Tulum">Tulum</a>, with winds of 40 mph (65 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Over the following 18 hours, the weak storm traversed the southern portion of the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucatán Peninsula">Yucatán Peninsula</a>. Upon entering the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> on October 3, Stan had weakened to a tropical depression;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> however, convection began to redevelop over the storm's center,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> allowing the system to re-attain tropical storm status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbcaption">

</div>
</div>
<p>A strong area of high pressure over the western Gulf of Mexico forced the storm to turn southwestward, back to the Mexican coastline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup> Increasingly deep cloud cover formed over the storm in response to a favorable environment over the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">Bay of Campeche</a>. Late on October 3, forecasters at National Hurricane Center noted a statistical rapid intensity index indicating a 49% chance of Stan undergoing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapid intensification</a> before its final landfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> This intensification did take place within 12 hours before the hurricane moved onshore. Overnight, the structure of Stan rapidly improved, with a <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">banding eye-feature</a> developing within unusually deep convection with cloud-top temperatures measured at −90 °C (−130 °F) by <a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">infrared</a> satellites.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Following this development, National Hurricane Center upgraded the storm to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_1" title="SSHS">Category 1 hurricane</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir–Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir–Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>. Around 1200 UTC on October 4, Stan made landfall near Punta Roca Partida, with winds of 80 mph (130 km/h). The storm also attained its lowest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">barometric pressure</a> of 977 mbar (hPa; 28.85 inHg)at this time. Not long after moving over the mountainous terrain of central Mexico, Stan rapidly weakened to a tropical depression and fully dissipated early on October 5 over the state of <a href="/wiki/Oaxaca" title="Oaxaca">Oaxaca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Stan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some 100,000 inhabitants of the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_los_Tuxtlas" title="Sierra de los Tuxtlas">Sierra de los Tuxtlas</a> region on the Gulf Coast were evacuated from their homes, and incidents of mild flooding as well as wind damage (such as uprooted trees and roofs ripped off houses) were reported from coastal areas of <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">Veracruz</a>, including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veracruz,_Veracruz" title="Veracruz, Veracruz">port of Veracruz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boca_del_R%C3%ADo,_Veracruz" title="Boca del Río, Veracruz">Boca del Río</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_Tuxtla" title="San Andrés Tuxtla">San Andrés Tuxtla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santiago_Tuxtla" title="Santiago Tuxtla">Santiago Tuxtla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minatitl%C3%A1n,_Veracruz" title="Minatitlán, Veracruz">Minatitlán</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coatzacoalcos" title="Coatzacoalcos">Coatzacoalcos</a>, as well as state capital <a href="/wiki/Xalapa" title="Xalapa">Xalapa</a> further inland. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_of_Mexico" title="Military of Mexico">armed forces</a> evacuated the inhabitants of a dozen or so towns on the coastal plain, between <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> <a href="/wiki/Tlacotalpan" title="Tlacotalpan">Tlacotalpan</a> in the west and the lakeside resort of <a href="/wiki/Catemaco" title="Catemaco">Catemaco</a> in the east.</p>

<p>Around the time of Stan's existence, torrential rainstorms dropped upwards of 20 inches (500 mm) of rain, causing severe flash floods, mud slides, and crop damage (particularly to the <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> crop which was close to harvest) over portions of Mexico and Central America, including <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>. Most of the rainstorms were non-tropical in nature and impossible to relate to the hurricane; however, the impact of the larger weather system can be considered as a whole.</p>
<p>Hundreds were reported missing and were feared dead throughout the region. One estimate put the death toll above 2,000 in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> alone. The final death toll will likely never be known due to the extensive decomposition of bodies in the <a href="/wiki/Mud" title="Mud">mud</a>. Note that only 80-100 of the deaths were estimated to have been as a result of Stan; the rest were not caused by Stan itself but were the result of the large system of non-tropical rains that had spawned the hurricane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twsat_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-twsat-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Most of the reported fatalities were as a result of the flooding and mudslides, although eight of the deaths in <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> were as a result of a boat carrying migrants from <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> that ran ashore. A large portion of the figure comes from one village alone, as a mudslide completely destroyed the village of <a href="/wiki/Panabaj" title="Panabaj">Panabaj</a> in Guatemala's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solol%C3%A1_department" title="Sololá department">Sololá department</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Stan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a tropical storm, Stan brought torrential rainfall and gusty winds to parts of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yucatan_Peninsula" title="Yucatan Peninsula">Yucatan Peninsula</a>. Flash flooding took place in several areas; however, no loss of life was reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Stan_2005_cropped.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="864" data-file-width="1280" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Hurricane_Stan_2005_cropped.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Stan_2005_cropped.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Hurricane_Stan_2005_cropped.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Stan_2005_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Hurricane_Stan_2005_cropped.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Stan_2005_cropped.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Stan_2005_cropped.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Hurricane Stan making landfall in <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">Veracruz</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>As the system progressed inland towards the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Madre_del_Sur" title="Sierra Madre del Sur">Sierra Madre del Sur</a> to the west of the <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Tehuantepec" title="Isthmus of Tehuantepec">Isthmus of Tehuantepec</a>, the states of <a href="/wiki/Oaxaca" title="Oaxaca">Oaxaca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a> were affected with torrential rains. Areas of Chiapas near the <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemalan</a> border were hit hard, particularly the coastal border town of <a href="/wiki/Tapachula" title="Tapachula">Tapachula</a>. In Tapachula the river overflowed its banks and caused tremendous damage (including the destruction of all the bridges leading in and out of the town), meaning that it was only accessible through the air. The state government reported that 33 rivers broke their banks and that an indeterminate number of homes, upwards of 20 bridges, and other infrastructure were smashed in the storm's wake.</p>
<p>Some areas in the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Norte_de_Puebla" title="Sierra Norte de Puebla">Sierra Norte</a>, in the central state of <a href="/wiki/Puebla" title="Puebla">Puebla</a>, were also flooded. Three people died in a mudslide at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Xochiapulco_Hill&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Xochiapulco Hill (page does not exist)">Xochiapulco Hill</a>.</p>
<p>In addition, <a href="/wiki/Pemex" title="Pemex">Pemex</a> evacuated 270 employees from its oil platforms in the Gulf of Mexico, although no damage was reported and the plants were restarted.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_the_Interior" title="Ministry of the Interior">Ministry of the Interior</a> declared states of emergency in the worst hit municipalities of five states: <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hidalgo_(Mexico)" title="Hidalgo (Mexico)">Hidalgo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oaxaca" title="Oaxaca">Oaxaca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puebla" title="Puebla">Puebla</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">Veracruz</a>. According to Mexican president Vicente Fox, Hurricane Stan wrought roughly 20 billion pesos ($1.9 billion USD) in damage throughout the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Honduras">Honduras</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Stan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Honduras">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Throughout Honduras, heavy rains produced by Hurricane Stan resulted in six fatalities and roughly $100 million in losses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-em-dat_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-em-dat-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guatemala">Guatemala</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Stan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Guatemala">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stan_Landslide_2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="330" height="333" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Stan_Landslide_2.jpg/220px-Stan_Landslide_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/64/Stan_Landslide_2.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stan_Landslide_2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Landslides affecting infrastructure, crops, and water sources in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>By October 11, 2005 at least 1,500 people were confirmed to have died, and up to 3,000 were believed missing. Many communities were overwhelmed, and the worst single incident appears to have occurred in <a href="/wiki/Panabaj" title="Panabaj">Panabaj</a>, an impoverished <a href="/wiki/Maya_peoples" title="Maya peoples">Maya</a> village in the highlands near <a href="/wiki/Lake_Atitl%C3%A1n" title="Lake Atitlán">Lake Atitlán</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solol%C3%A1_department" title="Sololá department">Sololá department</a>. This volcanic lake was so overwhelmed by the torrential rains that many of the small, Mayan villages covering the shores experienced landslides from above. Some of the towns were so overwhelmed by the slides that the mayor has declared them graveyards, and all people who are missing are counted as dead. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Piedra_Grande&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Piedra Grande (page does not exist)">Piedra Grande</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(place)" title="Hamlet (place)">hamlet</a> in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_Sacatep%C3%A9quez,_Guatemala" title="San Pedro Sacatepéquez, Guatemala">San Pedro Sacatepéquez</a>, was also destroyed. Floods and mudslides obliterated the community of about 1,400 people, and it was feared that most or all of the population of the community lost their lives. The government stated that it did not know what was going on in the southwest of the country, and particularly in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Marcos_department" title="San Marcos department">San Marcos department</a> because a vital <a href="/wiki/Bridge" title="Bridge">bridge</a> was destroyed at <a href="/wiki/El_Palmar,_Quetzaltenango" title="El Palmar, Quetzaltenango">El Palmar, Quetzaltenango</a>, cutting the region off from the rest of the country. There were reported <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Petrol" title="Petrol">petrol</a> shortages, including in <a href="/wiki/Quetzaltenango" title="Quetzaltenango">Quetzaltenango</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="El_Salvador">El Salvador</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Stan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: El Salvador">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The eruption of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Ana_volcano" title="Santa Ana volcano">Santa Ana volcano</a>, located near the capital <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador" title="San Salvador">San Salvador</a>, on October 1, 2005 compounded the problems, which led to even more destructive floods and mudslides from Stan.</p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a> was declared. According to the director of El Salvador's National Emergency Centre, 300 communities were affected by the floods, with over 54,000 people forced to flee their homes. A state of emergency also was called for in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> by <a href="/wiki/President_of_Guatemala" title="President of Guatemala">President</a> <a href="/wiki/%C3%93scar_Berger" title="Óscar Berger">Óscar Berger</a> where 36,559 people were reported in emergency shelters. Some <a href="/wiki/Looting" title="Looting">looting</a> was also reported, a scene reminiscent of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a> five weeks previous.</p>
<p>A spokesman for the Salvadoran <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> said that "the emergency is bigger than the rescue capacity, we have floods everywhere, bridges about to collapse, landslides and dozens of roads blocked by mudslides". The <a href="/wiki/Pan-American_Highway" title="Pan-American Highway">Pan-American Highway</a> was cut off by mudslides leading into the capital, <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador" title="San Salvador">San Salvador</a>, as well as several other roads. 72 deaths were confirmed in El Salvador.</p>
<p><br/></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Stan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>
<p>Following the severe damage and extensive loss of life in Stan's wake, the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming#Retirement" title="Tropical cyclone naming">retired</a> the name <i>Stan</i> from the circulating list of Atlantic hurricane names. For the 2011 season, Stan was replaced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Sean_(2011)" title="Tropical Storm Sean (2011)">Sean</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
