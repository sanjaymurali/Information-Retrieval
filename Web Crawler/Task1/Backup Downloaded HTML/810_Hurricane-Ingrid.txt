<p><b>Hurricane Ingrid</b> was one of two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones" title="Tropical cyclones">tropical cyclones</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Manuel" title="Hurricane Manuel">Hurricane Manuel</a>, to strike Mexico within a 24‑hour period, the first such occurrence since 1958. Ingrid was the ninth <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">named storm</a> and second hurricane of the <a href="/wiki/2013_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2013 Atlantic hurricane season">2013 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. It formed on September 12 in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> from a broad disturbance that also spawned Manuel in the eastern Pacific. After initially moving westward toward <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">Veracruz</a>, Ingrid turned northeastward away from the coast. Favorable conditions allowed it to attain hurricane status on September 14, and the next day Ingrid attained peak winds of 140 km/h (85 mph). Subsequently, increased <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> weakened the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">convection</a> as the storm turned more to the northwest and west. On September 16, Ingrid made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> just south of <a href="/wiki/La_Pesca" title="La Pesca">La Pesca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a> in northeastern Mexico as a strong tropical storm, and dissipated the next day. The hurricane was also the last one to form in the Gulf of Mexico until <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hermine" title="Hurricane Hermine">Hurricane Hermine</a> in 2016.</p>
<p>The combined impacts of hurricanes Ingrid and Manuel affected two-thirds of Mexico, killing 192 people and causing <a href="/wiki/Mexican_peso" title="Mexican peso">MXN$75</a> billion, or roughly <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>5.7 billion in damage. Most of the effects were due to Manuel, though Ingrid was directly responsible for at least 32 deaths and $20 billion (MXN, US$1.5 billion) in damage. The two storms precipitated 162 billion m<sup>3</sup> (5.7 trillion cu ft) of water, the equivalent of filling every dam in Mexico. Rainfall from the storm peaked at 511 mm (20.1 in) in <a href="/wiki/Tuxpan" title="Tuxpan">Tuxpan</a>, Veracruz. The rains caused widespread flooding, damaging at least 14,000 houses and hundreds of roads and bridges. In Tamaulipas, where the storm made landfall, the rainfall damaged crops and flooded rivers. The effects of the storm spread into <a href="/wiki/South_Texas" title="South Texas">South Texas</a>, causing high tides and some flooding. After the storm, the Mexican government declared several municipalities to be in states of emergency. Relief agencies distributed food and aid to the hardest hit areas, although in Tamaulipas, residents had to rely on assistance from the local <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Cartel" title="Gulf Cartel">Gulf Cartel</a>. The names Ingrid and Manuel were both later <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">retired</a> due to their impacts.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Ingrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ingrid_2013_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1236" data-file-width="2000" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Ingrid_2013_track.png/220px-Ingrid_2013_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Ingrid_2013_track.png/330px-Ingrid_2013_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Ingrid_2013_track.png/440px-Ingrid_2013_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ingrid_2013_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On August 28, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> exited the west coast of Africa, which moved westward across the Atlantic Ocean without development. On September 2, an area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">convection</a>, or thunderstorms, developed along the northern portion of the wave, but was eventually absorbed into <a href="/wiki/2013_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Tropical_Storm_Gabrielle" title="2013 Atlantic hurricane season">Tropical Storm Gabrielle</a> north of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The wave continued westward through the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> into a large area of <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">cyclonic</a> <a href="/wiki/Zonal_flow" title="Zonal flow">flow</a> at the surface, which extended across <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> into the eastern Pacific. Around September 9, the broad system developed two areas of disturbed weather – one in the eastern Pacific would become <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Manuel_(2013)" title="Hurricane Manuel (2013)">Hurricane Manuel</a>, and the other developing over the northwestern Caribbean. The latter system slowly organized, developing a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low pressure area</a> on September 11. Subsequently, the system moved over the <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucatán Peninsula">Yucatán Peninsula</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Although land interaction prevented immediate development, conditions favored further development in the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">Bay of Campeche</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Early on September 12, the low emerged into the bay, and at 1800 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> the <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> (NHC) assessed that Tropical Depression Ten developed about 280 km (170 mi) east-northeast of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veracruz,_Veracruz" title="Veracruz, Veracruz">Veracruz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> This was confirmed by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">Hurricane Hunters</a> flight indicating the presence of a closed low-level circulation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-d1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-d1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 13, convection and organization increased and the depression strengthened into Tropical Storm Ingrid, while weak steering currents resulted in little movement of the cyclone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-d4_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-d4-4">[4]</a></sup> Partially due to the presence of nearby Hurricane Manuel in the eastern Pacific basin on the other side of Mexico, significant <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> existed in the vicinity of Ingrid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-d5_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-d5-5">[5]</a></sup> Nonetheless, strong convection and intermittent development of an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> allowed for strengthening of the storm on September 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-d9_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-d9-6">[6]</a></sup> Ingrid strengthened into a hurricane – the second of the season – that afternoon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-d10_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-d10-7">[7]</a></sup> Around that time, the hurricane turned sharply northeastward due to a <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a> over eastern Mexico and a <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> over the southeastern United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Additional strengthening took place thereafter, and Ingrid reached a peak intensity of 140 km/h (85 mph) early on September 15 while starting to move northward and begin a northwest turn towards the Mexican coastline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-d11_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-d11-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Afterward Ingrid reached peak winds, shear increased and began to weaken the hurricane as it approached the Mexican coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-d13_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-d13-9">[9]</a></sup> The center became displaced to the edge of the convection, and NHC forecaster Daniel Brown noted that Ingrid "[did] not resemble a classic hurricane in satellite pictures."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> At around 1115 UTC on September 16, Ingrid made landfall just south of <a href="/wiki/La_Pesca" title="La Pesca">La Pesca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a> in northeastern Mexico, after having weakened into a strong tropical storm with 100 km/h (65 mph) winds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Near landfall, the Hurricane Hunters reported flight-level winds of 120 km/h (75 mph) which, after adjusting to surface winds, confirmed the weakening.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-d17_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-d17-11">[11]</a></sup> The storm moved ashore less than 24 hours after Tropical Storm Manuel struck the Pacific coast of Mexico on <a href="/wiki/Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Michoacán">Michoacán</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtcr_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtcr-12">[12]</a></sup> making it the first time since 1958 that tropical cyclones struck both coasts of the country within one day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-red_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-red-13">[13]</a></sup> Ingrid rapidly weakened into a tropical depression over land,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and although convection temporarily reorganized,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> the circulation dissipated on September 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations_and_impact">Preparations and impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Ingrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations and impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Several <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">tropical cyclone warnings and watches</a> were posted in anticipation of the storm. Upon the development of Ingrid into a tropical cyclone at 2100 UTC on September 12, the Government of Mexico issued a tropical storm warning from <a href="/wiki/Coatzacoalcos" title="Coatzacoalcos">Coatzacoalcos</a> to <a href="/wiki/Nautla,_Veracruz" title="Nautla, Veracruz">Nautla, Veracruz</a>. At 1500 UTC on September 13, the tropical storm warning was extended northward to <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Rojo_(Mexico)" title="Cabo Rojo (Mexico)">Cabo Rojo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">Veracruz</a>, with a tropical storm watch northward to <a href="/wiki/La_Pesca" title="La Pesca">La Pesca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a>. Later, a hurricane warning was issued between Cabo Rojo to La Pesca, with a tropical storm watch to Bahia Algodones, Tamaulipas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pemex" title="Pemex">Pemex</a>, the oil company operated by the Mexican government, evacuated workers from three platforms in the Gulf of Mexico due to the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp917_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp917-17">[17]</a></sup> In Tamaulipas, classes were canceled in 27 municipalities,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and all classes were temporarily canceled throughout Veracruz.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-rw-19">[19]</a></sup> The threat of the storm caused events for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grito_de_Dolores" title="Grito de Dolores">Mexican Independence Day</a> to be canceled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> After a restriction on the news in 2010 due to the local <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Drug_War" title="Mexican Drug War">drug war</a>, members of a citizen alert system in Tamaulipas used <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> to notify about flooding, missing people, and the need for assistance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Early in its duration, Ingrid produced tropical storm force winds along the coast of Veracruz. While making landfall, it also brought tropical storm force winds along the northeastern Mexican coast. Sustained winds at La Pesca peaked at 80 km/h (50 mph), with gusts to 105 km/h (65 mph). The NHC remarked that Ingrid "likely caused above normal tides" in northeastern Mexico, but there was no data to back up the statement. Interacting with Hurricane Manuel on the Pacific coast and the broad cyclonic flow, Ingrid dropped heavy rainfall across eastern Mexico, primarily in <a href="/wiki/Tabasco" title="Tabasco">Tabasco</a>, Veracruz, and <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a> states. In <a href="/wiki/Tuxpan" title="Tuxpan">Tuxpan</a>, Veracruz, rainfall totaled 511 mm (20.1 in) over 10 days, while at the Presa Vicente Guerrero dam in Tamaulipas, precipitation reached 502 mm (19.8 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Surface_runoff" title="Surface runoff">Surface runoff</a> from the storm spread to the Pacific coast of Mexico, producing flooding in Guerrero in combination with Manuel. The impacts from both storms produced 162 billion m<sup>3</sup> (5.7 trillion cu ft) of water, the equivalent of filling every dam in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The combined effects of hurricanes Ingrid and Manuel affected about two-thirds of Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aon_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-aon-23">[23]</a></sup> The rains from Ingrid caused flooding and landslides across Mexico,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> causing many rivers to rise,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aon_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-aon-23">[23]</a></sup> and isolating towns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shoichet_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-shoichet-24">[24]</a></sup> In Veracruz alone, the rains flooded 68 rivers, which damaged 121 roads and 31 bridges, including two destroyed bridges. About 14,000 houses were damaged to some degree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-suman_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-suman-25">[25]</a></sup> Heavy rainfall forced 23,000 people to evacuate their homes, 9,000 of whom went to emergency shelters,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deaths_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-deaths-26">[26]</a></sup> some forced to leave by the Mexican army in high risk areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-suman_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-suman-25">[25]</a></sup> Evacuees who did not reside in shelters generally went to the houses of friends and family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-rw-19">[19]</a></sup> Also in Veracruz, flooding killed about 20,000 livestock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wspa_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-wspa-27">[27]</a></sup> Along the coast of Tamaulipas, damage occurred from <a href="/wiki/Soto_la_Marina,_Tamaulipas" title="Soto la Marina, Tamaulipas">Soto la Marina</a> to <a href="/wiki/La_Pesca" title="La Pesca">La Pesca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-rw-19">[19]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A1nuco_River" title="Pánuco River">Pánuco River</a> in Tamaulipas rose above its banks, flooding two poor towns along its path and damaging adjacent roads. Also in the state, the storm damaged local <a href="/wiki/Sorghum" title="Sorghum">sorghum</a> fields.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wspa_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-wspa-27">[27]</a></sup> Two people in the state required rescue after their truck was swept away by a river.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp917_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp917-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Throughout Mexico, Ingrid killed 32 people, mostly due to flooding and mudslides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> The two storms collectively killed at least 192 people and caused <a href="/wiki/Mexican_peso" title="Mexican peso">MXN$</a>75 (<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a> 5.7 billion). Manuel was responsible for majority of the overall effects, although Ingrid still left an estimated <a href="/wiki/Mexican_peso" title="Mexican peso">MXN</a>$20 billion (US$1.5 billion) in estimated economic losses, according to <a href="/wiki/Aon_(company)" title="Aon (company)">AON Benfield</a>; insured damages totaled $MXN3 billion ($US230 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aon_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-aon-23">[23]</a></sup> A total of six fatalities occurred in the states of <a href="/wiki/Hidalgo_(state)" title="Hidalgo (state)">Hidalgo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puebla" title="Puebla">Puebla</a>. Three of which were caused after a vehicle was swept off a road, while three other people died after their home was buried by a mudslide;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shoichet_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-shoichet-24">[24]</a></sup> another death occurred in Hidalgo after a house collapsed on a woman in the town of <a href="/wiki/Tepehuac%C3%A1n_de_Guerrero" title="Tepehuacán de Guerrero">Tepehuacán de Guerrero</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Twelve people died after a landslide smashed a bus in <a href="/wiki/Altotonga_(municipality)" title="Altotonga (municipality)">Altotonga</a>, Veracruz,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deaths_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-deaths-26">[26]</a></sup> and three people died in Tamaulipas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>The fringes of the storm extended into southern Texas, where winds gusted to tropical storm force, and rainfall was around 25–75 mm (0.98–2.95 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Thunderstorms and high tides affected the coastline, with tides reaching 0.76 m (2.5 ft) above normal, causing beaches to close due to flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath_and_retirement">Aftermath and retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Ingrid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath and retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The twin impacts of hurricanes Ingrid and Manuel prompted officials in Mexico to declare a state of emergency in 155 <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Mexico" title="Municipalities of Mexico">municipalities</a> in Veracruz, Tamaulipas, Chiapas, Oaxaca, Guerrero, and Chihuahua.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-red_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-red-13">[13]</a></sup> The nation's Health Ministry sent medical crews across the country to affected areas in an effort to prevent the spread of disease; the agency also suggested residents boil their water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Officials opened shelters in Tamaulipas for residents whose houses were damaged,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> housing about 2,000 people in the weeks following the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The Mexican food bank sent about 800 tons of food to the hardest-hit parts of the country in Guerrero, Veracruz, and Sinaloa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In addition, the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Red_Cross" title="Mexican Red Cross">Mexican Red Cross</a> delivered 186 tons of food to Veracruz alone. Volunteers delivered about 600 blankets and various cleaning supplies to the town of <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A1nuco,_Veracruz" title="Pánuco, Veracruz">Pánuco</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Following the storms, the National Civil Protection Coordination received the authority to prevent housing construction in areas at risk for mudslides and flooding; the law had passed in 2012, but initially lacked the authority to enforce it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Some residents in Tamaulipas complained at the slow pace of receiving aid. In response, the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Cartel" title="Gulf Cartel">Gulf Cartel</a> brought relief items to <a href="/wiki/Aldama_Municipality,_Tamaulipas" title="Aldama Municipality, Tamaulipas">Aldama</a>, with one columnist for <i><a href="/wiki/El_Universal_(Mexico_City)" title="El Universal (Mexico City)">El Universal</a></i> suggesting that this was to gain favor with local residents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Because of the severe damage caused by the storm in Mexico, the name <i>Ingrid</i> was later retired by the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>, and will never again be used for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_hurricane" title="North Atlantic hurricane">North Atlantic hurricane</a>. It was replaced with <i>Imelda</i> for the 2019 Atlantic hurricane season. The name Manuel was also retired from the Pacific naming list and was replaced with <i>Mario</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
