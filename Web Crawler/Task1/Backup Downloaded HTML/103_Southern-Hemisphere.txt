<p>The <b>Southern Hemisphere</b> is the half <a href="/wiki/Sphere" title="Sphere">sphere</a> of <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> which is south of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>. It contains all or parts of five <a href="/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">continents</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia_(continent)" title="Australia (continent)">Australia</a>, about 90% of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, the southern third of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and several southern islands off the continental <a href="/wiki/Mainland" title="Mainland">mainland</a> of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>), four <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">oceans</a> (<a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">South Atlantic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">Southern</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">South Pacific</a>) and most of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islands" title="Pacific Islands">Pacific Islands</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>. Its surface is 80.9% water, compared with 60.7% water in the case of the Northern Hemisphere, and it contains 32.7% of Earth's land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Due to the tilt of <a href="/wiki/Earth%27s_rotation" title="Earth's rotation">Earth's rotation</a> relative to the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ecliptic" title="Ecliptic">ecliptic plane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">summer</a> is from December to March and <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">winter</a> is from June to September. September 22 or 23 is the vernal <a href="/wiki/Equinox" title="Equinox">equinox</a> and March 20 or 21 is the autumnal equinox. The <a href="/wiki/South_Pole" title="South Pole">South Pole</a> is in the middle of the southern hemispherical region.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Hemisphere&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Southern Hemisphere <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climates</a> tend to be slightly milder than those at similar latitudes in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a>, except in the <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctic</a> which is colder than the <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">Arctic</a>. This is because the Southern Hemisphere has significantly more ocean and much less land; water heats up and cools down more slowly than land.</p>

<p>In the Southern Hemisphere the sun passes from east to west through the north, although north of the <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Capricorn" title="Tropic of Capricorn">Tropic of Capricorn</a> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mean_solar_time" title="Mean solar time">mean sun</a> can be directly overhead or due south at midday. The Sun rotating through the north causes an apparent right-left trajectory through the sky unlike the left-right motion of the Sun when seen from the Northern Hemisphere as it passes through the southern sky. Sun-cast shadows turn anticlockwise throughout the day and <a href="/wiki/Sundial" title="Sundial">sundials</a> have the hours increasing in the anticlockwise direction. During <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">solar eclipses</a> viewed from a point to the south of the Tropic of Capricorn, the Moon moves from left to right on the disc of the Sun (see, for example, photos with timings of the <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_November_13,_2012" title="Solar eclipse of November 13, 2012">solar eclipse of November 13, 2012</a>), while viewed from a point to the north of the Tropic of Cancer (i.e., in the Northern Hemisphere), the Moon moves from right to left during solar eclipses.</p>
<p>Cyclones and tropical storms spin clockwise in the Southern Hemisphere (as opposed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anticlockwise" title="Anticlockwise">anticlockwise</a> in the Northern Hemisphere) due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coriolis_effect" title="Coriolis effect">Coriolis effect</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The southern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperateness" title="Temperateness">temperate zone</a>, a subsection of the Southern Hemisphere, is nearly all oceanic. This zone includes the southern tip of <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>; the southern half of <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>; parts of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, going south from <a href="/wiki/Adelaide" title="Adelaide">Adelaide</a>, and all of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sagittarius_(constellation)" title="Sagittarius (constellation)">Sagittarius</a> constellation that includes the <a href="/wiki/Milky_Way" title="Milky Way">galactic centre</a> is a southern constellation and this, combined with clearer skies, makes for excellent viewing of the night sky from the Southern Hemisphere with brighter and more numerous stars.</p>
<p>Forests in the Southern Hemisphere have special features which set them apart from those in the Northern Hemisphere. Both <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> share, for example, unique <a href="/wiki/Beech" title="Beech">beech</a> species or <i><a href="/wiki/Nothofagus" title="Nothofagus">Nothofagus</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> has members of the closely related genera <a href="/wiki/Lophozonia" title="Lophozonia">Lophozonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fuscospora" title="Fuscospora">Fuscospora</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Eucalyptus" title="Eucalyptus">eucalyptus</a> is native to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> but is now also planted in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a> for pulp production and, increasingly, <a href="/wiki/Biofuel" title="Biofuel">biofuel</a> uses.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Hemisphere&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Approximately 800,000,000 humans live in the Southern Hemisphere, representing only 10–12% of the total global human population of 7.3 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> is the most spoken language in the Southern Hemisphere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Continents_and_microcontinents">Continents and microcontinents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Hemisphere&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Continents and microcontinents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> (approximately one third, from south of <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a> in the east to south of <a href="/wiki/Libreville" title="Libreville">Libreville</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gabon" title="Gabon">Gabon</a> in the west)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a> (the entire continent and its associated islands are wholly within the Southern Hemisphere)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> (the very southern island portion including <a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">East Timor</a>, most of <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> and a few <a href="/wiki/Islet" title="Islet">islets</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldives</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Australia_(continent)" title="Australia (continent)">Australia</a> (the entire mainland is in the Southern Hemisphere)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> (mostly, from south of the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_River" title="Amazon River">Amazon River</a> mouth in the east to south of <a href="/wiki/Quito" title="Quito">Quito</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> in the west)</li>
