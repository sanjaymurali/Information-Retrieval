<p><b>Expedition 12</b> (2005) was the 12th expedition to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>, launched from <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> using the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federal_Space_Agency" title="Russian Federal Space Agency">Russian</a> <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-7" title="Soyuz TMA-7">Soyuz TMA-7</a> spacecraft. The crew landed back in Kazakhstan on 8 April 2006 with the addition of the first Brazilian astronaut, <a href="/wiki/Marcos_Pontes" title="Marcos Pontes">Marcos Pontes</a>.</p>
<p>American entrepreneur <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Olsen" title="Gregory Olsen">Gregory Olsen</a> was launched in the <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-7" title="Soyuz TMA-7">Soyuz TMA-7</a> spacecraft and returned with <a href="/wiki/Expedition_11" title="Expedition 11">Expedition 11</a> on <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-6" title="Soyuz TMA-6">Soyuz TMA-6</a> on 11 October 2005 thereby becoming the third <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_tourist" title="Space tourist">space tourist</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spacewalks">Spacewalks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Spacewalks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There were two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">spacewalks</a> outside the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">ISS</a> during Expedition 12. MacArthur and Tokarev participated in both of them.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="EVA_1">EVA 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: EVA 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first EVA was on 7 November 2005 for 5 hours and 22 minutes. There were two main objectives, both of which were completed. The first was to install and set up a new camera on the <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Truss_Structure" title="Integrated Truss Structure">P1 Truss</a> which was later used in the installation of more <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Truss_Structure" title="Integrated Truss Structure">truss segments</a>. The second was to jettison the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Floating_Potential_Probe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Floating Potential Probe (page does not exist)">Floating Potential Probe</a> which was a failed instrument, designed to measure the station's electrical potential and compare it to the surrounding <a href="/wiki/Plasma_(physics)" title="Plasma (physics)">plasma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="EVA_2">EVA 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: EVA 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The second spacewalk took place on 3 February 2006 and lasted 5 hours and 43 minutes. The astronauts jettisoned an old Russian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orlan_space_suits" title="Orlan space suits">Orlan spacesuit</a>, named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SuitSat-1" title="SuitSat-1">SuitSat-1</a>, that was equipped with a radio for broadcasts to students around the world. The suit reached the end of its operation life in 2004. They also retrieved the Biorisk experiment, photographed a sensor for a micrometeoroid experiment, and tied off the surviving <a href="/wiki/Umbilical_cable" title="Umbilical cable">umbilical</a> of the mobile transporter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 29 March 2006 a total <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">solar eclipse</a> took place, and the adjacent picture was taken by the Expedition 12 crew. It clearly shows the shadow of the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a> being cast on the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concert">Concert</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Concert">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While wake-up music is a tradition aboard space shuttle missions, the ISS crew generally use an alarm clock to wake up. Expedition 12 astronauts received a special treat on 3 November 2005 when <a href="/wiki/Paul_McCartney" title="Paul McCartney">Paul McCartney</a> performed <i>Good Day Sunshine</i> and <i>English Tea</i> in a first ever concert linkup from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arrowhead_Pond" title="Arrowhead Pond">Arrowhead Pond</a> in Anaheim, California on his <i>US</i> tour. The event was broadcast live on <a href="/wiki/NASA_TV" title="NASA TV">NASA TV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chronology_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-chronology-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
