<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">South Pacific High</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Clima_de_Chile.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="404" data-file-width="384" height="210" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Clima_de_Chile.JPG/200px-Clima_de_Chile.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Clima_de_Chile.JPG/300px-Clima_de_Chile.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3e/Clima_de_Chile.JPG 2x" width="200"/></a>

Sketch showing the normal location of the Pacific High west of Peru and Chile</div>
</div>

<p>The <b>South Pacific High</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Subtropics" title="Subtropics">subtropical</a> <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">anticyclone</a> located in the southeast <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. The area of high <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">atmospheric pressure</a> and the presence of the <a href="/wiki/Humboldt_Current" title="Humboldt Current">Humboldt Current</a> in the underlying ocean make the west coast of <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> extremely arid. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sechura_desert" title="Sechura desert">Sechura</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atacama_desert" title="Atacama desert">Atacama deserts</a>, as the whole <a href="/wiki/Climate_of_Chile" title="Climate of Chile">climate of Chile</a>, are heavily influenced by this semi-permanent high-pressure area.</p>
