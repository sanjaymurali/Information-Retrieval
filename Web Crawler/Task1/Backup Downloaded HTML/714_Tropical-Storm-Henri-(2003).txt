<p><b>Tropical Storm Henri</b> was a moderate <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Intensity_classifications" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical storm</a> that formed in the <a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">2003 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. The eighth storm of the season, Henri was one of six tropical cyclones to hit the United States in the year. Henri formed from a tropical wave in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> in early September, and crossed over <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> as a tropical depression. Its remnants later moved into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_States" title="Mid-Atlantic States">Mid-Atlantic</a> before dissipating completely.</p>
<p>Henri caused little damage as a tropical cyclone. In Florida, it dropped heavy <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">rainfall</a>, though damage was limited to minor <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">flooding</a> damage. In <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, damage was greater, where heavy rainfall damaged hundreds of houses and businesses. The resulting floods in Delaware were described as a <a href="/wiki/100-year_flood" title="100-year flood">1 in 500 year event</a>. The total damage by Henri along its path amounted to $19.6 million (2003 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>, $21.5 million 2006 USD), but no deaths were reported.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Henri_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Henri_2003_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Henri_2003_track.png/220px-Henri_2003_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Henri_2003_track.png/330px-Henri_2003_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Henri_2003_track.png/440px-Henri_2003_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Henri_2003_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On August 22, a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> moved off the coast of Africa, and it moved westward across the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> without developing significantly. On September 1 the wave axis entered the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>, and upon doing so <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convection</a> steadily organized around a low-level center of circulation. The system moved northward and developed into Tropical Depression Twelve on September 3 while located about 300 <a href="/wiki/Mile" title="Mile">miles</a> (480 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kilometer" title="Kilometer">kilometers</a>) west of <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">Tampa, Florida</a>. Embedded within a slow <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">mid-latitude</a> <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">trough</a>, the depression moved eastward and strengthened into Tropical Storm Henri on September 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Henri_Remnants_(2003).JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="255" data-file-width="262" height="214" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Henri_Remnants_%282003%29.JPG/220px-Henri_Remnants_%282003%29.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c7/Henri_Remnants_%282003%29.JPG 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Henri_Remnants_(2003).JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Remnants of Henri making landfall on <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> on September 12</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Despite strong southwesterly vertical <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">shear</a>, Henri continued intensifying while moving eastward, and reached a peak strength of 60 mph (95 km/h) later on September 5. Shortly thereafter, though, the shear greatly weakened the storm, and it was downgraded to a tropical depression. Henri was not able to recover its intensity, and made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> near <a href="/wiki/Clearwater,_Florida" title="Clearwater, Florida">Clearwater, Florida</a> on September 6 as a 35 mph (55 km/h) tropical depression, and quickly crossed the state as it accelerated to the northeast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Despite initial predictions of re-intensification over open waters due to potentially lower shear,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hendisc13_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-hendisc13-2">[2]</a></sup> Henri failed to re-strengthen and degenerated into a remnant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low pressure area</a> on September 8 off the coast of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The broad and disorganized remnant low remained nearly stationary due to a <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> of high pressure to its north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Residual convection within the remnants of Henri remained disorganized, but forecasters kept watch for the potential for redevelopment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-two11_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-two11-3">[3]</a></sup> However, it moved inland near <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras" title="Cape Hatteras">Cape Hatteras</a> on September 12 without reorganizing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-two12_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-two12-4">[4]</a></sup> The remnants continued to the north and dissipated on September 17 over <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-hpc-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Henri_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> issued a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">Tropical Storm Warning</a> from <a href="/wiki/Englewood,_Florida" title="Englewood, Florida">Englewood</a> to <a href="/wiki/Indian_Pass,_Florida" title="Indian Pass, Florida">Indian Pass, Florida</a> while Henri was a tropical depression; however, warnings were discontinued by the time Henri made landfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup> Flood warnings were issued across the state prior to the storm making landfall, with predictions of 5 to 10 inches (125 – 255 mm) of rainfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fla_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-fla-6">[6]</a></sup> As a result of the storm's approach, twelve shelters were placed on standby. Similarly, the Hurricane Shelter Information Hotline was placed on standby and ready to be activated within 10 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shelt_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-shelt-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Levy_County,_Florida" title="Levy County, Florida">Levy County</a> officials declared a state of emergency. There, sand bags and sand were sent to <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Key,_Florida" title="Cedar Key, Florida">Cedar Key</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yankeetown,_Florida" title="Yankeetown, Florida">Yankeetown</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Inglis,_Florida" title="Inglis, Florida">Inglis</a> in anticipation for storm surge and flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flprep_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-flprep-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Henri_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Henri_2003_rainfall.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="854" data-file-width="728" height="258" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Henri_2003_rainfall.gif/220px-Henri_2003_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Henri_2003_rainfall.gif/330px-Henri_2003_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Henri_2003_rainfall.gif/440px-Henri_2003_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Henri_2003_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rainfall from Henri</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Henri dropped heavy rainfall along its path, with the worst of the <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">flooding</a> occurring in <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>. The storm caused $19.6 million in damage (2003 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>), primarily in Delaware.</p>
<h3><span id="Florida,_Bahamas,_and_Bermuda"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Florida.2C_Bahamas.2C_and_Bermuda">Florida, Bahamas, and Bermuda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Henri_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Florida, Bahamas, and Bermuda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prior to making landfall, the storm produced strong waves on the Florida west coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fla_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-fla-6">[6]</a></sup> Following a summer of tropical moisture in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, Henri brought more heavy rainfall to the <a href="/wiki/Aquifer#Saturated_versus_unsaturated" title="Aquifer">saturated</a> state,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flsummer_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-flsummer-9">[9]</a></sup> peaking at 9.09 in (230 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Hialeah,_Florida" title="Hialeah, Florida">Hialeah</a> in the southeast portion of the state. Two other areas experienced over 7 in (180 mm), though most areas received only light rainfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-hpc-5">[5]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Hernando_County,_Florida" title="Hernando County, Florida">Hernando County</a>, a stationary thunderstorm dropped over 5 in (125 mm) of rain in around an hour. It caused a rapid flooding of roads, though quickly retreated. Damage was minor, due to lack of many homes in the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hernanfldam_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-hernanfldam-10">[10]</a></sup> A feeder band in <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_County,_Florida" title="Charlotte County, Florida">Charlotte County</a> dropped 7 in (180 mm) of rain in three hours, flooding numerous streets and homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charfladam_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-charfladam-11">[11]</a></sup> Throughout Florida, damage was minimal, and there were no deaths. However, lightning from a feeder band injured a man in <a href="/wiki/Lee_County,_Florida" title="Lee County, Florida">Lee County</a>, while an indirect injury occurred from a car crash due to hydroplaning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>, outer <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">rainbands</a> from Henri dropped around 1 inch (25 mm) of rain. Winds in the archipelago gusted to 32 mph (51 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unofbah_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-unofbah-12">[12]</a></sup> Just days after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fabian" title="Hurricane Fabian">Hurricane Fabian</a> struck <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>, moisture from Henri brought thunderstorms and heavy rainfall and thunderstorms totaling to 2.44 inches (62 mm) at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bermuda_International_Airport" title="Bermuda International Airport">airport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bws_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-bws-13">[13]</a></sup> This hindered cleanup efforts, though caused no known damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mid-Atlantic">Mid-Atlantic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Henri_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mid-Atlantic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>, rainfall was generally light, with the exception of a few areas receiving over 3 inches (75 mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-hpc-5">[5]</a></sup> Henri produced greater amounts of rain in <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, with a peak of 9.02 in (229 mm) in <a href="/wiki/Hockessin,_Delaware" title="Hockessin, Delaware">Hockessin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dedam_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-dedam-15">[15]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Downingtown,_Pennsylvania" title="Downingtown, Pennsylvania">Downingtown, Pennsylvania</a>, over eight inches (203 mm) of rain fell in around six hours, while Doppler Radar estimated that <a href="/wiki/Kennett_Square,_Pennsylvania" title="Kennett Square, Pennsylvania">Kennett Square</a> received over 10 inches (255 mm) in a five-hour period. The heavy rainfall led to record discharge rates along the <a href="/wiki/Red_Clay_Creek" title="Red Clay Creek">Red Clay Creek</a>, which also had a record crest peaking at just below 26 feet (8 m). Parts of the creek saw a <a href="/wiki/100-year_flood" title="100-year flood">500-year flood</a>, which has a 0.2% chance of occurring in any given year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deriv_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-deriv-16">[16]</a></sup> Numerous rivers in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> also crested above their flood stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-padam_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-padam-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>, the flooding damaged numerous houses, including 194 in the <a href="/wiki/Glenville,_Delaware" title="Glenville, Delaware">Glenville</a> area. The rapid flooding trapped numerous people in their cars and homes, forcing at least one rescue by helicopter. Those people were evacuated to nearby schools. The severe flooding washed out most of a bridge in Hockessin<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dedam_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-dedam-15">[15]</a></sup> and destroyed 6 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wilmington_%26_Western_Railroad" title="Wilmington &amp; Western Railroad">Wilmington &amp; Western Railroad</a> bridges,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-derailrebuild_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-derailrebuild-18">[18]</a></sup> causing about $5 million (2003 USD, $5.5 million 2006 USD) in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-derail_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-derail-19">[19]</a></sup> The railroad bridges were still being rebuilt as of 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-derail2_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-derail2-20">[20]</a></sup> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Greenbank_Mill&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Greenbank Mill (page does not exist)">Greenbank Mill</a>, a historic <a href="/wiki/Gristmill" title="Gristmill">gristmill</a> complex, saw $450,000 in damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grm_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-grm-21">[21]</a></sup> Damage in Delaware totaled to $16.1 million (2003 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>, $17.5 million 2006 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dedam_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-dedam-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Flash flooding in Pennsylvania resulted in 2,600 emergency 911 calls and around 100 rescues for cars or houses. The flooding destroyed 12 homes, and damaged 336, over half of them severely. Wet soil downed trees and power lines, causing power outages to 109,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PECO_Energy" title="PECO Energy">PECO Energy</a> customers. High floodwaters damaged 22 bridges and closed 2 indefinitely,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-padam_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-padam-17">[17]</a></sup> while the flooding closed several roads, including a portion of <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_1" title="U.S. Route 1">U.S. Highway 1</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chadds_Ford,_Pennsylvania" title="Chadds Ford, Pennsylvania">Chadds Ford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-padam2_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-padam2-22">[22]</a></sup> Damage in Pennsylvania totaled to $3.5 million (2003 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>, $3.8 million 2006 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-padam_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-padam-17">[17]</a></sup> The impacts were severely compounded the following week by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">Hurricane Isabel</a> across the region.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Henri_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 23, just days after the storm moved through, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> declared <a href="/wiki/New_Castle_County,_Delaware" title="New Castle County, Delaware">New Castle County, Delaware</a> as a disaster area following the effects of Henri and later <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">Hurricane Isabel</a>. The declaration designated the affected citizens eligible for grants to pay for temporary housing, house repairs, and serious disaster-related expenses. The declaration also allowed for federal funding for 75% of the repair cost for replacing public facilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-defema_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-defema-23">[23]</a></sup> By two months after the storm, 659 residents had applied for disaster aid through the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">Federal Emergency Management Agency</a> (FEMA), totaling to just over $1 million (2003 USD, $1.1 million 2006 USD). 141 small businesses applied for loans, totaling to around $2.5 million (2003 USD, $2.75 million 2006 USD). In addition, FEMA received 183 applications for public assistance, which would be used for rebuilding public roads and buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deaid_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-deaid-24">[24]</a></sup> Over twenty volunteer organizations met to establish a long-term committee to find resources for disaster recovery needs. One goal sought by the committee was to find a permanent housing solution for every one who was displaced from their houses from the storms. Volunteers also helped remove ruined appliances and furniture to local landfills, totaling to more than 300 tons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-devolunt_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-devolunt-25">[25]</a></sup> State and county governments in Delaware purchased 171 homes following the damage in the Glenville area, the highest number of houses purchased in the state due to storm damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-debuy_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-debuy-26">[26]</a></sup> The house purchasing was done to mitigate the flood damage by restoring the area as a <a href="/wiki/Wetland" title="Wetland">wetland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dewetland_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-dewetland-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 26, President Bush also declared <a href="/wiki/Chester_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Chester County, Pennsylvania">Chester County, Pennsylvania</a> as a disaster area following the damage of Henri, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">Tropical Storm Isabel</a>, and severe flooding unrelated to either tropical cyclone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paid_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-paid-28">[28]</a></sup> By a month after the declaration, 342 homeowners and business owners applied for disaster aid, totaling to around $600,000 (2003 USD, $660,000 2006 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paaid_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-paaid-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
