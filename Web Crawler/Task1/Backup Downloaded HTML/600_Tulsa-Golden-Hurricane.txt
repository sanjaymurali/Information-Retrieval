<p>The <b>Golden Hurricanes</b> are the <a href="/wiki/Sport" title="Sport">athletic</a> teams that represent the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tulsa" title="University of Tulsa">University of Tulsa</a>. These teams are referred to as the Tulsa Golden Hurricane (or variously as Hurricane, TU or Tulsa). Before adopting the name Golden Hurricane in 1922, the University of Tulsa (TU) had many unofficial team nicknames including Kendallites (from TU's predecessor institution <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_Kendall_College" title="Henry Kendall College">Henry Kendall College</a>), Presbyterians (from the university's founding by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Presbyterian_Church_(U.S.A.)" title="Presbyterian Church (U.S.A.)">Presbyterian Church</a>), Tulsans, Tigers, Orange and Black and Yellow Jackets. The name "Golden Tornadoes" was chosen by TU football coach H.M. Archer (1922–24) based on new gold and black uniforms (rather than the previous orange and black) and a remark made during practice of the team "roaring through opponents" (during a season when TU went undefeated, including wins over <a href="/wiki/Texas_A%26M_University" title="Texas A&amp;M University">Texas A&amp;M</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Arkansas" title="University of Arkansas">University of Arkansas</a>). However, it was quickly discovered that the same name had been chosen in 1917 by <a href="/wiki/Georgia_Tech_Yellow_Jackets" title="Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets">Georgia Tech</a>. Archer then substituted the term "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">hurricane</a>" for "<a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornado</a>" and a team vote prior to leaving for the game against Texas A&amp;M confirmed the official nickname as "Golden Hurricane".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Tulsa Golden Hurricane is a member of the <a href="/wiki/American_Athletic_Conference" title="American Athletic Conference">American Athletic Conference</a> (The American). The Golden Hurricane were members of <a href="/wiki/Conference_USA" title="Conference USA">Conference USA</a> from 2005 to 2014, winning 40 conference championships, including 16 postseason titles and nine regular season crowns (including three West Division titles). It's the most C-USA titles (Rice is second with 16) since the league re-aligned in 2005–06. It was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Athletic_Conference" title="Western Athletic Conference">Western Athletic Conference</a> from 1996–2004 and the <a href="/wiki/Missouri_Valley_Conference" title="Missouri Valley Conference">Missouri Valley Conference</a> from 1935 to 1996. In 1985, the MVC dropped football so Tulsa participated as an <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I_FBS_independent_schools" title="NCAA Division I FBS independent schools">Independent</a> in football, but remained as an MVC member in other sports, including basketball. Prior to 1935, Tulsa was a member of several conferences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 2, 2013, Tulsa announced that in July 2014 it would leave C-USA for the <a href="/wiki/Big_East_Conference_(1979%E2%80%932013)" title="Big East Conference (1979–2013)">Big East Conference</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> which would rename itself the following day as the <a href="/wiki/American_Athletic_Conference" title="American Athletic Conference">American Athletic Conference</a> (The American).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tulsa has won six national championships (three NCAA): four in women's golf and two in men's basketball. The University of Tulsa currently fields a varsity team in eight men's sports and ten women's sports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Football">Football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tulsa_Golden_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Tulsa_Golden_Hurricane_football" title="Tulsa Golden Hurricane football">Tulsa Golden Hurricane football</a></div>
<p>Tulsa has the smallest undergraduate enrollment of all schools that participate in NCAA <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Division_I_(NCAA)" title="Division I (NCAA)">Bowl Sub-Division</a> <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">football</a>. The football team is coached by <a href="/wiki/Philip_Montgomery" title="Philip Montgomery">Philip Montgomery</a>, who was the Offensive Coordinator at Baylor for 7 years. He replaced <a href="/wiki/Bill_Blankenship" title="Bill Blankenship">Bill Blankenship</a> who replaced <a href="/wiki/Todd_Graham" title="Todd Graham">Todd Graham</a> who had replaced <a href="/wiki/Steve_Kragthorpe" title="Steve Kragthorpe">Steve Kragthorpe</a>, who led the team to three <a href="/wiki/Bowl_game" title="Bowl game">bowl games</a> in four seasons before accepting the head coaching job at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Louisville" title="University of Louisville">University of Louisville</a> after the 2006–7 season. Graham, a former Tulsa <a href="/wiki/Defensive_coordinator" title="Defensive coordinator">defensive coordinator</a>, returned to the team after serving as the head coach at <a href="/wiki/Rice_University" title="Rice University">Rice University</a> for one season.</p>
<p>Tulsa has appeared in five <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bowl_games" title="Bowl games">bowl games</a> between the 2003 and 2008 seasons, including four straight: the <a href="/wiki/2005_Liberty_Bowl" title="2005 Liberty Bowl">2005 Liberty Bowl</a>, <a href="/wiki/2006_Armed_Forces_Bowl" title="2006 Armed Forces Bowl">2006 Armed Forces Bowl</a>, <a href="/wiki/2008_GMAC_Bowl" title="2008 GMAC Bowl">2008 GMAC Bowl</a>, and <a href="/wiki/2009_GMAC_Bowl" title="2009 GMAC Bowl">2009 GMAC Bowl</a>. Tulsa's football teams have appeared in a total of 16 bowl games and Tulsa was the first university to play in five straight New Year's Day bowl games: the <a href="/wiki/1942_Sun_Bowl" title="1942 Sun Bowl">1942 Sun Bowl</a>, <a href="/wiki/1943_Sugar_Bowl" title="1943 Sugar Bowl">1943 Sugar Bowl</a>, <a href="/wiki/1944_Sugar_Bowl" title="1944 Sugar Bowl">1944 Sugar Bowl</a>, <a href="/wiki/1945_Orange_Bowl" title="1945 Orange Bowl">1945 Orange Bowl</a>, and <a href="/wiki/1946_Oil_Bowl" title="1946 Oil Bowl">1946 Oil Bowl</a>. Tulsa football finished the 1991 season ranked 21st in the nation by the <a href="/wiki/AP_Poll" title="AP Poll">Associated Press</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coaches_Poll" title="Coaches Poll">USA Today/Coaches</a> polls. During the 2008 season Tulsa football was ranked as high as 19th nationally by the AP poll, and 18th in both the <a href="/wiki/Bowl_Championship_Series" title="Bowl Championship Series">BCS</a> rankings and <a href="/wiki/Coaches_Poll" title="Coaches Poll">USA Today/Coaches</a> poll. Tulsa also led all Division I FBS schools in total offense in 2008 (7,978 yds) and in 2007 (7,832 yds). Tulsa football finished the 2010 season ranked 24th in the <a href="/wiki/AP_Poll" title="AP Poll">Associated Press</a> poll and finished the 2012 season ranked 25th in the <a href="/wiki/Coaches_Poll" title="Coaches Poll">USA Today/Coaches</a> poll.</p>
<p>The University of Tulsa has three <a href="/wiki/Pro_Football_Hall_of_Fame" title="Pro Football Hall of Fame">Pro Football Hall of Fame</a> inductees. <a href="/wiki/Steve_Largent" title="Steve Largent">Steve Largent</a> and the late <a href="/wiki/Jim_Finks" title="Jim Finks">Jim Finks</a> were members of the 1995 class, while <a href="/wiki/Bob_St._Clair" title="Bob St. Clair">Bob St. Clair</a> was a 1990 inductee. TU shares the lead for the most NFL Hall of Famers among the Division I FBS football schools in the state of Oklahoma. The University of Tulsa can also take much of the credit for developing the <a href="/wiki/Forward_pass" title="Forward pass">passing</a> game in collegiate football in the 1960s. Tulsa led the nation in passing in 1962, 1963, 1964, 1965 and 1966. Quarterbacks <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Rhome" title="Jerry Rhome">Jerry Rhome</a> (1964) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billy_Guy_Anderson" title="Billy Guy Anderson">Billy Guy Anderson</a> (1965) and receiver <a href="/wiki/Howard_Twilley" title="Howard Twilley">Howard Twilley</a> (1964–65) set most of the NCAA passing and receiving records, some of which still stand today.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Basketball">Basketball</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tulsa_Golden_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Basketball">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Tulsa_Golden_Hurricane_men%27s_basketball" title="Tulsa Golden Hurricane men's basketball">Tulsa Golden Hurricane men's basketball</a></div>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Tulsa_Golden_Hurricane_women%27s_basketball" title="Tulsa Golden Hurricane women's basketball">Tulsa Golden Hurricane women's basketball</a></div>
<p>The Tulsa Golden Hurricane <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> program has had a history of success, including <a href="/wiki/National_Invitation_Tournament" title="National Invitation Tournament">NIT</a> championships in 1981 and 2001, an <a href="/wiki/Elite_Eight" title="Elite Eight">Elite Eight</a> appearance in 2000, three Sweet Sixteen appearances, and 14 trips to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NCAA_Men%27s_Division_I_Basketball_Championship" title="NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Championship">Big Dance</a>. The Golden Hurricane have a <a href="/wiki/Oral_Roberts%E2%80%93Tulsa_basketball_rivalry" title="Oral Roberts–Tulsa basketball rivalry">long-standing basketball rivalry</a> with <a href="/wiki/Oral_Roberts_Golden_Eagles_men%27s_basketball" title="Oral Roberts Golden Eagles men's basketball">Oral Roberts Golden Eagles</a>. The two teams have exchanged a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Traveling_trophy" title="Traveling trophy">traveling trophy</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor%27s_Cup_(Oral_Roberts%E2%80%93Tulsa)" title="Mayor's Cup (Oral Roberts–Tulsa)">Mayor's Cup</a>. Tulsa is also known for developing many coaches who have gone on to great success in the NCAA, including current <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Texas_Tech_men%27s_basketball_coach&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Texas Tech men's basketball coach (page does not exist)">Texas Tech men's basketball coach</a> <a href="/wiki/Tubby_Smith" title="Tubby Smith">Tubby Smith</a>, who won a national championship while he coached at the <a href="/wiki/Kentucky_Wildcats_men%27s_basketball" title="Kentucky Wildcats men's basketball">University of Kentucky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kansas_Jayhawks_men%27s_basketball" title="Kansas Jayhawks men's basketball">Kansas'</a> <a href="/wiki/Bill_Self" title="Bill Self">Bill Self</a>, and one of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arkansas_Razorbacks_basketball" title="Arkansas Razorbacks basketball">Arkansas'</a> most notable former coaches, <a href="/wiki/Nolan_Richardson" title="Nolan Richardson">Nolan Richardson</a>, who is the only head coach to win the NJCAA tournament (with Western Texas Junior College), the NIT (with Tulsa), and the NCAA tournament (with Arkansas). The current men's basketball head coach is <a href="/wiki/Frank_Haith" title="Frank Haith">Frank Haith</a>.</p>
<p>In 2006, the Tulsa women's basketball team earned its first appearance in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NCAA_Women%27s_Division_I_Basketball_Championship" title="NCAA Women's Division I Basketball Championship">NCAA tournament</a> by winning Conference USA's regular-season and tournament championships. The Golden Hurricane's accomplishment came after back-to-back 19-win seasons and <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_National_Invitation_Tournament" title="Women's National Invitation Tournament">WNIT</a> appearances in 2004 and 2005. Tulsa also appeared in the 2002 WNIT, making it to the second round.</p>
<h3><span id="Men's_soccer"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Men.27s_soccer">Men's soccer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tulsa_Golden_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Men's soccer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Tulsa_Golden_Hurricane_men%27s_soccer" title="Tulsa Golden Hurricane men's soccer">Tulsa Golden Hurricane men's soccer</a></div>

<p>The Tulsa Men's Soccer team has a strong history since it became a varsity sport in 1980. TU has won four conference championships (1991 MVC, 2007, 2008, 2009 C-USA) and eight conference tournaments (1991 MVC, 2007, 2008, 2009, 2012 C-USA, 2014, 2015, 2016 AAC). Tulsa holds the NCAA record for both consecutive home wins and consecutive home games without a loss, set during the same stretch of 39 games from 1988 to 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The Golden Hurricane has twice reached the elite-eight of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NCAA_Men%27s_Soccer_Championship" title="NCAA Men's Soccer Championship">NCAA Men's Soccer Championship</a>, falling to eventual champion <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Hoosiers#Men.27s_Soccer" title="Indiana Hoosiers">Indiana</a> in 2004 and top seeded <a href="/wiki/Akron_Zips_men%27s_soccer" title="Akron Zips men's soccer">Akron</a> in 2009. Tulsa has participated in 11 NCAA tournaments (1991, 2003, 2004, 2007, 2008, 2009, 2010, 2012, 2014, 2015, 2016) and has been frequently ranked in the top 25. The school has produced first team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Division_I_First-Team_All-American_(soccer)" title="Division I First-Team All-American (soccer)">All-American</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Pore" title="Ryan Pore">Ryan Pore</a>, who currently plays for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portland_Timbers_(USL)" title="Portland Timbers (USL)">Portland Timbers</a>, and previously played for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Wizards" title="Kansas City Wizards">Kansas City Wizards</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a>. <a href="/wiki/Terry_Boss" title="Terry Boss">Terry Boss</a> of <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Sounders_FC" title="Seattle Sounders FC">Seattle Sounders FC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lawson_Vaughn" title="Lawson Vaughn">Lawson Vaughn</a> of <a href="/wiki/D.C._United" title="D.C. United">D.C. United</a> are former Golden Hurricane currently playing in MLS. <a href="/wiki/Dominic_Cervi" title="Dominic Cervi">Dominic Cervi</a> plays for <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic F.C.</a> of <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, and fellow goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Tyrel_Lacey" title="Tyrel Lacey">Tyrel Lacey</a> has signed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Lyn_Oslo" title="FC Lyn Oslo">FC Lyn Oslo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Since 1995, the head coach has been Tom McIntosh, a prominent figure in Tulsa soccer for over 20 years. The team plays in the <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Soccer_%26_Track_Stadium" title="Hurricane Soccer &amp; Track Stadium">Hurricane Soccer &amp; Track Stadium</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tennis">Tennis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tulsa_Golden_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tennis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Golden Hurricane Tennis program has string of success, including men's Missouri Valley championships in 1995 and 1996, men's Conference USA championships in 2006, 2007, 2008, 2009, and 2011 and women's Conference USA championships in 2007, 2008, 2010, and 2011. In 2007, Tulsa's top-ranked player <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arnau_Brugu%C3%A9s-Davi" title="Arnau Brugués-Davi">Arnau Brugués-Davi</a> ranked as high as #1 in the nation and a four time All-American, advanced to the quarterfinals of the singles competition at the <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Men%27s_Tennis_Championship" title="NCAA Men's Tennis Championship">NCAA Men's Tennis Championship</a>, improving on his 2006 round of sixteen appearance. The University of Tulsa also boasts one of the nation's top tennis facilities, the <a href="/wiki/Michael_D._Case_Tennis_Center" title="Michael D. Case Tennis Center">Michael D. Case Tennis Center</a>, which was host to the 2004 and 2008 NCAA tennis championships.</p>
<h3><span id="Women's_golf"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Women.27s_golf">Women's golf</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tulsa_Golden_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Women's golf">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>TU has won four team national titles in women's golf: the 1980 and 1982 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AIAW_Champions#Golf" title="AIAW Champions">AIAW</a> titles and the 1982 and 1988 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NCAA_Women%27s_Golf_Championship" title="NCAA Women's Golf Championship">NCAA</a> titles, with the 1988 title later vacated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Golfers <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Lopez" title="Nancy Lopez">Nancy Lopez</a> (1976), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kathy_Baker_(golfer)" title="Kathy Baker (golfer)">Kathy Baker</a> (1982 AIAW), and <a href="/wiki/Melissa_McNamara" title="Melissa McNamara">Melissa McNamara</a> (1988) have won individual national titles. Other notable Golden Hurricane female golfers are <a href="/wiki/Stacy_Prammanasudh" title="Stacy Prammanasudh">Stacy Prammanasudh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Robbins" title="Kelly Robbins">Kelly Robbins</a>. The team has won 12 conference championships:</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Baseball">Baseball</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tulsa_Golden_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Baseball">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Tulsa_Golden_Hurricane_baseball" title="Tulsa Golden Hurricane baseball">Tulsa Golden Hurricane baseball</a></div>
<p>Tulsa's baseball team played in the <a href="/wiki/College_World_Series" title="College World Series">College World Series</a> twice, finishing in second and third place. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1969_College_World_Series" title="1969 College World Series">1969</a> the Golden Hurricane made it to the final game of the double-elimination tournament before losing to <a href="/wiki/Arizona_State_Sun_Devils_baseball" title="Arizona State Sun Devils baseball">Arizona State</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> First baseman Steve Caves and third baseman Les Rogers were named to the all-tournament team. In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1971_College_World_Series" title="1971 College World Series">1971</a> Tulsa lasted until the next-to-last game of the tournament before being eliminated. First baseman <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Tabb" title="Jerry Tabb">Jerry Tabb</a> was named the tournament's most valuable player; pitcher <a href="/wiki/Steve_Rogers_(baseball)" title="Steve Rogers (baseball)">Steve Rogers</a> and outfielder <a href="/wiki/Steve_Bowling" title="Steve Bowling">Steve Bowling</a> were also named to the all-tournament team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Madden_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Madden-14">[14]</a></sup> Tulsa held the number one ranking in the polls for part of the 1972 season<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klein_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Klein-15">[15]</a></sup> before being knocked out of that year's playoffs at the district level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Madden_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Madden-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Of the Tulsa baseball players who later played in the major leagues, the most successful was Steve Rogers, who pitched for 12 years with the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos</a> and was selected to five <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="Major League Baseball All-Star Game">All-Star</a> teams. Other Hurricanes who played in the majors included <a href="/wiki/Bud_Bloomfield" title="Bud Bloomfield">Bud Bloomfield</a>, Steve Bowling, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Calvert" title="Mark Calvert">Mark Calvert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mardie_Cornejo" title="Mardie Cornejo">Mardie Cornejo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Sember" title="Mike Sember">Mike Sember</a>, and Jerry Tabb.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tulsa dropped its baseball program in 1980, citing rising costs and the need to fund a full women's sports program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Gene Shell, who had coached the program from 1965 to 1980, finished his career at Tulsa with a 478–199 record,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and was the fourth winningest coach in college baseball when the program ended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In recent years, the university has been reported on several occasions to be considering whether to restore the baseball program; a formal study was conducted in 2009 after the <a href="/wiki/Tulsa_Drillers" title="Tulsa Drillers">Tulsa Drillers</a> moved into their new <a href="/wiki/ONEOK_Field" title="ONEOK Field">downtown stadium</a>, leaving <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drillers_Stadium" title="Drillers Stadium">Drillers Stadium</a> potentially available for college baseball, but the school concluded that the financial demands of adding a new sport were more than it could accept.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Klein_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Klein-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mascot">Mascot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tulsa_Golden_Hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Mascot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1994 to 2009, Tulsa's mascot was <i>Captain 'Cane</i>, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthropomorphized" title="Anthropomorphized">anthropomorphized</a> golden <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">hurricane</a> with human attributes such as biceps, clothes, and a perpetual smirk. This representation of Captain 'Cane was named by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_(USA)" title="Fox Sports (USA)">Fox Sports</a> as the second worst mascot in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> From 1977 to 1994, the mascot was known as <i>Hurc</i> or <i>Huffy</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TU_unveils_new_Captain_.27Cane_mascot_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-TU_unveils_new_Captain_.27Cane_mascot-22">[22]</a></sup> In 2009, Captain Cane was redesigned as a human superhero with the power to summon weather.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TU_unveils_new_Captain_.27Cane_mascot_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-TU_unveils_new_Captain_.27Cane_mascot-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
