<p>The <b>hurricane</b> is a sweet alcoholic drink made with <a href="/wiki/Rum" title="Rum">rum</a>, fruit juice, and <a href="/wiki/Syrup" title="Syrup">syrup</a> or <a href="/wiki/Grenadine" title="Grenadine">grenadine</a>. It is one of many popular drinks served in <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>.</p>
<p>The creation of this <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Passion_fruit" title="Passion fruit">passion fruit</a>–colored relative of a <a href="/wiki/Daiquiri" title="Daiquiri">daiquiri</a> is credited to New Orleans tavern owner <a href="/wiki/Pat_O%27Brien%27s_Bar" title="Pat O'Brien's Bar">Pat O'Brien</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Southern_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Southern-1">[1]</a></sup> The bar allegedly started as a <a href="/wiki/Speakeasy" title="Speakeasy">speakeasy</a> called Mr. O'Brien's Club Tipperary and the password was "storm's brewin'<span style="padding-left:.2em;">"</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Southern_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Southern-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 1940s, he needed to create a new drink to help him get rid of all of the less-popular rum that local distributors forced him to buy before he could get a few cases of more popular liquors such as scotch and other whiskeys.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He poured the concoction into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_lamp" title="Hurricane lamp">hurricane lamp</a>–shaped glasses and gave it away to sailors.</p>
<p>The drink caught on, and it has been a mainstay in the <a href="/wiki/French_Quarter" title="French Quarter">French Quarter</a> ever since. It is more commonly served in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disposable" title="Disposable">disposable</a> <a href="/wiki/Plastic_cup" title="Plastic cup">plastic cup</a>, as New Orleans laws permit <a href="/wiki/Drinking_in_public" title="Drinking in public">drinking in public</a> and leaving a bar with a drink, but the city prohibits public drinking from glass containers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The hurricane cocktail is made differently on the islands of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>. The drink is composed of various measures of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coffee_liqueur" title="Coffee liqueur">coffee liqueur</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/151_rum" title="151 rum">151 rum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irish_cream" title="Irish cream">Irish cream</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Grand_Marnier" title="Grand Marnier">Grand Marnier</a>. It is commonly found in the downtown bars of <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">Nassau</a>.</p>
