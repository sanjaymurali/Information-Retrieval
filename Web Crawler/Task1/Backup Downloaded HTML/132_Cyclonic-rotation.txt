<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Cyclonic rotation</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Cyclonic rotation</b> or <b>circulation</b> is movement in the same direction as the <a href="/wiki/Earth%27s_rotation" title="Earth's rotation">Earth's rotation</a>, as opposed to <a href="/wiki/Anticyclonic_rotation" title="Anticyclonic rotation">anticyclonic rotation</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coriolis_effect" title="Coriolis effect">Coriolis effect</a> causes cyclonic rotation to be in a counterclockwise direction in the northern hemisphere, and clockwise in the southern hemisphere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-about-1">[1]</a></sup> A closed area of winds rotating cyclonically is known as a <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">cyclone</a>.</p>
