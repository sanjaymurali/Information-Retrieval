<p><b>Severe Tropical Cyclone Rewa</b> affected six countries and caused 22 deaths on its 28-day journey across the South Pacific Ocean in December 1993 and January 1994. Cyclone Rewa developed from a tropical disturbance on 28 December south of <a href="/wiki/Nauru" title="Nauru">Nauru</a>. After forming, Rewa moved southwest through the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a>, crossing the <a href="/wiki/160th_meridian_east" title="160th meridian east">160th meridian east</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basins#South_Pacific_Ocean" title="Tropical cyclone basins">South Pacific basin</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basins#Australian_region" title="Tropical cyclone basins">Australian region</a>. The cyclone began to strengthen steadily and turned southward, paralleling the eastern Australian coast through 31 December. Rewa reached its initial peak intensity as a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">Category 4</a> tropical cyclone on 2 January. It maintained this intensity for about 12 hours before an increase in <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> induced its weakening by 3 January. The cyclone turned southeastward and moved back into the South Pacific basin on 4 January, before it passed over <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia" title="New Caledonia">New Caledonia</a> between 5–6 January. After affecting New Caledonia, Rewa weakened to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" title="Tropical depression">tropical depression</a> and turned northwestward before re-entering the Australian basin on 10 January.</p>
<p>Over the following days, the cyclone showed signs of restrengthening and executed an elongated cyclonic loop to the southeast of <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>. Rewa subsequently entered a phase of quick intensification while proceeding southeastward, peaking in intensity as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_scale" title="Australian scale">Category 5 severe tropical cyclone</a>. It recurved toward the southwest while gradually weakening for several days. Although forecasters had predicted Rewa to make <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> near <a href="/wiki/Mackay,_Queensland" title="Mackay, Queensland">Mackay, Queensland</a>, the cyclone began interacting with an <a href="/wiki/Upper_tropospheric_cyclonic_vortex" title="Upper tropospheric cyclonic vortex">upper-level trough</a> on 18 January, causing it to divert to the southeast and move along the Queensland coast. Rewa transitioned into an <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">extratropical cyclone</a> on 20 January, with its remnants bringing heavy rain to <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> three days later.</p>
<p>The cyclone caused the deaths of 22 people on its course, affecting parts of the Solomon Islands, Papua New Guinea, <a href="/wiki/Eastern_states_of_Australia" title="Eastern states of Australia">Eastern Australia</a>, New Caledonia, <a href="/wiki/Vanuatu" title="Vanuatu">Vanuatu</a> and New Zealand. Nine people in a banana <a href="/wiki/Dinghy" title="Dinghy">dinghy</a> en route to <a href="/wiki/Rossel_Island" title="Rossel Island">Rossel Island</a> went missing at the height of the storm; they were presumed drowned after wreckage from their boat turned up at the island. In Queensland, three people died in traffic accidents caused by the storm, and another fatality occurred when a boy became trapped in a storm pipe. One death took place in New Caledonia, while flooding caused eight drownings in Papua New Guinea. After this usage of the name Rewa, the name was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_retired_South_Pacific_tropical_cyclone_names#Tropical_cyclone_names_retired_in_the_1990s" title="List of retired South Pacific tropical cyclone names">retired</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Rewa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rewa_1993_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="812" data-file-width="1024" height="174" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Rewa_1993_track.png/220px-Rewa_1993_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Rewa_1993_track.png/330px-Rewa_1993_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Rewa_1993_track.png/440px-Rewa_1993_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rewa_1993_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Track map of Tropical Cyclone Rewa, based on data from the Joint Typhoon Warning Center.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>During 26 December, the <a href="/wiki/Fiji_Meteorological_Service" title="Fiji Meteorological Service">Fiji Meteorological Service</a> started to monitor a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" title="Tropical depression">tropical depression</a> that had developed within the <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</a>, about 575 km (355 mi) to the south-east of <a href="/wiki/Nauru" title="Nauru">Nauru</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_FMS_Report_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_FMS_Report-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_BT_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_BT-2">[2]</a></sup> Over the next few days the depression gradually developed and organised further, as it moved towards the west-southwest under the influence of a north-easterly steering flow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_FMS_Report_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_FMS_Report-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DTDSDEC93_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-DTDSDEC93-3">[3]</a></sup> During 28 December, the JTWC classified the depression as Tropical Cyclone 05P, before the FMS named it Rewa as it had become a category 1 tropical cyclone on the Australian tropical cyclone intensity scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hanstrum_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hanstrum-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wellington_BT_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wellington_BT-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_ATCR_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_ATCR-6">[6]</a></sup> Over the next few days, the system gradually intensified under the influence of favourable upper-level winds while it moved towards the south-southwest, passing through the Solomon Islands on 29 December and affecting the southeastern islands of <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DTDSDEC93_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-DTDSDEC93-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> As it moved through the Solomon Islands, Rewa moved out of the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basins#South_Pacific_Ocean" title="Tropical cyclone basins">South Pacific basin</a> and into the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basins#Australian_region" title="Tropical cyclone basins">Australian region</a>, where the Australian <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Meteorology" title="Bureau of Meteorology">Bureau of Meteorology</a> (BOM) took the primary warning responsibility for the system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hanstrum_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hanstrum-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diary_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Diary-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 30 December, the JTWC reported that Rewa had become equivalent to a category 1 hurricane on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_hurricane_scale" title="Saffir-Simpson hurricane scale">Saffir-Simpson hurricane scale</a> <small>(SSHS)</small>; early the next day the BoM reported that the system had developed into a category 3 severe tropical cyclone, and an <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a> became visible on satellite imagery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_FMS_Report_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_FMS_Report-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_BT_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_BT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BoM_TC_Database_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-BoM_TC_Database-9">[9]</a></sup> The system then abruptly turned and started to move towards the south parallel to the Queensland coast because of an interaction with an upper-level trough of low pressure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DTDSDEC93_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-DTDSDEC93-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hanstrum_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hanstrum-4">[4]</a></sup> On 2 January both the JTWC and the BoM reported that Rewa had reached its peak intensity, with the JTWC reporting that Rewa had peaked with <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">1-minute windspeeds</a> of 230 km/h (145 mph), equivalent to a category 4 hurricane on the SSHWS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_BT_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_BT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hanstrum_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Hanstrum-4">[4]</a></sup> Meanwhile, the BoM reported that the system had peaked with 10-minute windspeeds of 205 km/h (125 mph) with a central pressure of 920 <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hectopascals</a> (27.17 <a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">inHg</a>), which made it a category 5 severe tropical cyclone on the Australian Scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hanstrum_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-Hanstrum-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BoM_TC_Database_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-BoM_TC_Database-9">[9]</a></sup> Rewa remained at its peak intensity for 24 hours before the system started to weaken during 3 January, as strengthening upper level north-westerlies increased vertical wind shear over the system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DTDSJAN94_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-DTDSJAN94-11">[11]</a></sup> Throughout that day, the cyclone started to move towards the south-east, before it started to take a more eastwards track as it approached 160°E.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>During 4 January, Rewa moved back into the South Pacific basin and continued eastward as a weakening category 3 severe tropical cyclone, with the FMS estimating the 10-minute sustained windspeeds at 150 km/h (90 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wellington_BT_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Wellington_BT-5">[5]</a></sup> During the next day, its eye became obscured after the mountains of New Caledonia affected Rewa's circulation by creating a hot and dry <a href="/wiki/Foehn_wind" title="Foehn wind">Foehn wind</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_FMS_Report_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_FMS_Report-1">[1]</a></sup> The FMS reported that the system had weakened into a category 2 tropical cyclone, while the JTWC reported that Rewa had become a tropical storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_BT_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_BT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> The system subsequently made landfall in the vicinity of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_Bay" title="Saint Vincent Bay">Saint Vincent Bay</a> on New Caledonia's south-western coast, before it emerged near <a href="/wiki/Thio,_New_Caledonia" title="Thio, New Caledonia">Thio</a> from the east coast during 6 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_FMS_Report_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_FMS_Report-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> By 7 January, Rewa's circulation had become exposed under the influence of upper level north-westerlies; as a result both the FMS and JTWC reported that Rewa had weakened into a depression.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_BT_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_BT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> Over the next 3 days, the residual low-level circulation was steered towards the northwest and Papua New Guinea in a southeasterly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trade_wind" title="Trade wind">trade wind</a> flow generated by a powerful area of high pressure located between New Zealand and <a href="/wiki/Kermadec_Islands" title="Kermadec Islands">Kermadec Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_FMS_Report_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_FMS_Report-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DTDSJAN94_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-DTDSJAN94-11">[11]</a></sup> During 10 January, Rewa's remnants moved out of the South Pacific basin and back into the Australian region while starting to re-intensify into a tropical cyclone, as a mid to upper level trough over eastern Australia increased in size.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DTDSJAN94_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-DTDSJAN94-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>As moved into the Australian region, Rewa began to move in a long arc to the northwest and then to the north, and on 13 January, both the JTWC and the <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a> National Weather Service reported that the system had re-intensified into a tropical cyclone around Papua New Guinea's southern islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_BT_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_BT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> Later that day, Rewa executed a sharp clockwise turn near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vanatinai" title="Vanatinai">Tagula Island</a> and started to move towards the southeast and the BoM's area of responsibility while gradually intensifying further.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-7"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> During 15 January, the BoM reported that the system had re-intensified into a category 3 severe tropical cyclone, while the JTWC reported that Rewa had become equivalent to a category 1 hurricane again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_BT_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_BT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wellington_BT_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Wellington_BT-5">[5]</a></sup> Later that day, the system started to rapidly intensify as an upper level trough approached the system. During the next day the JTWC reported that the system had peaked with 1-minute sustained windspeeds of 230 km/h (145 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_BT_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_BT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-8"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> The BoM then reported later that day, that Rewa had peaked with 10-minute sustained windspeeds of 205 km/h (125 mph), which made it a category 5 severe tropical cyclone on the Australian tropical cyclone intensity scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BoM_TC_Database_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-BoM_TC_Database-9">[9]</a></sup> After it had peaked in intensity the system recurved towards the south-southwest, and started to gradually weaken.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_BT_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_BT-2">[2]</a></sup> By 18 January the JTWC reported that Rewa had weakened into a tropical storm, while throughout that day the BoM assessed Rewa as a category 3 severe tropical cyclone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_BT_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_BT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-9"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> During the next day, the BoM reported that Rewa had weakened into a category 2 tropical cyclone as it recurved and started to move towards the south-east about 265 km (165 mi) to the east of <a href="/wiki/Mackay,_Queensland" title="Mackay, Queensland">Mackay, Queensland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-10"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wellington_BT_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Wellington_BT-5">[5]</a></sup> Over the next couple of days the system moved towards the south-southeast along the Queensland coast while maintaining its intensity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wellington_BT_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-Wellington_BT-5">[5]</a></sup> During 21 January, the BoM and the JTWC reported that Rewa had weakened below cyclone strength, as the system moved back into the South Pacific basin for the third and final time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hanstrum_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-Hanstrum-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_ATCR_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_ATCR-6">[6]</a></sup> Rewa's remnants were last noted late on 23 January, by New Zealand's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metservice" title="Metservice">Metservice</a>, bringing heavy rain to New Zealand, while dissipating over water about 400 km (250 mi) to the east of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wellington,_New_Zealand" title="Wellington, New Zealand">Wellington, New Zealand</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hanstrum_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-Hanstrum-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wellington_BT_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-Wellington_BT-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DTDSJAN94_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-DTDSJAN94-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations_and_impact">Preparations and impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Rewa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations and impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Twenty-two people lost their lives in accidents caused by Cyclone Rewa, while it affected parts off the Solomon Islands, Papua New Guinea, Australia, New Caledonia, Vanuatu and New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AAP1_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-AAP1-12">[12]</a></sup> Due to the impact of this storm, the name Rewa was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_retired_South_Pacific_tropical_cyclone_names#Tropical_cyclone_names_retired_in_the_1990s" title="List of retired South Pacific tropical cyclone names">retired</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPAC_TCOP_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-SPAC_TCOP-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="The_Solomon_Islands,_New_Caledonia,_Vanuatu_and_New_Zealand"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Solomon_Islands.2C_New_Caledonia.2C_Vanuatu_and_New_Zealand">The Solomon Islands, New Caledonia, Vanuatu and New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Rewa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Solomon Islands, New Caledonia, Vanuatu and New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Solomon Islands were affected by the developing tropical cyclone between 28 – 30 December and was the first <a href="/wiki/Island_country" title="Island country">Island country</a> to be impacted by Rewa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sol_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sol-14">[14]</a></sup> As it developed into a tropical cyclone on 28 December, the system passed to the north of the outer lying <a href="/wiki/Atoll" title="Atoll">atolls</a> of <a href="/wiki/Malaita_Province" title="Malaita Province">Malaita Province</a> in the Solomon Islands. Rewa then passed over the southern tip of <a href="/wiki/Malaita" title="Malaita">Malaita Island</a>, before passing to the south of <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal" title="Guadalcanal">Guadalcanal Island</a> and the north of <a href="/wiki/Rennell_Island" title="Rennell Island">Rennell Island</a> during 29 December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTWC_BT_2-10"><a href="#cite_note-JTWC_BT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-11"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cyclone Rewa started to affect New Caledonia on 5 January, before it passed over <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia" title="New Caledonia">Grand Terre</a> Island later that day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MFNC_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-MFNC-15">[15]</a></sup> Rewa dumped over 300 mm (12 in) of rain on parts of the archipelago, which made all of the major rivers overflow and burst their banks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MFNC_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-MFNC-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Earthweek_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Earthweek-16">[16]</a></sup> Several landslides and a death were recorded within New Caledonia, while on the <a href="/wiki/Loyalty_Islands" title="Loyalty Islands">Loyalty Island</a> of Mare, waves from Rewa partially destroyed the harbour wall at Tadine's port.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MFNC_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-MFNC-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The system had no effect on Vanuatu while it passed through the Solomon Islands between 28 – 30 December; however the southern islands of Vanuatu were affected by the cyclone after it had passed through New Caledonia during 5 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vanuatu_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vanuatu-17">[17]</a></sup> As it moved towards the north-west between 6–8 January, Rewa brought high seas and strong winds to parts of the island nation including the provinces of <a href="/wiki/Shefa_Province" title="Shefa Province">Shefa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tafea_Province" title="Tafea Province">Tafea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vanuatu_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Vanuatu-17">[17]</a></sup> Some damage was recorded to Port Villa's harbours <a href="/wiki/Intertidal_zone" title="Intertidal zone">intertidal zone</a>, as the system passed about 175 km (110 mi) to the southeast of the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vanuatu_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-Vanuatu-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between 19–24 January, Rewa's remnants, a slow moving <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">weather front</a> over <a href="/wiki/Fiordland" title="Fiordland">Fiordland</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Southerly" title="Southerly">southerly</a> brought widespread rainfall and flooding to New Zealand's South Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Niwa_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Niwa-18">[18]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Westland_District" title="Westland District">Westland</a>, Fiordland and the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Lakes_(New_Zealand)" title="Southern Lakes (New Zealand)">Southern Lakes</a>, landslides and flooding occurred on 21 January, while several roads and bridges were damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Niwa_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Niwa-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Papua_New_Guinea">Papua New Guinea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Rewa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Papua New Guinea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cyclone Rewa affected Papua New Guinea on two separate occasions while it was active, with the cyclone first affecting the archipelago between 28 December and 1 January, before grazing the archipelago between 12-14 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-12"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPAC_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-SPAC-19">[19]</a></sup> Before Rewa affected the country, cyclone <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">alerts and warnings</a> were issued for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vanatinai" title="Vanatinai">Sudest</a>, Rossel and Samarai islands, while authorities urged people not to go to beaches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AAP1_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-AAP1-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PNG_Floods_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-PNG_Floods-21">[21]</a></sup> As it affected the archipelago, Rewa brought heavy rainfall, high seas, and wind gusts of up to 100 km/h (60 mph) to parts of the archipelago including Sudest, Rossel and Samarai islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AAP1_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-AAP1-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9miss_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-9miss-22">[22]</a></sup> The hardest hit areas were communities near major river systems, with the cyclone blocking roads, destroying a church, bridges, homes and gardens with vital crops such as coffee and <a href="/wiki/Copra" title="Copra">copra</a> destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PNG_Floods_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-PNG_Floods-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-floods_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-floods-23">[23]</a></sup> In total, Cyclone Rewa left around 3500 people homeless and caused 17 deaths, 8 of which were due to flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PNG_Floods_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-PNG_Floods-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-floods_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-floods-23">[23]</a></sup> Nine other people went missing while travelling to Rossel Island after their boat was caught up in high seas associated with Rewa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9miss_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-9miss-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13_dead_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-13_dead-24">[24]</a></sup> They were later presumed dead by the National Disaster and Emergency Service, after a local search and rescue mission found wreckage of the boat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AAP1_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-AAP1-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9miss_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-9miss-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13_dead_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-13_dead-24">[24]</a></sup> At 00:00 UTC on 30 December, the automatic weather station at <a href="/wiki/Rossel_Island" title="Rossel Island">Jingo</a> on Rossel island recorded a maximum sustained wind of 55 km/h (35 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Rewa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the opening days of 1994, Rewa moved southwards parallel to the Queensland coast; however, because it was located about 600 km (370 mi) to the north-east of Mackay, it was too far away from the coast for there to be any direct impact on Queensland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wellington_BT_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-Wellington_BT-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-No_relief_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-No_relief-26">[26]</a></sup> The only indirect impact was higher surf, from which several people had to be rescued from before the cyclone started to move towards New Caledonia during 4 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Earthweek_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Earthweek-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-No_relief_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-No_relief-26">[26]</a></sup> As Rewa affected Queensland for the second time, watches and warnings were issued for various parts of Queensland by TCWC Brisbane, who predicted a landfall near Mackay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diary_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Diary-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alert_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Alert-27">[27]</a></sup> On 18  January local disaster committees met to consider evacuating people, while people who were on vacation in national parks were alerted about Rewa by a helicopter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alert_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-Alert-27">[27]</a></sup> A military training exercise that was due to take place in <a href="/wiki/Shoalwater_Bay" title="Shoalwater Bay">Shoalwater Bay</a> had to be cancelled, with army personal evacuated to <a href="/wiki/Rockhampton" title="Rockhampton">Rockhampton</a> to avoid being cut off by flood waters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alert_27-2"><a href="#cite_note-Alert-27">[27]</a></sup> Along the coast, several ports including <a href="/wiki/Gladstone,_Queensland" title="Gladstone, Queensland">Gladstone</a> were shut with large vessels told to head to sea and small vessels told to batten down and prepare for the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alert_27-3"><a href="#cite_note-Alert-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 19 January Rewa started to affect Queensland with torrential rain and storm force windspeeds which caused some damage along the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-13"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup> However it did not make its predicted landfall near Mackay, instead it recurved to the south-southeast and came to within 100 km (60 mi) of the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rewa_TCR_7-14"><a href="#cite_note-Rewa_TCR-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-easing_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-easing-28">[28]</a></sup> Two men off <a href="/wiki/Yeppoon" title="Yeppoon">Yeppoon</a>'s coast were rescued from a fishing trawler by an army <a href="/wiki/Sikorsky_UH-60_Black_Hawk" title="Sikorsky UH-60 Black Hawk">Black Hawk</a> helicopter after high seas damaged the trawler's propeller and snapped its heavy anchor chain, leaving it drifting helplessly in the cyclone's path.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-easing_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-easing-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-impacts_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-impacts-29">[29]</a></sup> The worst affected island was <a href="/wiki/Lady_Elliot_Island" title="Lady Elliot Island">Lady Elliot</a> as it bore the brunt of the wind, while on <a href="/wiki/Heron_Island_(Queensland)" title="Heron Island (Queensland)">Heron Island</a>, several rare trees and bird rookeries were either destroyed or severely damaged. As Rewa interacted with an upper trough of low pressure on 19 January, heavy rainfall and thunderstorms were observed in parts of Brisbane and the Gold Coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brisbane_flooding_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brisbane_flooding-30">[30]</a></sup> Brisbane received over 144 mm (5.7 in) in just six hours, which led to some localised flash flooding in parts of the city and four deaths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-impacts_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-impacts-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brisbane_flooding_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-Brisbane_flooding-30">[30]</a></sup> Three of the deaths were from people crashing their cars, while the other death occurred when a person went surfing Brisbane's flood water and got trapped in a storm drain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-impacts_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-impacts-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Within Brisbane, 100 homes and 20 cars were damaged by the flood waters, while a <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Shield" title="Sheffield Shield">Sheffield Shield</a> <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> match between Western Australia and Queensland was delayed, after <a href="/wiki/The_Gabba" title="The Gabba">the Gabba</a> resembled a small lake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
