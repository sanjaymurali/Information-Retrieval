<p><b>Hurricane Anita</b> was a powerful <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">Atlantic hurricane</a> during an otherwise quiet <a href="/wiki/1977_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1977 Atlantic hurricane season">1977 Atlantic hurricane season</a>. The first <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> of the season, Anita developed from a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> on August 29 in the north-central <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. It tracked westward into an area with conditions favorable for further development, and quickly intensified into a hurricane by late on August 30. Initially, Anita was forecast to strike <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, though a building <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> turned it to the west-southwest. The hurricane rapidly strengthened to attain peak winds of 175 mph (280 km/h), and on September 2 Anita made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> in eastern <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a> as a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">Category 5</a> hurricane. It quickly weakened as it crossed <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, and after briefly redeveloping into a tropical depression in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, Anita dissipated on September 4 to the south of the <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Peninsula" title="Baja California Peninsula">Baja California Peninsula</a>.</p>
<p>The hurricane produced light rainfall and high tides along the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="Gulf Coast of the United States">Gulf Coast of the United States</a>. Some low-level flooding was reported, but damage was slight. In Mexico, the hurricane caused strong winds and moderate rainfall. The winds caused extensive damage to villages in northeastern Mexico, with about 25,000 people left homeless. The rainfall, reaching over 17.52 inches (445 mm), caused flooding and mudslides which killed eleven people in Tamaulipas. Overall damage is unknown.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">Meteorological history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Anita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Anita-11_1977_track.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Anita-11_1977_track.png/220px-Anita-11_1977_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Anita-11_1977_track.png/330px-Anita-11_1977_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Anita-11_1977_track.png/440px-Anita-11_1977_track.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Anita-11_1977_track.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir–Simpson scale">Saffir–Simpson scale</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">tropical wave</a> moved off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> on August 16. The wave axis tracked steadily westward, and on August 23 convection increased after the wave passed beneath a cold-core upper-level low. By August 27, the wave axis was located over <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, with the area of convection located between <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a> after tracking northwestward. The disturbance crossed southern Florida, and after entering the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> an <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">anticyclone</a> provided favorable conditions for further development. It tracked slowly westward at 5 mph (7 km/h), and after developing a surface circulation the system developed into a tropical depression on August 29 while located about 230 miles (370 km) south-southwest of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Orleans,_Louisiana" title="New Orleans, Louisiana">New Orleans, Louisiana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>With a <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">ridge</a> of high pressure to its north, the depression tracked westward. Favorable conditions persisted for several days prior to the arrival of the depression, and as such the depression quickly intensified into Tropical Storm Anita. The storm tracked slowly over warm water temperatures, and throughout its duration it was embedded within warm, moist tropical air; Anita rapidly organized and attained hurricane status late on August 30;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">[1]</a></sup> this is the latest date for the first hurricane since <a href="/wiki/1967_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1967 Atlantic hurricane season">1967</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">[2]</a></sup> Initially it threatened to strike <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, though building high pressures to the north of the hurricane turned Anita to the west-southwest. The hurricane developed a well-defined <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">eye</a>, and Hurricane Anita began to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">rapidly intensify</a> late on September 1 after attaining <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">major hurricane</a> status. During a two-day period up to its peak intensity, Anita deepened at a rate of 2 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a> per hour, and on September 2 the hurricane reached peak winds of 175 mph (280 km/h) while located just offshore northeastern <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, making Anita a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">Category 5</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hurricane Anita maintained its strength as it approached land, and at 1100 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a> on September 2 it made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">landfall</a> on <a href="/wiki/Soto_la_Marina,_Tamaulipas" title="Soto la Marina, Tamaulipas">Soto la Marina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a>, about 145 miles (235 km) south of <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Texas" title="Brownsville, Texas">Brownsville, Texas</a> or 80 miles (130 km) north of <a href="/wiki/Tampico" title="Tampico">Tampico</a>, Mexico. Moving ashore with a pressure of 926 <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a>, Anita was the third most intense tropical cyclone to strike the nation, and was the most intense hurricane to hit from the Gulf of Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPAC_HURDAT_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-EPAC_HURDAT-3">[3]</a></sup> It rapidly weakened over the mountainous terrain of Mexico, and on September 3, about 25 hours after moving ashore, Anita emerged into the eastern <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> as a tropical depression. Reclassified as <a href="/wiki/1977_Pacific_hurricane_season#Tropical_Depression_Eleven" title="1977 Pacific hurricane season">Tropical Depression Eleven</a>, the system continued to the west, and weakened further after encountering cooler water temperatures. The depression gradually lost its deep convection, and on September 4 it dissipated off of the southern tip of the <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Peninsula" title="Baja California Peninsula">Baja California Peninsula</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gunther_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gunther-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Anita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Anita_near_landfall.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="614" data-file-width="783" height="196" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Hurricane_Anita_near_landfall.PNG/250px-Hurricane_Anita_near_landfall.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Hurricane_Anita_near_landfall.PNG/375px-Hurricane_Anita_near_landfall.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Hurricane_Anita_near_landfall.PNG/500px-Hurricane_Anita_near_landfall.PNG 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Anita_near_landfall.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Hurricane Anita near its Mexico landfall</div>

<p>Shortly after first developing, one oil company evacuated its oil rigs off of <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> by helicopter, with several other companies beginning to evacuate unneeded workers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi829_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-upi829-5">[5]</a></sup> Ultimately, 7,000 oil workers were removed from offshore oil platforms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">[1]</a></sup> The threat of the developing disturbance prompted officials to close a state park in southern Louisiana. The <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">National Hurricane Center</a> advised small craft along the northern Gulf Coast to remain at port.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi829_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-upi829-5">[5]</a></sup> Initially, Anita was predicted to continue tracking west-northwestward and make landfall near the Texas/Louisiana border. As a result, schools in <a href="/wiki/Cameron_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Cameron Parish, Louisiana">Cameron Parish, Louisiana</a> were closed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi830_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-upi830-6">[6]</a></sup> and on August 30 a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">hurricane watch</a> was issued for the southwestern Louisiana and northeastern Texas coastlines. After the track shifted further to the south, the watch was replaced with a hurricane warning between <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Texas" title="Brownsville, Texas">Brownsville</a> and just south of <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">Corpus Christi, Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">[1]</a></sup> An emergency shelter was opened in <a href="/wiki/Brownwood,_Texas" title="Brownwood, Texas">Brownwood, Texas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">Army</a> trucks were prepared to assist in evacuations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-830baytown_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-830baytown-7">[7]</a></sup> An official from the <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> recommended evacuation for all residents east of <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">Galveston, Texas</a> living in an area below 5 feet (1.5 m) above sea level; about 20,000 left <a href="/wiki/Cameron,_Louisiana" title="Cameron, Louisiana">Cameron, Louisiana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi831_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-upi831-8">[8]</a></sup> In all, about 58,000 people evacuated coastal locations in Louisiana and Texas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Though the National Hurricane Center did not issue hurricane warnings for Mexico, officials warned the Mexican government of the potential danger of the hurricane. Across the northeastern coastline of the country, 35,000 people evacuated prior to the arrival of the hurricane,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">[1]</a></sup> including all of the residents in the village where the hurricane struck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi92_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-upi92-9">[9]</a></sup> The Mexican army assisted in evacuations and preparing emergency shelters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap902_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap902-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Anita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Anita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the precursor tropical disturbance crossed southern <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, it dropped light rainfall of over 1 inch (25 mm) in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" title="Miami, Florida">Miami</a> area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-rain-11">[11]</a></sup> Later, as a developing tropical depression, the system produced gusty winds and heavy rainfall in southern <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi829_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-upi829-5">[5]</a></sup> one station near <a href="/wiki/Galliano,_Louisiana" title="Galliano, Louisiana">Galliano</a> reported over 3 inches (76 mm) of precipitation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-rain-11">[11]</a></sup> Hurricane Anita produced a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Storm_tide" title="Storm tide">storm tide</a> of 2 feet (0.6 m) above normal in <a href="/wiki/Grand_Isle,_Louisiana" title="Grand Isle, Louisiana">Grand Isle</a>, which resulted in hundreds of families being asked to leave their homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi831_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-upi831-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The hurricane produced above-normal tides along the <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> coastline, which closed several highways including <a href="/wiki/Texas_State_Highway_87" title="Texas State Highway 87">Texas State Highway 87</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi831_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-upi831-8">[8]</a></sup> The northern periphery of Hurricane Anita produced light to moderate rainfall across the southern portion of the state, which peaked at 4.97 inches (126 mm) at <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_City,_Texas" title="Rio Grande City, Texas">Rio Grande City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-rain-11">[11]</a></sup> Damage in the state was minor; on <a href="/wiki/South_Padre_Island,_Texas" title="South Padre Island, Texas">South Padre Island</a> damage was limited to a few broken windows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi93_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-upi93-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Anita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Anita_radar.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="429" data-file-width="536" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Anita_radar.PNG/250px-Anita_radar.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Anita_radar.PNG/375px-Anita_radar.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Anita_radar.PNG/500px-Anita_radar.PNG 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Anita_radar.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Hurricane Anita near landfall</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Hurricane Anita made landfall in a sparsely populated portion of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>; as a result, few meteorological statistics exist. A station near where the hurricane moved ashore recorded 17.52 inches (445 mm) of precipitation in six hours, which resulted in severe flooding and mudslides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">[1]</a></sup> Winds along the eastern Mexico coastline were estimated at over 100 mph (160 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap92_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap92-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The hurricane caused extensive damage to fishing and farming communities in northeastern Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">[1]</a></sup> Strong winds destroyed thousands of homes and left about 25,000 people homeless, according to newspaper reports from Mexico City. The winds destroyed the roofs of most buildings near where the hurricane moved ashore,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap904_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap904-14">[14]</a></sup> and also caused widespread power outages which disrupted communications. Heavy rainfall destroyed or obstructed roadways across the region,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-902ap_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-902ap-15">[15]</a></sup> with some flooded avenues in <a href="/wiki/Tampico" title="Tampico">Tampico</a> and reports of flooded highways near <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_Victoria" title="Ciudad Victoria">Ciudad Victoria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap92_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap92-13">[13]</a></sup> Eleven people were killed in floods and mudslides across <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TSG1_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-TSG1-16">[16]</a></sup> In all, at least 50,000 people were affected by the hurricane in Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oas_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-oas-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Anita&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">List of retired Atlantic hurricane names</a></div>
<p>The name Anita was retired following this storm, and will never be used for an Atlantic hurricane again. Shortly after the hurricane made landfall, the government of Mexico sent two trucks of food to the areas of greatest impact.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap92_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-ap92-13">[13]</a></sup> Additionally, officials permitted full use of the Mexican railway system to facilitate the distribution of aid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap902_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap902-10">[10]</a></sup> Three municipalities were also declared disaster areas following the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TSG1_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-TSG1-16">[16]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, the combination of moderate rainfall and high tides into low-lying marshland led to a mosquito outbreak near <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">Galveston</a> following the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gdn93_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-gdn93-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
