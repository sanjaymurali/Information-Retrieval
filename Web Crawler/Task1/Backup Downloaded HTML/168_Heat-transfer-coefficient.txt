<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Heat transfer coefficient</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>heat transfer coefficient</b> or <b>film coefficient</b>, or <b>film effectiveness</b>, in <a href="/wiki/Thermodynamics" title="Thermodynamics">thermodynamics</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineering" title="Mechanical engineering">mechanics</a> is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proportional_(mathematics)" title="Proportional (mathematics)">proportionality constant</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Heat_flux" title="Heat flux">heat flux</a> and the thermodynamic driving force for the flow of heat (i.e., the temperature difference, Δ<i>T</i>):</p>

<dd>Δ<i>T</i>: difference in temperature between the solid surface and surrounding fluid area, K.</dd>

<p>It is used in calculating the <a href="/wiki/Heat_transfer" title="Heat transfer">heat transfer</a>, typically by <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convection</a> or <a href="/wiki/Phase_transition" title="Phase transition">phase transition</a> between a fluid and a solid. The heat transfer coefficient has <a href="/wiki/International_System_of_Units" title="International System of Units">SI</a> units in watts per squared meter kelvin: W/(m<sup>2</sup>K).</p>
<p>The heat transfer coefficient is the reciprocal of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thermal_insulance" title="Thermal insulance">thermal insulance</a>. This is used for building materials (<a href="/wiki/R-value_(insulation)" title="R-value (insulation)">R-value</a>) and for <a href="/wiki/Clothing_insulation" title="Clothing insulation">clothing insulation</a>.</p>
<p>There are numerous methods for calculating the heat transfer coefficient in different heat transfer modes, different fluids, flow regimes, and under different <a href="/wiki/Thermal_hydraulics" title="Thermal hydraulics">thermohydraulic</a> conditions. Often it can be estimated by dividing the <a href="/wiki/Thermal_conductivity" title="Thermal conductivity">thermal conductivity</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convection</a> fluid by a length scale. The heat transfer coefficient is often calculated from the <a href="/wiki/Nusselt_number" title="Nusselt number">Nusselt number</a> (a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dimensionless_number" title="Dimensionless number">dimensionless number</a>). There are also <a class="external text" href="https://durathermfluids.com/resources/calculators/" rel="nofollow">online calculators</a> available specifically for <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Heat-transfer_fluid_(disambiguation)" title="Heat-transfer fluid (disambiguation)">heat transfer fluid</a> applications. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Convective_heat_transfer_correlations">Convective heat transfer correlations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heat_transfer_coefficient&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Convective heat transfer correlations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Often during their use, heat exchangers collect a layer of fouling on the surface which, in addition to potentially contaminating a stream, reduces the effectiveness of heat exchangers.
