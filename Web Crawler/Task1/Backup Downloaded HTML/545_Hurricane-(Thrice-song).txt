<p>"<b>Hurricane</b>" is a single by American rock band <a href="/wiki/Thrice" title="Thrice">Thrice</a>, off of their studio album <i><a href="/wiki/To_Be_Everywhere_Is_to_Be_Nowhere" title="To Be Everywhere Is to Be Nowhere">To Be Everywhere Is to Be Nowhere</a></i>. It peaked at number 24 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Mainstream_Rock_(chart)" title="Mainstream Rock (chart)">Mainstream Rock Songs</a> chart in August 2017.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Thrice_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for the song was teased on April 3, 2017,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and released on April 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TeamRock1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-TeamRock1-3">[3]</a></sup> The song was the highest viewed new video in its debut week in April on <a href="/wiki/Loudwire" title="Loudwire">Loudwire</a>'s Top 10 Video Countdown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Themes_and_composition">Themes and composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Thrice_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Themes and composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song has been described as very ominous and doom-laden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The song opens with gentle, melodic and melancholic clean guitar notes, followed by vocals by Kensrue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CR1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-CR1-6">[6]</a></sup> The verses show a calm, soothing conversation of two lovers speaking, about how to avoid an upcoming hurricane, while the chorus erupts with large, <a href="/wiki/Distortion_(music)" title="Distortion (music)">distorted guitars</a> and intense vocals, representing the coming of the storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CR1_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-CR1-6">[6]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frontman" title="Frontman">Frontman</a> Dustin Kensrue outlined the ideas and concepts explored in the song:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Thrice being a heavier kind of band and me being a bit drawn to the bigger issues of life in general, all that gravitates a lot of times towards writing pretty weighty, heavy lyrics. [<i>To Be Everywhere Is to Be Nowhere</i>] was no exception – I'm listening to it with everything going on and there's just this weight and heaviness to it. 'Hurricane' is a good example of my trying to find something that fits there – the beauty and calm of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Verse_(music)" title="Verse (music)">verses</a> slamming into this <a href="/wiki/Refrain" title="Refrain">huge thing</a>, and I'm trying to find something that works with that and I end up writing something where I end up questioning why all these beautiful moments in time are always shattered by something – because that's what the music feels likes its doing."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TeamRock1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-TeamRock1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Despite <a href="/wiki/Teppei_Teranishi" title="Teppei Teranishi">Teppei Teranishi</a> typically being the band's <a href="/wiki/Lead_guitar" title="Lead guitar">lead guitarist</a>, Kensrue both wrote and played the song's lead guitar part in the song, something attributed to the fact that he wrote the part, and because he felt it sounds better coming from his particular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guitar_rig" title="Guitar rig">guitar rig</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guitar_pedal" title="Guitar pedal">guitar pedal</a> setup, specifically the <a href="/wiki/Electro-Harmonix" title="Electro-Harmonix">Electro-Harmonix</a> POG 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
