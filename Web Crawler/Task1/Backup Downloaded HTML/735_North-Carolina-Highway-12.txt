<p><b>North Carolina Highway 12</b> (<b>NC 12</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Highway_System" title="North Carolina Highway System">state highway</a> that traverses the northeastern coastline of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, linking the peninsulas and islands of the northern <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">Outer Banks</a>. Most sections of NC 12 are two lanes wide, and there are also two ferry routes which maintain continuity of the route as it traverses the Outer Banks region. NC 12 is part of the <b>Outer Banks Scenic Byway</b>, a <a href="/wiki/National_Scenic_Byway" title="National Scenic Byway">National Scenic Byway</a>. The first NC 12 appeared on the 1924 North Carolina Official Map and at its height ran from NC 30 in Pollocksville to NC 48 near Murfreesboro. Over time it was replaced by both US 258 and NC 58 and ceased to exist in 1958. The current NC 12 first appeared on the 1964 state highway map running from US 158 in Nags Head to Ocracoke. In 1976 NC 12 was extended to US 70 on the mainland and in 1987 was extended north to Corolla.</p>

<p>North Carolina Highway 12 begins at <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_70_in_North_Carolina" title="U.S. Route 70 in North Carolina">US 70</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unincorporated_community" title="Unincorporated community">unincorporated community</a> of Sea Level. From there NC 12 travels Northeast along Cedar Island Road to <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Island,_North_Carolina" title="Cedar Island, North Carolina">Cedar Island</a>. Once the road enters Cedar Island it turns northwest running along the Cedar Bay all the way to the Cedar Island-<a href="/wiki/Ocracoke,_North_Carolina" title="Ocracoke, North Carolina">Ocracoke</a> ferry. After arriving at Ocracoke the road immediately runs along the western side of Silver Lake in the eastern side of the town. After leaving Ocracoke NC 12 enters the Cape Hatteras National Seashore. NC 12 runs along the middle of the island all the way until it reaches the Cape Hatteras-Ocracoke Ferry in Point Beach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-google_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-google-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After arriving at Hatteras Village, NC 12 turns left onto Coast Guard Road. The road follows Coast Guard Road along the northern part of the town before turning back into N Carolina 12. NC 12 runs along a narrow strip of land in the middle of the island before going through <a href="/wiki/Frisco,_North_Carolina" title="Frisco, North Carolina">Frisco</a>. After passing through Frisco the road goes north of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Buxton_Woods_Coastal_Reserve&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Buxton Woods Coastal Reserve (page does not exist)">Buxton Woods Coastal Reserve</a> before going through <a href="/wiki/Buxton,_North_Carolina" title="Buxton, North Carolina">Buxton</a> and passing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras_Lighthouse" title="Cape Hatteras Lighthouse">Cape Hatteras Lighthouse</a>. Continuing northward it passes through the communities of <a href="/wiki/Avon,_North_Carolina" title="Avon, North Carolina">Avon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salvo,_North_Carolina" title="Salvo, North Carolina">Salvo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Waves,_North_Carolina" title="Waves, North Carolina">Waves</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rodanthe,_North_Carolina" title="Rodanthe, North Carolina">Rodanthe</a>. It crosses the temporary <a href="/wiki/New_Inlet" title="New Inlet">New Inlet</a> bridge (the inlet reopened by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irene" title="Hurricane Irene">Hurricane Irene</a> in 2011) and a few miles north the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Herbert_C._Bonner" title="Herbert C. Bonner">Herbert C. Bonner</a> bridge over <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Inlet" title="Oregon Inlet">Oregon Inlet</a>, separating <a href="/wiki/Pea_Island" title="Pea Island">Pea Island</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bodie_Island" title="Bodie Island">Bodie Island</a>. Nearby is the <a href="/wiki/Bodie_Island_Lighthouse" title="Bodie Island Lighthouse">Bodie Island Lighthouse</a> and visitor center. NC 12 then continues north, where it intersects <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_64_in_North_Carolina" title="U.S. Route 64 in North Carolina">US 64</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Route_158_in_North_Carolina" title="U.S. Route 158 in North Carolina">US 158</a> south of the town of <a href="/wiki/Nags_Head,_North_Carolina" title="Nags Head, North Carolina">Nags Head</a>. NC 12 runs through Nags Head along the <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Dare" title="Virginia Dare">Virginia Dare</a> Trail just east of US 158. The road continues north through <a href="/wiki/Kill_Devil_Hills,_North_Carolina" title="Kill Devil Hills, North Carolina">Kill Devil Hills</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kitty_Hawk,_North_Carolina" title="Kitty Hawk, North Carolina">Kitty Hawk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southern_Shores,_North_Carolina" title="Southern Shores, North Carolina">Southern Shores</a>. NC 12 enters Corolla along Ocean Trail and continues along the west bank through the town. NC 12 ends just north of Corolla and south of the Currituck Banks National Estuarine Research Reserve. Ferries along the route of NC 12 are operated by the Ferry Division of the <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Department_of_Transportation" title="North Carolina Department of Transportation">North Carolina Department of Transportation</a> (NCDOT).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-google_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-google-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2013_map_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-2013_map-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The highway is the eastern most primary route in the state.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Outer_Banks_Scenic_Byway">Outer Banks Scenic Byway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Carolina_Highway_12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Outer Banks Scenic Byway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Outer Banks Scenic Byway begins at the intersection of US 70 and Merrimon Road. The Outer Banks Scenic Byway follows US 70 East to NC 12 on Cedar Island. The byway then continues onto NC 12 North near Atlantic. The byway then crosses the Ocracoke-Cedar Island Ferry north. It follows NC 12 north the rest of the way to the intersection of US 64 and NC 12 in Nags Head where it has its northern terminus. The Outer Banks Scenic Byway spans approximately 131 Miles and takes about 6 Hours to drive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Carolina_Highway_12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nc12route.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="810" data-file-width="454" height="357" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Nc12route.jpg/200px-Nc12route.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Nc12route.jpg/300px-Nc12route.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Nc12route.jpg/400px-Nc12route.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nc12route.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
North Carolina Highway 12 is routed along the Outer Banks. Yellow indicates roadbed while blue indicates the ferry routes</div>

<p>NC 12 first appeared on the 1924 State highway map running from Kinston to NC 40 south of <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_North_Carolina" title="Halifax, North Carolina">Halifax</a>. NC 12 went from Kinston northwest to Snow Hill where it met up with <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Highway_102" title="North Carolina Highway 102">NC 102</a>. From there the road went north to <a href="/wiki/Farmville,_North_Carolina" title="Farmville, North Carolina">Farmville</a> where it met up with <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Highway_91" title="North Carolina Highway 91">NC 91</a>. From there it continued north to <a href="/wiki/Scotland_Neck,_North_Carolina" title="Scotland Neck, North Carolina">Scotland Neck</a> passing through <a href="/wiki/Tarboro,_North_Carolina" title="Tarboro, North Carolina">Tarboro</a>. In Scotland Neck NC 12 turned to the west and ended at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Highway_40" title="North Carolina Highway 40">NC 40</a> south of Halifax.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1924_map_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-1924_map-5">[5]</a></sup> By 1933, NC 12 was rerouted to Rich Square and extended south to <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_17_in_North_Carolina" title="U.S. Route 17 in North Carolina">US 17</a>/<a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Highway_30" title="North Carolina Highway 30">NC 30</a>; at the same time, US 258 was routed along of the routing of NC 12 north of Kinston.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1933_map_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-1933_map-6">[6]</a></sup> By 1935, NC 12 was truncated to Kinston with US 258 getting the routing north of Kinston.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1935_map_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-1935_map-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1958 the last portion of NC 12 south of Kinston was renumbered as NC 58<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1957_map_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-1957_map-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1958_map_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-1958_map-9">[9]</a></sup> NC 12 shows up on the 1964 map running from Ocracoke to Whalebone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1964_map_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-1964_map-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1976 NC 12 was extended onto the mainland to connect with US 70<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1975_map_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-1975_map-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1976_map_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-1976_map-12">[12]</a></sup> In 1987, NC 12 was extended north of Nags Head along the Virginia Dare Trail (then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_158" title="US 158">Business US 158</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1986_map_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-1986_map-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1987_map_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-1987_map-14">[14]</a></sup> NC 12 was extended further to Corolla, its present northern terminus, a year later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1987_map_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-1987_map-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1988_map_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-1988_map-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hatteras Island was cut in two on September 18, 2003 by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">Hurricane Isabel</a> which opened a new inlet 3,000 feet (910 m) wide and 30 feet (9.1 m) deep through the community of Hatteras Village on the southern end of Hatteras Island. This new inlet was temporarily named the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isabel_Inlet" title="Isabel Inlet">Isabel Inlet</a> after the hurricane. Road access along NC 12 was temporarily severed until the island was repaired and restored by sand pumped ashore by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="United States Army Corps of Engineers">Army Corps of Engineers</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2007, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subtropical_Storm_Andrea" title="Subtropical Storm Andrea">Subtropical Storm Andrea</a> caused high winds to push waves over dunes and onto the highway on Hatteras Island, leaving water a foot deep and sand 2 to 3 feet (0.91 m) deep in some places.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>NC 12 was severed in two places by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irene" title="Hurricane Irene">Hurricane Irene</a> in late August 2011. The road was breached by two small inlets, about 200 feet (61 m) across apiece, in the <a href="/wiki/Pea_Island_National_Wildlife_Refuge" title="Pea Island National Wildlife Refuge">Pea Island National Wildlife Refuge</a>, and north of <a href="/wiki/Rodanthe,_North_Carolina" title="Rodanthe, North Carolina">Rodanthe</a>. As a result, the only way to access Hatteras Island was by ferry. On October 10, 2011, a temporary bridge opened over the largest breach. The bridge, which is 662 feet (202 m) long, could be in place for more than 10 years while other solutions are thought out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hr101011_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-hr101011-17">[17]</a></sup> As <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Sandy" title="Hurricane Sandy">Hurricane Sandy</a> battered the East Coast in October 2012, it has left portions of NC 12 inundated with salt water and sand. That forced the closure of the road, leaving the remaining people on the Outer Banks isolated from mainland North Carolina.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In late October 2012, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Sandy" title="Hurricane Sandy">Hurricane Sandy</a> washed out and damaged a portion of the road at the S-curves north of <a href="/wiki/Rodanthe,_North_Carolina" title="Rodanthe, North Carolina">Rodanthe</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Pea_Island_National_Wildlife_Refuge" title="Pea Island National Wildlife Refuge">Pea Island National Wildlife Refuge</a>. Emergency ferry service was established from Rodanthe to Stumpy point ferry terminals. As in 2011, the ferry service became the lifeline for Hatteras Island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Noreasters and storms repeatedly caused breaches since the road was repaired in December 2012. A state of emergency was declared and $20.8 million of emergency federal funding was secured to construct a more permanent repair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
