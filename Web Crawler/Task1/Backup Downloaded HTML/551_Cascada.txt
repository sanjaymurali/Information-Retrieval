<p><b>Cascada</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/ɑː/: 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> for <i>Waterfall</i>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_people" title="German people">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance music</a> act founded in 2004 by <a href="/wiki/Singing" title="Singing">singer</a> <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Horler" title="Natalie Horler">Natalie Horler</a>, <a href="/wiki/DJ_Manian" title="DJ Manian">DJ Manian</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DJ_Yanou" title="DJ Yanou">DJ Yanou</a>. They are best known for their hit singles "<a href="/wiki/Everytime_We_Touch_(Cascada_song)" title="Everytime We Touch (Cascada song)">Everytime We Touch</a>", "<a href="/wiki/What_Hurts_the_Most" title="What Hurts the Most">What Hurts the Most</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Evacuate_the_Dancefloor_(song)" title="Evacuate the Dancefloor (song)">Evacuate the Dancefloor</a>". Cascada have sold over 30 million albums worldwide, an estimated 15 million digital downloads, and have an estimated net worth of over 7 million. Cascada represented Germany at the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2013" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2013">Eurovision Song Contest 2013</a> in <a href="/wiki/Malm%C3%B6" title="Malmö">Malmö</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. While being one of the most successful acts of the dance music genre itself, Cascada were named the most successful German act of the 21st century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cascada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2004–05:_Early_career"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2004.E2.80.9305:_Early_career">2004–05: Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cascada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2004–05: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When Natalie Horler was 17, she was doing studio work for various DJs. Eventually she met Yanou and DJ Manian. Originally, they released music under the name Cascade, but due to <a href="/wiki/Kaskade" title="Kaskade">Kaskade</a>, another artist with a similar name, threatening legal action, they changed it to Cascada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> They simultaneously released music under the names of <a href="/wiki/Siria" title="Siria">Siria</a> and Akira as well, but ended those projects due to the success of Cascada. Under Andorfine Records, they produced their debut and hit single, "Miracle", and its follow-up, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_(Cascada_song)" title="Bad Boy (Cascada song)">Bad Boy</a>", in Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> This caught the attention of the American dance label <a href="/wiki/Robbins_Entertainment" title="Robbins Entertainment">Robbins Entertainment</a>. They negotiated a contract and "Miracle" was released in 2004. However, it did not attract much attention, so Cascada offered them "Everytime We Touch".</p>
<h3><span id="2005–07:_Everytime_We_Touch"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2005.E2.80.9307:_Everytime_We_Touch">2005–07: <i>Everytime We Touch</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cascada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2005–07: Everytime We Touch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cascada experienced mainstream success in the United Kingdom and the United States almost a year after releasing their second American single, "<a href="/wiki/Everytime_We_Touch_(Cascada_song)" title="Everytime We Touch (Cascada song)">Everytime We Touch</a>", which interpolates the chorus of a 1992 <a href="/wiki/Maggie_Reilly" title="Maggie Reilly">Maggie Reilly</a> <a href="/wiki/Everytime_We_Touch_(Maggie_Reilly_song)" title="Everytime We Touch (Maggie Reilly song)">song of the same name</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The song gained platinum and gold certifications across the globe, being certified platinum by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Soon after the popularity of the single skyrocketed, an album was recorded and released in a matter of months,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and a music video for "Everytime We Touch" was also distributed for promotion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>A total of eight singles were released from the album, four of which have been released in the United Kingdom: "<a href="/wiki/Everytime_We_Touch_(Cascada_song)" title="Everytime We Touch (Cascada song)">Everytime We Touch</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Truly_Madly_Deeply" title="Truly Madly Deeply">Truly Madly Deeply</a>" (originally by <a href="/wiki/Savage_Garden" title="Savage Garden">Savage Garden</a>),"<a href="/wiki/Miracle_(Cascada_song)" title="Miracle (Cascada song)">Miracle</a>" and "A Neverending Dream" (originally by <a href="/wiki/X-Perience" title="X-Perience">X-Perience</a>), all of which gained top 10 status apart from "A Neverending Dream", which charted at #46.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The U.S. saw the re-release of "Miracle" shortly after "Everytime We Touch"'s breakthrough, receiving high radio rotation and reaching the lower ends of the U.S. charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Their album <i><a href="/wiki/Everytime_We_Touch_(album)" title="Everytime We Touch (album)">Everytime We Touch</a></i> has experienced success in the UK Album chart where it remained up in the top forty for twenty-four weeks, peaking at #2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The album experienced success on the U.S. charts as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The success of the album gained them two <a href="/wiki/World_Music_Awards" title="World Music Awards">World Music Award</a> nominations, winning them World's Best-Selling German Artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Additionally, the album has sold over 2 million copies worldwide.</p>
<h3><span id="2007–09:_Perfect_Day"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.9309:_Perfect_Day">2007–09: <i>Perfect Day</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cascada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2007–09: Perfect Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Horler first announced that Cascada were working on their second album during a Japanese interview, which was posted on their blog on MySpace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> There are a few cover songs on the album, most notably <a href="/wiki/Avril_Lavigne" title="Avril Lavigne">Avril Lavigne</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Sk8er_Boi" title="Sk8er Boi">Sk8er Boi</a>", <a href="/wiki/Pink_(singer)" title="Pink (singer)">P!nk</a>'s "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Just_Like_A_Pill" title="Just Like A Pill">Just Like A Pill</a>", <a href="/wiki/Patti_Smith" title="Patti Smith">Patti Smith</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Because_the_Night" title="Because the Night">Because the Night</a>" and the <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Steele" title="Jeffrey Steele">Jeffrey Steele</a> song "<a href="/wiki/What_Hurts_the_Most" title="What Hurts the Most">What Hurts the Most</a>", as well as two tracks, "Endless Summer" and "I Will Believe It", that were previously recorded by Horler and DJ Manian under the discontinued <i>Siria</i> name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> However, the U.S. release replaced the Siria songs,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> P!nk, and Avril Lavigne covers with three newer tracks, because they did not have the North American rights. Two of these songs were "Faded", a cover of a <a href="/wiki/Kate_DeAraugo" title="Kate DeAraugo">Kate DeAraugo</a> song, and "Holiday", a cover of a Jenny May song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The second album was released at the end of 2007 in the UK, and several parts of Northern Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Perfect_Day_(Cascada_album)" title="Perfect Day (Cascada album)">Perfect Day</a></i> was released to the remaining markets, in the first quarter of 2008, with the U.S. release being the last.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Horler also stated in a French interview, before the release, that she would take a hand in writing parts of the album;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> however, she did not write any of the final tracks on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The worldwide lead single, "What Hurts the Most", was released in late 2007 and early 2008, along with its B-side, a cover of <a href="/wiki/Wham!" title="Wham!">Wham!</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Last_Christmas" title="Last Christmas">Last Christmas</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The single had high success, reaching the top ten in most countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The U.K. and German follow-up to "What Hurts the Most", is "<a href="/wiki/What_Do_You_Want_from_Me%3F_(Cascada_song)" title="What Do You Want from Me? (Cascada song)">What Do You Want From Me?</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> originally thought to be called "Tell Me Why".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The third release from the album in the UK and Germany was "Because the Night".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The United States instead received both "Faded" and "Perfect Day" as singles, however each of these releases received little airplay outside of the dance-radio circuit.</p>
<h3><span id="2009–10:_Evacuate_the_Dancefloor"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9310:_Evacuate_the_Dancefloor">2009–10: <i>Evacuate the Dancefloor</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cascada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2009–10: Evacuate the Dancefloor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 June 2009, "<a href="/wiki/Evacuate_the_Dancefloor_(song)" title="Evacuate the Dancefloor (song)">Evacuate the Dancefloor</a>" was released in the UK. The release, just four days after the <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Michael_Jackson" title="Death of Michael Jackson">death of Michael Jackson</a>, was expected to chart within the top ten, underneath a plethora of the deceased King of Pop's former hits, due to a resurgence of sales after the news. To the surprise of many, the single debuted at No. 1, over Jackson's "<a href="/wiki/Man_in_the_Mirror" title="Man in the Mirror">Man in the Mirror</a>". The song went on to achieve top five success in Australia, Canada, France, Germany, Ireland, and New Zealand, as well as top ten success in many more. The song slowly rose on the U.S. chart after a late September release, eventually reaching a peak of No. 25 after a seventeen-week climb, becoming the group's second top-forty hit. The song has become a club staple, used in many dance-</p>

The album, also named <i><a href="/wiki/Evacuate_the_Dancefloor_(album)" title="Evacuate the Dancefloor (album)">Evacuate the Dancefloor</a></i>, was released a week later on 6 July in the UK and Germany. The album continued Cascada's progression into electro-pop, the change was welcomed by mainstream audiences and fans, yet many fans still remorsed the euro-pop feel, which some felt was integral to the Cascada project. The album itself did not mirror the success of the single, yet it did chart in the top ten of many territories, including the UK. However, the album did not have lasting sales and quickly fell out of the charts. "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dangerous_(Cascada_Song)" title="Dangerous (Cascada Song)">Dangerous</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Fever_(Cascada_song)" title="Fever (Cascada song)">Fever</a>" were follow-up singles released from the album, the former in the UK, and the latter in the US, Australia, and the rest of Europe, neither experiencing success in terms of radio spins, digital, or physical sales. Nevertheless, the group toured extensively to promote the album, even opening for <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a> during a stop in Germany.</p>
<h3><span id="2010–11:_Original_Me"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9311:_Original_Me">2010–11: <i>Original Me</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cascada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010–11: Original Me">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A new single "<a href="/wiki/Pyromania_(song)" title="Pyromania (song)">Pyromania</a>" was released in 2010, continuing their new electropop sound. A second single, "<a href="/wiki/Night_Nurse_(Cascada_song)" title="Night Nurse (Cascada song)">Night Nurse</a>", was released along with a music video featuring Horler in full body paint of various colors in many scenes. This single was never actually released until a year later on 16 December 2011 when it got an exclusive release in the U.S.</p>
<p>On the weekend of 26 March, Cascada shot two music videos for the upcoming singles "<a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_(Cascada_song)" title="San Francisco (Cascada song)">San Francisco</a>" and "Au Revoir". Both videos were directed by Lisa Mann and choreographed by Luther Brown, and both singles were featured on the album <i><a href="/wiki/Original_Me" title="Original Me">Original Me</a></i> which was released on 20 June 2011 (UK),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> and 29 November 2011 in the US.</p>
<p>After the release of the album, Horler posed for Playboy Deutschland in July 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> to mixed reactions from fans and critics. On 1 September 2011, US label Robbins Entertainment announced on their message board that Cascada and Robbins have amicably parted ways, and Cascada were released from the label, citing creative differences as the reason for their departure. It was also announced that <i><a href="/wiki/Original_Me" title="Original Me">Original Me</a></i> would still be released, under their Zooland label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="2012–13:_The_Best_of_Cascada,_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2013_and_Acoustic_Sessions"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9313:_The_Best_of_Cascada.2C_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2013_and_Acoustic_Sessions">2012–13: <i>The Best of Cascada</i>, Eurovision Song Contest 2013 and <i>Acoustic Sessions</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cascada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2012–13: The Best of Cascada, Eurovision Song Contest 2013 and Acoustic Sessions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 7 January 2012, Horler joined the judging panel of season 9 of German singing contest <i><a href="/wiki/Deutschland_sucht_den_Superstar" title="Deutschland sucht den Superstar">Deutschland sucht den Superstar</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> On 16 March 2012, the music video for "Summer of Love" premiered in Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> and was released on 30 March followed by the compilation album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Back_on_the_Dancefloor" title="Back on the Dancefloor">Back on the Dancefloor</a></i>.</p>
<p>On 24 May 2012, Horler posted a video blog, announcing that they were getting ready to release the next single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> In early June, a video clip of Horler performing a cover of <a href="/wiki/Corona_(band)" title="Corona (band)">Corona</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/The_Rhythm_of_the_Night" title="The Rhythm of the Night">The Rhythm of the Night</a>" surfaced online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> "The Rhythm of the Night" was released on 22 June 2012 in Germany and in the USA,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> with the music video for the song premiering on Cascada's Facebook page on the same day as the release.</p>
<p>On 12 November 2012, Horler announced via Facebook that their long-awaited Christmas Album would be released and would feature her father, <a href="/wiki/David_Horler" title="David Horler">David Horler</a>, playing the trombone on two of the tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> On 30 November 2012, the album <i>It's Christmas Time</i> was released digitally via Zooland Records, and features 11 well known Christmas songs, as well as the brand new track "Somewhere At Christmas Time" written especially for the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 17 December 2012, the north German regional broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Norddeutscher_Rundfunk" title="Norddeutscher Rundfunk">NDR</a> announced that Cascada would take part in the German national final <i><a href="/wiki/Germany_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2013" title="Germany in the Eurovision Song Contest 2013">Unser Song für Malmö</a></i> for a chance to represent Germany at the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2013" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2013">Eurovision Song Contest 2013</a> with their single "<a href="/wiki/Glorious_(Cascada_song)" title="Glorious (Cascada song)">Glorious</a>". Cascada won the German national final which was held on 14 February 2013 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hannover" title="Hannover">Hannover</a>, and therefore will represent Germany in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2013" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2013">Eurovision Song Contest 2013</a>. Despite being considered one of the favorites to win the competition (placing the song in 4th place among OGAE voting), on 18 May 2013, Cascada's "Glorious" earned 18 points in the Eurovision Final, putting them in 21st place.</p>
<p>On 8 February 2013, Cascada released the single "Glorious" followed by the release of their second greatest hits album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_of_Cascada" title="The Best of Cascada">The Best of Cascada</a></i>, on 29 March 2013. After the Eurovision Song Contest 2013, Cascada released a second single "The World is in My Hands" on 2 Aug.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> They also returned to the studio to work on their promised acoustic album which was released as <i>Acoustic Sessions</i> on 1 November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-itunes.apple.com_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-itunes.apple.com-46">[46]</a></sup> The album features acoustic versions of past hits from the band as well as a few covers of other songs. The most notable cover version was "You" performed by both Natalie and <a href="/wiki/Robin_Stjernberg" title="Robin Stjernberg">Robin Stjernberg</a>.</p>
<h3><span id='2014:_"Blink"_and_"Madness"'></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014:_.22Blink.22_and_.22Madness.22">2014: "Blink" and "Madness"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cascada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title='Edit section: 2014: "Blink" and "Madness"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 12 December 2013, German DJ/producer CJ Stone revealed that he was working on a remix for the latest Cascada release. He shared with eager fans that the single is titled "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blink_(Cascada_song)" title="Blink (Cascada song)">Blink</a>" and would be released in early 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> On 10 January 2014, Natalie posted on <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> that they were busy planning the music video for the new single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> "Blink" was released on 28 March in Germany with the video premier on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> The track was originally recorded by pop group U.V.U.K.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> In April 2014, "Blink" was released in Australia and France, and also began receiving airplay in the UK. Eventually, "Blink" was released in the UK as track on a Clubland compilation. Robbins Entertainment also announced that they would release "Blink" in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>A recent interview with Natalie confirmed that the group currently has no plans to release a 5th studio album just yet, however Natalie did mention that they will be back in the studio very soon to work on the next single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 7 September 2014 Cascada revealed that the new single, "Madness" featuring Manchester-based rapper Tristan 'Tris' Henry, will be released 26 September. A few days later, the artwork for the single was posted on Facebook.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Madness was released on iTunes on 26 September worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> A video for "Madness" premiered on YouTube on 30 September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="2015:_&quot;Reason&quot;_and_Natalie's_pregnancy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015:_.22Reason.22_and_Natalie.27s_pregnancy">2015: "Reason" and Natalie's pregnancy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cascada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2015: &quot;Reason&quot; and Natalie's pregnancy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After teasing fans with news of a new single and video shoot, Natalie confirmed via Facebook that they will be releasing a re-work of "Reason", which was released on 20 February 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> The track was originally released in 2004, under their previous alias 'Diamond', which was one of many side projects Natalie, Manian and Yanou were involved in before they were successful with Cascada. The video was shot in Mallorca, Spain and premiered on 20 February 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> On 28 May 2015, it was announced that Natalie would be taking a short break due to her pregnancy. All upcoming shows were cancelled, and fans were rest-assured that new music was "in the works", with Natalie posting several times during the summer that she was in the studio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> In September 2015, Natalie gave birth to her daughter Jamie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>Natalie returned to the stage in December 2015, with upcoming shows in Norway, Austria, Germany and the UK where she will also be performing at London's <a href="/wiki/G-A-Y" title="G-A-Y">G-A-Y</a> on New Year's Eve along with <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (UK TV series)">The X Factor</a></i> contestant <a href="/wiki/Fleur_East" title="Fleur East">Fleur East</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016:_Tour">2016: Tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cascada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2016: Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the better part of 2016, Natalie had been busy touring parts of Norway and the United Kingdom. There had been no official statement regarding a new album or single, but Natalie has been in the studio working with Yanou on new material.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Brazilian producer Cassiano collaborated with Cascada on a song called "Praise You", which is a cover of <a href="/wiki/Shackles_(Praise_You)" title="Shackles (Praise You)">Shackles (Praise You)</a> by <a href="/wiki/Mary_Mary" title="Mary Mary">Mary Mary</a>. It was released on 10 June 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> A brand new song titled "A.Y.O." was added to the songs repertory on the ASCAP website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> "A.Y.O." is noted to be written by Allan Eshuijs, who has worked with Cascada in the past on multiple songs including 'Evacuate', and <a href="/wiki/Daniella_Pavicic" title="Daniella Pavicic">Daniella Pavicic</a>, an artist and songwriter who shared the same label Robbins Entertainment with Cascada. In a recent interview with Eurovision Deutschland, Natalie mentioned that two new singles are coming soon, and that they are preparing to shoot a new music video. She also stated that they are focusing on individual singles and touring at the moment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id='2017:_"Run"_&amp;_"Playground"'></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017:_.22Run.22_.26_.22Playground.22">2017: "Run" &amp; "Playground"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cascada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title='Edit section: 2017: "Run" &amp; "Playground"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a two-year break from releasing music, Cascada released a new single "Run" on 27 January 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> During a recent interview, Natalie stated that they plan to release singles instead of a full album, and they already have a new song lined up to be released in the next few months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup><br/>
On April 20, Cascada released their new single "Playground", the official song of the <a href="/wiki/2017_IIHF_World_Championship" title="2017 IIHF World Championship">2017 Ice Hockey World Championship</a> held in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cologne,_Germany" title="Cologne, Germany">Cologne, Germany</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris,_France" title="Paris, France">Paris, France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
