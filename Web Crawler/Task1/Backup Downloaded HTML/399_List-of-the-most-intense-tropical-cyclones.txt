<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">List of the most intense tropical cyclones</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>Tropical cyclone intensity is a complex topic. Winds are often used to measure intensity as they commonly cause notable impacts over large areas, and most popular <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">tropical cyclone scales</a> are organized around sustained wind speeds. However variations in the averaging period of winds in different basins make inter-comparison difficult. In addition, other impacts like rainfall, storm surge, area of wind damages, and tornadoes can be significant variations in storms with similar wind speeds. Pressure is often used to compare tropical cyclones because measurements is easier to measure and consistent. Tropical cyclones can attain some of the lowest pressures over large areas on Earth. However, although there is strong connection between lowered pressures and higher wind speeds, storms with the lowest pressures may not have the highest wind speeds, as each storm's relationship between wind and pressure is slightly different.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><br/>
In the most recent and reliable records, most tropical cyclones which attained a pressure of 900 <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hPa</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">mbar</a>) (26.56 <a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">inHg</a>) or less occurred in the Western North <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. The strongest <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">tropical cyclone</a> recorded worldwide, as measured by minimum central <a href="/wiki/Pressure" title="Pressure">pressure</a>, was <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Tip" title="Typhoon Tip">Typhoon Tip</a>, which reached a pressure of 870 hPa (25.69 inHg) on October 12, 1979.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jtwc_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-jtwc-2">[2]</a></sup> The following list is subdivided by <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basins" title="Tropical cyclone basins">basins</a>. Data listed are provided by the official <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Centre" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre">Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre</a>, unless otherwise noted. On October 23, 2015, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Patricia" title="Hurricane Patricia">Hurricane Patricia</a> attained the strongest 1-minute sustained winds on record at 215 mph (345 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Atlantic_Ocean">South Atlantic Ocean</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_the_most_intense_tropical_cyclones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: South Atlantic Ocean">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/South_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="South Atlantic tropical cyclone">South Atlantic tropical cyclone</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cyclone_Catarina_2004.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4000" data-file-width="4000" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Cyclone_Catarina_2004.jpg/220px-Cyclone_Catarina_2004.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Cyclone_Catarina_2004.jpg/330px-Cyclone_Catarina_2004.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Cyclone_Catarina_2004.jpg/440px-Cyclone_Catarina_2004.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cyclone_Catarina_2004.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

</div>

<p>Due to the fact that tropical cyclones in the South Atlantic are rare, there is no official tropical cyclone season for this region. Despite this, there have been several significant tropical cyclones in the South Atlantic region, notably <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Catarina" title="Hurricane Catarina">Hurricane Catarina</a> in March 2004. Tropical and subtropical cyclones with an intensity of below 1000 hPa (29.53 inHg) are listed.</p>
