<p><b>Expedition 48</b> was the 48th expedition to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Williams_(astronaut)" title="Jeffrey Williams (astronaut)">Jeffrey Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aleksey_Ovchinin" title="Aleksey Ovchinin">Aleksey Ovchinin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Skripochka" title="Oleg Skripochka">Oleg Skripochka</a> transferred from <a href="/wiki/Expedition_47" title="Expedition 47">Expedition 47</a>. Expedition 48 began upon the departure of <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-19M" title="Soyuz TMA-19M">Soyuz TMA-19M</a> in 7 July 2016 01:36 UTC<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-start_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-start-1">[1]</a></sup> and concluded upon the departure of <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-20M" title="Soyuz TMA-20M">Soyuz TMA-20M</a> on September 6, 2016. The crew of <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_MS-01" title="Soyuz MS-01">Soyuz MS-01</a> were transferred to <a href="/wiki/Expedition_49" title="Expedition 49">Expedition 49</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crew_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Crew-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crew2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Crew2-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<tr>
<td colspan="4">The main objective of this EVA was to retract a thermal radiator on the port truss. This radiator was supposed to be retracted during Expedition 45 EVA 2 (November 6, 2015) but was not completed. The radiator had been deployed on Expedition 33 EVA 1 (November 1, 2012) in an attempt to isolate a coolant leak. Additional completed tasks included installing HD Video Cameras on the port/outboard side of the station (one zenith, one nadir), applying additional torque to <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Truss_Structure#Solar_alpha_rotary_joint" title="Integrated Truss Structure">SARJ</a> bolts, photographing the inside of the SARJ, tying back a protective blanket covering hardware which will be robotically manipulate later, and tying back Crew and Equipment Translation Aid (CETA) Cart brake handles to keep them out of the SARJ rotation envelope.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></td>
