<p><b>Indonesian Agency for Meteorological, Climatological and Geophysics</b> (<i><b>Badan Meteorologi, Klimatologi, dan Geofisika</b></i> or simply <b>BMKG</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesian</a> non-departmental government agency for <a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">meteorology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Climatology" title="Climatology">climatology</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Geophysics" title="Geophysics">geophysics</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Indonesian_Agency_for_Meteorology,_Climatology_and_Geophysics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Its history began on 1841 with individual observation conducted by Dr. Onnen, the head of hospital in <a href="/wiki/Bogor" title="Bogor">Bogor</a>, and was established as a formal government institution on 1866 by the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a> government by the name of <i>Magnetisch en Meteorologisch Observatorium</i>. The agency name changed several times and its current name was given on 6 September 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tropical_Cyclone_Warning_Centre">Tropical Cyclone Warning Centre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Indonesian_Agency_for_Meteorology,_Climatology_and_Geophysics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tropical Cyclone Warning Centre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 1986 the BMKG, has run a Tropical Cyclone Warning Centre (TCWC), within their headquarters in Jakarta.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jak_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jak-2">[2]</a></sup> Over the next 12 seasons, the TCWC named and issued international warnings for the area from the Equator to 10°S between 90°E and 125°E. In 1998, the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a>’s RA V Tropical Cyclone Committee recommended that <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Meteorology" title="Bureau of Meteorology">TCWC Perth</a>, in Australia take over warning responsibility on an interim basis until the BMKG's staff had the training to run the TCWC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jak_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jak-2">[2]</a></sup> TCWC Perth then took over the warning and naming responsibilities until the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007-08_Australian_region_cyclone_season" title="2007-08 Australian region cyclone season">2007-08 season</a> when they handed it back to TCWC Jakarta.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jak_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Jak-2">[2]</a></sup> The first depression to be named by TCWC Jakarta came later that year when Cyclone Durga became a Tropical Cyclone within their area of responsibility.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jak_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Jak-2">[2]</a></sup> During the next two seasons TCWC Jakarta, monitored several tropical cyclones in the <a href="/wiki/Typhoon" title="Typhoon">North Western Pacific Ocean</a> and the Australian region. At the start of the 2010-11 season, TCWC Jakarta's area of responsibility was then extended out to include the region from the Equator to 10°S between 125°E and 141°E.</p>
