<p>For the World War II "Deception Division" called 48th Infantry Division, see <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_deception_formations_of_World_War_II" title="United States Army deception formations of World War II">United States Army deception formations of World War II</a>.</p>
<p>The <b>48th Armored Division</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Division_(military)" title="Division (military)">division</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Army_National_Guard" title="United States Army National Guard">United States Army National Guard</a> from September 1946 until 1968. Most of its units were part of the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Army_National_Guard" title="Florida Army National Guard">Florida Army National Guard</a> and the Georgia Army National Guard. From 1946 to 1955 it was an infantry division. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> the denotation <b>48th Infantry Division</b> was a 'phantom division' created for Operation Quicksilver, part of Operation Fortitude South II.</p>

<h3><span id="48th_Infantry_Division_(National_Guard)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="48th_Infantry_Division_.28National_Guard.29">48th Infantry Division (National Guard)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=48th_Armored_Division_(United_States)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 48th Infantry Division (National Guard)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 48th "Hurricane" Infantry Division was formed on 15 September 1946 of Florida and Georgia National Guardsmen. The division conducted its first annual training from 18 July to 1 August 1948 at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Jackson_(South_Carolina)" title="Fort Jackson (South Carolina)">Fort Jackson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span id="48th_Armored_Division_(National_Guard)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="48th_Armored_Division_.28National_Guard.29">48th Armored Division (National Guard)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=48th_Armored_Division_(United_States)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 48th Armored Division (National Guard)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 15 September 1946, the division was reorganized as an armored division<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/124th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="124th Infantry Regiment (United States)">124th Infantry Regiment</a> of the Florida ARNG was assigned on 5 July 1946 to the 48th Infantry Division. It was broken up on 1 November 1955 and its elements, the 124th Armored Infantry Battalion and the 154th Armored Infantry Battalion were both assigned to the 48th Armored Division. The 124th and 154th Armored Infantry Battalions were consolidated 15 April 1959 to form the 124th Infantry, a parent regiment under the Combat Arms Regimental System, to consist of the 1st and 2d Armored Rifle Battalions, elements of the 48th Armored Division.</p>
<p>"To prepare for challenges in Western Europe, the new troop basis authorized the conversion of four National Guard infantry divisions to armored divisions."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Georgia, and Florida agreed to convert and on 1 November 1955 the 48th Division was redesignated as an armored division. When the <a href="/wiki/51st_Infantry_Division_(United_States)" title="51st Infantry Division (United States)">51st Infantry Division</a> was inactivated in 1963, some of its units, both from Florida and South Carolina, were assigned to the 48th Armored Division. On 1 October 1957 the 48th Armored Division headquarters was transferred from Macon, Georgia, to Jacksonville, Florida, as Major General Maxwell Snyder took command.</p>
<p>When the Army National Guard experienced its next major reorganization in 1967, the 48th Armored Division was chosen for inactivation, which occurred on 1 January 1968.</p>
<p>Soldiers and units in Florida were assigned to the <a href="/wiki/53rd_Infantry_Brigade_Combat_Team_(United_States)" title="53rd Infantry Brigade Combat Team (United States)">53rd Infantry Brigade Combat Team</a>. The number "48" was carried on by the Georgia National Guard and is today the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/48th_Infantry_Brigade_Combat_Team" title="48th Infantry Brigade Combat Team">48th Infantry Brigade Combat Team</a>.</p>
