<p><i><b>Café Racers</b></i> is the eighth studio album by <a href="/wiki/Kim_Carnes" title="Kim Carnes">Kim Carnes</a>, released in 1983 (see <a href="/wiki/1983_in_music" title="1983 in music">1983 in music</a>).</p>
<p>The album spawned three chart singles in the United States, "<a href="/wiki/Invisible_Hands_(song)" title="Invisible Hands (song)">Invisible Hands</a>", "<a href="/wiki/You_Make_My_Heart_Beat_Faster_(And_That%27s_All_That_Matters)" title="You Make My Heart Beat Faster (And That's All That Matters)">You Make My Heart Beat Faster (And That's All That Matters)</a>", and "I Pretend" which charted on various <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> charts. "The Universal Song" was also released as a single in West Germany, Austria, Switzerland, the Netherlands and Scandinavia.</p>
<p>The song "I'll Be Here Where the Heart Is" was included on the chart-topping, six times Platinum <a href="/wiki/Flashdance_(soundtrack)" title="Flashdance (soundtrack)"><i>Flashdance</i> soundtrack</a> which received a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for Best Album Of Original Score Written for a Motion Picture at 26th Annual Grammy Awards. It was also released as a single in Germany, Netherlands, Spain and France.</p>
<p>Kim's album, meanwhile, was a commercial failure, running out of steam at #97 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> album chart.</p>
