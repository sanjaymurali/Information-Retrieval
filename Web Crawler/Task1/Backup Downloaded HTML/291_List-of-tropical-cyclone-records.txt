<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_tropical_cyclone_records&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dd><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Alpha"><b><a href="#ref_Alpha">^α</a></b> Although Luis produced the highest confirmed wave height for a tropical cyclone, it is possible that <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ivan" title="Hurricane Ivan">Hurricane Ivan</a> produced a wave measuring 131 feet (40 m).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></span></dd>
<dd><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Beta"><b><a href="#ref_Beta">^β</a></b> It is believed that reconnaissance aircraft overestimated wind speeds in tropical cyclones from the 1940s to the 1960s, and data from this time period is generally considered unreliable. Consequently, Typhoon Nancy may not have sustained Category 5 winds for such a long duration.</span></dd>
</dl>
