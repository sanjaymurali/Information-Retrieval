<p>"<b>Hurricane</b>" is a song written by American rock band <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Seconds_to_Mars" title="Thirty Seconds to Mars">Thirty Seconds to Mars</a> that is featured on their third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_War" title="This Is War">This Is War</a></i>. The song was written by lead vocalist and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Jared_Leto" title="Jared Leto">Jared Leto</a> and produced by Leto, <a href="/wiki/Flood_(producer)" title="Flood (producer)">Flood</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steve_Lilywhite" title="Steve Lilywhite">Steve Lilywhite</a>. There are two versions of this song, one of which is included on the album and another which is a collaboration with rapper <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, titled "<b>Hurricane 2.0</b>", which was released as the fourth single from the album in November 2010. This version only appears on the deluxe version. The latter has a variation in certain parts of the track. "Hurricane" was awarded <a href="/wiki/Kerrang!_Award_for_Best_Single" title="Kerrang! Award for Best Single">Best Single</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Kerrang!_Awards_2011" title="Kerrang! Awards 2011">Kerrang! Awards 2011</a>. The thirteen-minute music video, directed by Leto under the pseudonym Bartholomew Cubbins, garnered controversy when it premiered on November 29, 2010, causing it to be banned by <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> because of its sexual content. An edited version, however, was put on heavy rotation on <a href="/wiki/MTV2" title="MTV2">MTV2</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_recording">Background and recording</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Thirty_Seconds_to_Mars_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and recording">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Leto wrote the piano ballad "Hurricane" in winter 2007 whilst in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>. In an interview, Jared said the following about the song:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">"I wrote 'Hurricane' in Berlin in the winter in 2007. It was winter, it was getting dark at like 3:30 in the afternoon, and it could go either way. It could be incredibly comforting or incredibly depressing. Thankfully, it was a little bit of both. It was inspiring nonetheless."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Thirty_Seconds_to_Mars_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The video, which runs for 13 minutes and 10 seconds and was directed by Leto under the <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">pseudonym</a> Bartholomew Cubbins, premiered on MTV on November 29, 2010.</p>
<p>The experimental short film is 12 minutes long and contains excerpts from the tracks "Escape" and "Night of the Hunter." It takes place in what is believed to be a dream, and explores a violent and sexual world where 30 Seconds to Mars are stalked and attacked by various figures known as "The Gimps", while a bisexual female couple known as Shae and Sunisa engage in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sex-bondage" title="Sex-bondage">sexual bondage activities</a> with Jared while he is confronted by hallucinations. Jared spends the entire video shirtless and being chased by a sledgehammer-wielding killer obsessed with trying to kill him. Shannon battles with a woman, who shares romantic feelings for him, ending with a kiss. Tomo, who liberates a woman from her abuser, receives a key as a gift. He composes the orchestral score of the song with three identical copies of himself.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Thirty_Seconds_to_Mars_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The music video for the song was banned by MTV<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and several other TV channels around the world. The video was <a href="/wiki/Censorship" title="Censorship">censored</a> and banned because of its elements of violence, nudity, and sex in an almost <a href="/wiki/Pornography" title="Pornography">pornographic context</a>. On November 28, 2010, Jared Leto posted a letter from an unnamed network about the censorship of the video on his blog. The list features the offending scenes, such as a woman running her finger on the anus of another <a href="/wiki/G-string" title="G-string">G-string</a> clad woman, which was classified as "restricted". Another offensive scene is the Rabbi, Priest, and Monk <a href="/wiki/Book_burning" title="Book burning">burning religious books</a>. It was these two shots which had made the video completely restricted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The video is still available online with a viewing certificate of 18+. The video uses the version of the song without <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, hence West does not make an appearance in the video.</p>

<p>During May 2009, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> posted a photo of himself, <a href="/wiki/Brandon_Flowers" title="Brandon Flowers">Brandon Flowers</a> (the frontman of <a href="/wiki/The_Killers" title="The Killers">The Killers</a>), and Jared Leto together and announced that he and Leto were working together on a song named "Hurricane". This collaboration was only included on an early version of "Hurricane", and West's work did not make it onto the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altpressreview_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-altpressreview-3">[3]</a></sup> Leto said that he,</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">"...had actually brought up [the idea of working with West] some time ago, but it's pretty unbelievable that it actually happened. [...] He came by here, he was here in the studio, and we did some initial kind of listening, and he did some singing, and we knew we needed to kind of follow up and finish things, so I went over to Hawaii [with] an engineer and a small crew, and we had a great time."</td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td class="cquotecite" colspan="3" style="border: none; padding-right: 4%; font-size: smaller; text-align: right;"><cite>— Jared Leto<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></cite></td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>West's vocal contribution to the song was ultimately removed because of legal issues surrounding the rights of each record company. Although it was not released on the original pressing of the album, Leto has said the track will be heard eventually.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The early leaked versions of the album had the version of "Hurricane" featuring Kanye West included. The deluxe version of <i>This Is War</i> included this version of the song as its first bonus track, retitled "Hurricane 2.0".</p>
