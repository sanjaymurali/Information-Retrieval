<p><b>Rain Joan of Arc Phoenix</b> (born November 21, 1972) is an American actress, musician, and singer. Phoenix has four siblings: two sisters, <a href="/wiki/Liberty_Phoenix" title="Liberty Phoenix">Liberty</a> and <a href="/wiki/Summer_Phoenix" title="Summer Phoenix">Summer</a>, and two brothers, actors <a href="/wiki/Joaquin_Phoenix" title="Joaquin Phoenix">Joaquin</a> and the late <a href="/wiki/River_Phoenix" title="River Phoenix">River Phoenix</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_Phoenix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Phoenix was born Rain Joan of Arc Bottom in <a href="/wiki/Crockett,_Texas" title="Crockett, Texas">Crockett, Texas</a>, the second child of John Lee Bottom and <a href="/wiki/Arlyn_Phoenix" title="Arlyn Phoenix">Arlyn Sharon</a> (née Dunetz). Her mother was born in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>, New York to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">Jewish</a> parents from <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> and Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jjounral_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jjounral-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Her father was a <a href="/wiki/Lapsed_Catholic" title="Lapsed Catholic">lapsed Catholic</a> from <a href="/wiki/Fontana,_California" title="Fontana, California">Fontana, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jjounral_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-jjounral-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1968, Phoenix's mother left her family and moved to California, meeting Phoenix's father while hitch-hiking. They married in 1969 and joined <a href="/wiki/Family_International" title="Family International">Children of God</a> and worked as missionaries in South America. It was there, at the age of three, that Phoenix (accompanied by elder brother River on guitar) first began to sing. They would perform on the streets of <a href="/wiki/Caracas" title="Caracas">Caracas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> for loose change while handing out Christian flyers to passers by. The family set out for California in order for the children to enter the entertainment industry. In West Los Angeles, Phoenix and her siblings would perform on the streets for crowds of people. Arlyn took a job at <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> and eventually secured all five Phoenix children with an agent.</p>
<p>Phoenix sang with brother River as an audience warm up act on the show <i>Real Kids</i> (1982).Various bit parts followed in TV shows including <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Ties" title="Family Ties">Family Ties</a></i> in 1987. Also in 1987, Phoenix landed a part in the feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Maid_to_Order" title="Maid to Order">Maid to Order</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Ally_Sheedy" title="Ally Sheedy">Ally Sheedy</a>.</p>
<p>At 15, Phoenix returned to her roots in music and formed the band <a href="/wiki/Aleka%27s_Attic" title="Aleka's Attic">Aleka's Attic</a> with her then 17-year-old brother River. River, along with bass player Josh McKay, wrote music and lyrics and Rain sang backup. The band secured gigs in and around their home town of <a href="/wiki/Gainesville,_Florida" title="Gainesville, Florida">Gainesville, Florida</a> By this time, River's career had taken off and so the band was often put on hold for River's demanding schedule. In 1988, Aleka's Attic were signed to a development deal with Island Records and in 1989 released the track "Across The Way" on a benefit album for the animal rights group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PETA" title="PETA">PETA</a>. In 1991, they made a tour of east coast clubs and colleges. At this time, Phoenix joined her brother River on the set of his tenth movie, <i><a href="/wiki/My_Own_Private_Idaho" title="My Own Private Idaho">My Own Private Idaho</a></i>. She met director <a href="/wiki/Gus_Van_Sant" title="Gus Van Sant">Gus Van Sant</a> to whom River suggested she would be perfect for a role in his upcoming project, a <a href="/wiki/Even_Cowgirls_Get_the_Blues_(film)" title="Even Cowgirls Get the Blues (film)">film adaptation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Robbins" title="Tom Robbins">Tom Robbins</a>'s cult novel, <i><a href="/wiki/Even_Cowgirls_Get_the_Blues_(novel)" title="Even Cowgirls Get the Blues (novel)">Even Cowgirls Get the Blues</a></i>. She was cast as head cowgirl Bonanza Jellybean, alongside <a href="/wiki/Uma_Thurman" title="Uma Thurman">Uma Thurman</a>.</p>
<h2><span id="River_Phoenix's_death"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="River_Phoenix.27s_death">River Phoenix's death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_Phoenix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: River Phoenix's death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A few months after the film's completion, Phoenix flew to Los Angeles with her brother <a href="/wiki/Joaquin_Phoenix" title="Joaquin Phoenix">Joaquin</a> to meet River during a break from <i>Dark Blood</i>. On the evening of October 30, 1993, they set out, joined by River's girlfriend <a href="/wiki/Samantha_Mathis" title="Samantha Mathis">Samantha Mathis</a>, for a night at the Los Angeles club <a href="/wiki/The_Viper_Room" title="The Viper Room">The Viper Room</a>, which is in part owned by <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Depp" title="Johnny Depp">Johnny Depp</a>. It would be the last evening Phoenix would spend with her brother; he died later that night from, according to the coroner's report, "acute multiple drug intoxication," namely an overdose of heroin and <a href="/wiki/Cocaine" title="Cocaine">cocaine</a>, a concoction called a "<a href="/wiki/Speedball_(drug)" title="Speedball (drug)">speedball</a>".</p>
<p>Phoenix dealt simultaneously with the loss of her older brother and the media circus that followed. Aleka's Attic, by that time consisting a different line-up, had been working on an album that River had been funding himself. The album was near completion so Phoenix began the long task of mixing and producing the scraps of recordings. The album has never been released.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Late_nineties">Late nineties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_Phoenix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Late nineties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1994, Phoenix joined <a href="/wiki/R.E.M." title="R.E.M.">R.E.M.</a> in the studio to record backup vocals on the song "Bang and Blame", to be released on the 1995 album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monster_(R.E.M.)" title="Monster (R.E.M.)">Monster</a></i>, an album dedicated to River. In 1995 Phoenix joined the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a> as a backup singer on their <i><a href="/wiki/One_Hot_Minute" title="One Hot Minute">One Hot Minute</a></i> tour. Chili Peppers bassist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_Balzary" title="Michael Balzary">Flea</a>, who appeared in <a href="/wiki/My_Own_Private_Idaho" title="My Own Private Idaho">My Own Private Idaho</a>, was close friends with River, and was there the night he died. The band wrote their song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transcending" title="Transcending">Transcending</a>" as a tribute to River. She also features in the video for R.E.M.'s "<a href="/wiki/At_My_Most_Beautiful" title="At My Most Beautiful">At My Most Beautiful</a>."</p>
<p>In 1997, Phoenix joined the <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk</a>/<a href="/wiki/New_wave_music" title="New wave music">new wave</a> band <a href="/wiki/The_Causey_Way" title="The Causey Way">The Causey Way</a>, which also included her younger sisters <a href="/wiki/Summer_Phoenix" title="Summer Phoenix">Summer</a> and Liberty. They disbanded in 2001 after the release of two albums and two EPs.</p>
<p>Phoenix returned to acting in 1998 with parts in <i><a href="/wiki/I_Woke_Up_Early_the_Day_I_Died" title="I Woke Up Early the Day I Died">I Woke Up Early the Day I Died</a></i> (1998), <i><a href="/wiki/O_(film)" title="O (film)">O</a></i> (2001), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harry_and_Max" title="Harry and Max">Harry and Max</a></i> (2004) and <i><a href="/wiki/Hitch_(film)" title="Hitch (film)">Hitch</a></i> (2005).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Papercranes">Papercranes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_Phoenix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Papercranes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Phoenix formed the band <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Papercranes_(band)" title="Papercranes (band)">Papercranes</a> in 2003 and is lead singer and lyricist. Their self-titled EP was released in 2004 and their first album, <i>Vidalia</i>, in 2006. Their album "Let's Make Babies in The Woods" was released in early 2011. Guest musicians include Phoenix's younger sisters Summer and Liberty, as well as actor <a href="/wiki/Dermot_Mulroney" title="Dermot Mulroney">Dermot Mulroney</a>, Libby Lavella, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_Balzary" title="Michael Balzary">Flea</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a>, who also was a guest musician on tracks for <a href="/wiki/Aleka%27s_Attic" title="Aleka's Attic">Aleka's Attic</a> in the '90s.</p>
<p>Phoenix continues to split her time between writing, recording and performing with the band and working on various TV and film projects. It was announced in 2010 that <a href="/wiki/Papercranes" title="Papercranes">Papercranes</a> had signed with <a href="/wiki/Manimal_Vinyl" title="Manimal Vinyl">Manimal Vinyl</a> and they released their latest second album <i>Let's Make Babies in the Woods</i> in January 2011.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_initiatives">Social initiatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rain_Phoenix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Social initiatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Phoenix, together with her brother <a href="/wiki/Joaquin_Phoenix" title="Joaquin Phoenix">Joaquin Phoenix</a>, is on the board of directors for <a href="/wiki/The_Lunchbox_Fund" title="The Lunchbox Fund">The Lunchbox Fund</a>, a non-profit organization which provides a daily meal to students of township schools in <a href="/wiki/Soweto" title="Soweto">Soweto</a>, South Africa.</p>
