<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Rain (opera)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><i><b>Rain</b></i>, originally entitled <i><b>Sadie Thompson</b></i> (1997), is an American opera by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_Owen_(composer)" title="Richard Owen (composer)">Richard Owen</a>, based on <a href="/wiki/Rain_(short_story)" title="Rain (short story)">the 1921 short story <i>Rain</i></a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Somerset_Maugham" title="Somerset Maugham">Somerset Maugham</a>. The first version, named not after the story title, but like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sadie_Thompson_(film)" title="Sadie Thompson (film)">the 1928 Gloria Swanson film</a> named after the female leading character, Sadie Thompson, was premiered 20 November 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The revised version of the opera, <i>Rain</i>, was premiered by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Camerata_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Camerata New York (page does not exist)">Camerata New York</a> at <a href="/wiki/Alice_Tully_Hall" title="Alice Tully Hall">Alice Tully Hall</a> 20 February, 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A recording of the 2003 performances was issued on <a href="/wiki/Albany_Records" title="Albany Records">Albany Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
